# Speaking Club Thematic & Grammatical Taxonomy

This document provides a comprehensive classification system mapping all COSYlanguages speaking club sessions (including English, French, and Russian versions) to primary themes, secondary/supplementary themes, and target grammatical structures. This detailed index makes it exceptionally easy for teachers to retrieve relevant speaking club materials and songs to complement their structured curriculum lessons.

---

## 2. Speaking Club Primary Mapping Guidelines

Each of the nine active speaking clubs has a default thematic center, but individual sessions can branch out widely.

1. **Keeping Up with Science 🔬**
   - *Default Main Theme:* `technology` / `nature` / `environment`
   - *Typical Secondary Themes:* `psychology`, `school` (education), `health_medicine`

2. **Let's Celebrate 🎉**
   - *Default Main Theme:* `social` / `art_culture`
   - *Typical Secondary Themes:* `people` (family/relationships), `time`

3. **The Greatest Quotes 💬**
   - *Default Main Theme:* `psychology` (philosophy/ethics) / `people`
   - *Typical Secondary Themes:* `art_culture` (literature/art), `emotions`, `language`

4. **Mind Matters 🧠**
   - *Default Main Theme:* `psychology`
   - *Typical Secondary Themes:* `emotions`, `body` (well-being), `people` (relationships)

5. **My Life With & Without 🚗**
   - *Default Main Theme:* `social` / `places` / `travel`
   - *Typical Secondary Themes:* `technology` (internet/devices), `animals` (pets), `shopping`

6. **Debatable & Relatable ⚖️**
   - *Default Main Theme:* `social` / `work`
   - *Typical Secondary Themes:* `people`, `technology`, `environment`

7. **Cinema Club 🎬**
   - *Default Main Theme:* `art_culture`
   - *Typical Secondary Themes:* `emotions`, `people`, `jobs` / `work`, `travel`

8. **Karaoke Club 🎤**
   - *Default Main Theme:* `music` / `art_culture`
   - *Typical Secondary Themes:* `language` (slang/accents), `emotions`, `social`

9. **I Couldn't Help But Wonder 🌌**
   - *Default Main Theme:* `psychology` (philosophy)
   - *Typical Secondary Themes:* `nature` (universe/cosmic), `time`

## 4. How Teachers Can Leverage This System (Pedagogical Delivery)

A thematic & grammatical mapping enables rapid, intentional lesson designs. Teachers can integrate these past sessions directly into standard General or Spoken courses:

1. **Curriculum Integration (Vocabulary Priming):**
   - *Example:* When teaching the "Environment & Ecology" module in Unit 4 of the B2 General Course, the teacher can assign **"Keeping Up with Science: Climate Scientist warming report"** or **"Cinema Night: Don't Look Up"** as the speaking club for that week. This ensures students practice the exact same active vocabulary under different interactive layouts.
2. **Grammar Consolidation:**
   - *Example:* A lesson on **Hypotheticals (Conditionals)** can be supplemented with **"Debatable & Relatable: The 4-Day Work Week"** (e.g., *"If you only worked 4 days, would your output decrease?"*) or **"Mind Matters: Murphy's Law"** (e.g., *"Whatever can go wrong, will go wrong"*).
3. **Slang & Regional Variety Accents:**
   - *Example:* To introduce students to standard Cockney or London working-class registers, the teacher can retrieve the **"Cinema Session: Beautiful Thing"** (slang: *geezer*, *sorted*, *skint*). For formal mid-Atlantic American registers, retrieve **"Cinema Session: Breakfast at Tiffany's"** or **"The Queen's Gambit"**.
4. **Active Recall Practice:**
   - *Example:* Teachers can use the exact 10 vocabulary items of a mapped speaking session to create customized active recall cards in the Practice tab before the live conversation begins, maximizing student engagement.

## 5. The Future of Themes: From Accidental Threads to Curated Monthly Streams

Historically, COSYlanguages speaking club topics were selected dynamically by teachers based on localized student interest. This created several brilliant "implicit" threads running quietly through our content. By codifying these accidental threads and bridging them with a deliberate "rotating seasonal theme" model, we can transition from disjointed sessions to structured, highly engaging cross-club monthly curricula.

### A. Existing "Implicit" Thematic Threads (Cross-Club Connections)
These threads already run deeply across **Wonder (🌌)**, **Karaoke (🎤)**, **Science (🔬)**, and other clubs. By making them deliberate, teachers can connect diverse linguistic assets (songs, debates, scientific texts) within a single course unit.

1. **Mortality & Impermanence**
   *   *Core Premise:* Speculative and existential reflections on endings, illness, and legacy.
   *   *Existing Mapped Material:*
       *   `assisted-dying.html` (Debatability of final medical choices)
       *   `unlikely-lovers.html` (Cinema Session: Terminal illness and romance)

### C. The "Let's Celebrate" Seasonal Anchor Model
Rather than scheduling holidays in isolation, **"Let's Celebrate" 🎉** will act as the rotating "Theme of the Season" coordinator. This anchors cross-club thematic streams to real calendar milestones that students intuitively expect.

*   **September – Autumn Equinox & Harvest (Theme: Preservation & Food Rituals)**
    *   *Celebrate:* Harvest festivals across the world.
    *   *My Life With/Without:* `ugly-produce-anti-waste.html` (Preserving food, reducing waste).
    *   *Karaoke:* Autumnal songs, songs of comfort.
*   **December – Winter Solstice & Year-End (Theme: Endings, Death & New Beginnings)**
    *   *Celebrate:* `lunar-new-year.html` and global solstice traditions.
    *   *Wonder:* Euthanasia, mortality, and the ending of historical cycles.
    *   *Quotes:* `favorite-days-not-happened.html` (Reflections on regret).
*   **May – Spring Awakening (Theme: Rebirth, Nature & Secrets)**
    *   *Celebrate:* May Day, Beltane, and spring festivals.
    *   *Science:* Sensory networks, animal cooperation, hidden powers.
    *   *Mind Matters:* Breaking habits, fresh psychological starts.
