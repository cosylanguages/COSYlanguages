## 2. Excluded Titles & Justifications

Some titles proposed in the raw brainstorming list are excluded from full production mapping to maintain strict quality standards and logical continuity in database management:

1. **The Devil Wears Prada 2** (Excluded):
   * *Justification:* No actual sequel exists in film format (only rumor / pre-production / book sequel). We instead merged and dedicated a top-tier session to the iconic original **The Devil Wears Prada 1**, ensuring the materials refer to concrete cinematic reference scenes.
2. **Good American Family** (Excluded):
   * *Justification:* Extremely obscure, lacking broad international availability and standard subtitles. Replaced with more widely accessible cultural milestones to make discussion highly relatable.
3. **Anesthesia (2015)** (Excluded):
   * *Justification:* Very low-profile indie movie. Hard for non-native students to find or relate to. We replaced it with **Still Alice** (same period/grief focus but far more commercially prominent and with premium pedagogical resources).

## 3. Pedagogical Content Standards

All Cinema Club events and sessions strictly implement COSYlanguages standards:
* **Level Mapping:** Every session belongs to a clear, standardized level descriptor (`starter`, `elementary`, `intermediate`, `upper_intermediate`, `advanced`).
* **The 10-10-10 Rule:** 10 core vocabulary items practiced in both Round 1 (discussion) and Round 2 (critical statements/agree-disagree) to move passive knowledge into active speech.
* **Types of English/Language:** Varieties like *British English*, *American English*, *Mid-Atlantic*, or foreign target languages (French, Russian, Spanish) are explicitly noted so students learn authentic regional variations and idiomatic structures.
* **Teachers Note Section:** Features 3-5 authentic corrections matching common language leaks observed during session analysis.
