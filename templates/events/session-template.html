✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
Developer Correction & Feedback Guidelines:
1. Do not cross out or strike through the whole sentence or phrase. Instead, accentuate/highlight/underline the specific error (using <u> tags or background classes) so students see exactly where the grammatical or lexical break occurs.
2. Level-Appropriate Diversification:
- Starter/Elementary (A1-A2): Direct grammatical corrections, spelling, and basic structural alignments (e.g., subject-verb agreement).
- Intermediate (B1-B2): Word choice improvements, better alternatives, and common prepositional collocations.
- Advanced (C1-C2): Sophisticated synonyms, more precise or specific lexical options, and stylistic/register adjustments.
He have went to Paris → He has gone to Paris (Underlined error: 'have' and auxiliary usage. Correction: Present perfect singular 'has' + past participle 'gone')
We discussed about the problem → We discussed the problem / had a discussion about the problem (Underlined prepositional error. Better collocation: 'discuss' is transitive and takes no preposition, or use 'have a discussion about')
His explanation was very clear → His explanation was exceptionally lucid / crystal clear / coherent (Elevating vocabulary with highly specific advanced options and sophisticated synonyms)
