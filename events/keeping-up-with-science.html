🔵 Round 1 — Questions & Analysis▼
Questions

Theoretical questions linked to the article. 10 main + 1 personal each.

1. What is the main discovery or idea in the article? Can you explain it in your own words?
★ Had you heard about this topic before? What did you already know?
2. Why do scientists think this discovery is important? What problem does it try to solve?
★ Does this feel relevant to your everyday life? Why or why not?
3. What evidence or experiments does the article describe to support the claim?
★ Do you find this evidence convincing?
4. Are there any limitations or risks mentioned in the article?
★ Does knowing the risks change how you feel about the discovery?
5. Who benefits most from this development — and who might not?
★ Do you think science tends to serve everyone equally in your country?

✏️ Discussion (Mistake Notes)▼
I am agree→I agree("agree" is a verb, not an adjective)
He make→He makes(3rd person singular: add -s)
+ "on the one hand … on the other hand" — great way to present two sides
I didn't knew→I didn't know(auxiliary "did" already marks past tense)
