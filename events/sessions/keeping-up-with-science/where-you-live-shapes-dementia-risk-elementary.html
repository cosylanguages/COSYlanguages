Resources

ScienceDaily Article 📖

This session explores how our living environment affects our brain health. A massive new study has found that where you live can influence your risk of developing dementia. We will talk about green spaces, fresh air, and how to stay healthy in our neighborhoods.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Risk
the possibility that something unpleasant or dangerous will happen.
Living near busy roads can increase the risk of breathing problems.
Environment
the surroundings or conditions in which a person, animal, or plant lives.
A clean environment is very important for our physical health.
Brain
the organ inside the head that controls thought, memory, feelings, and activity.
Reading books and solving puzzles keeps your brain healthy and active.
Healthy ≠ Sick
in good physical or mental condition ≠ affected by physical or mental illness.
Eating fresh vegetables helps you stay healthy, but eating too much sugar can make you sick.
Massive
exceptionally large; huge.
Scientists analyzed a massive amount of medical data for this study.
Neighborhood
a district or community within a town or city.
There is a very friendly atmosphere in our small neighborhood.
Discover
find unexpectedly or during a search.
Researchers discover new facts about human biology every single day.
Study
a detailed investigation and analysis of a subject or situation.
The medical study lasted for more than ten years.
Protect
keep safe from harm or injury.
Wearing a helmet helps protect your head when riding a bicycle.
Influence
have an effect on the character, development, or behavior of someone or something.
Your friends can have a big influence on your daily choices.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • Do you live in a quiet countryside area or a noisy city center?
  • What do you do to keep your brain healthy and active?
🔵 Round 1 — Theoretical Discussion▲
Discussion
How can our living environment influence our daily health?
★ Do you prefer living in a bustling big city or a quiet countryside town?
What is the biggest health risk in a polluted city?
★ How easily can you find a public park or green garden near your current home?
Why is it important for scientists to conduct a massive study on brain health?
★ Do you feel that your local neighborhood is safe and pleasant for walking?
What activities can help protect our brain as we get older?
★ How does the amount of natural sunlight in your home affect your daily mood?
How does your neighborhood feel in the summer compared to the winter?
★ What is your favorite way to stay active and get fresh air on the weekends?
Do you think cities should spend more money to build green spaces?
★ Is there a lot of traffic noise or air pollution where you currently live?
Why is sleep very important for a healthy brain?
★ Did your childhood home have a garden or big trees nearby where you played?
Do you walk every day to stay active and fit?
★ How often do you interact with or greet your neighbors in your daily routine?
How do scientists discover new things about our body?
★ What is the most peaceful and quiet place you have visited in your region?
What is the healthiest meal you prepared this week?
★ Do you think the layout of a city can encourage people to lead healthier lives?

Predictions

Green spaces will become mandatory in all urban architecture projects.
★ Would you support a complete ban on cars in city centers to reduce noise and pollution?
Solving puzzles every day will prevent all brain illnesses.
★ Should employers offer outdoor green-working hours to prevent mental fatigue?
All schools will teach kids about the importance of fresh air and nature.
★ Would you use a map app that recommends walking routes based on clean air and low noise?
Future houses will change their location automatically to find the best weather.
★ Do you think real estate values should be determined by environmental health indexes?
In the future, people will only live in communities with shared gardens and parks.
★ Would you live in a futuristic eco-commune with shared gardens and no private cars?
Smart necklaces will monitor air pollution around us and warn us to go inside.
★ What is the most important change you would make to improve your current neighborhood?
Future schools will be built entirely in the middle of forests to help children focus.
★ Do you think the next generation will be healthier physically because of smart cities?
All transportation in future cities will be completely silent and electric.
★ How do you personally protect your peace of mind and mental quiet in a busy world?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I am live in a green city. → I live in a green city. (Verb conjugation: do not use the auxiliary 'am' with the active verb 'live'.)
He is more healthy than me. → He is healthier than me. (Comparative suffix: 'healthy' becomes 'healthier'.)
There is many parks here. → There are many parks here. (Plural agreement: use 'are' when talking about multiple parks.)
