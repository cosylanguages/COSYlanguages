Full Article 📖

This session explores exciting scientific research showing that the human brain can continue to improve and adapt even into a person's 90s. We will discuss the concept of neuroplasticity and the cognitive exercises that help maintain brain health in later life. Participants will reflect on the lifestyle choices, physical activities, and mental challenges that support healthy aging.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Neuroplasticity
the ability of the brain to form and reorganize synaptic connections.
Learning a new skill stimulates neuroplasticity at any age.
Aging
the process of growing old.
Healthy aging involves both physical exercise and mental stimulation.
Improve
to make or become better.
Your memory can actually improve if you practice regularly.
Adapt
to become adjusted to new conditions.
The brain can adapt to damage by finding new neural pathways.
Cognitive
relating to the mental action or process of acquiring knowledge.
Reading books is an excellent cognitive exercise for seniors.
Lifelong
lasting or remaining throughout a person's life.
She has a lifelong interest in learning foreign languages.
Retain
to continue to have something or keep in mind.
Active learners can retain information much better as they age.
Stimulation
encouragement of something to make it more active.
Social interaction provides great mental stimulation for elderly people.
Decline
a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, or quality.
A healthy lifestyle can slow down cognitive decline.
Wellness
the state of being in good health, especially as an actively pursued goal.
Mental wellness is as important as physical fitness.
