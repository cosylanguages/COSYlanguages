Full Article 📖

This session explores exciting scientific research showing that the human brain can continue to improve and adapt even into a person's 90s. We will discuss the concept of neuroplasticity and the cognitive exercises that help maintain brain health in later life. Participants will reflect on the lifestyle choices, physical activities, and mental challenges that support healthy aging.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Neuroplasticity
the ability of the brain to form and reorganize synaptic connections.
Learning a new skill stimulates neuroplasticity at any age.
Aging
the process of growing old.
Healthy aging involves both physical exercise and mental stimulation.
Improve
to make or become better.
Your memory can actually improve if you practice regularly.
Adapt
to become adjusted to new conditions.
The brain can adapt to damage by finding new neural pathways.
Cognitive
relating to the mental action or process of acquiring knowledge.
Reading books is an excellent cognitive exercise for seniors.
Lifelong
lasting or remaining throughout a person's life.
She has a lifelong interest in learning foreign languages.
Retain
to continue to have something or keep in mind.
Active learners can retain information much better as they age.
Stimulation
encouragement of something to make it more active.
Social interaction provides great mental stimulation for elderly people.
Decline
a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, or quality.
A healthy lifestyle can slow down cognitive decline.
Wellness
the state of being in good health, especially as an actively pursued goal.
Mental wellness is as important as physical fitness.

🟠 Warm-up▲
Memory and Mind
  • What is the oldest age of someone you know personally who is still very sharp?
  • Do you think our brain naturally gets worse as we grow older?
🔵 Round 1 — Theoretical Discussion▲
Questions
How does the concept of neuroplasticity change our view of aging?
★ What daily habits do you think are most important for keeping your mind sharp?
Can our cognitive abilities actually improve or adapt in our 90s?
★ Have you ever tried learning a new language or instrument just for fun?
What kind of cognitive tasks are most effective for elderly individuals?
★ Who is the oldest person you know, and how do they stay active?
How does a habit of lifelong learning protect the brain from decline?
★ Do you prefer doing puzzles, reading, or playing physical sports to challenge yourself?
Why is it important to retain mental active habits?
★ How do you handle forgetfulness or minor memory slips in your daily life?
How does social stimulation contribute to mental wellness?
★ Do you find that physical exercise helps you think more clearly?
Is physical exercise as important as mental exercise for brain health?
★ What is a complex skill you learned recently that took a lot of mental effort?
How does stress affect our cognitive abilities over time?
★ Do you think modern technology makes our brains lazier or more active?
Why do some people experience faster decline than others?
★ How often do you step out of your comfort zone to try something completely new?
What role does nutrition play in maintaining neural wellness?
★ What is your favorite way to wind down and give your brain a deep rest?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Projections
In the future, the retirement age will increase to 80 because our brains will stay healthy longer.
★ Would you take a daily pill if it guaranteed your brain would stay 20 years younger?
Brain training software will eventually replace traditional textbooks in schools.
★ Do you think society treats elderly people as wise contributors or as a burden?
We will discover a medical cure that completely stops cognitive decline in the elderly.
★ If you lived to be 100, what kind of project or hobby would you like to pursue?
Future societies will value the wisdom of senior citizens more than the speed of young workers.
★ Should there be a mandatory retirement age, or should people work as long as they want?
People will live active and healthy lives past 120 years old by the end of this century.
★ Would you allow a brain chip to monitor and optimize your cognitive speed?
Computers and AI will make human cognitive training unnecessary.
★ How do you hope to maintain your mental independence when you get older?
By 2040, governments will provide free lifetime education to encourage active aging and brain health.
★ Do you think future technology will make retirement homes completely obsolete?
In the future, a person's attitude towards aging will be recognized as more important than their genes in determining brain health.
★ What is the most important piece of wisdom you want to pass down to future generations?
In the future, we will have mandatory brain assessments for drivers over the age of 75.
★ Do you believe that wisdom only comes with old age, or can young people be wise too?
Future doctors will prescribe learning a second language as the single most effective way to prevent dementia.
★ How has your own perspective on aging changed over the last ten years?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
My grandfather is 90 years→My grandfather is 90 years old(Specify 'old' or omit)
I hope to live until 100→I hope to live to 100(Use 'to' for age limits)
It depend of your genes→It depends on your genes(Preposition 'on' after depend)
