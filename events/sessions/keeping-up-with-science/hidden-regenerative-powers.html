Full Article 📖

This session covers recent studies suggesting that human tissues possess hidden regenerative capabilities similar to some lower vertebrates. We will discuss the biological mechanisms of wound healing and the potential of regenerative medicine to restore damaged organs. We will also touch upon the future ethical implications and possibilities of longevity sciences.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Regenerative
relating to the regrowth of damaged or lost tissue.
Regenerative medicine aims to regrow organs in laboratories.
Tissue
any of the distinct types of material of which animals or plants are made.
Muscle tissue is made of specialized cells that can contract.
Heal
to become sound or healthy again.
A healthy immune system helps the body heal wounds quickly.
Mechanism
a system of parts working together in a machine or a process.
Scientists are studying the biological mechanism of cell division.
Vertebrate
an animal of a large group distinguished by the possession of a backbone.
Lizards are vertebrates known for their ability to regrow tails.
Capability
the power or ability to do something.
Humans have some natural capability to regenerate liver tissue.
Longevity
long life or existence.
Good genes and healthy food contribute to longevity.
Organ
a part of the body that has a specific vital function.
The heart is a vital organ that pumps blood.
Restore
to return something to its former condition.
Doctors hope this new therapy can restore damaged heart tissue.
Biological
relating to biology or living organisms.
Our biological clock regulates sleep patterns.

🟠 Warm-up▲
Healing and Biology
  • Have you ever broken a bone? How long did it take to heal?
  • What is one animal ability you wish humans had?
🔵 Round 1 — Theoretical Discussion▲
Questions
What kind of regenerative power do humans currently possess?
★ Have you ever had a scar or wound that healed remarkably well over time?
How does muscle tissue adapt and grow after exercise?
★ Do you find that your body recovers quickly from intense physical exercise or illness?
What is the biological mechanism behind wound healing?
★ What is your personal approach to helping your body heal when you catch a cold?
Why can some vertebrates regrow entire limbs while humans cannot?
★ Do you find human biology and anatomy fascinating or slightly unsettling?
How could regenerative medicine improve human longevity?
★ Have you ever donated blood or tissue? What was the experience like?
What are the challenges of regenerating a complex organ like the heart?
★ How much do you trust modern medicine compared to your body's natural healing?
How can science restore lost sensory functions using biotechnology?
★ What is the most remarkable recovery from injury or illness you have ever witnessed?
What is the role of stem cells in biological repair?
★ Do you believe that a positive mindset can physically speed up biological healing?
How do lifestyle choices affect our body's healing capability?
★ How often do you think about the biological limits of the human body?
To what extent should we interfere with natural biological limits?
★ What daily habit do you believe contributes most to your physical vitality?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Projections
In the future, humans will be able to regrow lost fingers or toes using genetic therapy.
★ Would you undergo genetic therapy to regain lost physical tissues or cartilage?
Regenerative medicine will eventually make traditional organ transplants obsolete.
★ Do you think extreme biological regeneration would make humans more reckless?
Biotechnological advancements in longevity will only benefit wealthy people.
★ Should scientists prioritize curing chronic pain over extending the human lifespan?
In the future, we will prohibit genetic modification aimed at extending the human lifespan beyond 100.
★ Would you want to live in a future where any damaged organ can be printed in a lab?
In the future, growing synthetic organs in a lab will be seen as completely ethical because it saves lives.
★ Do you think artificial organs will eventually perform better than our biological ones?
In the future, human cloning for the purpose of organ harvesting will be banned worldwide.
★ How do you think society would change if the human average lifespan reached 150?
Future armies will use regenerative therapies to quickly heal injured soldiers.
★ Would you support the creation of safe synthetic biological enhancements for athletes?
We will develop a method to reverse muscle tissue aging within the next 20 years.
★ Do you think humans will ever lose their natural healing abilities by relying too much on medicine?
Living forever would make human existence boring and meaningless.
★ What is the most important medical breakthrough you hope to see in your lifetime?
We will eventually discover that nature intentionally limited human regeneration to prevent overpopulation.
★ Would you choose to have a minor cosmetic flaw regenerated if it was completely free?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The lizards can to regrow tail→Lizards can regrow their tails(Can + infinitive without 'to', plural noun)
He was operated last week→He had an operation last week(Passive structure vs having an operation)
I am agree with scientifics→I agree with the scientists(Agree is a verb, noun 'scientists')
