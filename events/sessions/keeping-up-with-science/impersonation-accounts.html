Topic

Cybersecurity and Digital Fraud

Impersonation accounts are a growing threat in the digital age, used for everything from social engineering to financial fraud. This session explores the technical and psychological tactics used by scammers to mimic real individuals and organizations. We will discuss how to identify these fraudulent profiles and the legal challenges of protecting our digital identities.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Fraudulent
obtained, done by, or involving deception, especially criminal deception.
The bank warned its customers about a series of fraudulent emails asking for passwords.
Verification
the process of establishing the truth, accuracy, or validity of something.
Two-step verification is an essential security measure for protecting your online accounts.
Identity
the fact of being who or what a person or thing is.
Digital identity theft can take months or even years to fully resolve.
Phishing
the fraudulent practice of sending emails to induce individuals to reveal personal information.
He realized the message was a phishing attempt when he noticed the suspicious URL.
Authentic
of undisputed origin; genuine.
Always check for the blue checkmark to ensure that a celebrity's profile is authentic.
Vulnerability ≠ Invulnerability
state of being exposed to the possibility of being attacked or harmed ≠ the state of being impossible to harm or defeat.
Embracing vulnerability is a far more courageous path than pretending to possess absolute invulnerability.
Credential
a qualification, achievement, personal quality, or aspect of a person's background.
You should never share your login credentials with anyone, even if they claim to be from tech support.
Malicious
intending or intended to do harm.
Malicious software can infect your computer through a single suspicious download.
Pretext
a reason given in justification of a course of action that is not the real reason.
The scammer contacted him under the pretext of needing help with a charity donation.
Surveillance ≠ Privacy
close observation of individuals ≠ the state of being free from public attention.
Constant surveillance is a threat to the fundamental human right to privacy.
