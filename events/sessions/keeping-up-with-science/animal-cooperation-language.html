Full Article 📖

This session explores the sophisticated communication systems that animals use to organize cooperative behaviors. We will analyze how different species coordinate tasks, warn of danger, and share resources using vocal, visual, and chemical signals. The discussion will compare these animal languages with human speech and social structures.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Coordinate
to bring the different elements of a complex activity into an efficient relationship.
Wolves coordinate their movements during a pack hunt.
Signal
a gesture, action, or sound that is used to send information.
Birds use a distinct high-pitched signal to warn of aerial predators.
Cooperative
involving mutual assistance in working towards a common goal.
Ants show highly cooperative behavior when building complex underground nests.
Species
a group of living organisms consisting of similar individuals.
There are several species of bees that live in highly organized colonies.
Sophisticated
having or showing a lot of experience and relations; complex.
Dolphins communicate using sophisticated clicks and whistles.
Vocal
relating to the human voice or animal vocalizations.
Meerkats use vocal calls to coordinate sentinel duties.
Warn
to inform someone or an animal in advance of an impending danger.
Monkeys make specific alarm calls to warn others of leopards.
Resource
a stock or supply of money, materials, staff, or food.
Animals must cooperate to protect their shared food resource from competitors.
Social structure
the internal institutionalized relationships in a group of animals.
The social structure of an elephant herd is led by an older matriarch.
Speech
the expression of or the ability to express thoughts and feelings by spoken sounds.
Human speech is more flexible than any animal communication system.
