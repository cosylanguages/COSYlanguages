Full Article 📖

This session is about a new science study on animal sounds. We will talk about how great apes, like chimpanzees, make laughter sounds when they play. We will also learn how these funny sounds help scientists understand the origin of human speech.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Laughter
the sound of laughing when you are happy or hear something funny.
Laughter is the best medicine for a sad day.
Origin
the point where something begins or starts.
Scientists are looking for the origin of human languages.
Speech
the ability to speak or express thoughts with spoken words.
His speech at the wedding was very beautiful and funny.
Ape
a large animal with no tail that is closely related to humans (e.g. chimpanzee).
The chimpanzee is a very smart ape that lives in Africa.
Reveal
to show or make something known to others.
These new facts reveal the secret of how birds migrate.
Sound
something that you can hear.
I love the sound of rain on my window at night.
Play
to do activities for fun and enjoyment.
The young puppies love to play in the green garden.
Similar
looking or sounding almost the same as something else.
Your new smartphone is very similar to mine.
Tickle
to touch someone lightly to make them laugh.
Children usually laugh a lot when you tickle their feet.
Scientist
a person who studies or has expert knowledge of science.
The scientist discovered a new species of plants in the forest.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The chimpanzees is smart→Chimpanzees are smart(Plural subject needs plural verb)
I am laugh a lot→I laugh a lot(Simple present structure)
It depend of animal→It depends on the animal(Depends + on, singular s)
