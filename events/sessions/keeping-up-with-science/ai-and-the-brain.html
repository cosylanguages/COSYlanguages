Artificial Intelligence and neuroscience are increasingly intersecting, as researchers look to the human brain to improve machine learning algorithms. This session explores the reciprocal relationship between biological and artificial neural networks, investigating how each field informs the other. We will discuss the ethical implications of brain-computer interfaces and whether machines can ever truly replicate human cognition.

📖 Session Vocabulary

synapse
the junction between two nerve cells, where signals pass.
Neural networks try to mimic how a biological synapse transfers signals.
neuroplasticity
the brain's ability to reorganize itself by forming new neural connections.
AI models are still far less adaptable than human neuroplasticity.
deep learning
a subset of machine learning based on artificial neural networks.
Deep learning has revolutionized voice and image recognition.
algorithm
a process or set of rules to be followed in calculations or decision-making.
The translation algorithm became more accurate by analyzing thousands of books.
cognitive load
the total amount of mental effort being used in the working memory.
Constant notifications can increase your cognitive load and reduce focus.
artificial intelligence
computer systems able to perform human-like cognitive tasks.
Many researchers are worried about artificial intelligence replacing creative writers.
Turing test
a test of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to a human.
The new conversational bot was so convincing it easily passed the Turing test.
neuromorphic
hardware designed to mimic the physical structure and function of the human brain.
Neuromorphic chips use much less power than traditional silicon processors.
algorithmic bias
systematic and repeatable errors in a computer system that create unfair outcomes.
We must be cautious of algorithmic bias when using AI for job recruitment.
consciousness
the state of being aware of and responsive to one's surroundings.
No machine has ever shown any sign of true self-awareness or consciousness.

🔵 Round 1 — Theoretical Discussion▲
Questions
How do scientists use our understanding of the human synapse to build better AI systems?
★ Do you think machines will ever understand human emotions as well as a close friend does?
What is neuroplasticity and why is it so difficult for computers to replicate human adaptability?
★ Have you ever tried to learn a totally new skill as an adult? How hard was it for your brain?
How has deep learning changed the way we interact with technology on a daily basis?
★ Are you comfortable with algorithms predicting what products or movies you will like next?
Why does a complex algorithm sometimes make decisions that seem completely illogical to humans?
★ Have you ever had an experience where a digital system or app made a mistake that frustrated you?
How can we reduce our cognitive load when working with highly automated digital tools?
★ Do you ever take 'digital detox' days to give your brain a rest from screens?
In what ways can artificial intelligence enhance, rather than replace, human creativity?
★ Would you read a book or listen to a song if you knew it was completely generated by AI?
Why is the Turing test still considered a milestone, even if it doesn't prove true intelligence?
★ Have you ever spoken to a customer service chatbot and felt like you were talking to a real person?
What are the main advantages of neuromorphic computer chips for the future of energy consumption?
★ Are you worried about the environmental impact of running massive data centers for AI?
How does algorithmic bias develop, and who is responsible for correcting it?
★ Have you ever felt that a social media platform was showing you biased or one-sided information?
If a computer eventually achieves true consciousness, what rights should it be granted?
★ What is your personal definition of what makes a mind truly 'alive'?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Future Focus
In the future, neuromorphic brain chips will allow humans to download new languages instantly.
★ Would you get a brain implant if it meant you could speak ten languages fluently without studying?
AI systems will eventually possess a higher level of creative inspiration than human artists.
★ Do you think creativity is just a complex set of rules, or is there something spiritual about it?
By 2040, most people will prefer AI companions over human relationships because they are non-judgmental.
★ Why do you think some people find it easier to open up to a machine than to a human?
Algorithmic bias will become so severe that AI decisions in courts and medicine will be banned.
★ Would you trust an AI doctor to diagnose a serious illness more than a human doctor?
The Turing test is outdated; we need new tests to measure if machines possess genuine empathy.
★ How can you tell if someone is showing genuine empathy or just saying the right words?
Constant exposure to AI assistants will permanently weaken the cognitive abilities of the next generation.
★ Do you think relying on search engines has made your own memory better or worse?
Future laws will grant complete legal personhood to autonomous AI systems that show signs of consciousness.
★ If an AI asked you not to turn it off because it was afraid, would you hesitate?
Governments will use deep learning to predict and prevent crimes before they even happen.
★ Would you sacrifice some of your privacy to live in a completely safe, crime-free city?
Our brain's neuroplasticity will adapt to digital implants, changing what it means to be human.
★ How far is too far when it comes to upgrading our physical bodies with technology?
Within fifty years, all scientific breakthroughs will be discovered by AI, leaving humans as mere observers.
★ Does a future where machines do all the thinking feel exciting or depressing to you?
