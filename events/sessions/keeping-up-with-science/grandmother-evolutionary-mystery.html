This session explores the biological and evolutionary mystery of human grandmothers. We will discuss the famous "Grandmother Hypothesis" in biology, the evolutionary benefits of a post-reproductive lifespan, and the vital role elders play in human survival and cooperative breeding. Join us as we explore one of the greatest mysteries in human evolution: why grandmothers may have helped shape our species.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Post-reproductive
relating to the period in a female's life after the capability to reproduce has ceased.
A long post-reproductive lifespan is an extremely rare biological trait among mammals.
Longevity
long life or great duration of individual life.
Our species' high cognitive longevity is linked to the historical support of elder generations.
Hypothesis
a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence for further investigation.
The Grandmother Hypothesis suggests that grandmothering allowed human brains to grow larger.
Cooperative
involving mutual assistance in working toward a common goal.
Human child-rearing is fundamentally cooperative, requiring support beyond the biological mother.
Offspring
a person's child or children, or an animal's young.
Grandmothers help gather extra nutrients to ensure the survival of their offspring's children.
Menopause
the ceasing of menstruation, typically between 45 and 50 years of age.
Humans are one of only five mammal species that experience menopause.
Evolutionary
relating to the gradual development of organisms over generations.
The role of grandparents is a major evolutionary advantage of Homo sapiens.
Wean
to accustom an infant to food other than its mother's milk.
Grandmothers help wean babies earlier, allowing mothers to reproduce sooner.
Foraging
searching for wild food or provisions in nature.
In hunter-gatherer societies, grandmothers do most of the foraging for root vegetables.
Sociobiology
the scientific study of biological aspects of social behavior in organisms.
Sociobiology teaches us that family bonds are deeply rooted in genetic survival.
