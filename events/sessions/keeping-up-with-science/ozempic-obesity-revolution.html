Full Article 📖

This session examines the scientific impact of Ozempic and similar GLP-1 drugs on weight management and obesity treatment. We will analyze the biological mechanisms of appetite suppression and how these medications are transforming modern healthcare. Participants will also discuss the potential long-term side effects and societal debates surrounding pharmaceutical weight loss.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Medication
a substance used for medical treatment, especially a medicine or drug.
He has to take his blood pressure medication every morning.
Revolution
a dramatic and wide-reaching change in conditions, attitudes, or operation.
The discovery of penicillin caused a revolution in medicine.
Obesity
the state of being grossly fat or overweight.
Obesity is a major risk factor for type 2 diabetes.
Treatment
medical care given to a patient for an illness or injury.
The patient responded very well to the new cancer treatment.
Suppress
to forcibly put an end to, or prevent the development of (e.g., appetite).
These weight loss injections work because they suppress hunger.
Satiety
the feeling or state of being sated or full.
Fiber-rich foods help you achieve a longer feeling of satiety.
Metabolism
the chemical processes that occur within a living organism in order to maintain life.
Regular physical exercise can help speed up your metabolism.
Chronic
persisting for a long time or constantly recurring.
Obesity is now officially classified as a chronic disease.
Side effect
a secondary, typically undesirable effect of a drug or medical treatment.
Nausea is a common side effect of many medications.
Prescribe
to advise and authorize the use of a medicine or treatment.
Doctors usually prescribe antibiotics only for bacterial infections.
