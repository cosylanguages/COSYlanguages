Culture Helps Your Body Stay Younger

Resources

ScienceDaily Article 📖

This session looks at how visiting museums, movies, and theater can keep our body and mind young. A new study shows that cultural activities reduce stress and help us live longer, happier lives. We will share our favorite museums and discuss what we like to do in our free time.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Museum
a building in which objects of historical, scientific, artistic, or cultural interest are stored and exhibited.
I love to visit the history museum on rainy Sunday afternoons.
Theater
a building or outdoor area in which plays and other dramatic performances are given.
We bought tickets to see a famous comedy play at the local theater.
Young ≠ Old
having lived or existed for only a short time ≠ having lived for a long time; far advanced in the life span.
Our young daughter loves to run around, while our old dog prefers to sleep near the fireplace.
Culture
the arts and other manifestations of human intellectual achievement.
Learning about the local culture makes traveling much more interesting.
Activity
a thing that a person or group does or has done.
Going for a bike ride is a very healthy weekend activity.
Stress
a state of mental or emotional strain or tension resulting from adverse or demanding circumstances.
Listening to classical music is a great way to reduce daily stress.
Reduce
make smaller or less in amount, degree, or size.
Regular physical exercise can help reduce high blood pressure.
Exhibition
a public display of works of art or items of interest.
There is a new photo exhibition starting at the gallery next week.
Beneficial
favorable or advantageous; resulting in good.
Eating fruits is highly beneficial for your immune system.
Longevity
long life or existence.
A healthy diet and regular social interactions promote longevity.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I am visit the museum often. → I visit the museum often. (Verb conjugation: do not use 'am' before active verbs in simple present tense.)
She is younger than him. → She is younger than him. (Comparative form is correct, but check pronunciation of 'younger'.)
