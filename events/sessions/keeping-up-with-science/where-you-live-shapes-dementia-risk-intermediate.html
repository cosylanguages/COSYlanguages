ScienceDaily Article 📖

This session analyzes a groundbreaking medical study linking geographical location to cognitive decline and dementia risk. We will explore how environmental factors, such as air pollution, socio-economic status, and proximity to green spaces, play a critical role in brain health. Participants will debate how urban planning should adapt to mitigate these risks for aging populations.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Dementia
a chronic or persistent disorder of the mental processes caused by brain disease or injury.
Early physical activity and healthy diets can significantly delay the onset of dementia.
Geographical
relating to the natural features, population, and industries of a region.
The study revealed massive geographical disparities in public healthcare access across the country.
Cognitive
relating to the mental action or process of acquiring knowledge and understanding.
Solving logical puzzles is a fantastic way to maintain cognitive sharpness as we age.
Mitigate ≠ Exacerbate
make something bad less severe, serious, or painful ≠ make a problem, bad situation, or negative feeling worse.
Planting thousands of trees can mitigate urban air pollution, whereas building massive coal factories will exacerbate it.
Proximity
closeness in space, time, or relationship.
Our home's proximity to a public park encourages us to go for daily evening walks.
Socio-economic
relating to or concerned with the interaction of social and economic factors.
A student's socio-economic background can significantly influence their educational opportunities.
Correlation
a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things.
The medical study proved a clear correlation between urban noise pollution and sleep disorders.
Prevalence
the state of being closely frequent or widespread; commonness.
The prevalence of cardiovascular diseases is much higher in countries with poor dietary habits.
Environmental
relating to the natural world and the impact of human activity on its condition.
Air pollution and water scarcity are the most urgent environmental problems of our generation.
Demographic
relating to the structure of populations.
The city's demographic profile has changed rapidly over the past twenty years due to migration.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
We discussed about the geographical factors. → We discussed the geographical factors. (Grammar check: 'discuss' is transitive, do not use the preposition 'about'.)
Living near the park is gooder for health. → Living near the park is better for health. (Comparative form: the comparative of 'good' is 'better'.)
