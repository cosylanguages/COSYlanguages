Full Article 📖

This session explores how the human brain processes and dismantles compound words at lightning speed. It highlights the fascinating discovery that the brain recognizes real compound words like 'football' much faster than pseudo-compounds or single nouns like 'shamrock'. We will investigate the neural pathways and cognitive steps involved in visual word recognition.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Compound word
a word made of two or more words.
The word 'sunflower' is a classic compound word.
Dismantle
to take something apart.
Our brain must dismantle compound words to understand them.
Lightning speed
an extremely fast movement or process.
Computers process information at lightning speed.
Neural pathway
a series of connected neurons sending signals in the brain.
Learning a language builds new neural pathways in the brain.
Pseudo-compound
a word that looks like a compound but is actually a single word.
The word 'shamrock' is considered a pseudo-compound by researchers.
Visual mask
a technique used in experiments to hide a word quickly.
The words were shown under a visual mask for only 60 milliseconds.
Recognition
identifying something from having encountered it before.
Visual recognition of objects happens automatically in the visual cortex.
Subconscious
part of the mind of which one is not fully aware.
Our subconscious mind handles many complex linguistic tasks.
Stimulus
something that causes a specific reaction.
The visual stimulus triggered a response in the test subjects.
Cognitive process
mental action or process of acquiring knowledge and understanding.
Reading involves several fast cognitive processes.
