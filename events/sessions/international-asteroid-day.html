📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Asteroid
a small rocky object that travels around the Sun.
Example: Scientists are tracking an asteroid that will pass close to Earth next year.
Impact
a collision between two objects.
Example: The impact of a large asteroid could cause serious damage.
Threat
something that may cause harm or danger.
Example: Experts study possible threats from space.
Detect
to discover or notice something.
Example: New telescopes can detect smaller asteroids than before.
Orbit
the path that an object follows around a planet, star, or moon.
Example: The asteroid follows an orbit around the Sun.
Awareness
knowledge or understanding of a situation.
Example: International Asteroid Day helps raise awareness about space hazards.
Predict
to say what is likely to happen in the future.
Example: Scientists can often predict where an asteroid will travel.
Survival
the state of continuing to live or exist.
Example: Human survival depends on solving many global challenges.
Planetary defense
efforts to protect Earth from dangerous objects in space.
Example: Several countries are investing in planetary defense programs.
Breakthrough
an important discovery or development.
Example: The new telescope was a major breakthrough in asteroid detection.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Space Exploration
  • What is the first thing that comes to your mind when you think of "outer space"?
  • Would you ever want to travel to space if you had the chance?
🔵 Round 1 — Discussion Questions (10 Questions)▲
Questions
1. Why do you think people often ignore the threat of an asteroid impact, even though it could affect everyone?
★ Have you ever worried about a natural or space-related danger?
2. Should governments spend more money on planetary defense, or are there more urgent problems on Earth?
★ If you had to choose, what global problem would you invest in first?
3. How important is it to detect dangerous asteroids as early as possible?
★ Do you usually prefer to detect problems early in your own life, or deal with them when they appear?
4. What kinds of scientific breakthroughs could improve our ability to predict asteroid impacts?
★ Which recent scientific breakthrough has impressed you the most?
5. How does learning about an asteroid's orbit help scientists protect Earth?
★ Have you ever learned something that completely changed how you understood a situation?
6. Why is public awareness about asteroid risks much lower than awareness of climate change or pandemics?
★ What topic do you think deserves much more public attention?
7. Could an asteroid impact change human history more than a major war or economic crisis? Why or why not?
★ Which historical event do you think changed the world the most?
8. Do movies about asteroids increase awareness, or do they give people unrealistic expectations?
★ Have you ever watched a disaster movie that made you think differently about real life?
9. How much can scientists realistically predict about events in space?
★ Are you someone who likes predicting the future, or do you prefer surprises?
10. Why could international cooperation be essential for humanity's survival if a dangerous asteroid were discovered?
★ Which countries do you think cooperate especially well in science today?

🟢 Round 2 — Agree or Disagree (Future-focused) (10 Units)▲
Debate
1. In the future, satellites will secretly detect asteroids decades early, but governments may hide some data to avoid global panic.
★ Would you prefer full transparency or controlled information in global crises?
2. A future breakthrough in physics may allow humans to slightly alter an asteroid’s orbit using sound waves or energy fields.
★ Which “impossible” technology would you want to see become real?
3. One day, asteroid mining companies might intentionally redirect small asteroids toward Earth for easier resource collection.
★ Should space resources be privately owned or strictly controlled by humanity?
4. In the future, an asteroid impact warning might appear only hours before collision because earlier detections are dismissed as errors.
★ Do you trust modern systems to warn us about real dangers?
5. Humanity might become so advanced in planetary defense that fake asteroid threats are used as political weapons.
★ How could societies protect themselves from misinformation about global threats?
6. Future AI systems may predict asteroid paths perfectly, but humans might ignore them because the threat feels emotionally unreal.
★ Do people usually ignore facts when they feel emotionally distant from danger?
7. A small asteroid could one day pass Earth unnoticed, not because we cannot detect it, but because monitoring systems stop sharing data globally.
★ Do you think international cooperation is improving or weakening?
8. In the future, people might develop “asteroid awareness fatigue,” where constant alerts reduce survival instincts instead of improving them.
★ Can too much information ever make people less safe?
9. A scientific breakthrough may allow us to change an asteroid’s orbit, but only at the cost of creating unpredictable side effects in space.
★ Would you accept risky solutions to prevent global disasters?
10. In the distant future, humanity’s survival may depend not on stopping asteroid impacts, but on choosing which ones to ignore strategically.
★ Should humans always try to prevent every global threat, or sometimes prioritize?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The asteroid impact in Earth→The asteroid impact on Earth(Preposition "on" for impact)
We need protect from asteroids→We need to protect ourselves from asteroids(Protect requires an object)
Scientists can to predict→Scientists can predict(Modal verbs don't use "to")
Space is very danger→Space is very dangerous(Adjective "dangerous")
It depends of the size→It depends on the size(Depends on)
