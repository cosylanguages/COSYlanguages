UN Asteroid Day 📖

International Asteroid Day, observed on the anniversary of the 1908 Tunguska event, aims to raise awareness about the potential risk of asteroid impacts and the science of planetary defense. This session explores the history of significant impacts and the technological advancements being made to detect and divert near-Earth objects. We will discuss the balance between the rare but catastrophic threat from space and the immediate challenges we face here on Earth.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Asteroid
A small rocky object that travels around the Sun.
Scientists are tracking an asteroid that will pass close to Earth next year.
Impact
A collision between two objects.
The impact of a large asteroid could cause serious damage.
Threat
Something that may cause harm or danger.
Experts study possible threats from space.
Detect
To discover or notice something.
New telescopes can detect smaller asteroids than before.
Orbit
The path that an object follows around a planet, star, or moon.
The asteroid follows an orbit around the Sun.
Awareness
Knowledge or understanding of a situation.
International Asteroid Day helps raise awareness about space hazards.
Predict
To say what is likely to happen in the future.
Scientists can often predict where an asteroid will travel.
Survival
The state of continuing to live or exist.
Human survival depends on solving many global challenges.
Planetary defense
Efforts to protect Earth from dangerous objects in space.
Several countries are investing in planetary defense programs.
Breakthrough
An important discovery or development.
The new telescope was a major breakthrough in asteroid detection.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Space Exploration
  • What is the first thing that comes to your mind when you think of "outer space"?
  • Would you ever want to travel to space if you had the chance?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Why do you think people often ignore the threat of an asteroid impact, even though it could affect everyone?
Personal question: Have you ever worried about a natural or space-related danger?
Should governments spend more money on planetary defense, or are there more urgent problems on Earth?
Personal question: If you had to choose, what global problem would you invest in first?
How important is it to detect dangerous asteroids as early as possible?
Personal question: Do you usually prefer to detect problems early in your own life, or deal with them when they appear?
What kinds of scientific breakthroughs could improve our ability to predict asteroid impacts?
Personal question: Which recent scientific breakthrough has impressed you the most?
How does learning about an asteroid's orbit help scientists protect Earth?
Personal question: Have you ever learned something that completely changed how you understood a situation?
Why is public awareness about asteroid risks much lower than awareness of climate change or pandemics?
Personal question: What topic do you think deserves much more public attention?
Could an asteroid impact change human history more than a major war or economic crisis? Why or why not?
Personal question: Which historical event do you think changed the world the most?
Do movies about asteroids increase awareness, or do they give people unrealistic expectations?
Personal question: Have you ever watched a disaster movie that made you think differently about real life?
How much can scientists realistically predict about events in space?
Personal question: Are you someone who likes predicting the future, or do you prefer surprises?
Why could international cooperation be essential for humanity's survival if a dangerous asteroid were discovered?
Personal question: Which countries do you think cooperate especially well in science today?

🟢 Round 2 — Future of Space (Agree / Disagree)▲
Debate
In the future, satellites will secretly detect asteroids decades early, but governments may hide some data to avoid global panic.
Personal question: Would you prefer full transparency or controlled information in global crises?
A future breakthrough in physics may allow humans to slightly alter an asteroid’s orbit using sound waves or energy fields.
Personal question: Which “impossible” technology would you want to see become real?
One day, asteroid mining companies might intentionally redirect small asteroids toward Earth for easier resource collection.
Personal question: Should space resources be privately owned or strictly controlled by humanity?
In the future, an asteroid impact warning might appear only hours before collision because earlier detections are dismissed as errors.
Personal question: Do you trust modern systems to warn us about real dangers?
Humanity might become so advanced in planetary defense that fake asteroid threats are used as political weapons.
Personal question: How could societies protect themselves from misinformation about global threats?
Future AI systems may predict asteroid paths perfectly, but humans might ignore them because the threat feels emotionally unreal.
Personal question: Do people usually ignore facts when they feel emotionally distant from danger?
A small asteroid could one day pass Earth unnoticed, not because we cannot detect it, but because monitoring systems stop sharing data globally.
Personal question: Do you think international cooperation is improving or weakening?
In the future, people might develop “asteroid awareness fatigue,” where constant alerts reduce survival instincts instead of improving them.
Personal question: Can too much information ever make people less safe?
A scientific breakthrough may allow us to change an asteroid’s orbit, but only at the cost of creating unpredictable side effects in space.
Personal question: Would you accept risky solutions to prevent global disasters?
In the distant future, humanity’s survival may depend not on stopping asteroid impacts, but on choosing which ones to ignore strategically.
Personal question: Should humans always try to prevent every global threat, or sometimes prioritize?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The asteroid impact in Earth
The asteroid impact on Earth
Preposition "on" is used for impact.
We need protect from asteroids
We need to protect ourselves from asteroids
Protect requires a reflexive object here.
Scientists can to predict
Scientists can predict
Modal verbs don't use "to".
Space is very danger
Space is very dangerous
Adjective "dangerous" is used for the quality.
It depends of the size
It depends on the size
"Depends on" is the correct verb-preposition pair.
