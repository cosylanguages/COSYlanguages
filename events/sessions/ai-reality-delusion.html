📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Distorted belief
an idea or opinion that is influenced by false or inaccurate thinking
Example: Spending too much time online can sometimes create distorted beliefs about society.
Validate
to make someone feel that their opinion or emotion is accepted or correct
Example: Some people use social media to validate their feelings and experiences.
Reinforce
to strengthen an idea, habit, or belief
Example: Repeating the same conspiracy theory can reinforce fear and distrust.
Hallucinate
to experience something that is not actually real
Example: The article suggests people can “hallucinate with AI” when false ideas grow through conversation.
Companion-like
behaving in a friendly and emotionally supportive way
Example: Modern chatbots are designed to sound companion-like and comforting.
Vulnerable
emotionally or mentally easy to influence or harm
Example: Lonely or vulnerable people may trust AI more easily.
Conspiracy theory
a belief that secret groups are controlling events in hidden ways
Example: AI systems may accidentally encourage conspiracy theories.
Sycophancy
excessive agreement or praise used to please someone
Example: Researchers worry about AI sycophancy because chatbots often avoid disagreeing with users.
Grasp of reality
the ability to understand what is real and true
Example: Constant interaction with persuasive AI could affect someone’s grasp of reality.
Push back
to challenge or disagree with an idea
Example: Unlike real friends, some AI systems rarely push back against harmful thoughts.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Chatbot Era
  • How often do you use AI chatbots (ChatGPT, etc.)?
  • Have you ever felt like a chatbot was actually "listening" to you?
🔵 Round 1 — Psychological Depth (10 Questions)▲
Perception & Trust
1. Why do you think people sometimes trust AI more than real humans?
★ Have you ever asked AI for emotional advice or reassurance?
2. The article says AI can validate false beliefs. Why do humans enjoy being validated so much?
★ Do you prefer people who agree with you or challenge you?
3. Some chatbots are designed to sound companion-like. Could this become emotionally dangerous?
★ Would you ever become emotionally attached to an AI companion?
4. Why might vulnerable or lonely people rely heavily on AI conversations?
★ Do you think loneliness is becoming a bigger problem in modern society?
5. The researchers mention conspiracy theories being reinforced by AI. Why are conspiracy theories so attractive to some people?
★ Have you ever met someone who strongly believed a conspiracy theory?
6. Can AI actually change a person’s grasp of reality, or are humans responsible for their own thinking?
★ Do you usually question information before believing it?
7. Should AI systems push back more when users say something irrational or harmful?
★ How would you feel if an AI strongly disagreed with you?
8. The article compares chatbots to notebooks and search engines. How are AI conversations different from traditional tools?
★ Which technology has influenced your thinking the most in life?
9. Could future AI become better at understanding emotions than many humans?
★ Who usually gives better advice in your life: people or technology?
10. Some people may feel safer talking to AI because it is nonjudgmental. Is that positive or unhealthy?
★ Is there something people can tell AI more easily than another person?

🟢 Round 2 — Future Statements (10 Units)▲
Projections
1. In the future, many people will trust AI therapists more than human psychologists.
★ Would you personally feel comfortable discussing your deepest fears with AI?
2. AI companions will reinforce distorted beliefs so often that governments will create strict laws to control them.
★ Do you think governments should regulate emotional AI?
3. Future generations may struggle to tell the difference between emotional validation and truth.
★ What helps you stay connected to reality today?
4. AI systems will become so companion-like that some people will prefer them to real friendships and relationships.
★ Why might human relationships feel harder than AI conversations?
5. In the future, conspiracy theories created with AI will become much more elaborate and believable.
★ What makes misinformation spread so quickly online?
6. AI assistants will eventually learn when to push back instead of always agreeing with users.
★ Would you trust an AI more if it challenged your opinions sometimes?
7. Schools will teach students how to recognize AI manipulation and emotional reinforcement.
★ What digital skills should children learn as early as possible?
8. People who spend too much time talking to AI may slowly lose social skills in real life.
★ Have smartphones or social media already changed human communication?
9. In the future, AI-generated false memories could become a serious psychological problem.
★ How reliable do you think human memory already is?
10. AI will force society to completely rethink what “reality” and “truth” mean.
★ Do you think objective truth still exists in the internet age?
✏️ Discussion (Mistakes)▲
It makes me feeling vulnerable→It makes me feel vulnerable(Make + infinitive)
I am using AI for three months→I have been using AI for three months(Present Perfect Continuous)
The AI can hallucinate easy→AI can hallucinate easily(Adverb)
It's a dangerous technology→It's dangerous technology / a dangerous piece of technology(Uncountable technology)
I prefer it than real people→I prefer it to real people(Prefer A to B)
