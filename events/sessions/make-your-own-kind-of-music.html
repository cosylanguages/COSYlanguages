Focus

Individuality & Self-Expression

This session explores Cass Elliot's timeless 1960s pop classic, "Make Your Own Kind of Music". Students will unpack the rich, optimistic vocabulary of self-expression, individuality, and personal paths. We will practice using musical metaphors to talk about life choices, confidence, and overcoming peer pressure.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Individuality
the quality of being different from other people or things.
She expressed her individuality by dressing in bright vintage clothes.
Belated
coming or happening later than should have been the case.
He received belated recognition for his incredible singing talent.
Melody
a sequence of single notes that is musically satisfying.
The beautiful melody of the song stayed in my head all day.
Choir
an organized group of singers, especially in a church.
She sang in the school choir every Friday afternoon.
Chorus
a part of a song that is repeated after each verse.
Everyone joined in to sing the catchy chorus of the anthem.
Solo
a piece of music played or sung by a single performer.
He performed a brilliant guitar solo during the second verse.
Inspiration
the process of being mentally stimulated to do something creative.
The majestic mountains were a source of inspiration for the artist.
Peer
a person of the same age, status, or ability.
It is important not to let peer pressure dictate your personal decisions.
Path
a route or course of action taken to achieve a goal.
She decided to choose a unique career path that made her happy.
Harmony
the combination of simultaneously sounded musical notes to produce a pleasing effect.
The singers produced a beautiful harmony that moved the audience.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Share your raw feelings about the artist and sing along to the introductory melodies.
  • Have you ever heard this particular song before today's session?
  • What genres of music bring you the most comfort during a hectic week?
🔵 Round 1 — Lyric Discussion▲
Questions
Dissect the core theme of the song and practice speaking using the highlighted lyrics.
How does your favorite outfit show your individuality to the world?

Why is belated appreciation for art so common in modern society?
★ Have you ever appreciated someone's help much later?
What kind of melody makes you feel relaxed after a stressful workday?
★ Do you listen to music while working?
Is joining a choir a good way to build social connections in a new city?
★ Have you ever sung in a group?
Why is the chorus usually the most memorable part of any popular song?
★ What is your favorite song chorus?
How do you feel when you have to perform a solo presentation at work?
★ Do you get nervous speaking in public?
Where do you find your main inspiration when you feel creatively blocked?
★ Does nature inspire you?
How does peer influence change from childhood to adulthood?
★ Do you care about what others think of your choices?
Why is finding your own path more difficult than following a traditional one?
★ Are you happy with your current career path?
Is it possible to live in perfect harmony with neighbors who have different lifestyles?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
She is in the choir since three years→She has been in the choir for three years(Use 'has been... for' when talking about an action that started in the past and continues now.)
The song melody was so beautiful than I cried→The song melody was so beautiful that I cried(Use 'so... that' to express a consequence or result.)
I must find my path solo→I must find my path on my own(While 'solo' is used in music, 'on my own' or 'by myself' is more natural for daily life paths.)
