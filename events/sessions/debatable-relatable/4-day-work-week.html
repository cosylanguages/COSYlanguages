Intermediate (B1)

Topic

Work-Life Balance / Productivity

The concept of a four-day work week is gaining global momentum as companies seek to balance productivity with employee well-being. This session explores the potential shift from the traditional five-day model to a more flexible, results-oriented schedule. We will discuss whether this change is a natural evolution of the modern workplace or a risky experiment for the global economy.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Burnout
physical or mental collapse caused by overwork or stress.
Many employees are suffering from burnout due to long hours.
Productivity
the effectiveness of productive effort, as measured by the rate of output per unit of input.
Studies show that shorter weeks can actually increase productivity.
Well-being
the state of being comfortable, healthy, or happy.
Employee well-being should be a priority for every company.
Retention
the ability of a company to keep its employees.
A 4-day week can significantly improve staff retention.
Overhead
the regular costs of running a business (rent, electricity, etc.).
Closing the office for an extra day can reduce overhead costs.
Flexibility
the quality of bending easily or being easily modified.
Modern workers value flexibility more than a high salary.
Trial
a formal test of something, typically in a workplace or laboratory.
The government is launching a national trial of the 4-day week.
Output
the amount of something produced by a person, machine, or industry.
Managers should focus on output rather than hours spent at a desk.
Commute
the journey between home and work.
A shorter work week means one less day of stressful commuting.
Implementation
the process of putting a decision or plan into effect.
The implementation of the new schedule took several months.

🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the advantages of both perspectives.
A: A 4-day work week significantly reduces professional burnout and improves mental well-being.
B: A 5-day week provides a more relaxed daily pace and prevents the immediate exhaustion of compressed working hours.
★ Have you ever suffered from work-related burnout, and how did you recover?
A: Shorter working weeks increase overall productivity because employees waste less time on unneeded tasks.
B: A 5-day week ensures constant availability and reliability for essential public services and international clients.
★ Do you feel more productive when working under tight deadlines or when you have extra time?
A: Companies offering 4-day weeks find it much easier to attract and keep top talent (improving retention).
B: Traditional schedules offer predictable staffing and stability for growing small businesses.
★ Would you choose a lower-paying job if it offered a guaranteed 3-day weekend?
A: Reducing commutes by 20% is a major environmental win and saves personal energy.
B: Consistent daily office hours make public transport planning more efficient for city networks.
★ How much time and energy do you spend on your daily commute to work or school?
A: An extra day off allows for better "life admin," like doctor appointments and household chores.
B: A 5-day week maintains a synchronized society where banks, shops, and offices are always accessible.
★ On which day of the week do you usually feel the most overwhelmed by personal chores?
A: More free time encourages people to exercise, prepare healthy meals, and focus on physical health.
B: A standard 5-day routine helps people maintain a disciplined and structured lifestyle throughout the week.
★ Do you find it easier to stay disciplined when your schedule is strictly structured?
A: Parents can spend more quality time with their children, reducing the high cost of childcare.
B: Current educational and childcare systems are perfectly aligned with the standard 5-day model.
★ How did your parents balance their work schedules with raising you when you were a child?
A: It forces managers to focus on results and output rather than "hours spent at a desk."
B: Being physically present for 5 days maximizes opportunities for spontaneous collaboration and mentorship.
★ Have you ever had a great idea during a casual, unplanned conversation with a colleague?
A: A 4-day week is the natural evolution of work, just like the transition from 6 to 5 days was.
B: The 5-day week is a proven global standard that provides psychological and economic stability.
★ Do you believe our current society is too obsessed with constant labor and productivity?
A: It promotes gender equality by allowing partners to share domestic responsibilities more evenly.
B: Consistent schedules allow for a fairer distribution of labor across all sectors, including manual work.
★ How are household chores divided in your home, and do you feel it is fair?

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the disadvantages of both perspectives.
A: Switching to 4 days might lead to a permanent "laziness spiral" where productivity never recovers.
B: Sticking to 5 days risks creating a workforce that is permanently burnt out and uninspired.
★ How do you handle days when you feel absolutely uninspired to do any productive work?
A: Compressed 4-day schedules can cause dangerous fatigue in high-stakes roles like surgery or driving.
B: Traditional 5-day schedules cause "presenteeism" where people are at desks but doing nothing useful.
★ Have you ever had to work while feeling physically exhausted? How did you cope?
A: A 4-day model might accidentally destroy the work-life balance for people who take on second jobs to survive.
B: A 5-day model destroys the work-life balance by leaving only two days for rest, chores, and family.
★ If you had a 3-day weekend, would you use it to rest or to earn extra income?
A: Essential services like schools will face massive staffing crises if they try to move to 4 days.
B: Essential services will lose their best people to "office jobs" if they refuse to modernize the 5-day week.
★ What is one public service in your city that you think desperately needs modernization?
A: Reducing office time by 20% could lead to a significant decay in professional mentorship and skills transfer.
B: Maintaining the 5-day office culture increases overhead costs and unnecessary corporate bureaucracy.
★ Who has been the most influential professional mentor in your life so far?
A: The lack of Friday availability could frustrate global clients and slow down international trade.
B: The rigidity of the 5-day week makes it impossible for the economy to adapt to sudden personal emergencies.
★ How does your current workplace handle sudden personal emergencies or schedule changes?
A: A 4-day mandate might force small businesses into bankruptcy due to higher hourly labor costs.
B: A 5-day mandate prevents the birth of new, agile businesses that want to experiment with different models.
★ Have you ever dreamed of starting your own business? What kind of work week would you implement?
A: Measuring output by "results" only might lead to people working 60 hours in 4 days just to meet quotas.
B: Measuring output by "hours" only encourages slow work and rewards the least efficient employees.
★ Do you work faster when you are evaluated on results rather than hours spent?
A: The extra day off might increase the "carbon footprint" as people drive and consume more during their free time.
B: Commuting 5 days a week is one of the biggest contributors to urban pollution and mental stress.
★ What is your favorite eco-friendly activity to do during your leisure time?
A: The transition to 4 days will be so chaotic that it might trigger a temporary recession.
B: Failing to implement a 4-day week will make our society lose its most creative and energetic young workers.
★ Do you think young people today have a fundamentally different view of work than older generations?
