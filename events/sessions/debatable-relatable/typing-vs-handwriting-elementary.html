Topic

Typing vs Handwriting

This session is about writing with a pen and typing on a keyboard. We will talk about which one is better and faster for our daily life. We will also learn simple words to describe our school and office habits.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Handwriting
writing with a pen or pencil in your hand.
His handwriting is very neat and clean.
Typing
writing words on a computer or phone keyboard.
I am learning fast typing in my computer class.
Keyboard
the board with keys that you use to write on a computer.
My laptop keyboard has small letters on it.
Pen
an object that you use to write with ink on paper.
Can I borrow your blue pen for a moment?
Paper
thin sheets of material used for writing, printing, or drawing.
Please write your name on this white sheet of paper.
Fast
moving or happening at high speed; not slow.
Typing on a computer is very fast and easy.
Slow
not moving quickly; taking a long time.
Writing by hand is slow but very enjoyable.
Notebook
a book with empty pages used for writing notes.
I write my English vocabulary list in a green notebook.
Comfortable
producing physical ease; pleasant and relaxing.
My office chair is very comfortable to sit on.
Mistake
an action or decision that is incorrect or wrong.
You can erase a pencil mistake easily with an eraser.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to writing methods.
  • Do you write notes in a physical book or on your phone?
  • What color of ink do you like to use for writing?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze simple arguments about handwriting and typing.
Idea A: Handwriting helps us remember words better than typing.
Idea B: Typing on a laptop is very fast and saves a lot of time.
Idea A: Writing with a pen on paper is quiet and peaceful.
Idea B: Using a keyboard is comfortable because you can delete mistakes easily.
Idea A: A physical notebook doesn't need electricity or internet connection.
Idea B: Typing is better for sending fast emails to our friends and family.
Idea A: Learning to write helps children develop their hand muscles.
Idea B: Writing on a screen allows us to use different fonts and sizes.
Idea A: A handwritten birthday card shows that you really care about the person.
Idea B: Digital documents are easy to copy and send to many people instantly.

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze simple drawbacks of both writing formats.
Idea A: Writing by hand can make your hand tired and is very slow.
Idea B: Making a mistake on a computer screen can erase all your work.
Idea A: If your handwriting is messy, other people cannot read your notes.
Idea B: Looking at a screen for hours causes eye strain and headaches.
Idea A: Carrying many physical books is heavy and uncomfortable.
Idea B: Keyboards make a lot of noise which can disturb other people.
Idea A: If you spill water on paper, your notes are ruined permanently.
Idea B: Typing on a keyboard does not develop your creative thinking skills.
Idea A: Preparing physical exams is a very slow task for school teachers.
Idea B: Laptops in class offer too many distractions (like playing games).
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I write more fast with laptop→I write faster with a laptop(Comparative form of 'fast' is 'faster')
I have many homeworks→I have a lot of homework(Homework is uncountable)
He work very slow→He works very slowly(Third-person singular 's', adverb 'slowly')
