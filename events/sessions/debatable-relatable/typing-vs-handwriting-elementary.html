Topic

Typing vs Handwriting

This session is about writing with a pen and typing on a keyboard. We will talk about which one is better and faster for our daily life. We will also learn simple words to describe our school and office habits.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Handwriting
writing with a pen or pencil in your hand.
His handwriting is very neat and clean.
Typing
writing words on a computer or phone keyboard.
I am learning fast typing in my computer class.
Keyboard
the board with keys that you use to write on a computer.
My laptop keyboard has small letters on it.
Pen
an object that you use to write with ink on paper.
Can I borrow your blue pen for a moment?
Paper
thin sheets of material used for writing, printing, or drawing.
Please write your name on this white sheet of paper.
Fast
moving or happening at high speed; not slow.
Typing on a computer is very fast and easy.
Slow
not moving quickly; taking a long time.
Writing by hand is slow but very enjoyable.
Notebook
a book with empty pages used for writing notes.
I write my English vocabulary list in a green notebook.
Comfortable
producing physical ease; pleasant and relaxing.
My office chair is very comfortable to sit on.
Mistake
an action or decision that is incorrect or wrong.
You can erase a pencil mistake easily with an eraser.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to writing methods.
  • Do you write notes in a physical book or on your phone?
  • What color of ink do you like to use for writing?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the advantages of both perspectives.
A: Handwriting helps us remember words better than typing.
B: Typing on a computer is very fast and saves a lot of time.
★ Do you remember information better when you write it with a pen or type it on a phone?
A: Writing with a pen on paper is quiet and peaceful.
B: Using a keyboard is comfortable because you can delete mistakes easily.
★ Do you get annoyed when you make spelling mistakes while writing on paper?
A: A physical notebook doesn't need electricity or internet connection.
B: Digital documents are easy to copy and send to many people instantly.
★ Have you ever lost your notes because your digital device ran out of battery?
A: Learning to write helps children develop their hand muscles.
B: Digital keyboards allow people with physical disabilities to communicate much more easily.
★ Do you think schools should spend more time teaching children beautiful handwriting?
A: A handwritten birthday card shows that you really care about the person.
B: Digital messages are perfect for sending fast emails to friends and family.
★ How do you feel when someone gives you a handwritten birthday card?
A: Writing in a diary helps you focus on your personal emotions.
B: Typing on a blog lets you connect with a global community of readers.
★ Have you ever kept a personal diary or written a blog post?
A: Paper notebooks are very cheap and easy to buy.
B: Screens allow us to use different fonts, colors, and text sizes.
★ What is your favorite font style or text size when you write on a computer?
A: Handwritten notes allow you to draw sketches and diagrams easily.
B: Digital software automatically corrects your spelling and grammar mistakes.
★ Do you rely a lot on auto-correct when writing messages on your phone?
A: A physical book of notes is a beautiful, personal object to keep.
B: Typing your notes keeps them perfectly neat and easy to read.
★ Is your own handwriting very neat and clean, or is it messy?
A: Writing on paper is completely free of digital notifications and distractions.
B: Modern voice-to-text software can write down your words instantly without any typing.
★ Do you use voice messages or voice-to-text features often in your daily life?

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the disadvantages of both perspectives.
A: Writing by hand can make your hand tired and is very slow.
B: Making a mistake on a computer screen can erase all your work.
★ Does your hand start to hurt if you write for more than ten minutes?
A: If your handwriting is messy, other people cannot read your notes easily.
B: Looking at a screen for hours causes eye strain and headaches.
★ How many hours a day do you spend looking at digital screens?
A: Carrying many physical books and notebooks is heavy and uncomfortable.
B: Keyboard keys make a lot of clicking noise that can disturb others.
★ Does the loud sound of typing in a quiet room or library annoy you?
A: If you spill water on paper, your notes are ruined permanently.
B: Complete reliance on digital typing leaves us vulnerable to power outages.
★ Have you ever spilled water or coffee on your books or notes?
A: Preparing physical paper exams is a very slow task for school teachers.
B: Laptops in class offer too many distractions (like playing games).
★ Do you get easily distracted by social media when you are trying to study?
A: You cannot easily reorganize or move paragraphs in a handwritten text.
B: Digital files can be easily hacked or accessed by unauthorized people.
★ How do you protect your personal digital accounts and data from being hacked?
A: Producing paper notebooks consumes natural forests and paper resources.
B: Discarded digital devices create toxic electronic waste for the planet.
★ Do you try to recycle your paper notes or old electronic devices?
A: You cannot make a backup copy of a unique handwritten notebook.
B: Typing on a screen can make us forget how to spell words manually.
★ Do you ever struggle to remember how to spell a word when writing by hand?
A: Finding a specific quote in a physical notebook is slow and tedious.
B: Typing on a flat screen lacks the satisfying tactile feel of pen and paper.
★ Do you prefer the feel of a real physical book or an e-reader?
A: Messy handwriting can lead to critical misunderstandings in medical prescriptions.
B: Online storage models make you pay recurring fees to keep your notes.
★ Would you pay a monthly subscription to keep your personal notes online?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I write more fast with laptop→I write faster with a laptop(Comparative form of 'fast' is 'faster')
I have many homeworks→I have a lot of homework(Homework is uncountable)
He work very slow→He works very slowly(Third-person singular 's', adverb 'slowly')
