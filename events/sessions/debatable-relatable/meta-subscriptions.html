Topic

Tech Monetization / Digital Divide

The introduction of paid subscription models on major social media platforms marks a significant shift in how we access digital spaces. This session explores whether paying for verification and privacy is a fair trade-off or if it creates a digital divide between users. We will discuss the future of "free" internet services and the evolving value of personal data in a monetized online world.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Subscription
a regular payment to access a service or content.
Many platforms now use subscription models instead of free access.
Personalization
adapting a service to fit individual preferences.
Personalization improves user experience on social media.
Privacy
the right to control personal data and information.
Privacy concerns are growing on digital platforms.
Revenue
money a company earns from its business activities.
Advertising is a major source of revenue for Meta.
Creator
someone who produces online content such as videos or posts.
Many creators depend on social media income.
Bot
an automated account or program that performs online actions.
Bots are often used for spam or fake engagement.
Customization
changing features to match personal needs or taste.
Customization lets users design their profiles.
Innovation
the development of new ideas or technologies.
AI is driving innovation in the tech industry.
Engagement
interaction between users and online content.
Higher engagement increases visibility of posts.
Digital divide
the gap between people with and without access to technology.
Paid AI tools may widen the digital divide.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Paid vs Free
  • Would you pay $10 a month to use Instagram without ads?
  • Do you think social media is a "right" or a "luxury"?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the advantages of both perspectives.
A: Subscription models create high-quality “quiet zones” online where users are treated as customers rather than products.
B: Free access ensures that social media remains a global “public square” where anyone can participate regardless of wealth.
★ Would you pay $10 a month to use Instagram or Facebook without any ads?
A: Paid users gain true ownership of their attention by eliminating manipulative, tracking-based advertising.
B: Free platforms allow billions of people in developing nations to access vital information and communication tools for free.
★ Do you consider social media to be a basic human right or a private luxury?
A: Reliable subscription revenue allows platforms to prioritize long-term user well-being over short-term "clickbait" metrics.
B: Free, ad-supported models force companies to innovate constantly to keep users engaged and satisfied.
★ Have you ever deleted a social media app because it was too addictive or toxic?
A: Paid AI personalization can evolve into a sophisticated “digital assistant” that actively filters out online stress and spam.
B: Free systems encourage serendipity and the discovery of unexpected content that can change a person's life perspective.
★ Have you ever discovered a life-changing book, video, or person purely by chance online?
A: Paid identity verification systems are the most effective way to eliminate bots and ensure a safe, human-only digital environment.
B: Free platforms protect the anonymity and safety of vulnerable voices and political activists who cannot risk using their real names.
★ Under what circumstances, if any, do you use an anonymous alias or fake name online?
A: Micro-subscriptions provide creators with a stable, predictable income that allows them to focus on deep, niche content.
B: Free platforms democratize fame, allowing unknown creators to "go viral" and reach a global audience overnight.
★ Who is your absolute favorite online creator, and would you pay directly to support them?
A: Paid tiers allow for highly customized "immersive social layers" that offer a superior and more productive user experience.
B: Universal free access ensures that everyone speaks the same digital language and shares the same cultural references.
★ Do you feel that different social networks create completely different cultural environments?
A: Subscription income can fund experimental, emotion-aware feeds that promote positive social interaction.
B: The pressure of the "attention economy" in free models leads to the fastest development of new, highly creative media formats.
★ What is the most creative or unique digital format you have interacted with recently?
A: Paid communities naturally shift toward “high-intent communication” where conversations are more respectful and intellectually rigorous.
B: Free platforms enable the "chaotic creativity" of memes and global movements that can trigger real-world social change.
★ Do you find online comment sections generally toxic, or have you had positive discussions?
A: Advanced, paid AI tools can provide users with a "cognitive superpower" to manage infinite information.
B: Free, open-source AI tools on public platforms are the only way to prevent a permanent global "intelligence divide."
★ How do you personally ensure that you stay well-informed without feeling overwhelmed by information?

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the disadvantages of both perspectives.
A: Social media could split into “silent luxury platforms” where influence and visibility are only available to the rich.
B: Free platforms risk becoming "digital landfills" where meaningful communication is buried under spam and low-quality ads.
★ If all platforms became paid, which single one would you keep, and which would you delete?
A: Paying for privacy might create a world where only the wealthy have the "human right" to keep their data secret.
B: Ad-supported free models train the entire population to accept constant surveillance as a "normal" part of modern life.
★ How concerned are you about private companies and advertisers tracking your online movements?
A: Companies might intentionally degrade the free experience until social media feels like a "digital prison" unless you pay.
B: Free platforms will always be forced to exploit human psychology and outrage to stay profitable for their advertisers.
★ Have you ever felt that an app was intentionally making your experience frustrating to get you to pay?
A: Paid "AI assistants" might eventually start making personal and financial decisions for you without you noticing.
B: The lack of personalization in free models forces users to waste hours of their lives every day scrolling through irrelevant noise.
★ To what extent do you trust algorithms to recommend things like books, movies, or financial advice?
A: A paid identity system could be stolen or hacked, leading to "high-stakes" identity theft that is impossible to recover from.
B: Anonymity on free platforms allows for a total collapse of social accountability, encouraging bullying and misinformation.
★ Have you ever witnessed or experienced online bullying? How did you respond?
A: Creators who rely on subscriptions might become "slaves" to their paying fans, losing all their artistic freedom.
B: Creators on free platforms are forced to produce sensationalist "viral" content that has zero long-term value for society.
★ Do you think creators produce better work when they are trying to go viral, or when they have a loyal, small audience?
A: Subscription models might create "gated digital communities" that never interact with the rest of the world.
B: The "one-size-fits-all" design of free platforms kills individual creativity and forces everyone to behave the same way.
★ Do you feel that your online social bubble represents the real world, or is it highly curated?
A: Tech giants might prioritize "paying" customers for new safety features, leaving free users exposed to digital dangers.
B: Without subscription revenue, platforms will eventually stop innovating and become broken, outdated relics.
★ Do you think tech companies should be legally forced to provide equal safety features to all users?
A: Paid platforms could lead to intellectual "echo chambers" where you never hear a different or challenging opinion.
B: Free platforms prioritize "outrage" and conflict because it generates the most ad revenue, destroying social peace.
★ How do you actively seek out viewpoints that are different from your own?
A: The "intelligence gap" between users with paid AI assistants and those without will become a new form of biological inequality.
B: Failing to monetize personal data means we will never fund the massive infrastructure needed for the next generation of the internet.
★ Do you believe that AI is a tool that enhances human intelligence, or does it make us lazier?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I'm not agree with paying→I don't agree with paying(Agree is a verb)
The most of people use WhatsApp→Most people use WhatsApp(Generalization)
It depends of the revenue model→It depends on the revenue model(Depends on)
I am using Facebook since ten years→I have been using Facebook for ten years(Present Perfect Continuous)
I saw a very interesting information on Instagram→I saw some very interesting information / a very interesting piece of information on Instagram(The word 'information' is uncountable and cannot be preceded by the indefinite article 'a')
