Topic

Human Cloning

This session addresses the highly controversial topic of human cloning and its bioethical challenges. We will debate the potential medical benefits versus the severe ethical and social concerns of duplicating human genetics. Participants will express their views on scientific progress and the limits of biotechnology.

🔞
Sensitive Topic Note: This session focuses on human cloning, genetic ethics, and the manipulation of life. Discussion is designed to address highly complex bioethical and societal boundaries.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Cloning
the process of producing similar populations of genetically identical individuals.
Therapeutic cloning could help us cure various degenerative diseases.
Duplicate
to make an exact copy of something.
It is impossible to completely duplicate a person's unique life experiences.
Bioethics
the ethics of medical and biological research.
Experts in bioethics are debating the moral boundaries of human editing.
Genetic
relating to genes or heredity.
Our genetic code determines many of our physical characteristics.
Controversial
giving rise or likely to give rise to public disagreement.
The decision to allow human cloning research remains highly controversial.
Progress
forward or onward movement towards a destination or goal.
Scientific progress should not happen at the expense of human dignity.
Biotechnology
the exploitation of biological processes for industrial and other purposes.
Biotechnology has advanced rapidly over the past few decades.
Limit
a restriction on the size or amount of something.
We must set a strict moral limit on genetic experiments.
Embryo
an unborn or unhatched offspring in the process of development.
Researchers are analyzing cell division in the early human embryo.
Alter
to change or cause to change in character or composition.
Cloning could permanently alter our definition of family and parenthood.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to science fiction and reality.
  • Have you ever watched a science fiction movie about clones?
  • If you had a clone of yourself, what task would you ask them to do?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the advantages of both perspectives.
A: Cloning can help infertile couples have genetically related children.
B: Natural reproduction preserves the unique genetic diversity and unpredictability of the human species.
★ How important is genetic relation compared to emotional bonding in a family, in your view?
A: We can use therapeutic cloning to duplicate healthy cells and replace damaged organs.
B: Organ donor registries provide a morally clear and altruistic way to save lives.
★ If science offered you a cloned organ to extend your lifespan, would you accept it?
A: In bioethics, some argue that cloned tissue could save millions of lives from degenerative diseases.
B: Traditional research focuses on non-destructive cell methods, respecting biological boundaries.
★ Do you think scientific research should have absolute freedom if its goal is to save lives?
A: Cloning endangered animal species can protect our planetary biodiversity.
B: Protecting natural habitats is a more sustainable way to conserve ecosystems than cloning animals in labs.
★ If you could bring back one extinct species (like a mammoth), which one would you choose?
A: Genetic engineering and cloning allow us to study complex human diseases in controlled lab environments.
B: Studying diverse human populations provides real-world epidemiological insights.
★ Are you generally optimistic or cautious about genetic discoveries reported in the news?
A: Cloning technology is a natural, exciting step in the progress of biological sciences.
B: Establishing strict legal limits on cloning protects society from unpredictable dystopian outcomes.
★ What is one scientific discovery in your lifetime that has most surprised or changed your world?
A: Modern biotechnology can eliminate inherited genetic conditions before birth through cloning lines.
B: Accepting genetic diversity, including natural differences and vulnerabilities, builds a more compassionate society.
★ Would you want to know your own genetic predispositions to diseases if you could?
A: Developing cloning skills elevates a nation's biotechnology sector and attracts global scientific talent.
B: Restricting biotechnology spending ensures that healthcare budgets are focused on basic, accessible medical needs.
★ Do you think there are some areas of nature that humans should simply never try to control?
A: Analyzing a cloned embryo helps us understand early developmental biology and cell growth.
B: Protecting human embryos from commercial experimentation maintains a clear ethical standard.
★ How do you feel when you read about highly complex bioethical debates in other countries?
A: Therapeutic breakthroughs could permanently alter how we treat heart disease and diabetes.
B: Encouraging healthy lifestyle habits is a much more effective and natural way to prevent chronic diseases.
★ How has modern medicine already improved or changed the daily life of someone you know?

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the disadvantages of both perspectives.
A: Cloning violates the unique identity and dignity of a human individual.
B: Banning cloning prevents infertile couples from accessing a potentially life-changing technology.
★ What is the most unique or irreplaceable aspect of your own personality?
A: Cloned children would face extreme psychological pressure to duplicate the achievements of their original.
B: Natural children are often forced to live up to unrealistic parental expectations anyway.
★ Have you ever felt pressure to live up to the expectations of your parents or siblings?
A: From the perspective of bioethics, treating human lives as experimental objects is morally unacceptable.
B: Refusing to experiment on embryos holds back medical progress that could cure living, breathing patients.
★ How do you define the moral value of a human life in your personal philosophy?
A: Unexpected genetic mutations in cloned embryos can lead to severe health defects and suffering.
B: Natural reproduction also carries a high risk of spontaneous genetic mutations and defects.
★ How do you handle situations in life where a decision leads to unexpected, negative consequences?
A: Human cloning is highly controversial and could cause a deep division in society.
B: Societies have always split over major technological steps, but they eventually adapt.
★ How do you usually handle deep disagreements with friends or family members on sensitive topics?
A: Unregulated scientific progress might lead to black markets for human clone organs.
B: Prohibiting cloning guarantees that only rich people will access it in unregulated offshore labs.
★ Do you believe that strict government laws are enough to prevent black markets and illegal trades?
A: Large corporations in biotechnology could patent human genetic lines for profit.
B: Patent protections are necessary to incentivize private companies to invest billions in medical research.
★ Do you think it is fair for private companies to own patents on natural resources or life forms?
A: If we do not set a strict moral limit, humanity might face dystopian scenario.
B: Overly defensive legal boundaries block positive innovations out of irrational fear of sci-fi scenarios.
★ What is your favorite dystopian movie or book, and why does it feel scary or relatable?
A: Using a human embryo solely as a resource for organs is ethically wrong.
B: Restricting the use of early cells devalues the lives of actual patients suffering from terminal organ failure.
★ In what ways can we make sure that human dignity is always respected in medical progress?
A: Cloning could permanently alter the structure of families and traditional parent-child relationships.
B: Traditional family structures are already evolving rapidly, and cloning is just another variation.
★ How has your own definition of "family" evolved as you have grown older?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The cloning is very dangerous→Cloning is very dangerous(Do not use definite article for general nouns)
I am think cloning is bad→I think cloning is bad(Simple present construction)
It is a controversial topic since years→It has been a controversial topic for years(Present Perfect + for duration)
