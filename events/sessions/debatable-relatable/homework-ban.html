Topic

Education / Work-Life Balance

The long-standing tradition of homework is facing increased scrutiny as educators and parents debate its true impact on learning and mental health. This session examines the arguments for and against banning mandatory assignments outside of school hours, focusing on the balance between academic achievement and personal growth. We will explore how removing homework might transform family dynamics, life skills development, and the overall educational experience.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Mandatory ≠ Voluntary
required by law or rules ≠ done, given, or acting of one's own free will.
School attendance is mandatory, but participating in extracurricular sports is completely voluntary.
Rote Learning
memorization technique based on repetition without understanding.
Rote learning might help you pass some basic exams, but it does not develop critical thinking.
Academic ≠ Extracurricular
relating to education and study ≠ activities done outside of the normal school course.
High grades are a great academic achievement, but we also need extracurricular interests.
Workload
the amount of work that is expected of someone.
The student workload has increased significantly over the past ten years.
Cognitive Development
the development of thinking, problem-solving, and decision-making skills.
Play and hands-on discovery are essential for a child's cognitive development.
Burnout
state of physical or mental collapse caused by overwork or stress.
A heavy homework workload can cause severe academic burnout in teenagers.
Autonomy
the right of self-government or independent choices.
Giving students more autonomy encourages them to become self-motivated learners.
Reinforce
to strengthen or support an existing idea, belief, or skill.
Assignments are designed to reinforce lessons taught in the classroom.
Counterproductive
having the opposite of the desired effect.
Giving too much homework can be counterproductive and destroy a student's curiosity.
Well-being
the state of being comfortable, healthy, or happy.
Schools must balance high grades with the physical and emotional well-being of students.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Homework Debate
  • Did you enjoy doing homework when you were at school?
  • How much time did you spend on assignments each day?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the advantages of both perspectives.
A: Banning homework allows children more autonomy over their free time, fostering a healthier balance.
B: Regular homework helps reinforce daily lessons and builds personal responsibility.
★ How did your family handle homework time when you were a child?
A: Without a heavy academic workload, students have time for extracurricular interests like music or sports.
B: Facing regular deadlines prepares students for mandatory responsibilities they will face in their adult careers.
★ What is one practical life skill you wish you had been taught in school?
A: Reducing study pressure outside of school hours significantly improves a child's psychological well-being.
B: Completing challenging assignments independently builds self-discipline and prevents future burnout.
★ Do you consider yourself highly organized, or do you tend to procrastinate?
A: Free exploration and play are far more beneficial for a young child's cognitive development.
B: Solving difficult assignments creates a sense of accomplishment that builds deep academic confidence.
★ When was the last time you felt a deep sense of accomplishment after learning something hard?
A: Self-directed projects are more effective because learning feels like a voluntary adventure rather than a chore.
B: Traditional assignments teach students how to concentrate deeply without constant teacher guidance.
★ Do you study or work better when you are completely alone, or in a social environment?
A: Eliminating after-school tasks helps close the privilege gap between students from different socio-economic backgrounds.
B: Challenging tasks allow advanced students to explore academic topics in greater depth than classroom time permits.
★ Have you ever pursued an interest or hobby deeply entirely on your own?
A: Banning assignments allows families to spend quality time together without constant arguments about schoolwork.
B: Managing homework workload helps students learn vital time-management skills before entering university.
★ Do you consider yourself a risk-taker, or do you prefer safe and predictable paths?
A: In the era of AI, we should avoid rote learning and prioritize real, human-centric physical experiences.
B: Creative assignments teach students how to use digital resources and AI tools responsibly.
★ What is your favorite way to learn that does not involve staring at a screen?
A: Children are naturally curious, and they will pursue knowledge independently if they aren't forced to study.
B: Structured assignments build the work ethic needed to complete tasks even when personal motivation is low.
★ How do you motivate yourself to do tasks that you find incredibly boring?
A: Removing traditional homework is not counterproductive; it actually increases long-term passion for learning.
B: Keeping the brain active after school hours maintains mental flexibility and learning habits.
★ If you could go back to university or school right now, what subject would you study?

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the disadvantages of both perspectives.
A: Total removal of after-school study might be counterproductive, leading to a decline in academic rigor.
B: Keeping mandatory assignments for life would turn homes into "mini-offices," ruining family well-being.
★ How do you draw a clear boundary between your working hours and your personal rest?
A: Without homework, we might rely purely on rote learning to pass exams because classroom time is too short.
B: A rigid homework system risks producing a society of "obedient followers" with no personal autonomy.
★ Do you find it difficult to maintain focus in today's world of short videos and notifications?
A: Banning homework might increase screen time, as teenagers struggle to fill their unstructured free time.
B: Sticking to a heavy study workload ensures students are too exhausted to discover who they are.
★ How do you spend your weekends when you have absolutely no plans or commitments?
A: Schools would have to rely on stressful final exams if they cannot evaluate ongoing academic progress.
B: Constant academic pressure causes severe burnout that damages a student's mental health for years.
★ Have you ever experienced a period of severe burnout? How did you recover?
A: If parents aren't involved in homework, they lose all insight into their child's intellectual and cognitive development.
B: If parents act as strict supervisors, the constant conflict over assignments can damage family relationships.
★ What is one thing you and your parents used to disagree about most when you were young?
A: A ban on homework might push rich families to pay for private tutors, widening the academic gap.
B: Traditional homework ensures that only children with a quiet, stable home can ever achieve high grades.
★ How quiet and comfortable was your study environment when you were growing up?
A: Without independent homework tasks, students will fail to build the self-discipline to work on their own.
B: Overworking students produces socially isolated individuals who struggle to cooperate with peers.
★ Do you prefer solving problems by yourself, or brainstorm-style with a team?
A: Homework is necessary to reinforce vocabulary, without which learning a foreign language takes twice as long.
B: If study is always mandatory, the joy of discovery is permanently replaced by the fear of failure.
★ When was the last time you learned something purely for the joy of it, with no fear of tests?
A: Banning assignments makes education feel voluntary, which might lead to a lack of professional work ethic.
B: A society obsessed with constant homework produces highly efficient citizens who cannot think creatively.
★ Do you think our world needs more disciplined executors or more visionary dreamers right now?
A: A complete ban might lead to the death of the intellectual expert and the rise of simplistic thinking.
B: A system built on relentless academic workload leads to the total exhaustion of the human spirit.
★ What is one question or topic that you have been deeply curious about recently?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The teacher gave me many homeworks → The teacher gave me a lot of homework (Homework is an uncountable noun and cannot be pluralized)
I stopped to do assignments → I stopped doing assignments (Stop + gerund means ending the action, whereas stop + infinitive means stopping another activity in order to do assignments)
If I was a student, I would... → If I were a student, I would... (Using the subjunctive 'were' in the second conditional to express hypothetical scenarios)
We discussed about the homework → We discussed the homework / had a discussion about the homework (The verb 'discuss' is transitive and does not take the preposition 'about')
I am agree with the ban → I agree with the ban (The word 'agree' is a verb in English, so we say 'I agree' rather than 'I am agree')
