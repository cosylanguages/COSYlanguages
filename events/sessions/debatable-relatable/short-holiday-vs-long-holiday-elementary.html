Short Holiday vs Long Holiday

18 July 2026

Home / Events / Debate Club / Short Holiday vs Long Holiday
← Back to Club

Duration

60 minutes

Languages

🇬🇧 English

Level

Elementary (A2)

Theme

Travel, Holidays, and Rest

In this session, we discuss the best way to spend our vacation time. Some people prefer taking many short holidays throughout the year, while others love having one long, relaxing trip. We will talk about travel plans, packing bags, and our favorite destinations.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Holiday
a day of festivity or recreation when no work is done.
I love to go to the beach during my summer holiday.
Travel
make a journey, typically of some length.
Many people like to travel to different countries by plane.
Short ≠ Long
measuring a small distance or duration ≠ measuring a great distance or duration.
A short weekend trip is very fun, but a long summer trip is more relaxing.
Vacation
an extended period of recreation, especially one spent away from home or in traveling.
We planned a winter vacation to go skiing in the mountains.
Relax
make or become less tense or anxious.
A warm bath helps me relax after a long day at school.
Destination
the place to which someone or something is going or being sent.
Paris is a very popular tourist destination for people from all over the world.
Plan
a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something.
Do you have a clear plan for your next weekend trip?
Pack
fill a suitcase or bag with clothes and other items needed for travel.
I need to pack my swimsuit and sunglasses for the beach trip.
Active
engaging or ready to engage in physically energetic pursuits.
I prefer an active holiday with hiking and swimming rather than sitting in a hotel.
Choose
pick out or select someone or something as being the best in a situation.
It is always difficult to choose between a mountain holiday and a seaside holiday.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • Do you prefer to spend your holidays near the sea or in the mountains?
  • What is the first thing you put in your suitcase when you pack?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages of Short Holidays▲
Debate
Analyze the advantages of both perspectives.
Taking a short holiday allows us to travel more frequently during the year.
★ How many short holidays did you take last year?
A short weekend trip is much easier to plan and prepare for than a long journey.
★ Do you enjoy planning all details of your travel, or do you decide on the spot?
It is much easier to pack a small bag than to prepare massive suitcases.
★ Can you travel for a weekend with just a small backpack?
Short trips allow us to explore many different destinations instead of just one.
★ What is your favorite near-by city to visit for a quick weekend?
We can easily stay active and energetic during a quick 3-day vacation.
★ Do you prefer walking a lot on holiday, or resting by the pool?
A long vacation is much better because it allows us to completely relax and forget about work.
★ How many days does it take you to stop thinking about work on holiday?
During a long trip, we can truly immerse ourselves in the local culture and language.
★ Have you ever lived in another country or city for more than a month?
We can travel to distant, exotic destinations that require long flights.
★ What is the most distant and exotic place you have ever visited?
A long holiday gives us enough time to do both active sports and lazy resting.
★ What is your favorite way to spend a lazy day on vacation?
Having one big vacation per year is much more satisfying than constantly traveling.
★ Would you prefer one 3-week trip or six 3-day trips in a year?

🟢 Round 2 — Advantages of Long Holidays▲
Debate
Analyze the disadvantages of both perspectives.
Short holidays are too brief, and you might feel sad when they end so quickly.
★ Have you ever felt that a weekend trip was over before you even started relaxing?
Frequent travel planning can be stressful and take up too much of your free time.
★ Do you find booking hotels and flights stressful or exciting?
Travel fatigue from quick journeys can make you feel more tired than before.
★ Have you ever returned from a holiday feeling like you need another holiday to rest?
You cannot fully experience a country's culture and lifestyle in just a few days.
★ Do you find it difficult to connect with local people during short trips?
Frequent short trips can end up being more expensive due to multiple flight costs.
★ Do you keep a strict budget when you travel?
Long holidays can be extremely expensive, requiring months of saving money.
★ What is the most expensive part of traveling for you (hotels, food, flights)?
Being away from home for a long time can make you feel homesick or miss your pets.
★ Do you get homesick easily when you are away from your home?
Returning to work after a long vacation is very difficult and can cause post-travel blues.
★ How do you feel on your very first day back at work after a long break?
Planning a long itinerary can be very complex and lead to arguments with travel partners.
★ Have you ever had a disagreement with someone you were traveling with?
If you don't like the destination, a long holiday can feel like a boring waste of time.
★ Have you ever visited a highly-rated place that you actually did not enjoy?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I am prefer long holidays. → I prefer long holidays. (Verb conjugation check: do not use 'am' before simple active verbs.)
It was a very good trip. → It was a wonderful / fantastic trip. (Style: use more expressive adjectives like 'wonderful' or 'fantastic'.)
I have been in France last year. → I went to France last year. (Past simple is required with specific past time markers like 'last year'.)
I am looking forward to go on holiday. → I am looking forward to going on holiday. ('Look forward to' is followed by a gerund/noun, not an infinitive.)
