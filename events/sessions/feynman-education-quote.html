Education vs Intelligence

12 July 2025

Duration

60 minutes

Languages

🇬🇧 🇫🇷 🇪🇸 🇮🇹 🇷🇺

Level

Intermediate (Adults)

Quote

“Never confuse education with intelligence; you can have a PhD and still be an idiot.” — Richard Feynman

📖 Session Vocabulary

To learn from experience
To gain understanding by doing things in real life.
Many people learn more from experience than from school.
To solve problems
To find answers or solutions to difficult situations.
Intelligence often means knowing how to solve problems.
To memorize
To remember information without necessarily understanding it.
Some students memorize facts but don’t understand them.
Real-life situation
Something that happens in everyday life, not in theory.
School doesn’t always prepare us for real-life situations.
To make decisions
To choose what to do in a situation.
Intelligent people are usually good at making decisions.
To understand deeply
To fully grasp meaning or importance.
Wisdom is about understanding deeply, not just knowing facts.
To apply knowledge
To use what you know in practice.
It’s important to apply knowledge, not just learn it.
To think logically
To think in a clear and organized way.
Critical thinking means thinking logically.
Life skills
Basic skills needed for everyday life.
Life skills are often missing in traditional education.
To make mistakes
To do something wrong and learn from it.
We often learn more by making mistakes.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲

What comes to mind when you hear the word "idiot"? Can a very smart person be an "idiot" in some situations?

🔵 Round 1 — Analysis (Education vs Intelligence)▲
Questions
Where do you personally draw the line between education and intelligence?
Personal question: Have you ever met someone who was highly educated but lacked practical judgment?
Why do formal qualifications sometimes fail to reflect real ability?
Personal question: In your experience, how often do diplomas match real competence?
What matters more in real life: knowledge, experience, or judgment?
Personal question: Which of these do you rely on most when making decisions?
Can intelligence exist without formal education, in your opinion?
Personal question: Do you know anyone who proves this in real life?
Why do some people with advanced degrees still make poor decisions?
Personal question: Have you ever been surprised by a “highly qualified” person’s mistake?
What role does thinking logically play compared to emotional or intuitive thinking?
Personal question: When do you trust logic, and when do you trust intuition?
How do you recognize real intelligence in everyday life?
Personal question: What signs make you think someone is genuinely intelligent?
Do modern education systems reward understanding, or performance and memorization?
Personal question: How was this reflected in your own education or career?
Is intelligence more about applying knowledge or acquiring it?
Personal question: Can you think of a situation where application mattered more than theory?
What is more dangerous: ignorance or false confidence from education?
Personal question: Have you ever seen someone overestimate their competence?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲
Debate: "Experience is the best teacher." One side argues for formal education, the other for learning from mistakes in real life. Use vocabulary like 'apply knowledge' and 'real-life situation'.
🟢 Round 2 — Agree or Disagree (Future of Intelligence & Education)▲
Debate
In the future, people with prestigious degrees may be seen as less useful than self-taught problem solvers.
Personal question: Would you feel comfortable in a world where your diploma matters less than your practical output?
Many “educated” professionals will be replaced not by AI, but by people who know how to use AI better than them.
Personal question: Do you think your current skills are enough to survive in that shift?
Intelligence will no longer be respected if it cannot produce fast, visible results.
Personal question: Do you think society already values speed over depth today?
The biggest risk in the future is not ignorance, but overconfidence from outdated education.
Personal question: Have you ever met someone who was “dangerously confident” in their knowledge?
In 20 years, some university degrees may become almost meaningless in real jobs.
Personal question: Which fields do you think will be most affected by this?
Employers will trust algorithm-based testing more than human interviews.
Personal question: Would you accept being evaluated by an AI instead of a person?
People who rely only on “what they learned at school” will slowly become irrelevant in the workplace.
Personal question: Do you feel your own knowledge is still evolving fast enough?
In the future, “intelligent people” may not be those who know more, but those who forget and relearn fastest.
Personal question: How easily do you adapt when your knowledge becomes outdated?
The gap between “highly educated” and “truly capable” people will become more visible—and uncomfortable.
Personal question: Have you ever seen this gap in your own professional environment?
One day, intelligence might be judged more like a performance score than a human quality.
Personal question: Would you want your intelligence to be measured constantly and publicly?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I make many mistake in my life.
I make many mistakes in my life.
Countable nouns after 'many' must be plural.
Education is not the same as clever.
Education is not the same as intelligence. / Being educated is not the same as being clever.
Compare nouns with nouns, or adjectives with adjectives.
