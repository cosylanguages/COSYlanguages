Focus

Anti-War & Grief

This session delves into Kate Bush's hauntingly beautiful and socially conscious song, "Army Dreamers". Students will discuss military terminology, anti-war themes, and the tragic loss of young lives. We will practice expressing grief, solace, mourning, and the clash between family destiny and institutional expectations.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Mourning
the expression of deep sorrow for someone who has died.
The entire nation was in mourning after the loss of their leader.
Consolation
the comfort received by a person after a loss or disappointment.
Knowing she did her best was her only consolation after the match.
Tragic
causing or characterized by extreme distress or sorrow.
The news of the sudden accident was deeply tragic.
Sacrifice
an act of giving up something valued for the sake of something else.
Parents often make a huge sacrifice to educate their children.
Soldier
a person who serves in an army.
The brave soldier returned home to his family after the deployment.
Illusion
a deceptive appearance or impression.
The warm weather created the illusion that spring had arrived early.
Destiny
the events that will necessarily happen to a particular person.
She believed it was her destiny to become a professional writer.
Grief
deep sorrow, especially that caused by someone's death.
She was overwhelmed with grief after her pet passed away.
Sorrow
a feeling of deep distress caused by loss or disappointment.
His heart was filled with sorrow as he looked at the old house.
Patriotism
devotion to and vigorous support for one's country.
They showed their intense patriotism by flying the national flag.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Share your raw feelings about the artist and sing along to the introductory melodies.
  • Have you ever heard this particular song before today's session?
  • What genres of music bring you the most comfort during a hectic week?
🔵 Round 1 — Lyric Discussion▲
Questions
Dissect the core theme of the song and practice speaking using the highlighted lyrics.
How do different cultures show respect during a period of public mourning?
★ Have you ever worn dark clothes to a memorial?
What words of consolation can you offer to a friend who lost their job?
★ Are you good at comforting people?
Why is the loss of youthful potential in war so deeply tragic?
★ Have you read any tragic history books?
What is the biggest personal sacrifice you have made for your career?
★ Do you regret that sacrifice?
How does the public perception of a soldier vary across different countries?
★ Do you have military members in your family?
How do governments maintain the illusion of an easy victory during conflicts?
★ Do you follow political news closely?
Do you believe we have total control over our personal destiny?
★ Do you believe in fate?
What is the healthiest way to process deep grief without shutting out friends?
★ Do you prefer solitude when sad?
How can a person transform their sorrow into something creative, like music?
★ Do you listen to sad music when you are down?
Where is the line between healthy patriotism and dangerous nationalism?
★ What makes you proud of your country?

🟢 Round 2 — Lyric Deep Dive & Debate▲
Debate
Evaluate these deep and controversial statements inspired by the song's philosophical themes.
In the future, public mourning will be conducted entirely in virtual spaces.
The best consolation for a difficult life is the achievement of material wealth.
All tragic events carry a hidden lesson that makes us stronger.
No career goal is worth making a major personal sacrifice for.
A professional soldier should be trained to exclude all emotions during duty.
The idea of absolute security in the modern world is a complete illusion.
Our destiny is predetermined by our biological genetics rather than our choices.
Processing grief is a lifelong journey that never truly ends.
To experience true joy, one must first feel deep sorrow.
Teaching intense patriotism in schools is outdated and promotes division.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
She was in mourning of her grandmother→She was in mourning for her grandmother(The correct preposition is 'mourning for' someone.)
His words were a big consolation of me→His words were a big consolation to me(Use 'consolation to' a person.)
The soldier returned back to his country→The soldier returned to his country(Avoid redundancy; 'return' already means 'to go or come back'.)
