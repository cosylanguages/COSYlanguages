Britannica Biography 📖

Steve Jobs' famous mantra from his Stanford commencement speech serves as a powerful call to lifelong learning and risk-taking. This session examines the importance of maintaining intellectual curiosity and the willingness to pursue unconventional paths in one's career and personal life. We will discuss how a "stay hungry, stay foolish" mindset can lead to innovation and resilience in an ever-changing world.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Hungry
having a strong desire or craving for something (e.g., knowledge or success).
To succeed in business, you must stay hungry for new opportunities.
Foolish
lacking good sense or judgment; unwise (used here as being willing to take risks).
Taking a risk that others think is foolish can lead to great discovery.
Mantra
a statement or slogan repeated frequently.
"Stay hungry, stay foolish" became a mantra for an entire generation.
Innovation
the action or process of innovating; a new method, idea, or product.
Steve Jobs is often cited as a master of technological innovation.
Curiosity
a strong desire to know or learn something.
Intellectual curiosity is essential for personal and professional growth.
Unconventional
not based on or conforming to what is generally done or believed.
He took an unconventional path to become a successful CEO.
Perseverance
persistence in doing something despite difficulty or delay in achieving success.
Perseverance is often the difference between failure and achievement.
Legacy
something handed down from an ancestor or a predecessor from the past.
The quote is part of the lasting legacy of Steve Jobs' 2005 speech.
Visionary
a person with original ideas about what the future will or could be like.
Steve Jobs was widely considered a visionary in the tech industry.
Intuition
the ability to understand something immediately, without the need for conscious reasoning.
Jobs often relied on his intuition when making big decisions.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Ambition & Risks
  • What is one thing you are "hungry" to learn right now?
  • What is the "most foolish" (riskiest) thing you have ever done?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Interpretations
What do you think Steve Jobs meant by "stay foolish"? (Consider the role of Hungry here.)
★ Do you find it hard to ignore what others think of you?
Why is it important to "stay hungry" even after achieving success? (Consider the role of Foolish here.)
★ Have you ever felt too comfortable or "full" in your career?
How does the fear of looking "foolish" prevent people from innovating? (Consider the role of Mantra here.)
★ When was the last time you asked a "stupid" question to learn something?
What role does intuition play in finding your "true path" in life? (Consider the role of Innovation here.)
★ Do you trust your gut more than your logic for big life choices?
How can we maintain curiosity in a world that values speed and efficiency?
★ Do you spend time exploring topics that have nothing to do with your job?
Is "staying foolish" a luxury for the rich, or a mindset for everyone? (Consider the role of Unconventional here.)
How has this quote influenced modern entrepreneurship and startup culture? (Consider the role of Perseverance here.)
Can someone be "too hungry" for success? What are the dangers? (Consider the role of Legacy here.)
Why did Jobs emphasize that "death is the destination we all share"? (Consider the role of Visionary here.)
If you followed this advice perfectly, how would your life change tomorrow? (Consider the role of Intuition here.)

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Debate / Deep Dive
True innovation only comes from those who are willing to be laughed at. (How does Hungry affect this statement?)
The most successful people in the future will be those who never stop being students. (How does Foolish affect this statement?)
Modern education systems kill the "foolishness" required for greatness. (How does Mantra affect this statement?)
It is impossible to find your "passion" through logical analysis alone. (How does Innovation affect this statement?)
Societal expectations are the biggest obstacle to individual happiness. (How does Curiosity affect this statement?)
AI will take over all the "logical" jobs, leaving only the "foolish" creative ones for humans. (How does Unconventional affect this statement?)
Hunger for success is more important than natural talent. (How does Perseverance affect this statement?)
A "foolish" person is more likely to be happy than a "wise" one. (How does Legacy affect this statement?)
We should prioritize finding what we love over finding what is practical. (How does Visionary affect this statement?)
Steve Jobs' advice is only for the elite who can afford to fail. (How does Intuition affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
He is more hungry than me→He is hungrier than I am(Comparative adjectives)
I am look forward to see the future→I am looking forward to seeing the future(Look forward to + -ing)
It is worth to take the risk→It is worth taking the risk(Worth + -ing)
He suggested me to stay foolish→He suggested (that) I stay foolish(Suggest structure)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
