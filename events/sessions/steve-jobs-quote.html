Quote

📖 Session Vocabulary

Hungry
having a strong desire or craving for something (success, knowledge, etc.)
Example: To succeed in this industry, you must remain hungry for innovation.
Foolish
lacking good sense or judgment; unwise
Example: Sometimes, the most "foolish" ideas lead to the biggest breakthroughs.
Curiosity
a strong desire to know or learn something
Example: Intellectual curiosity is the foundation of all great learning.
Innovation
the action or process of innovating; a new method, idea, product, etc.
Example: Steve Jobs is often cited as a master of technological innovation.
Risk-taking
the act or fact of doing something that involves danger or risk in order to achieve a goal
Example: Risk-taking is essential for anyone who wants to change the world.
Ambition
a strong desire to do or to achieve something, typically requiring determination and hard work
Example: Her ambition led her to start her own company at age 20.
Convention
a way in which something is usually done, especially within a particular area or activity
Example: To be "foolish" often means ignoring social conventions.
Perseverance
persistence in doing something despite difficulty or delay in achieving success
Example: It took years of perseverance to bring the iPhone to market.
Intuition
the ability to understand something immediately, without the need for conscious reasoning
Example: Jobs famously told students to "have the courage to follow your heart and intuition."
Legacy
something left or handed down by a predecessor
Example: The quote is part of the lasting legacy of Steve Jobs' 2005 speech.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Introduction to the Quote
  • What was the last thing you were truly "hungry" to learn?
  • When was the last time you did something that others thought was "foolish"?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
Questions
1. What do you think Steve Jobs meant by "stay foolish"?
★ Do you find it hard to ignore what others think of you?
2. Is it possible to be successful without being "hungry"?
★ What motivates you more: passion or necessity?
3. Why do many people lose their "foolishness" or curiosity as they get older?
★ Do you feel more or less curious now than you were 10 years ago?
4. How can "foolishness" lead to better problem-solving in a professional environment?
★ Have you ever proposed a "crazy" idea that actually worked?
5. What is the difference between being "foolish" and being "reckless"?
★ How do you measure risk in your life?
6. Does society reward those who follow conventions or those who break them?
7. Can a person be "too hungry" for success? What are the dangers?
8. How does intuition play a role in making big life decisions?
9. Why is it important to "stay hungry" even after you have achieved your goals?
10. If you had to choose one word from the quote to live by, which would it be: Hungry or Foolish?

🟢 Round 2 — Philosophical Application▲
Debate
1. "Stay hungry" is a recipe for permanent unhappiness because you are never satisfied.
2. In the age of AI, "staying foolish" (human intuition) is more valuable than "staying smart" (data).
3. Schools should grade students on their curiosity rather than their test scores.
4. Most "foolish" people are actually just lucky when they succeed.
5. You cannot "stay hungry" if you don't have a clear purpose in life.
6. Following your heart is dangerous advice for people with practical responsibilities.
7. Innovation is 1% "foolishness" and 99% hard work.
8. Modern social media makes it impossible to "stay foolish" because of constant judgment.
9. The world would be a better place if everyone was less "hungry" for power.
10. Steve Jobs' advice is only for the elite who can afford to fail.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
He gave me a foolish advice→He gave me some foolish advice(Uncountable advice)
It depends of your ambition→It depends on your ambition(Depends on)
I am work very hard→I work very hard / I am working very hard
I stay hungry for success→I am staying hungry for success / I want to stay hungry
If you are foolish, you win→If you are foolish, you will win(First conditional)
