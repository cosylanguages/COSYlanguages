This session covers recent studies suggesting that human tissues possess hidden regenerative capabilities similar to some lower vertebrates. We will discuss the biological mechanisms of wound healing and the potential of regenerative medicine to restore damaged organs. We will also touch upon the future ethical implications and possibilities of longevity sciences.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Regenerative
relating to the regrowth of damaged or lost tissue.
Regenerative medicine aims to regrow organs in laboratories.
Tissue
any of the distinct types of material of which animals or plants are made.
Muscle tissue is made of specialized cells that can contract.
Heal
to become sound or healthy again.
A healthy immune system helps the body heal wounds quickly.
Mechanism
a system of parts working together in a machine or a process.
Scientists are studying the biological mechanism of cell division.
Vertebrate
an animal of a large group distinguished by the possession of a backbone.
Lizards are vertebrates known for their ability to regrow tails.
Capability
the power or ability to do something.
Humans have some natural capability to regenerate liver tissue.
Longevity
long life or existence.
Good genes and healthy food contribute to longevity.
Organ
a part of the body that has a specific vital function.
The heart is a vital organ that pumps blood.
Restore
to return something to its former condition.
Doctors hope this new therapy can restore damaged heart tissue.
Biological
relating to biology or living organisms.
Our biological clock regulates sleep patterns.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to healing.
  • Have you ever broken a bone? How long did it take to heal?
  • What is one animal ability you wish humans had?
🔵 Round 1 — Theoretical Discussion▲
Questions
Discuss human regenerative biology and its future potential.
What kind of regenerative power do humans currently possess?
★ How quickly does your body usually heal from minor scratches or cuts?
How does muscle tissue adapt and grow after exercise?
★ What is your favorite type of physical exercise to build strength?
What is the biological mechanism behind wound healing?
★ Do you use any natural remedies to help minor wounds heal faster?
Why can some vertebrates regrow entire limbs while humans cannot?
★ What animal ability do you find most fascinating?
How could regenerative medicine improve human longevity?
★ How do you feel about the idea of living past 150 years?
What are the challenges of regenerating a complex organ like the heart?
★ How do you take care of your cardiovascular health?
How can science restore lost sensory functions using biotechnology?
★ Which of your five senses do you value the most?
What is the role of stem cells in biological repair?
★ Have you ever read articles about stem cell research?
How do lifestyle choices affect our body's healing capability?
★ Do you think you get enough sleep to allow your body to recover?
To what extent should we interfere with natural biological limits?
★ Are you optimistic or pessimistic about genetic modification in humans?

Projections
Debate the ethical and scientific future of regenerative technologies.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The lizards can to regrow tail→Lizards can regrow their tails(Can + infinitive without 'to', plural noun)
He was operated last week→He had an operation last week(Passive structure vs having an operation)
I am agree with scientifics→I agree with the scientists(Agree is a verb, noun 'scientists')
