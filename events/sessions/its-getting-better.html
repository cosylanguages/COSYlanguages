This session celebrates Cass Elliot's energetic, uplifting pop single, "It's Getting Better". Students will study cheerful vocabulary, expressions of relief, and optimistic outlooks on personal improvement. We will discuss our dreams, brighter futures, and finding harmony in everyday life.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Optimism
hopefulness and confidence about the future.
Her natural optimism helped her stay happy during difficult times.
Improvement
the action of making or becoming better.
There is a noticeable improvement in her spoken English skills.
Relief
a feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety.
It was a huge relief to hear that the exam was over.
Sunshine
direct sunlight unbroken by cloud, or a source of cheerfulness.
We sat on the balcony to enjoy the warm morning sunshine.
Bright
giving out or reflecting a lot of light, or promising.
The young student has a very bright future ahead of her.
Dream
a cherished aspiration, ambition, or ideal.
Her dream is to open a small bakery on a peaceful island.
Path
a route or course of action taken to achieve a goal.
She followed a clear path to achieve her academic goals.
Future
the period of time that will come after the present.
We must plan carefully to protect our environment for the future.
Cheer
shout for joy or encouragement, or comfort.
The crowd began to cheer as the band walked onto the stage.
Harmony
the state of being in agreement or concord.
They worked together in perfect harmony to complete the project.

🟢 Round 2 — Lyric Deep Dive & Debate▲
Debate
Evaluate these deep and controversial statements inspired by the song's philosophical themes.
In the future, constant optimism will be seen as a form of emotional denial.
Continuous personal improvement is a exhausting trend that prevents people from being happy.
True relief can only be felt after experiencing severe physical or mental stress.
Living in a place without regular sunshine is the main cause of workplace burnout.
A bright intellect is less important for professional success than high emotional intelligence.
Following your wildest dream is a reckless decision that usually leads to financial ruin.
There is no single correct path; we are all wandering aimlessly through life.
The future of humanity is looking much safer than our chaotic history.
It is impossible to cheer up someone who is determined to be miserable.
Total social harmony is a dangerous illusion that requires the suppression of dissent.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English→There is a big improvement in my English(Use the preposition 'in' after 'improvement'.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news→It was a great relief to hear the news(Use 'relief to' followed by an infinitive verb.)
She wants to cheer up her sister→She wants to cheer her sister up(For pronoun objects, the pronoun must go between 'cheer' and 'up' (e.g., 'cheer her up'). For noun objects, both are acceptable, but 'cheer her sister up' sounds very natural.)
