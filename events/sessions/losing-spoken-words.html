📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Decline
a gradual decrease in something over time
Example: The study found a decline in the number of spoken words.
Accumulate
to increase little by little over a period of time
Example: Small changes can accumulate and create big effects.
Interaction
communication or activity between people
Example: Daily interactions help us feel connected to others.
Coincide
to happen at the same time as something else
Example: The decline in speech coincided with the rise of social media.
Steeper
faster or more extreme
Example: Young people showed a steeper decline in spoken communication.
Reliance
dependence on something
Example: Many people have a strong reliance on smartphones.
Cue
a signal that helps someone understand something
Example: Tone of voice provides emotional cues during conversations.
Loneliness
the feeling of being alone or disconnected from others
Example: Frequent conversations may reduce loneliness.
Wellbeing
a person's overall health, happiness, and comfort
Example: Social interaction is important for wellbeing.
Digital communication
communication using technology such as texting, email, or social media
Example: Digital communication has changed how people interact.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • How many hours a day do you spend talking vs. texting?
  • Do you feel that you're speaking less now than you were five years ago?
🔵 Round 1 — Understanding the Research (10 Questions)▲
Questions
1. The study reports a long-term decline in spoken words. Could speaking less actually make some people happier or more productive, or is this decline mostly negative?
★ If someone counted your spoken words tomorrow, would the number surprise you?
2. The researchers argue that small changes accumulate over time. Which tiny everyday habit do you think has had the biggest hidden effect on the way humans communicate?
★ What's one communication habit you've developed in the last five years?
3. The article counts every interaction, from deep conversations to chatting with a barista. Should all conversations be valued equally, or do only meaningful interactions really matter?
★ Which unexpected interaction has brightened your day recently?
4. The decline seems to coincide with smartphones and social media, but coincidence isn't always causation. What other social or cultural changes might explain the trend?
★ Which invention has changed the way you communicate the most?
5. Younger people experienced a much steeper decline in spoken communication. Is this because technology changed young people, or because young people changed technology?
★ Do you think teenagers today are better or worse communicators than you were at their age?
6. The researchers wonder whether text messages can replace emotional cues like tone of voice or facial expressions. Which emotions are hardest to communicate digitally?
★ Have you ever completely misunderstood someone's message because you couldn't hear their voice?
7. Our growing reliance on digital communication may change friendships. Could future friendships become stronger online than offline?
★ Is there someone you mainly communicate with online but still feel very close to?
8. The researchers suggest fewer conversations may increase loneliness. Could someone talk to hundreds of people every week and still feel lonely? Why?
★ What kind of conversation makes you feel genuinely understood?
9. If spoken conversations support our wellbeing, should talking be considered as important as exercise or sleep for a healthy lifestyle?
★ When do you usually have your best conversations: morning, afternoon or late at night?
10. The study asks whether digital communication can replace spoken language. If you designed the next experiment, what surprising question would you investigate?
★ What communication habit would you be willing to change after reading this article?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

Debate the following statement as a group:

"The quality of our silence is often a better measure of intimacy than the quantity of our words."
🟢 Round 2 — Future Focus (10 Statements)▲
Agree or Disagree?
1. If the decline in spoken conversations continues, future generations may lose the ability to comfortably talk to complete strangers.
★ Would you miss spontaneous conversations if they almost disappeared?
2. As more digital habits accumulate, speaking aloud could become as unusual as writing letters by hand is today.
★ Which old communication habit do you think people should never abandon?
3. Within 50 years, AI will understand emotional cues so well that many people will trust it more than their closest friends.
★ If an AI always understood your emotions perfectly, would you choose it over talking to a person?
4. Our growing reliance on digital communication will eventually make silence between two people feel more natural than conversation.
★ Do you think silence is comfortable or awkward?
5. As everyday interactions become rarer, governments will create "National Conversation Days" to improve public wellbeing.
★ Would you actually participate in a day where everyone was encouraged to talk to strangers?
6. If young people continue to show a steeper decline in spoken communication, future parents may need lessons on how to have real conversations with their own children.
★ What communication skill should every future parent have?
7. Future hospitals may treat loneliness by prescribing daily face-to-face conversations instead of only medication.
★ Who would be your ideal conversation partner if talking became part of healthcare?
8. If digital communication becomes almost perfect, many people will choose to live without speaking for weeks or even months.
★ Could you imagine voluntarily giving up speaking for a month?
9. One day, employers may value people who enjoy real conversations more than people with perfect technical skills because spoken communication will become exceptionally rare.
★ Which human skill do you think will become the most valuable in the future?
10. If humans continue speaking less for the next hundred years, the greatest loss won't be words—it will be our ability to understand each other's emotions without technology.
★ Which human ability would you never want technology to replace?
