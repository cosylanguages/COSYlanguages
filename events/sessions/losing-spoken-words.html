Full Article 📖

New research suggests that modern lifestyle and technology are leading to a significant decrease in the number of spoken words we use in daily interaction. This session explores the causes of this "verbal shrinking," from the rise of digital messaging to the decline of community-based social spaces. We will discuss the long-term impact on our cognitive abilities, emotional expression, and the quality of our interpersonal relationships.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Linguistic decay
the gradual loss of words or grammatical structures in a language over time.
Some experts are concerned about linguistic decay caused by rapid texting.
Interaction
reciprocal action or influence between two or more people.
Face-to-face interaction is essential for maintaining social skills.
Monosyllabic
consisting of one syllable (often used to describe short, unhelpful answers).
He gave a monosyllabic reply when I asked how his day was.
Cognitive load
the amount of information that working memory can hold at one time.
Deep conversation requires a higher cognitive load than simple messaging.
Eloquence
fluent or persuasive speaking or writing.
His eloquence made him a very effective public speaker.
Vocabulary range
the total number of different words a person knows and uses.
Reading diverse books is the best way to expand your vocabulary range.
Isolation
the state of being alone or set apart from others.
Digital communication can sometimes lead to a sense of social isolation.
Nuance
a subtle difference in meaning, expression, or sound.
A rich vocabulary allows you to express every small nuance of emotion.
Digital distraction
losing focus on a task or conversation because of a digital device.
Digital distraction is often cited as a cause for shorter real-life conversations.
Interpersonal
relating to relationships or communication between people.
The company is looking for candidates with strong interpersonal skills.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Daily Words
  • Do you talk more or type more during an average day?
  • Who is the most "talkative" person you know?
🔵 Round 1 — Theoretical Discussion▲
Questions
Why do you think we are losing so many spoken words every day? (Consider the role of Linguistic decay here.)
★ Have you noticed your own conversations getting shorter lately?
How has digital messaging changed the way we express our emotions? (Consider the role of Interaction here.)
★ Do you use emojis to replace words when you are tired of typing?
What is the impact of "verbal shrinking" on our ability to think deeply? (Consider the role of Monosyllabic here.)
★ Do you find it harder to concentrate on a long story than you used to?
Why is face-to-face interaction considered "high-bandwidth" compared to texting? (Consider the role of Cognitive load here.)
★ When was the last time you had a truly deep, 2-hour conversation?
Does having a smaller vocabulary make it harder to understand our own feelings? (Consider the role of Eloquence here.)
★ Are there some feelings that you find impossible to put into words?
How does technology influence the variety of words we use with our friends? (Consider the role of Vocabulary range here.)
What are the social consequences of losing small talk in public spaces? (Consider the role of Isolation here.)
Is "linguistic decay" a natural part of evolution or a problem we should fix? (Consider the role of Nuance here.)
How does reading books help preserve our spoken vocabulary? (Consider the role of Digital distraction here.)
If you could only speak 500 words a day, who would you choose to talk to? (Consider the role of Interpersonal here.)

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Projections
By 2050, spoken language will be replaced by direct brain-to-brain digital signals. (How does Linguistic decay affect this statement?)
Future generations will communicate almost entirely through images and short video clips. (How does Interaction affect this statement?)
Eloquence will become a "lost art," only practiced by a small intellectual elite. (How does Monosyllabic affect this statement?)
AI will eventually write all our messages, leading to total linguistic standardization. (How does Cognitive load affect this statement?)
We will lose the ability to have complex political or philosophical debates because of simple language. (How does Eloquence affect this statement?)
Schools will eventually stop teaching "public speaking" as a required skill. (How does Vocabulary range affect this statement?)
Human relationships will become shallower because we no longer have the words to describe them. (How does Isolation affect this statement?)
New "micro-languages" will emerge that are optimized for speed rather than meaning. (How does Nuance affect this statement?)
The value of a "good listener" will increase as people talk less and less. (How does Digital distraction affect this statement?)
We will eventually realize that "silence" is more valuable than any amount of spoken words. (How does Interpersonal affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is less words today→There are fewer words today(Fewer for countable nouns)
It is a very good news→It is very good news(Uncountable news)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
It is depend on you→It depends on you(Verb vs Adjective)
I have 25 years old→I am 25 years old(Age with 'to be')
