Perhaps our greatest errors of judgment arise not from a lack of intelligence, but from the quiet, unseen shortcuts our brains take to keep us comfortable. This session explores cognitive biases as a maze of human vulnerabilities rather than mere clinical flaws. Together, we will gently examine our own patterns of thinking, seeking a more lucid, objective relationship with reality.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Cognitive Bias
systematic pattern of deviation from rationality.
Confirmation bias is a very common cognitive bias.
Confirmation Bias
favoring information that confirms existing beliefs.
He only reads news that agrees with him because of confirmation bias.
Hindsight Bias
tendency to see events as predictable after they have happened.
"I knew it all along" is a classic sign of hindsight bias.
Anchor
to rely too heavily on the first piece of information offered.
The initial price anchors the negotiation.
Heuristic
mental shortcut that allows people to solve problems quickly.
Heuristics are useful but can lead to cognitive biases.
Rationality
the quality of being based on or in accordance with reason or logic.
Emotions can often cloud our rationality.
Inference
a conclusion reached on the basis of evidence and reasoning.
His inference was based on several observed patterns.
Correlation
a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things.
Correlation does not always imply causation.
Perception
the way in which something is regarded, understood, or interpreted.
Our perception of risk is often biased by recent events.
Dissonance
lack of agreement; inconsistency.
Cognitive dissonance occurs when we hold two conflicting beliefs.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Spot the Bias
  • Perhaps when we buy things we do not need simply because they are "on sale," we are trying to buy a version of ourselves we secretly hope to become. Have you ever noticed this subtle lure?
  • Most of us are convinced that we see the world with perfect clarity, while others are hopelessly biased. Do you think you are more or less biased than the average soul?
🔵 Round 1 — Deep Discussion▲
Questions
Perhaps being completely certain of something is merely a shield against the anxiety of not knowing. Can you recall a time when your absolute certainty was shattered by a new perspective? (Consider the role of Cognitive Bias here.)
★ Have you ever tried to intentionally look for information that contradicts your opinion?
Perhaps the retrospective "I knew it all along" is a gentle delusion to make a chaotic world feel predictable. Why do we seek shelter in this comfort? (Consider the role of Confirmation Bias here.)
★ Can you think of a recent event where you felt hindsight bias?
Perhaps our desires are anchored not by what things are worth, but by the first impression we are presented with. How does this initial anchor govern your relationship with spending? (Consider the role of Hindsight Bias here.)
★ Do you usually check prices from different sources before buying something expensive?
Perhaps we defend our opinions so fiercely because changing our minds feels like losing a piece of who we are. Why is it so difficult to let go? (Consider the role of Anchor here.)
★ Has anyone ever successfully changed your mind about a deeply held belief?
Perhaps social media doesn't just connect us; it reflects a distorted version of our own thinking, trapping us in a curated echo chamber. Have you noticed your "feed" only showing you what you already believe? (Consider the role of Heuristic here.)
★ Have you noticed your "feed" only showing you things you already like or believe?
Perhaps beauty is an unfair proxy for goodness. Have you ever assumed someone was exceptionally wise or trustworthy simply because they were charming or well-dressed? (Consider the role of Rationality here.)
★ Have you ever assumed someone was smart just because they were well-dressed or attractive?
Perhaps we fear what is dramatic and highly publicized, while ignoring the quiet, everyday risks that actually shape our lives. Why are we so easily swayed by what is memorable? (Consider the role of Inference here.)
★ Are you more afraid of things that are frequently in the news, even if they are statistically rare?
Perhaps to be human is to be biased, and the dream of complete objectivity is itself an illusion. How do you practice a gentle, conscious management of your own mental shortcuts? (Consider the role of Correlation here.)
★ What is your personal strategy for making objective decisions?
Perhaps the desire to belong is stronger than the desire to be right, leading us to stay silent when a group moves toward a bad decision. Have you ever stayed quiet to preserve harmony? (Consider the role of Perception here.)
★ Have you ever stayed silent in a meeting because everyone else agreed on a bad idea?
Perhaps the most dangerous bias is the one we refuse to see in ourselves. Which cognitive illusion do you believe poses the greatest threat to our collective empathy today? (Consider the role of Dissonance here.)
★ Do you think education can help people overcome these mental traps?

🟢 Round 2 — Conditional Scenarios▲
Debate
Perhaps we purchase things to quieten an internal restlessness. If you hadn't been influenced by confirmation bias, would you still have made your last major purchase? (How does Cognitive Bias affect this statement?)
Perhaps true fairness is an algorithmic dream. If you were designing a hiring process, how would you account for the subconscious shortcuts we all make? (How does Confirmation Bias affect this statement?)
Perhaps we only see the logic of our lives in retrospect. If you had recognized the "halo effect" years ago, would you have chosen different companions? (How does Hindsight Bias affect this statement?)
Perhaps mental friction is the price of genuine thought. If we could never make quick, biased assumptions, would we be paralyzed by the complexity of daily life? (How does Anchor affect this statement?)
Perhaps our political disagreements are merely psychological defense mechanisms magnified by technology. If social media disappeared, would our debates become gentler? (How does Heuristic affect this statement?)
Perhaps we are all sleepwalking through our own histories. If you had understood your cognitive distortions sooner, would your career have taken a gentler path? (How does Rationality affect this statement?)
Perhaps we find comfort in machines because they lack the messy complexity of human emotion. Would you trust a perfectly rational AI to make your most intimate decisions? (How does Inference affect this statement?)
Perhaps bias is simply our evolutionary armor, designed to keep us alive rather than accurate. If we were purely objective, would we survive an emergency? (How does Correlation affect this statement?)
Perhaps a new culture is a mirror that reveals the biases we didn't know we had. If you relocated tomorrow, which of your mental shortcuts would be exposed as mere cultural habits? (How does Perception affect this statement?)
Perhaps the ultimate act of maturity is to welcome cognitive dissonance as an opportunity to grow. If you could eliminate just one bias from your mind, which would it be? (How does Dissonance affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I suggest to read the article→I suggest reading the article / I suggest that we read the article(Gerund pattern after 'suggest' vs infinitive)
Our biases can easy cheat our perception→Our biases can easily distort/deceive our perception(Adverb position + elevated verb choice 'distort' or 'deceive' instead of 'cheat')
I am agree with your perspective on confirmation bias→I share/concur with your perspective on confirmation bias(Elevating register with 'share' or 'concur' instead of incorrect auxiliary verb structure)
This is depending of the cognitive context→This depends on / is dependent on the cognitive context(Preposition collocation: 'depends on' / adjective structure 'dependent on')
The marketer made a prejudice based on our choices→The marketer formed a prejudice / was prejudiced against us based on our choices(Noun collocation: 'form/have a prejudice' vs incorrect light verb usage)
