Saudade is a deep emotional state of nostalgic or profound melancholic longing for an absent something or someone that one cares for and loves. This session explores the beauty of untranslatable words and the complex human emotions they represent. We will discuss how embracing such feelings can lead to a richer, more empathetic understanding of the human experience.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Longing
a strong wish for something or someone.
He felt a deep longing to return to his childhood home.
Melancholy
a feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause.
The rainy afternoon filled her with a sense of gentle melancholy.
Untranslatable
not able to be expressed in another language.
Many Portuguese speakers believe that 'saudade' is completely untranslatable.
Nostalgia
a sentimental longing or wistful affection for the past.
Looking at old photographs always triggers a wave of nostalgia.
Wistful
having or showing a feeling of vague or regretful longing.
She gave a wistful smile as she remembered her days at university.
Evocative
bringing strong images, memories, or feelings to mind.
The smell of lavender was highly evocative of her grandmother's garden.
Bittersweet
arousing pleasure tinged with sadness or pain.
It was a bittersweet moment when they said goodbye at the airport.
Poignant
evoking a keen sense of sadness or regret.
The film's ending was incredibly poignant and left the audience in tears.
Ephemeral
lasting for a very short time.
The beauty of the sunset is ephemeral, fading within minutes.
Resonance
the quality in a sound or image of being deep, full, and reverberating.
His words had a deep emotional resonance with everyone in the room.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Definition of Saudade: "The presence of absence."
  • Have you ever felt a deep connection to a place you've never been?
  • What is one word in your native language that has no direct English translation?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
What is the specific object of your longing when you feel homesick or lonely?
How does listening to certain music evoke a sense of melancholy in your soul?
Why do you think some emotions are untranslatable across different cultures?
Is nostalgia always a positive feeling, or can it prevent us from living in the present?
When was the last time you felt wistful while looking at old family photographs?
Which smells or tastes are most evocative of your childhood home?
Why are bittersweet endings in movies often more memorable than happy ones?
Can you share a poignant moment from your life that completely changed your perspective?
How does the ephemeral nature of life make our experiences more valuable?
How can a simple word have such a deep resonance across different generations?

Debate
A world without longing would be a world without ambition or creativity.
Constant melancholy is a sign of deep intelligence and emotional maturity.

Embracing the ephemeral nature of relationships is the only way to avoid heartbreak.

I am feeling nostalgia→I feel nostalgia / I'm feeling nostalgic(Noun vs Adjective)
The book is very poignant for me→The book is very poignant / The book really resonates with me(Usage of poignant)
It is depend of the person→It depends on the person(Structure and preposition)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
It is depend on you→It depends on you(Verb vs Adjective)
