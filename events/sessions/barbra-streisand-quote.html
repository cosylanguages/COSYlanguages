📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Self-image
the way a person sees themselves, including appearance and personality
Example: Her self-image improved after she started acting classes.
Unconventional
different from what is usual or expected
Example: His unconventional voice made him stand out in the competition.
To stand out
to be noticeably different from others, often in a positive way
Example: Her style helps her stand out in a crowded industry.
Upbringing
the way a child is raised and educated by their family
Example: His strict upbringing influenced his discipline and work ethic.
Perception
the way people understand or see something or someone
Example: Public perception of celebrities can change quickly.
Confidence
belief in one’s own abilities or value
Example: Confidence plays a key role in public speaking.
Stereotype
a fixed, oversimplified idea about a group of people
Example: She challenged stereotypes about how singers should look.
Identity
the qualities and beliefs that make a person who they are
Example: Music became a key part of his identity.
Ambition
a strong desire to do or to achieve something
Example: Her ambition to become a star was clear from a young age.
Insecurity
uncertainty or anxiety about oneself; lack of confidence
Example: Overcoming insecurity is a major part of personal growth.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Introduction to the Quote “I knew that with a mouth like mine, I just hadda be a star or something.” — Barbra Streisand
  • What do you think Barbra Streisand meant by "a mouth like mine"?
  • Is it important to be "born for" a certain career?
🔵 Round 1 — Understanding & Discussion (10 Units)▲
Analysis
1. Do you think Barbra Streisand’s self-image was influenced more by society or by her own confidence?
★ Have you ever felt your self-image was affected by how others saw you?
2. How can an unconventional appearance or voice become an advantage in building a career?
★ Do you consider any of your traits unconventional in a positive way?
3. How important is it for a person to stand out in creative industries like music or acting?
★ In what situations do you prefer to stand out, and when do you prefer not to?
4. Can upbringing shape whether someone believes they can become a “star”? How?
★ What part of your upbringing influenced your ambitions the most?
5. Do you think public perception of beauty has changed in the last 20 years?
★ Have your own perceptions of beauty changed over time?
6. How does confidence affect the way a person uses their voice or personality in public?
★ When do you feel most confident speaking in front of others?
7. Can stereotypes about appearance stop talented people from being successful?
★ Have you ever felt limited by a stereotype (personally or socially)?
8. How does personal identity influence career choices in creative fields?
★ What aspects of your identity are most important to you?
9. Do people sometimes become successful because they transform their “weaknesses” into strengths?
★ Have you ever turned a challenge into an advantage in your life?
10. Would you say society today is more accepting of unconventional stars than in the past?
★ Do you feel more accepted for who you are today than before?

🟢 Round 2 — Future Problems, People & Solutions (10 Units)▲
Future Focus
1. In the future, social media will worsen self-image problems for young people unless stronger education is introduced.
💡 What solutions could schools or platforms introduce?
2. Future workplaces will value unconventional personalities more, but only if bias in hiring is reduced.
💡 What systems could reduce hiring bias?
3. More people will struggle to stand out because AI will create similar content from everyone.
💡 How can individuals stay unique in an AI-driven world?
4. Future children’s upbringing will be heavily influenced by digital environments instead of family.
💡 How can families protect identity development?
5. Public perception of beauty will become more diverse, but misinformation may still create new standards.
💡 How can society prevent harmful beauty trends?
6. In the future, lack of confidence will be one of the biggest barriers to career success.
💡 What tools (education, tech, coaching) could help build confidence?
7. Stereotypes will not disappear unless AI systems and media actively reduce bias in content creation.
💡 How can technology help remove stereotypes?
8. People’s identity will become more digital, creating confusion between real and online selves.
💡 How can individuals protect a stable identity?
9. Future societies may struggle when people cannot turn insecurity into motivation due to constant comparison online.
💡 What strategies could reduce harmful comparison?
10. The biggest challenge in the future will be helping people with unconventional traits feel included in mainstream success.
💡 What policies or cultural changes could support inclusion?
