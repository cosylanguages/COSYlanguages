Quote

"Algorithms and artificial intelligence are the opposite of taking risks and to me that is the opposite of making art."

This session debates the provocative quote by pop icon Madonna about the role of algorithms and artificial intelligence in creative industries. We will discuss whether machine-generated art lacks the vulnerability, error, and risk-taking that define true human creativity. Participants will explore the future of painting, music, and writing in an age of automated production.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Algorithm
a process or set of rules to be followed in calculations or other problem-solving operations, especially by a computer.
Streaming platforms use an advanced algorithm to suggest new songs.
Opposite
a person or thing that is totally different from or the reverse of someone or something else.
Cold is the natural opposite of hot.
Risk
a situation involving exposure to danger or loss.
Every great creative project involves some level of personal risk.
Art
the expression or application of human creative skill and imagination, typically in a visual form.
She believes that cooking delicious meals is a beautiful form of art.
Predictable
behaving or occurring in a way that is expected.
Machine-generated plots in books are often very predictable.
Vulnerability ≠ Invulnerability
state of being exposed to the possibility of being attacked or harmed ≠ the state of being impossible to harm or defeat.
Embracing vulnerability is a far more courageous path than pretending to possess absolute invulnerability.
Automate
to make a process operate automatically by using computers or machines.
You can automate many repetitive tasks in an office easily.
Authenticity
the quality of being authentic, genuine, or true.
Listeners appreciate the raw authenticity of her early vocal recordings.
Formulaic
produced according to a formula or standard recipe; unoriginal.
The blockbuster movie felt highly formulaic and uninspiring.
Imperfection
a fault, blemish, or undesirable feature.
A tiny imperfection in a handmade vase reveals its human origin.

Short introduction to modern art.

  • Have you ever used an AI tool to write a poem or generate an image?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Interpretations
Discuss Madonna's views on algorithms and risk in art.
What does Madonna mean when she says algorithms are the opposite of taking risks?
★ What is the biggest personal risk you have ever taken to pursue a dream?
Why is taking a risk essential for making great art?
★ Do you consider yourself a risk-taker in your daily choices?
Why is algorithm-based entertainment highly predictable?
★ Do you find streaming platform recommendations accurate or uninspiring?
How does expressing vulnerability build a connection with an audience?
★ Is it easy for you to share your vulnerable moments with friends?
Can we truly automate the process of creative songwriting?
★ Do you enjoy songs that are heavily edited with computer effects?
Why is authenticity becoming a rare commodity in modern media?
★ Who is the most authentic artist or public figure you follow?
Why do many blockbuster movies feel highly formulaic?
★ What is your favorite movie genre to watch when you want to relax?
Why does a minor human imperfection add beauty to a painting?
★ Do you value handmade crafts more than mass-produced items?
Will artificial intelligence ever feel real emotions like sorrow or joy?
★ Do you think AI could ever act as an empathetic therapist?
How should artists adapt to the rise of generative technology?
★ What creative skill would you most like to learn next?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Debate
Debate these predictions about algorithms, AI, and human culture.
In the future, technology will enable us to digitize and share our experience of Algorithm directly. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ How would you personally adapt if Algorithm became entirely obsolete in tomorrow's world?
The rapid speed of modern society will eventually make Opposite an obsolete concept. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ Would you be willing to use technology to artificially boost your capacity for Opposite?
Future educational systems will prioritize the cultivation of Risk over traditional academic skills. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ How do you want future generations of your family to remember your relationship with Risk?
As artificial intelligence advances, machines will exhibit a superior capacity for Art compared to humans. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ If you could design a futuristic school, how would you teach the value of Art to children?
In the coming decades, Predictable will become a highly commercialized luxury commodity. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ Do you fear a future where Predictable is fully managed or monitored by digital algorithms?
The survival of human civilization will depend on our collective ability to practice Vulnerability. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ How do you personally prepare for a future where Vulnerability is increasingly rare?
Future generations will look back at our current understanding of Automate with deep skepticism. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ If you could send a single message about Automate to people living 100 years in the future, what would it be?
Global digital connectivity will make true Authenticity incredibly difficult to maintain in the future. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ Would you choose to live in a futuristic utopia that completely guarantees Authenticity at the cost of your freedom?
In the future, social status will be determined by a person's capacity for Formulaic rather than wealth. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ What role do you hope Formulaic will play in your own personal future over the next decade?
A world fully governed by logic will have no space left for the mystery of Imperfection. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ If a machine could perfectly replicate your experience of Imperfection, would you still value it?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The AI can to make art→AI can make art(Can + infinitive without 'to', omit 'the' for general technology)
I am agree with Madonna→I agree with Madonna(Agree is a verb)
It depend of your preference→It depends on your preference(Depends + on, singular verb s)
