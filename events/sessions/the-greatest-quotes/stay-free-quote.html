Quote

“None of us really have a choice in anything but we do have a choice to be ourselves. Stay free”

This session explores the profound idea that true freedom begins with the choice to be ourselves despite external pressures. We will delve into the concepts of authenticity and social conformity, examining why it is often difficult to remain independent in a world of expectations. Participants will discuss the balance between societal roles and personal identity in their quest to "stay free".

☕ The Philosophers' Ledger: Café Debates

"Let us see how many interpretations exist! There is never only one correct answer to any profound human question." — Oscar Wilde

📖 Session Vocabulary

Authentic
being real and true to your own personality, values, and beliefs.
He stayed authentic even when others wanted him to change.
Identity
the qualities, beliefs, and characteristics that make a person who they are.
Traveling helped her understand her identity better.
Pressure
influence or force that encourages someone to behave in a certain way.
Many teenagers feel pressure to fit in with their peers.
Conform
to change your behavior or opinions to match those of a group.
He refused to conform to social expectations.
Independent
able to think, act, and make decisions without relying on others.
She became more independent after moving to another city.
Values
important principles or beliefs that guide a person's decisions and actions.
Honesty and respect are among his strongest values.
Expectation
a belief that something should happen or that someone should behave in a particular way.
There is often an expectation that young people attend university.
Individuality
the qualities and characteristics that make someone unique.
The company encourages employees to express their individuality.
Freedom ≠ Slavery
the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants ≠ the state of being a slave or completely controlled.
They fought courageously to defend their freedom and escape from a lifetime of cruel slavery.
Self-expression
the act of showing your thoughts, feelings, or personality through words, actions, or creativity.
Art gives people a powerful way of self-expression.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Choice & Authenticity
  • What does "staying free" mean to you personally?
  • Do you think we have as much choice in life as we believe?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Interpretations
Do you think it's possible to "be ourselves" 100% of the time? (Consider the role of Authentic here.)
★ When do you feel most like your true self?
How do social expectations conflict with individual freedom? (Consider the role of Identity here.)
★ Have you ever made a choice just to please your family or society?
What is the difference between "being free" and "having choices"? (Consider the role of Pressure here.)
★ Can you feel free even when you have limited options?
How does technology influence our ability to stay authentic? (Consider the role of Conform here.)
★ Do you feel a need to perform or filter yourself on social media?
Why do we often feel we "have no choice" in our daily responsibilities? (Consider the role of Independent here.)
★ What part of your daily routine feels like a real choice to you?
Can someone be authentic in a career that requires strict conformity? (Consider the role of Values here.)
How does travel help people discover their true identity? (Consider the role of Expectation here.)
Is "staying free" a selfish act or a necessary one for mental health? (Consider the role of Individuality here.)
What are the long-term consequences of constantly conforming to others? (Consider the role of Freedom here.)
If you could change one life decision to be more "yourself," which would it be? (Consider the role of Self-expression here.)

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Debate / Deep Dive
In the future, people may not notice when AI starts choosing small things for them. (How does Authentic affect this statement?)
Future people might feel “free,” but their choices will be guided by algorithms. (How does Identity affect this statement?)
Pressure to show a “perfect life” online will become stronger than family pressure.
People will conform to what algorithms decide is “normal” rather than to society.
Education systems will focus more on “fitting into systems” than on identity. (How does Independent affect this statement?)
Authenticity will be seen as a problem because it is harder to control than behavior. (How does Values affect this statement?)
People might lose their inner voice because they rely more on external feedback. (How does Expectation affect this statement?)
Future self-expression might be so filtered that no one knows what is real anymore. (How does Individuality affect this statement?)
Values will change faster than identity, making people unsure what they truly believe. (How does Freedom affect this statement?)
The biggest future paradox: unlimited choice, but feeling completely stuck. (How does Self-expression affect this statement?)
