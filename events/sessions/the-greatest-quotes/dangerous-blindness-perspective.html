Topic

Cognitive Biases and Narrow Perspectives

This session explores the concept of "dangerous blindness," which refers to the inability to see the world from any perspective other than one's own. We will discuss the psychological and social risks of living in an echo chamber where our biases are constantly reinforced. Join us as we examine how expanding our intellectual horizons can prevent the costly mistakes caused by a narrow worldview.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Perspective
a particular attitude toward or way of regarding something; a point of view.
Gaining a global perspective is essential for solving international environmental problems.
Blindness
the state of being unable to see or perceive something.
Cognitive blindness often prevents us from noticing errors in our own logical reasoning.
Bias
prejudice in favor of or against one thing, person, or group compared with another.
The selection committee was accused of having a clear bias toward candidates from Ivy League schools.
Reinforce
strengthen or support (an existing feeling, idea, or habit).
Social media algorithms often reinforce our existing beliefs by showing us only what we like.
Insular
ignorant of or uninterested in cultures, ideas, or peoples outside one's own experience.
Living in an insular community can make it difficult to adapt to a multicultural workplace.
Fallacy
a mistaken belief, especially one based on unsound argument.
The idea that "more expensive is always better" is a common consumer fallacy.
Echo chamber
an environment in which a person encounters only beliefs or opinions that coincide with their own.
It is important to read news from different sources to avoid being trapped in an echo chamber.
Intellectual
relating to the intellect; involving great mental capacity.
Engaging in intellectual debates can help you sharpen your own critical thinking skills.
Dogma
a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true.
Scientific progress often requires challenging the established dogma of the previous generation.
Empathy ≠ Apathy
the ability to understand other's feelings ≠ a lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern.
Hearing people's thoughts taught him true empathy, replacing his lifelong cold apathy.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Think about a time you were completely wrong about something.
  • Why is it so hard to admit that we have "blind spots"?
  • Do you usually seek out information that proves you are right or information that challenges you?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
How does your professional perspective change when you work with people from completely different industries?
★ When was the last time you had to confront Perspective in your own personal journey?
What is the most dangerous form of social blindness currently affecting our modern society?
★ How comfortable are you with allowing Blindness to guide your daily decisions?
How can we identify our own internal bias before it leads us to make a poor decision?
★ Have you ever felt a deep sense of conflict regarding Bias in a relationship?
Why do we tend to reinforce our own opinions by hanging out with people who already agree with us?
★ Can you share a moment from your childhood where Reinforce felt completely natural?
In what ways can an insular upbringing limit a person's future career opportunities?
★ How do you personally nurture Insular when you feel emotionally exhausted?
Can you name a common fallacy that people often use when discussing politics or religion?
★ Have you ever had to choose between social conformity and personal Fallacy?
How has the rise of the internet turned the whole world into a collection of small echo chambers, especially when considering the role of Echo chamber?
★ Who in your life best embodies the essence of Echo chamber, and what have they taught you?
Why is intellectual humility often more valuable than being the smartest person in the room?
★ In what area of your life do you feel Intellectual is most desperately needed right now?
Is it ever acceptable to follow a dogma without questioning its origins or its effects?
★ How has your personal understanding of Dogma shifted over the last five years?
How does developing empathy help us overcome the "dangerous blindness" of our own ego?
★ If you had to describe your personal experience with Empathy in one word, what would it be?

Debate

★ How do you want future generations of your family to remember your relationship with Bias?
As artificial intelligence advances, machines will exhibit a superior capacity for Reinforce compared to humans. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ If you could design a futuristic school, how would you teach the value of Reinforce to children?

★ Do you fear a future where Insular is fully managed or monitored by digital algorithms?

★ How do you personally prepare for a future where Fallacy is increasingly rare?

★ If you could send a single message about Echo chamber to people living 100 years in the future, what would it be?

★ If a machine could perfectly replicate your experience of Empathy, would you still value it?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
It is a blindness spot→It is a blind spot(Idiomatic expression)
He is bias toward them→He is biased toward them(Noun vs Adjective)
I am live in an echo chamber since years→I have lived in an echo chamber for years / I've been living in an echo chamber for years(Present Perfect + Duration)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
It is depend on you→It depends on you(Verb vs Adjective)
