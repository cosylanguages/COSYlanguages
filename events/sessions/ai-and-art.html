📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Originality
the ability to think independently and creatively
Example: The designer's work is known for its originality.
Aesthetic
concerned with beauty or the appreciation of beauty
Example: The painting has a very modern aesthetic.
Canvas
a strong, coarse unbleached cloth used as a surface for painting
Example: The artist prepared the canvas before starting to paint.
Masterpiece
a work of outstanding artistry, skill, or workmanship
Example: Many consider the Mona Lisa to be Da Vinci's masterpiece.
Inspiration
the process of being mentally stimulated to do or feel something creative
Example: Travel is a great source of inspiration for many artists.
Generative
having the power or function of generating, producing, or reproducing
Example: Generative AI can create completely new images from text.
Subjective
based on or influenced by personal feelings, tastes, or opinions
Example: Art is highly subjective; what one person loves, another may hate.
Craftsmanship
skill in a particular craft
Example: You can see the incredible craftsmanship in this handmade furniture.
Copyright
the exclusive legal right to reproduce, publish, sell, or distribute a work
Example: There are many legal battles over AI and copyright.
Provocative
causing annoyance, anger, or another strong reaction, especially deliberately
Example: The artist is known for his provocative and controversial works.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Creativity Definitions
  • What makes someone "creative"?
  • Can a machine have a "soul"?
🔵 Round 1 — Hot Takes (10 Questions)▲
Debate
1. An AI-generated painting just sold for $400,000. Is it "art"?
★ Would you pay for a piece of art if you knew a machine made it?
2. Does AI "create" or does it just "remix" human work?
3. If a robot writes a book, who owns the copyright?
4. Is the value of art in the final product or the human effort behind it?
5. Should AI be banned from art competitions?
6. Will AI eventually become "better" than human artists?
7. Can AI ever understand the "meaning" of what it produces?
8. How does AI change the job market for graphic designers?
9. Should artists be compensated when their work is used to train AI?
10. Is "algorithmic art" just a temporary trend?

🟢 Round 2 — Dilemmas (10 Units)▲
Future Focus
1. In 20 years, will human artists be a luxury or obsolete?
2. If AI can produce perfect music for your mood, will you still listen to human bands?
3. Is it "cheating" for an architect to use AI to design a building?
4. Should we use AI to finish incomplete works by dead masters?
5. Will AI-generated influencers replace real human models?
6. Is creativity a biological mystery or just a logical process?
7. Should governments regulate the amount of AI content on television?
8. If an AI "paints" a masterpiece, can we call the AI an "artist"?
9. How will AI change how we teach art in schools?
10. What is the one thing AI will never be able to do in art?
