Offline Access

Sonder is the realization that each random passerby is living a life as vivid and complex as your own. This session explores the profound impact of this realization on our capacity for empathy and social connection. We will discuss how recognizing the hidden depth in everyone we meet can transform our daily interactions and our understanding of the world.

☕ The Philosophers' Ledger: Café Debates

"Let us see how many interpretations exist! There is never only one correct answer to any profound human question." — Oscar Wilde

📖 Session Vocabulary

Sonder
the profound feeling of realizing that everyone has a complex life.
Walking through the crowded station, he was struck by a sudden sense of sonder.
Passerby
a person who happens to be going past something or someone.
The street musician played his violin as every passerby ignored him.
Vivid
producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind.
He had a vivid memory of his first day at school.
Intricate
very complicated or detailed.
The novel's plot was so intricate that I had to read it twice.
Anonymity
the condition of being anonymous or unknown.
The artist preferred anonymity and never showed his face in public.
Ephemeral
lasting for a very short time.
Our interaction was ephemeral, but it left a lasting impression on me.
Internalize
make (attitudes or behavior) part of one's nature by learning or unconscious assimilation.
It takes time to internalize the values of a new culture.
Multifaceted
having many sides or aspects.
The problem is multifaceted and cannot be solved with a simple answer.
Subconscious
concerning the part of the mind of which one is not fully aware.
Our subconscious fears often influence our daily decisions.
Perspective
a particular way of regarding something; a point of view.
Traveling to different countries gives you a much broader perspective on life.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Analyze the concept: "Everyone has a story you know nothing about."
  • When was the last time you were surprised by the hidden depth of a stranger?
  • Do you find the idea of billions of complex lives overwhelming or beautiful?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
How often do you experience a sense of sonder while commuting or walking in a crowded city?
Do you ever wonder about the destination and purpose of a random passerby you see on the street?
Can you describe a vivid moment when you realized you were just a background character in someone else's story?
Why is it so easy to forget the intricate details of other people's lives when we are stressed?
Does the anonymity of a large city make it easier or harder to feel empathy for others?
How do ephemeral interactions with strangers sometimes leave the deepest impact on our mood?
Is it possible to truly internalize the idea that every single person is as important as you are?
In what ways are the personalities of your closest friends more multifaceted than they first appeared?
How does our subconscious bias affect how we judge people we only see for a few seconds?
How can changing your perspective to include the lives of others reduce your own personal anxieties?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Debate
True sonder is impossible because we can never truly understand another person's mind.
If we spoke to every passerby, we would eventually realize that we are all the same.
A vivid imagination is more important for empathy than actual life experience.
Modern social media has made our lives less intricate by reducing us to simple profiles.
The anonymity of the internet is the biggest obstacle to global empathy.
Most human connections are ephemeral and therefore not worth pursuing deeply.
We should internalize the stories of others to become better world citizens.
Every single human conflict is multifaceted and can never be solved by force.
Our subconscious mind is actually more empathetic than our conscious logical mind.
A global perspective is the only way to ensure the survival of the human race.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
Every people has a life→Every person has a life / All people have lives(Subject-verb agreement)
It makes me to think about others→It makes me think about others(Make + object + bare infinitive)
I am interesting in their stories→I am interested in their stories(-ed vs -ing)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
It is depend on you→It depends on you(Verb vs Adjective)
