Quote

“Don't take criticism from someone you wouldn't take advice from.”

Jim Kwik's advice on managing feedback is a vital lesson in protecting one's mental energy and self-esteem. This session explores the psychological difference between constructive criticism and harmful judgment, and how to identify whose opinions truly matter for our growth. We will discuss strategies for filtering the noise of external expectations and building a resilient mindset in an age of constant social feedback.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Criticism
negative or constructive feedback about someone or something.
She accepted the criticism and improved her presentation.
Advice
guidance or recommendations offered to help someone.
He gave me advice on how to handle stress at work.
Judgment
forming an opinion, often quickly or critically.
Try not to make judgment before hearing the full story.
Feedback
information about performance or behavior used for improvement.
The teacher gave detailed feedback on my essay.
Perspective
a particular way of thinking about something.
From her perspective, the decision made sense.
Validation
recognition that someone’s feelings or ideas are valid.
He was looking for validation from his peers.
Influence
the power to affect someone’s decisions or behavior.
Friends can have a strong influence on our choices.
Resilience ≠ Fragility
the capacity to recover quickly from difficulties ≠ the quality of being easily broken or damaged.
True resilience is forged by understanding and accepting our own emotional fragility.
Bias
a tendency to favor one side unfairly.
We all have bias when evaluating our own work.
Self-worth
confidence in your own value as a person.
Building self-worth helps you ignore unnecessary negativity.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Inner Circle
  • Whose advice do you trust the most in your life?
  • What was the last piece of criticism you received?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Interpretations
What does this quote mean to you in terms of credibility and who deserves to give constructive criticism?
★ Can you recall a time when credibility changed your reaction to feedback?
How do you decide whose feedback or criticism to trust, and how does professional judgment influence that decision? (Consider the role of Advice here.)
★ Has your process changed with more self-awareness?
Is it easy or difficult for you to accept constructive criticism? Why? (Consider the role of Judgment here.)
★ What type of feedback most strongly affects your self-worth?
Can criticism be useful even when it feels uncomfortable? How do you separate constructive criticism from projection?
★ Have you experienced feedback that improved your perspective later?
Who gives you the most reliable feedback, and why is their perspective trustworthy?
★ Is it experience, honesty, or something else in their credibility?
How do you react when you disagree with someone’s opinion about you, using emotional detachment if possible? (Consider the role of Validation here.)
★ Do you react immediately or reflect first to avoid misinterpretation?
Have you ever ignored criticism due to bias and later regretted it in your feedback loop? (Consider the role of Influence here.)
★ What did it teach you about self-awareness?
Do social media opinions affect people’s self-worth and need for validation? (Consider the role of Resilience here.)
★ How do you manage external influence and validation?
What’s the key difference between constructive criticism and negativity or judgment? (Consider the role of Bias here.)
★ Can people always recognize this difference in real life?
How important is internal validation when receiving criticism and building resilience? (Consider the role of Self-worth here.)
★ Does confidence change how you interpret feedback or your emotional detachment?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Projections
In the future, credibility will matter less than popularity when people judge constructive criticism online.
AI-driven feedback systems will improve human professional judgment, not replace it. (Consider the role of Advice here.)
Strong self-awareness will become a mandatory workplace skill for handling digital criticism. (Consider the role of Judgment here.)
Communication tools will eventually be able to distinguish constructive criticism from projection. (Consider the role of Feedback here.)
Future generations will rely exclusively on internal validation rather than external opinions. (Consider the role of Perspective here.)
Social media platforms will eventually eliminate the "like" button to protect users' self-worth. (Consider the role of Validation here.)
Emotional detachment will be taught in schools as a core survival skill. (Consider the role of Influence here.)
Feedback loops in the workplace will become 100% automated and non-human in the future. (Consider the role of Resilience here.)
AI will eventually eliminate all human bias from professional evaluation systems. (Consider the role of Bias here.)
The concept of "criticism" will be replaced by "collaborative adjustment" in the next century. (Consider the role of Self-worth here.)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
He gave me many advices→He gave me a lot of advice(Uncountable advice)
I don't like to be criticized→I don't like being criticized(Like + -ing)
It is worth to listen to him→It is worth listening to him(Worth + -ing)
I suggest you to take the advice→I suggest (that) you take the advice(Suggest structure)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
