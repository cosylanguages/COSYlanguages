Quote

“Don't take criticism from someone you wouldn't take advice from.”

📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Criticism
negative or constructive feedback about someone or something
Example: She accepted the criticism and improved her presentation.
Advice
guidance or recommendations offered to help someone
Example: He gave me advice on how to handle stress at work.
Judgment
forming an opinion, often quickly or critically
Example: Try not to make judgment before hearing the full story.
Feedback
information about performance or behavior used for improvement
Example: The teacher gave detailed feedback on my essay.
Perspective
a particular way of thinking about something
Example: From her perspective, the decision made sense.
Validation
recognition that someone’s feelings or ideas are valid
Example: He was looking for validation from his peers.
Influence
the power to affect someone’s decisions or behavior
Example: Friends can have a strong influence on our choices.
Resilience
the ability to recover from difficulties or criticism
Example: Her resilience helped her overcome failure.
Bias
a tendency to favor one side unfairly
Example: We all have bias when evaluating our own work.
Self-worth
confidence in your own value as a person
Example: Building self-worth helps you ignore unnecessary negativity.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Inner Circle
  • Whose advice do you trust the most in your life?
  • What was the last piece of criticism you received?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis of the Quote (10 Questions)▲
Personal Perspective
1. What does this quote mean to you in terms of credibility and who deserves to give constructive criticism?
★ Can you recall a time when credibility changed your reaction to feedback?
2. How do you decide whose feedback or criticism to trust, and how does professional judgment influence that decision?
★ Has your process changed with more self-awareness?
3. Is it easy or difficult for you to accept constructive criticism? Why?
★ What type of feedback most strongly affects your self-worth?
4. Can criticism be useful even when it feels uncomfortable? How do you separate constructive criticism from projection?
★ Have you experienced feedback that improved your perspective later?
5. Who gives you the most reliable feedback, and why is their perspective trustworthy?
★ Is it experience, honesty, or something else in their credibility?
6. How do you react when you disagree with someone’s opinion about you, using emotional detachment if possible?
★ Do you react immediately or reflect first to avoid misinterpretation?
7. Have you ever ignored criticism due to bias and later regretted it in your feedback loop?
★ What did it teach you about self-awareness?
8. Do social media opinions affect people’s self-worth and need for validation?
★ How do you manage external influence and validation?
9. What’s the key difference between constructive criticism and negativity or judgment?
★ Can people always recognize this difference in real life?
10. How important is internal validation when receiving criticism and building resilience?
★ Does confidence change how you interpret feedback or your emotional detachment?

🟢 Round 2 — Future Statements (10 Units)▲
Agree or Disagree
1. In the future, credibility will matter less than popularity when people judge constructive criticism online.
★ Will influence replace expertise in shaping opinions?
2. AI-driven feedback systems will improve human professional judgment, not replace it.
★ Would you trust machine-generated feedback about yourself?
3. Strong self-awareness will become a key skill for handling increasing amounts of digital criticism.
★ Can self-awareness realistically keep up with online pressure?
4. People will become better at distinguishing constructive criticism from projection as communication tools improve.
★ Or will misinterpretation still increase?
5. Future generations will rely less on external validation and more on internal validation.
★ What might cause this shift — or prevent it?
6. Social media will continue to distort people’s self-worth, even if platforms introduce better moderation systems.
★ Can technology reduce emotional impact of judgment?
7. Emotional detachment will become an essential workplace skill in highly feedback-driven environments.
★ Could too much detachment reduce empathy?
8. In the future, feedback loops at work will become faster but less personal.
★ Would that improve or weaken development?
9. AI will reduce bias in evaluation systems, making professional judgment more objective.
★ Can bias ever be fully eliminated?
10. People will always struggle with misinterpretation of criticism, even with better communication technology.
★ Why do you think this problem persists?
