B2 – C2

Topic

AI literacy, critical thinking, and managing diverse learner needs.

The "Future-Proof Educator" session delves into the critical skills and mindsets required for teachers to thrive in an increasingly automated world. We explore the balance between leveraging AI for productivity and maintaining the essential human connections that drive deep learning. This discussion aims to define the professional identity of the modern educator and how to adapt to the shifting expectations of C1-C2 level learners.

📖 Session Vocabulary

AI Literacy
the ability to understand, use, and critically evaluate AI technologies.
Teachers need to develop AI literacy to guide students in the ethical use of large language models.
Differentiated Instruction
providing different students with different avenues to learning in the same classroom.
Differentiated instruction is essential when teaching a class with widely varying proficiency levels.
Translanguaging
multilingual speakers using their languages as an integrated communication system.
Translanguaging allows students to use their first language to help them understand complex concepts.
Authentic Assessment
evaluation requiring students to perform real-world tasks demonstrating meaningful application.
Asking students to write a real letter to a local politician is a form of authentic assessment.
Higher-Order Thinking Skills
skills such as analysis, evaluation, and creation that go beyond fact recall.
The lesson was designed to challenge the students' higher-order thinking skills (HOTS).
Inquiry-Based Learning
active learning starting with questions or problems rather than facts.
Inquiry-based learning encourages students to conduct their own research and find solutions.
Classroom Management
techniques used to keep students organized, focused, and productive.
Effective classroom management is the key to preventing disruptive behavior in large groups.
Neurodiversity
the idea that neurological differences are natural variations, not deficits.
Inclusive education requires an understanding of neurodiversity, such as autism and ADHD.
Growth Mindset
the belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed through dedication.
Students with a growth mindset view mistakes as opportunities to learn rather than failures.
Summative Assessment
assessment focused on the final outcome of a program or unit.
Final exams are the most common form of summative assessment in traditional schooling.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Think about the modern classroom.
  • Have you ever used ChatGPT or other AI to help you learn a language? How?
  • In your opinion, what is the most difficult type of student to teach?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
1. A: Authentic 'AI-resistant' tasks (like oral exams) are the only way to measure real progress now.
B: Essays are still valuable if we grade the "thinking process" and research logs rather than just the final text. (Consider the role of AI Literacy here.)
2. A: AI makes perfect linguistic accuracy less important than the ability to verify and curate information.
B: Flawless linguistic control remains the ultimate mark of a true professional and should be prioritized. (Consider the role of Differentiated Instruction here.)
3. A: Critical thinking and ethics should be the primary focus of C1 instruction, replacing advanced grammar drills.
B: Deep grammatical mastery is the foundation that allows for high-level critical expression. (Consider the role of Translanguaging here.)
4. A: Teachers should explicitly teach "AI prompting" as a core literacy for the modern world.
B: Focusing on AI tools distracts from the manual linguistic skills students need for brain development. (Consider the role of Authentic Assessment here.)
5. A: Classroom time should be spent 100% on spontaneous oral interaction to prevent AI-assisted cheating.
B: Writing remains a critical cognitive exercise that must be protected even if it is harder to monitor. (Consider the role of Higher-Order Thinking Skills here.)
6. A: AI literacy should be a mandatory subject for every teacher before they are allowed to enter a classroom.
B: Subject expertise and empathy are more important for a teacher than being a technology expert. (Consider the role of Inquiry-Based Learning here.)
7. A: Automation will eventually solve the global shortage of high-quality teachers.
B: Automation will only lead to a two-tier system where only the rich can afford human teachers. (Consider the role of Classroom Management here.)
8. A: AI companions can provide the emotional support and patience that overworked human teachers lack.
B: True empathy requires a shared biological experience that a machine can only simulate, never feel. (Consider the role of Neurodiversity here.)
9. A: Digital transparency allows us to track every step of a student's learning journey in real-time.
B: Constant digital monitoring creates a "panopticon" effect that destroys student creativity and privacy. (Consider the role of Growth Mindset here.)
10. A: The best educator is a "facilitator" who guides students through the infinite sea of digital info.
B: The best educator is a "master" who provides a definitive model of excellence for students to follow. (Consider the role of Summative Assessment here.)
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

Brainstorm: Design a language task that is impossible for an AI to complete successfully without human intervention.

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
1. A: Teaching children is exponentially more difficult due to their volatility and attention spans.
B: Teaching adults is harder because of their "fixed mindsets" and deep-seated performance anxiety. (How does AI Literacy affect this statement?)
2. A: Standardized exams are the only objective way to measure the ROI of an education system.
B: Exams create a "washback effect" that forces teachers to teach to the test rather than for life. (How does Differentiated Instruction affect this statement?)
3. A: "English Only" policies in class are essential to force the brain to stop translating internally.
B: Translanguaging (using L1 strategically) is a human right that respects the learner's identity and saves time.
4. A: Neurodiversity is a beautiful concept but practically impossible to manage in a class of 30 students.
B: If a teacher isn't planning for neurodiversity, they are essentially failing a significant part of their class. (How does Authentic Assessment affect this statement?)
5. A: Classroom management is about enforcing clear, non-negotiable rules for the safety of everyone.
B: Real management is about building relationships so that rules become unnecessary. (How does Higher-Order Thinking Skills affect this statement?)
6. A: Inquiry-based learning is too slow and chaotic for students who need results within a strict timeframe.
B: Telling a student the answer is a "shortcut to nowhere"; they must discover the knowledge themselves.
7. A: Professional burnout is an inevitable result of the emotional labor required in modern teaching.
B: Burnout is a systemic failure of school management, not an inherent part of the teaching profession. (How does Classroom Management affect this statement?)
8. A: The "Sage on the Stage" model provides the authority and clarity that students secretly crave.
B: The "Guide on the Side" model empowers students to take ownership of their own educational destiny. (How does Neurodiversity affect this statement?)
9. A: Digital distractions are so powerful that they have rendered traditional deep-reading tasks impossible.
B: Digital tools allow for a "hyper-textual" depth that previous generations could never have imagined. (How does Growth Mindset affect this statement?)
10. A: The biggest risk is the total commodification of education, turning it into a "fast-food" service.
B: The biggest risk is that education becomes an irrelevant relic of the pre-digital age. (How does Summative Assessment affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
It depends of the student → It depends on the student (Collocation: depend + on)
The AI can makes mistakes → AI can make mistakes (Modal verbs + bare infinitive)
I am agree with the point → I agree with the point ('Agree' is a verb, not an adjective)
