What is Fusion? 📖

📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Fusion
the process of joining two or more things together to form a single entity
Example: Nuclear fusion powers the sun and other stars.
Fission
the action of dividing or splitting something into two or more parts
Example: Traditional nuclear power plants use fission to generate electricity.
Plasma
an ionized gas consisting of positive ions and free electrons
Example: In a fusion reactor, the fuel must be heated until it becomes plasma.
Sustainability
the ability to be maintained at a certain rate or level
Example: Fusion is seen as a key to long-term energy sustainability.
Breakthrough
a sudden, dramatic, and important discovery or development
Example: Scientists recently achieved a major breakthrough in net energy gain.
Isotopes
forms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons
Example: Deuterium and tritium are isotopes of hydrogen used in fusion.
Magnetic confinement
using magnetic fields to contain fusion fuel in the form of a plasma
Example: The tokamak design uses magnetic confinement to stabilize the reaction.
Net energy gain
when the energy generated is greater than the energy required to start the reaction
Example: Achieving net energy gain is the primary goal of commercial fusion research.
Abundance
a very large quantity of something
Example: Hydrogen is found in great abundance in the Earth's oceans.
Radioactive waste
hazardous waste that contains radioactive material
Example: One advantage of fusion is that it produces very little long-lived radioactive waste.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Star in a Jar
  • If you could have a small "sun" in your basement providing unlimited power, would you want it?
  • What is the most "futuristic" thing you've heard about energy lately?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis (10 Questions)▲
Questions
1. What is fusion energy and how does it differ from fission?
★ Had you learned about this difference in school?
2. Why is fusion often called the "holy grail" of clean energy?
★ Do you think "holy grail" is a good metaphor for scientific goals?
3. What are the main technical challenges scientists face in achieving stable fusion?
★ Does the complexity of the task make you more or less hopeful?
4. How would a successful fusion reactor change the global economy?
★ Which industry in your country would be affected most?
5. Do you think governments should invest more in fusion research compared to wind or solar?
★ If you were in charge of the budget, where would the money go?
6. What role does "net energy gain" play in the success of fusion?
7. How safe is fusion compared to traditional nuclear power?
8. Could fusion energy help us reach "net zero" faster?
9. Is fusion energy too far in the future to be relevant for today's climate crisis?
10. If you were a scientist, would you dedicate your career to fusion research?

🟢 Round 2 — Future Focus (10 Units)▲
Projections
1. In 50 years, fusion energy will be the primary source of power for all major cities.
2. The cost of electricity will become almost zero once fusion is perfected.
3. Fusion will solve the water crisis by making mass desalination affordable.
4. Oil-producing countries will face total economic collapse because of fusion.
5. We will have portable fusion reactors for space travel within the next century.
6. Fusion energy will lead to a new era of global peace as energy competition ends.
7. Private companies will control fusion technology, creating a new "energy elite".
8. Fusion research will eventually prove that we don't need "renewables" like wind and solar.
9. The first commercial fusion plant will be built in an unexpected country, not a superpower.
10. Humanity will waste even more energy once it becomes abundant and "free".
✏️ Discussion (Mistakes)▲
The government should invest more→Governments should invest more(Generalizing with plurals)
I am looking forward to see it→I am looking forward to seeing it(Looking forward to + -ing)
It didn't happened yet→It hasn't happened yet(Present Perfect for 'yet')
Explain me the process→Explain the process to me(Direct/Indirect object)
The scientist suggested us that...→The scientist suggested that we...(Suggest structure)
