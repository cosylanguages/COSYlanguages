This session explores the sharp satirical sci-fi comedy-drama, Don't Look Up. We will discuss political denialism, media distraction, scientific authority, and the comet metaphor for climate change. Students will master high-value conversational terms and sharp American media slang.

📖 Session Vocabulary

1. Generic Discussion Vocabulary

Denialism
the practice of denying the validity of scientific facts.
Climate denialism is a major theme represented in the movie's plot.
Satire
the use of humor or irony to criticize stupidity or vices.
The film uses sharp satire to mock how politicians handle existential threats.
Metaphor
a thing regarded as representative or symbolic of something else.
The approaching space rock is a clear metaphor for climate change.
Distraction
a thing that prevents someone from giving full attention.
Pop star breakups are used as a massive distraction from scientific warnings.
Conspiracy
a secret plan by a group to do something harmful.
Many citizens believed the comet was a government conspiracy.
Credibility
the quality of being trusted and believed in.
The young scientist struggled to maintain her credibility on the morning talk show.
Apathy
lack of interest or concern about something important.
Public apathy is highly dangerous when dealing with a global crisis.
Speculate
to form a theory or conjecture without firm evidence.
The talk show hosts chose to speculate about the scientists' love lives instead of the data.
Catastrophe
an event causing great and sudden damage or suffering.
Only immediate global cooperation can prevent this impending catastrophe.
Consequence
a result or effect of an action or condition.
Ignoring scientific facts will have a devastating consequence for the planet.

2. Authentic Film Quotes & Expressions

Don't look up!
the political slogan urging people to ignore the impending space rock.
The populist president shouted 'Don't look up!' during her rally.
Media spin
a form of propaganda achieved through biased interpretation.
The government used media spin to convince the public that the comet had valuable resources.
Comet threat
the physical danger of a massive space rock colliding with Earth.
Astronomers calculated that the comet threat was one hundred percent real.
Doomsday
the last day of the world's existence.
Many citizens started buying survival kits as doomsday approached.
Sound the alarm
an idiom meaning to warn people about an impending danger.
We need to find a credible way to sound the alarm before it is too late.
Peer-reviewed
evaluation of scientific work by other experts in the same field.
Their comet discovery was fully peer-reviewed and verified by top astronomers.
Apocalyptic
describing the complete destruction of the world.
The visual effects portrayed a truly terrifying, apocalyptic scenario.
Media coverage
the attention or space given to an event by news networks.
The scientific warning received very little media coverage initially.
Hysteria
exaggerated or uncontrollable emotion among a group.
The talk show hosts accused the scientists of causing unnecessary hysteria.
Head in the sand
an idiom meaning to refuse to confront or acknowledge a problem.
We cannot continue to bury our heads in the sand; the comet is coming.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Introduce your thoughts on scientific communication and social media.
  • Why do serious global issues often get overshadowed by celebrity rumors online?
  • Should scientists receive formal training in media presentation?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
In-depth discussion about denialism, political spin, and public apathy.
What are the psychological causes behind public denialism of verified facts?
★ Have you encountered denialism about important issues in daily life?
Why is satire often more effective at exposing political flaws than serious news articles?
★ What is your favorite satirical movie or show?
Why do screenwriters use a giant space rock as a metaphor for environmental issues?
★ What other symbols of environmental threats have you noticed in cinema?
What is your biggest digital distraction when trying to study or work?
★ How do you avoid distraction when focusing on language learning?
Why are conspiracy theories so exceptionally popular on social media networks?
★ Why do people find conspiracy theories so fascinating?
How can scientists improve their credibility when explaining complex data to the public?
★ What makes a public speaker feel highly credible to you?
Why does the modern overflow of daily news leads to a state of general public apathy?
★ Do you sometimes feel a sense of apathy towards global news?
Is it ethical for morning talk show hosts to speculate on serious matters for higher ratings?
★ Have you seen media outlets speculate about fake rumors?
What is the most effective method for preparing a nation for a sudden catastrophe?
★ How does your city prepare for natural disasters?
What is the final consequence of choosing to ignore climate science for decades?
★ What ecological consequence are you most worried about?

🟢 Round 2 — Speculation & Public Policy Dilemmas▲
Predictions
Evaluate the following future-focused scenarios regarding media and science.
In the future, automated AI fact-checkers will completely eliminate media spin and conspiracy theories.
Ignoring the final consequence of resource depletion will lead to a global civilization collapse.
Most people will choose to ignore a verified catastrophe warning if it requires changing their luxury lifestyles.
Public apathy will be solved by gamifying news and rewarding citizens for tracking serious events.
It is impossible to establish scientific credibility when your funding comes directly from commercial enterprises.
A major distraction like virtual entertainment will become a mandatory government service to keep people calm.
Sharp satire movies will become the most important source of genuine political information for young people.
We should expect a future where scientists must speculate on potential gains to secure public interest.
The metaphor of a comet will be forgotten because we will develop automated planetary defense networks.
Pure denialism will be treated as a psychological condition and treated in standard clinics.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
They didn't believe that the comet was real→They didn't believe the comet was real(Exclude the conjunction 'that' in direct beliefs clauses for more natural conversational English)
The movie is a clear metaphor of climate change→The movie is a clear metaphor for climate change(The noun 'metaphor' requires the preposition 'for')
The politicians want to distract us from facts→The politicians want to distract us from the facts(Use the definite article 'the' when referring to specific objective scientific facts)
