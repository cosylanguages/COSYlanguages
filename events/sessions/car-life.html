📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Mobility
the ability to move or be moved freely and easily
Example: Owning a car provides a great sense of personal mobility.
Infrastructure
the basic physical and organizational structures (e.g. buildings, roads, power supplies) needed for the operation of a society
Example: Many cities are investing in bicycle infrastructure to reduce car use.
Public transport
buses, trains, and other forms of transport that are available to the public, charge set fares, and run on fixed routes
Example: Using public transport is often cheaper and greener than driving.
Congestion
the state of being extremely full or blocked, especially with traffic
Example: Traffic congestion in the city center is a major problem during rush hour.
Maintenance
the process of preserving a condition or situation or the state of being preserved (e.g. fixing a car)
Example: The annual maintenance of a car can be very expensive.
Autonomy
the right or condition of self-government; independence
Example: For many teenagers, getting a driving license represents their first real taste of autonomy.
Carbon footprint
the total amount of greenhouse gases produced to directly and indirectly support human activities
Example: Selling my car was the fastest way to reduce my personal carbon footprint.
Commute
travel some distance between one's home and place of work on a regular basis
Example: My daily commute takes 45 minutes by train but over an hour by car.
Walkability
a measure of how friendly an area is to walking
Example: I moved to this neighborhood because of its high walkability and local shops.
Depreciation
a reduction in the value of an asset with the passage of time
Example: Car depreciation is a major financial downside of buying a new vehicle.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Driver's Seat
  • Do you have a driving license? If so, when did you get it?
  • What is the longest car journey you have ever taken?
🔵 Round 1 — Personal Depth (10 Questions)▲
Narrative
1. How does owning a car affect your sense of freedom and autonomy?
★ Do you enjoy the act of driving, or is it just a tool?
2. What are the biggest stresses associated with car ownership?
★ Have you ever had a car breakdown at a bad time?
3. How does the quality of local public transport influence your decision to own a car?
★ Are you satisfied with the transport in your city?
4. Why do many people view their car as an extension of their personality?
★ If your personality was a car, what model would it be?
5. How has the cost of fuel and maintenance changed your driving habits?
★ Do you track your car expenses carefully?
6. In what ways does car-free living improve or complicate your social life?
7. Does the "walkability" of a city change how you feel about your car?
8. How did you feel when you first learned to drive?
9. Why are cars still seen as a "status symbol" in many cultures?
10. Have you ever tried a car-sharing or car-pooling service?

🟢 Round 2 — Speculative Analysis (10 Units)▲
Projections
1. If personal cars were banned in city centers tomorrow, how would your life change immediately?
2. Without cars, would people spend more time interacting with their local community?
3. If autonomous (self-driving) cars become standard, will we still need to learn how to drive?
4. How would the design of our homes change if we didn't need garages or driveways?
5. Without the stress of driving in traffic, would humans become less aggressive overall?
6. If we had perfect public transport, would anyone still choose to own a private vehicle?
7. How would the tourism industry change if people couldn't take independent road trips?
8. Without cars, would children have more freedom to play outside?
9. If fuel becomes too expensive for the middle class, will cars become a "luxury for the rich" again?
10. Can a society truly be "modern" without relying on cars?
