📖 Session Vocabulary

breakthrough
a sudden important discovery
Example: Scientists made a breakthrough in cancer research.
peer-reviewed
checked by other experts
Example: This journal only publishes peer-reviewed articles.
hypothesis
a proposed explanation
Example: Their hypothesis was proved correct by the experiment.
algorithm
a set of rules followed by a computer
Example: The social media algorithm shows you content you like.
neural network
a computer system modeled on the human brain
Example: Deep learning uses artificial neural networks.
cognitive
relating to the process of thought
Example: Sleep is essential for cognitive performance.
dataset
a collection of related data
Example: The AI was trained on a massive image dataset.
automation
the use of machines to do work
Example: Automation is changing the manufacturing industry.
bias
prejudice in favor of or against something
Example: We must be aware of algorithmic bias in AI.
Turing test
a test of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior
Example: Many modern chatbots can almost pass the Turing test.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Introduction to AI & the Brain Many people think AI is only for robots, but it's already in our pockets. Have you ever felt like your phone knows what you're thinking?
  • What's the first thing you do on your phone every morning?
  • Do you trust AI recommendations (music, movies)?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
Questions
1. What is the main discovery or idea in the article?
★ Had you heard about this topic before?
2. Why do scientists think this discovery is important?
★ Does this feel relevant to your life?
3. What evidence does the article describe to support the claim?
★ Do you find this evidence convincing?
4. Are there any limitations or risks mentioned?
★ Does knowing the risks change your feeling about the discovery?
5. Who benefits most from this development?
★ Does science serve everyone equally in your country?
6. How does AI mimic human learning?
7. What are the ethical concerns of brain-AI interfaces?
8. Could AI ever truly "feel" like a human?
9. Is our brain being "rewired" by constant tech use?
10. What is the most surprising thing you learned from the article?

🟢 Round 2 — Deep Dive▲
Debate / Future Focus
1. In the future, scientific breakthroughs will solve climate change before governments do.
2. AI will replace scientists within 50 years.
3. Brain chips should be mandatory for high-risk jobs.
4. AI will eventually write better poetry than humans.
5. In 100 years, there will be no difference between human and artificial thought.
6. Schools should ban AI-generated assignments entirely.
7. Governments should tax robots to support human workers.
8. Privacy is dead in the age of AI.
9. AI companions are a healthy solution for loneliness.
10. The first AI "citizen" should have the right to vote.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I suggest to use AI→I suggest using AI / I suggest that we use AI(Suggest + gerund)
The most of neural networks→Most neural networks(Generalization)
Actually, AI is everywhere→Currently, AI is everywhere(Actually vs Currently)
It is a very interesting news→It is very interesting news(Uncountable news)
If we would have better technology...→If we had better technology...(Second conditional)
