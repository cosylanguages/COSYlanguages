Artificial Intelligence and neuroscience are increasingly intersecting, as researchers look to the human brain to improve machine learning algorithms. This session explores the reciprocal relationship between biological and artificial neural networks, investigating how each field informs the other. We will discuss the ethical implications of brain-computer interfaces and whether machines can ever truly replicate human cognition.

📖 Session Vocabulary

breakthrough
a sudden important discovery.
Scientists made a breakthrough in cancer research.
peer-reviewed
checked by other experts.
This journal only publishes peer-reviewed articles.
hypothesis
a proposed explanation.
Their hypothesis was proved correct by the experiment.
algorithm
a set of rules followed by a computer.
The social media algorithm shows you content you like.
neural network
a computer system modeled on the human brain.
Deep learning uses artificial neural networks.
cognitive
relating to the process of thought.
Sleep is essential for cognitive performance.
dataset
a collection of related data.
The AI was trained on a massive image dataset.
automation
the use of machines to do work.
Automation is changing the manufacturing industry.
bias
prejudice in favor of or against something.
We must be aware of algorithmic bias in AI.
Turing test
a test of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior.
Many modern chatbots can almost pass the Turing test.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Introduction to AI & the Brain Many people think AI is only for robots, but it's already in our pockets. Have you ever felt like your phone knows what you're thinking?
  • What's the first thing you do on your phone every morning?
  • Do you trust AI recommendations (music, movies)?
🔵 Round 1 — Theoretical Discussion▲
Questions
What is the main discovery or idea in the article? (Consider the role of breakthrough here.)
★ Had you heard about this topic before?
Why do scientists think this discovery is important? (Consider the role of peer-reviewed here.)
★ Does this feel relevant to your life?
What evidence does the article describe to support the claim? (Consider the role of hypothesis here.)
★ Do you find this evidence convincing?
Are there any limitations or risks mentioned? (Consider the role of algorithm here.)
★ Does knowing the risks change your feeling about the discovery?
Who benefits most from this development? (Consider the role of neural network here.)
★ Does science serve everyone equally in your country?
How does AI mimic human learning? (Consider the role of cognitive here.)
What are the ethical concerns of brain-AI interfaces? (Consider the role of dataset here.)
Could AI ever truly "feel" like a human? (Consider the role of automation here.)
Is our brain being "rewired" by constant tech use? (Consider the role of bias here.)
What is the most surprising thing you learned from the article? (Consider the role of Turing test here.)

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Future Focus
In the future, scientific breakthroughs in AI will solve climate change before governments do. (How will breakthrough impact our future?)
★ Would you trust an AI-led environmental policy?
AI will replace human scientists within the next 50 years. (How does peer-reviewed affect this statement?)
★ Do you think machines can have "curiosity"?
Brain-AI chips should be mandatory for high-risk jobs like surgery or aviation. (How does hypothesis affect this statement?)
★ Would you allow a computer to be connected to your brain?
AI will eventually write more emotionally moving poetry than any human. (How does algorithm affect this statement?)
★ Does the source of art matter more than the feeling it gives you?
In 100 years, there will be no measurable difference between human and artificial thought. (How does neural network affect this statement?)
★ Is there something about the brain that can never be digitized?
Schools should ban AI-generated assignments to protect human brain development. (How does cognitive affect this statement?)
★ Did you use any "shortcuts" when you were a student?
Governments should tax robot labor to support human workers replaced by automation. (How does dataset affect this statement?)
★ Is a "universal basic income" the best solution for the AI era?
Personal privacy will become a luxury that only the extremely wealthy can afford. (How does automation affect this statement?)
★ What is the most private thing about you that you'd never share with an app?
AI companions will become a healthy and standard solution for global loneliness. (How does bias affect this statement?)
★ Could you ever be "best friends" with a software program?
The first AI to pass a consciousness test should be granted the right to vote. (How does Turing test affect this statement?)
★ How would you define "consciousness" in a machine?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I suggest to use AI→I suggest using AI / I suggest that we use AI(Suggest + gerund)
The most of neural networks→Most neural networks(Generalization)
Actually, AI is everywhere→Currently, AI is everywhere(Actually vs Currently)
It is a very interesting news→It is very interesting news(Uncountable news)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
