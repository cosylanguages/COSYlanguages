Britannica Article 📖

This session addresses the highly controversial topic of human cloning and its bioethical challenges. We will debate the potential medical benefits versus the severe ethical and social concerns of duplicating human genetics. Participants will express their views on scientific progress and the limits of biotechnology.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Cloning
the process of producing similar populations of genetically identical individuals.
Therapeutic cloning could help us cure various degenerative diseases.
Duplicate
to make an exact copy of something.
It is impossible to completely duplicate a person's unique life experiences.
Bioethics
the ethics of medical and biological research.
Experts in bioethics are debating the moral boundaries of human editing.
Genetic
relating to genes or heredity.
Our genetic code determines many of our physical characteristics.
Controversial
giving rise or likely to give rise to public disagreement.
The decision to allow human cloning research remains highly controversial.
Progress
forward or onward movement towards a destination or goal.
Scientific progress should not happen at the expense of human dignity.
Biotechnology
the exploitation of biological processes for industrial and other purposes.
Biotechnology has advanced rapidly over the past few decades.
Limit
a restriction on the size or amount of something.
We must set a strict moral limit on genetic experiments.
Embryo
an unborn or unhatched offspring in the process of development.
Researchers are analyzing cell division in the early human embryo.
Alter
to change or cause to change in character or composition.
Cloning could permanently alter our definition of family and parenthood.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to science fiction and reality.
  • Have you ever watched a science fiction movie about clones?
  • If you had a clone of yourself, what task would you ask them to do?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the arguments in favor of cloning technologies.
Idea A: Cloning can help infertile couples have genetically related children.
Idea B: We can use therapeutic cloning to duplicate healthy cells and replace damaged organs.
Idea A: In bioethics, some argue that cloned tissue could save millions of lives from degenerative diseases.
Idea B: Cloning endangered animal species can protect our planetary biodiversity.
Idea A: Genetic engineering and cloning allow us to study complex human diseases in controlled lab environments.
Idea B: This technology is a natural step in the progress of biological sciences.
Idea A: Modern biotechnology can eliminate inherited genetic conditions before birth.
Idea B: Rejecting cloning means setting a arbitrary limit on medical science.
Idea A: Analyzing a cloned embryo helps us understand early developmental biology.
Idea B: Therapeutic breakthroughs could permanently alter how we treat heart disease and diabetes.

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
Analyze the arguments against cloning technologies.
Idea A: Cloning violates the unique identity and dignity of a human individual.
Idea B: Cloned children would face extreme psychological pressure to duplicate the achievements of their original.
Idea A: From the perspective of bioethics, treating human lives as experiments is morally unacceptable.
Idea B: Unexpected genetic mutations in cloned embryos can lead to severe health defects.
Idea A: Human cloning is highly controversial and could cause a deep division in society.
Idea B: Unregulated scientific progress might lead to black markets for human clone organs.
Idea A: Large corporations in biotechnology could patent human genetic lines for profit.
Idea B: If we do not set a strict moral limit, humanity might face dystopian scenarios.
Idea A: Using a human embryo solely as a resource for organs is ethically wrong.
Idea B: Cloning could permanently alter the structure of families and traditional parent-child relationships.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The cloning is very dangerous→Cloning is very dangerous(Do not use definite article for general nouns)
I am think cloning is bad→I think cloning is bad(Simple present construction)
It is a controversial topic since years→It has been a controversial topic for years(Present Perfect + for duration)
