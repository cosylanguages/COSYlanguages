📖 Session Vocabulary

Resilience
the ability to recover quickly from difficulties
Example: Children often develop resilience by overcoming challenges.
Independence
the ability to act without relying on others
Example: Independence helps people make their own decisions.
Adaptability
the ability to adjust to new situations
Example: Adaptability is important in a rapidly changing world.
Discipline
self-control and the ability to follow rules
Example: Discipline can help people achieve long-term goals.
Perspective
a particular way of seeing or understanding something
Example: Traveling can change your perspective on life.
Community
a group of people who support one another
Example: Strong communities often help raise children together.
Self-expression
showing your thoughts, feelings, and personality
Example: Art can be a form of self-expression.
Resourcefulness
the ability to solve problems creatively
Example: Resourcefulness helps people succeed when resources are limited.
Guidance
advice or help that directs someone
Example: Good guidance can prevent costly mistakes.
Empathy
the ability to understand another person's feelings
Example: Empathy improves relationships.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲

Let's get started with some quick reflections on your own upbringing:

  • What is one "old-fashioned" rule you had to follow as a child?
  • What is one modern freedom you wish you had when you were younger?
  • In one word, how would you describe your childhood upbringing?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages▲
Debate

1. Focus vs. Exploration

A: Strong discipline in old upbringing may have helped children stay focused even without constant supervision.

B: Greater freedom in modern upbringing may help children discover talents that previous generations never had the chance to explore.

★ Which is more important for success: discipline or freedom?

2. Authority vs. Understanding

A: Clear parental authority may have reduced confusion about right and wrong.

B: Modern guidance may help children understand why rules exist instead of simply obeying them.

★ Did you prefer being told what to do or having things explained?

3. Security vs. Confidence

A: Strong family structure may have created a greater sense of security during difficult times.

B: Greater emotional support may help children develop stronger self-confidence.

★ What gave you more confidence growing up: structure or support?

4. Maturity vs. Experience

A: Early responsibility may have prepared children to become independent adults sooner.

B: Greater independence may help children learn important life lessons through their own experiences.

★ At what age should children start making important decisions?

5. Unity vs. Honesty

A: Greater respect for elders may have helped families stay united across generations.

B: Greater expression may help children build more honest relationships with their parents.

★ Is respect or honesty more important in a family?

6. Survival vs. Innovation

A: Strong discipline may have prepared people better for military service, dangerous jobs, or major crises.

B: Greater expression may help people adapt to careers that require creativity and innovation.

★ Which skills will be more valuable in the future: discipline or creativity?

7. Order vs. Progress

A: Strong authority may have helped communities react quickly during emergencies.

B: Greater freedom may lead to more new ideas and social progress.

★ Does society need more authority or more freedom?

8. Predictability vs. Personality

A: Traditional structure may have reduced stress because people had clearer life expectations.

B: Greater independence allows people to create lifestyles that fit their personalities.

★ Would you rather have a clear path or create your own?

9. Resilience vs. Risk-taking

A: Strong responsibility from an early age may have made young people more mature.

B: Modern support may help children take risks without fearing failure.

★ Is failure a good teacher?

10. Crisis vs. Transformation

A: If society faced a long-term crisis, old-fashioned discipline, respect, and responsibility might become valuable again.

B: If technology transforms society, independence, expression, and freedom might become even more important.

★ Which upbringing would prepare people better for the world of 2050?

🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

In pairs or small groups, discuss which values from each "era" of upbringing you would keep if you were raising a child today:

The "Old" Pack

  • Discipline & Rules
  • Respect for Elders
  • Early Responsibility
  • Family Structure

The "Modern" Pack

  • Self-expression
  • Emotional Support
  • Independence
  • Creative Freedom

Can you create a perfect "Hybrid" upbringing?

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages▲
Debate

1. 🧠 “The Hidden Switch Effect”

A: Extreme discipline in old upbringing may create adults who obey rules so well that they struggle when rules suddenly disappear in real life (e.g., during crises or fast-changing societies).

B: Extreme freedom in modern upbringing may create adults who feel lost the moment they enter systems that require structure (jobs, armies, institutions).

★ Would you feel safer in a world with too many rules or no rules at all?

2. 🧩 “The Authority Paradox”

A: Strong authority may look stable, but it can produce adults who secretly distrust authority and only follow rules when being watched.

B: Weak authority may create adults who constantly question everything—including necessary rules—leading to decision paralysis.

★ Do you trust authority more when it is strict or flexible?

3. 🎭 “The Emotional Overload Future”

A: Low expression upbringing may lead to adults who suddenly explode emotionally later in life when they can no longer suppress feelings.

B: High expression upbringing may lead to people who experience emotions so intensely that everyday life feels mentally exhausting.

★ Is it better to feel less or feel too much?

4. 🧭 “The Guidance Collapse”

A: Traditional guidance may fail in a future world where old advice becomes completely irrelevant (careers, relationships, AI society).

B: Modern fragmented guidance (parents, internet, influencers, AI) may create people who can’t tell which direction is truly theirs.

★ Who do you trust most for life advice today?

5. 🪞 “The Respect Reversal”

A: Strong respect culture may accidentally protect unfair systems because people avoid questioning them.

B: Low respect culture may lead to generational conflict where nobody trusts anyone older than them.

★ Can too much respect become dangerous?

6. 🧱 “The Structure Trap”

A: Strong structure may create people who panic when life becomes unpredictable and no plan exists.

B: Weak structure may create people who constantly restart their lives without finishing anything.

★ Do you function better with strict structure or chaos?

7. ⚖️ “The Responsibility Shock”

A: Early responsibility may cause silent burnout in adulthood—people who never learned to rest.

B: Delayed responsibility may create adults who experience a sudden “real life shock” when independence finally hits.

★ Did adulthood feel gradual or sudden for you?

8. 🔄 “The Independence Illusion”

A: Limited independence may create adults who appear obedient but feel internally disconnected from their own life choices.

B: Extreme independence may create people who reject help even when they desperately need support.

★ Is asking for help a strength or weakness?

9. 🌐 “The Freedom Overload World”

A: Low freedom may prevent people from discovering their true identity altogether.

B: High freedom may lead to a future where people constantly change identity, career, and relationships—never feeling stable.

★ Is stability or freedom more important for happiness?

10. 🤖 “The Future Simulation Twist”

A: If society returns to strict discipline, authority, and structure, people might become highly efficient—but emotionally disconnected from personal desires.

B: If society evolves toward extreme expression, independence, and freedom, people might become creative—but struggle to cooperate in large systems (even with AI managing everything).

★ In a future run by AI, should humans be more disciplined or more free?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
In the old times...→In the past... / Historically...(More natural phrasing)
Parents was more strict.→Parents were stricter.(Subject-verb agreement)
I grown up in a small town.→I grew up in a small town.(Past simple of 'grow')
It depends of the situation.→It depends on the situation.(Preposition 'on')
Nowadays people is more free.→Nowadays people are freer.(Subject-verb agreement)
