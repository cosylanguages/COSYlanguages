📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

Dual
consisting of two parts, elements, or aspects.
He has a dual citizenship in both countries.
Opposite: Single
Conflict
a serious disagreement or argument.
The story explores the conflict between duty and personal desire.
Opposite: Harmony
Reflect
throw back heat, light, or sound without absorbing it.
The mirror will reflect your image perfectly.
Opposite: Absorb
Personality
the combination of characteristics that form character.
She has a bright and outgoing personality.
Opposite: Anonymity
Mirror
a reflective surface, now typically of glass.
She looked in the mirror to adjust her scarf.
Opposite: Original

🗣️ Life, Actions & Connection

Opposite
having a position on the other or further side.
They live on the opposite side of the street.
Opposite: Same
Dialogue
conversation between two or more people.
The play is filled with witty and engaging dialogue.
Opposite: Monologue
In harmony
consistent or in agreement with each other.
They live in perfect harmony with nature.
Opposite: In conflict
Acceptance
the action of consenting to receive or undertake.
Her acceptance of the job offer was a major milestone.
Opposite: Rejection

💬 Stage 4: Discussion▲

🔵 Round 1 — Lyric Analysis & Discussion

How does the narrator in 'me & I' view themselves? What internal conflicts or aspirations do they reveal?
★ When was the last time you felt a conflict between how you feel inside and how you must act?
What is the main message of 'me & I' about authenticity and self-expression? What does it teach us about standing up for ourselves?
★ How easy or difficult is it for you to speak up and defend your opinions in a crowd?
What feelings of determination, pride, or vulnerability are emphasized in ABBA's vocal delivery?
★ Which of these emotions do you find most powerful and inspiring in art or music?

🟢 Round 2 — Broad Themes & Philosophy Debate

In a world that often demands conformity, how can we successfully discover and maintain our true identity?
How should we define success and self-worth? Is it determined by external approval or internal satisfaction?
What are the best ways to cope with expectations from family, friends, or society while remaining true to oneself?
✨ Stage 5: Language Focus▲

Present Simple of State & Reflection

Using verbs of perception and state to describe internal conflicts and dual personalities.

Let's Practise — Three Examples:
  • Sometimes I feel there are two different people inside me.
  • I look in the mirror and analyze my own expressions.
  • She accepts her flaws and learns to live with them.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)

📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

Deceptive
giving an appearance or impression different from the true one.
The calm sea can be highly deceptive during a storm.
Opposite: Honest
Gaze
look steadily and intently.
He would gaze at the stars for hours in the backyard.
Opposite: Glance
Disguise
give a new appearance to hide identity.
She wore a wig as a clever disguise.
Opposite: Reveal
Regret
feel sad, repentant, or disappointed over.
He expressed deep regret for his past mistakes.
Opposite: Satisfaction
Warning
a statement or event indicating possible danger.
The loud siren served as a warning to evacuate.
Opposite: Encouragement

🗣️ Life, Actions & Connection

Lure
tempt a person or animal to do something.
The sweet smell of pie would lure us into the kitchen.
Opposite: Repel
Painful
causing physical or mental pain.
It was a painful memory that she preferred to forget.
Opposite: Painless
Obsession ≠ Indifference
state of being obsessed with someone or something ≠ lack of interest, concern, or sympathy.
Her passionate devotion bordered on obsession, while his attitude was one of complete indifference.
Illusion
a thing that is likely to be wrongly perceived.
The oasis in the desert was just an optical illusion.
Opposite: Reality
Trust
firm belief in the reliability or truth.
Trust is the foundation of any strong relationship.
Opposite: Distrust

💬 Stage 4: Discussion▲

🔵 Round 1 — Lyric Analysis & Discussion

What kind of environment is the narrator in 'angeleyes' experiencing? How does it reflect their inner state of mind?
★ How does your physical surroundings usually affect your emotions and creativity?
What is the song's perspective on loneliness and emotional distress? Is there any comfort or hope offered?
★ Where do you go or what do you do to find comfort when you are feeling low?
How do the melody and ABBA's delivery capture the weight of grief, longing, or stillness?
★ Do you prefer listening to sad music or happy music when you are going through a difficult time?

🟢 Round 2 — Broad Themes & Philosophy Debate

In our hyper-connected digital world, why is the feeling of loneliness and isolation actually increasing?
How can individuals cope with moments of intense emotional hardship or feeling 'stuck' in life?
Why does society sometimes view expressing sadness or vulnerability as a weakness, and how can we change this?
✨ Stage 5: Language Focus▲

The Modal Verb 'Must' for Obligation and Deduction

We use 'must' to express a strong obligation or a logical deduction directly from the lyrics.

Let's Practise — Three Examples:
  • We must dare to make our own music.
  • The song is so beautiful, she must be a great artist.
  • You must listen carefully to fill in the missing gaps.
