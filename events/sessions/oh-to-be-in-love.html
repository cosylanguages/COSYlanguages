Infatuation & Euphoria

This session unpacks Kate Bush's poetic and romantic song, "Oh To Be in Love". We will explore high-level British English vocabulary for describing intense emotions, infatuation, and romantic euphoria. Students will debate vulnerability, being bewitched by love, and total emotional surrender.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Infatuation
an intense but short-lived passion or admiration for someone.
Her sudden crush on the actor was a typical teenage infatuation.
Euphoria
a feeling or state of intense excitement and happiness.
The team experienced pure euphoria after winning the championship.
Vulnerable
exposed to the possibility of being attacked or harmed.
Opening up about your deep fears makes you feel highly vulnerable.
Intense
of extreme force, degree, or strength.
They worked under intense pressure to complete the project on time.
Devotion
love, loyalty, or enthusiasm for a person or cause.
Her absolute devotion to her family was admired by everyone.
Rapture
a feeling of intense pleasure or joy.
She listened to the beautiful opera performance with absolute rapture.
Sensation
a physical feeling or perception, or a widespread reaction.
The sudden cold wind caused a shivering sensation on her skin.
Bewitched
enchanted or delighted as if by magic.
He was completely bewitched by her beautiful singing voice.
Surrender
the action of yielding or giving up control to another.
They refused to surrender their rights during the negotiation.
Heartache
emotional pain or distress, especially caused by romance.
The sudden breakup caused her months of silent heartache.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
She was bewitched from her beautiful voice→She was bewitched by her beautiful voice(Use 'bewitched by' to indicate the source of enchantment.)
It was an intense pressure on him→It was intense pressure on him(The noun 'pressure' is uncountable here, so do not use the indefinite article 'an'.)
They refused to surrender under their opponents→They refused to surrender to their opponents(Use the preposition 'to' after 'surrender'.)
