Full Article 📖

This session covers recent neurobiology studies exploring the body's largest sensory and neural networks. We will analyze how these intricate biological pathways are specifically built to control pain, react to injuries, and combat diseases. The discussion will highlight the future of neurological therapies in managing chronic pain conditions.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Nervous system
the network of nerve cells and fibers which transmits nerve impulses between parts of the body.
Our nervous system coordinates rapid motor reactions to external stimuli.
Sensory
relating to sensation or the physical senses; transmitting impulses from sense organs.
Sensory receptors in our skin detect changes in temperature and pressure.
Network
a group or system of interconnected people or things.
The brain consists of a highly complex network of billions of neurons.
Pain
highly unpleasant physical sensation caused by illness or injury.
Nerve damage can cause chronic pain that is very difficult to treat.
Injury
an instance of being injured; physical damage to the body.
He recovered quickly from his knee injury after physical therapy.
Disease
a disorder of structure or function in a human, animal, or plant.
Medical researchers are striving to cure many hereditary diseases.
Intricate
very complicated or detailed.
The human eye has an intricate network of tiny blood vessels.
Therapy
treatment intended to relieve or heal a disorder.
Physical therapy can help restore movement after a stroke.
Chronic
persisting for a long time or constantly recurring.
Many elderly people suffer from chronic joint inflammation.
Pathway
a route, way, or path (especially a neural or metabolic one).
Scientists mapped the specific neural pathway that regulates fear.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to reflexes and feelings.
  • Have you ever touched something hot and reacted without thinking?
  • Do you think pain is always a bad thing, or is it useful?
🔵 Round 1 — Theoretical Discussion▲
Questions
Analyze how biological networks manage physical sensations.
How does the nervous system transmit sensory information to the brain?
★ When was the last time you felt high physical strain or muscle fatigue?
Why are our sensory receptors so densely concentrated in our hands and face?
★ Are your hands easily affected by very cold weather?
How does an intricate neural network coordinate complex movements?
★ Do you practice any sport that requires high hand-eye coordination?
Why is the feeling of pain essential for our survival from injury?
★ What is the most painful physical injury you have ever recovered from?
How does a chronic disease alter our daily life and mental health?
★ How do you take care of your personal health when you have a cold?
What makes nerve pathways so intricate and difficult to study?
★ Have you ever watched a documentary about how the brain works?
How does modern medical therapy help relieve long-term pain?
★ What therapy (like massage or warm baths) helps you relax?
Why do some injuries take much longer to heal than others?
★ How long did it take for your body to fully recover from your last illness?
How does stress affect our physical sensitivity to discomfort?
★ Do you feel more physical tension when you have a busy week at work?
To what extent should we use painkillers to block natural responses?
★ Do you take medicine immediately when you have a headache, or do you wait?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Projections
Debate these predictions about neurobiology and medical engineering.
In the future, we will have neural chips that can selectively turn off chronic pain signals permanently.
We will develop fully synthetic sensory networks for prosthetic limbs that feel exactly like real skin.
Future societies will use virtual reality therapies to treat most neurological diseases without any pills.
We will discover how to completely regenerate damaged spinal cord tissues within 20 years.
The definition of 'pain' will change as we learn to control our brain's sensory interpretations.
Future medicine will focus on customizing therapies based on a person's exact genetic network.
Blocking all physical pain would be dangerous and lead to a shorter average lifespan.
We should use genetic engineering to create future humans who do not feel any physical discomfort.
Mental stress is the primary cause of chronic physical pain in modern populations.
Artificial intelligence will become better at diagnosing complex neurological disorders than human doctors by 2040.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The nerve system is complex→The nervous system is complex(Use adjective 'nervous' instead of noun 'nerve')
I am wait for recovery→I am waiting for recovery(Present continuous structure)
It depend of your age→It depends on your age(Depends + on preposition)
