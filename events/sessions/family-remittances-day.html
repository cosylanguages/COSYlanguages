📖 Session Vocabulary

Remittance
Money sent to family in another country.
Many workers send remittances to support their parents back home.
Migrant worker
A person who moves to another country for work.
Migrant workers often send money to their families.
Financial support
Money or help given to someone in need.
Remittances are a key form of financial support.
Living expenses
Money needed for daily life (food, rent, transport).
Remittances often cover living expenses.
Bank transfer
Moving money electronically between accounts.
Most remittances are sent via bank transfers.
Fee
Money paid for sending money (transaction fee).
High fees reduce the value of remittances.
Developing country
A country with lower income and industrial growth.
Many remittances go to developing countries.
Household income
Total money a family earns.
Remittances increase household income.
Economic impact
Effect on the economy.
Remittances have a strong economic impact.
Financial inclusion
Access to banking and financial services.
Digital tools improve financial inclusion.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲

Have you ever had to send money to someone in another city or country? What was the reason, and was it easy to do?

🔵 Round 1 — Analysis (Understanding Remittances)▲
Questions
Why do many migrant workers choose to send a remittance instead of returning home?
Personal question: Have you ever supported someone financially abroad through a bank transfer?
How important is financial support from abroad for families in developing countries?
Personal question: Would you depend on remittances for your living expenses if you had to?
What is the economic impact of remittances on a country’s growth?
Personal question: Do you think individual financial actions can change national economies?
How do high fees (transaction fees) affect the value of a remittance?
Personal question: Would you change your service provider if fees were lower?
Can remittances significantly increase household income in poor regions?
Personal question: How important is stable income in your own life decisions?
How has technology improved financial inclusion for migrant workers?
Personal question: Which digital banking tools do you trust for a bank transfer?
Why do people in developing countries rely heavily on remittances?
Personal question: Would you prefer financial independence or external support?
Should governments regulate companies that handle remittances more strictly?
Personal question: Do you think financial systems today are fair for everyone?
What emotional role do remittances play beyond just financial support?
Personal question: Would you feel pressure if your family depended on you financially?
Will remittances remain important in the future global economy?
Personal question: Do you think migration will increase or decrease in the future?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲
Role-play: One person is a migrant worker who wants to send money home, and the other is a bank employee explaining the fees and the transfer process. Practice using 'fee', 'bank transfer', and 'financial inclusion'.
🟢 Round 2 — Future Thinking (Agree / Disagree)▲
Debate
In the future, remittances will be sent instantly with zero fees (transaction fees).
Personal question: Should sending money globally be completely free?
Migrant workers will no longer need to leave their countries to provide financial support.
Personal question: Would you prefer remote work instead of migration?
Artificial intelligence will manage all bank transfers automatically.
Personal question: Would you trust AI with your personal finances?
Financial inclusion will become universal thanks to digital banking systems.
Personal question: Do you think everyone will have equal access to financial tools?
Remittances will completely replace traditional welfare systems in some developing countries.
Personal question: Should families rely more on private support or government aid?
In the future, household income will depend more on global remittances than local jobs.
Personal question: Would you prefer a global or local income source?
Governments will fully control the flow of remittances for economic stability.
Personal question: Do you think this control is positive or dangerous?
The economic impact of remittances will become stronger than foreign investment.
Personal question: Which is more important for a country: investment or family money flows?
Sending a remittance will become an automatic background process, without human involvement.
Personal question: Should financial support remain a personal emotional act?
In the future, global migration will decrease because financial support can happen digitally.
Personal question: Do you think physical migration will still be necessary?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I send money for my family.
I send money to my family.
We use 'to' for the recipient of the money.
The fees are very high and big.
The fees are very high.
'High' is the standard adjective for fees, prices, and costs.
