📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Diversity
the presence of different cultures, languages, backgrounds, or perspectives.
Example: Diversity can make communities more creative.
Exposure
experience of something new or different.
Example: Travel gives people exposure to different lifestyles.
Perspective
a particular way of seeing or understanding something.
Example: Different perspectives can improve decision-making.
Adaptability
the ability to adjust to new situations.
Example: Diversity often develops adaptability.
Misunderstanding
a situation where people interpret something incorrectly.
Example: Language differences can sometimes cause misunderstandings.
Inclusive
welcoming and involving different kinds of people.
Example: Inclusive workplaces often attract talented employees.
Identity
the qualities and characteristics that make a person unique.
Example: Language is an important part of identity.
Stereotype
a simplified and often unfair belief about a group.
Example: Stereotypes can create barriers between people.
Globalized
connected across different countries and cultures.
Example: Today's economy is highly globalized.
Cultural exchange
sharing ideas, traditions, and customs between cultures.
Example: Cultural exchange helps people understand each other better.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • How would you describe the diversity of your hometown?
  • If you could live in any culture for a month, which one would it be?
🔵 Round 1 — My Life With Diversity (10 Units)▲
Narrative

1. How would diversity influence your daily life if you regularly interacted with people from many cultures, languages, and backgrounds?

Personal: What is the most interesting cultural difference you have ever experienced?

2. Would greater exposure to different traditions make people more curious, adventurous, or open-minded?

Personal: What new tradition or lifestyle would you like to experience?

3. How could having access to many different perspectives help society solve future problems that seem impossible today?

Personal: Has someone ever changed your perspective on an important issue?

4. Would growing up in a highly diverse society improve people's adaptability when technology, jobs, and lifestyles change rapidly?

Personal: How adaptable do you think you are?

5. Could occasional misunderstandings actually become valuable opportunities to learn about other people?

Personal: Have you ever learned something useful from a misunderstanding?

6. How might an extremely inclusive society look in 100 years if people from every background felt equally welcome?

Personal: What makes you feel included in a group?

7. How could exposure to many cultures strengthen rather than weaken a person's identity?

Personal: Which part of your identity is most important to you?

8. Would greater diversity eventually reduce harmful stereotypes, or would people simply create new ones?

Personal: What stereotype about your country annoys you most?

9. In an increasingly globalized world, could diversity become one of the most valuable skills a person can have?

Personal: What international skill would you like to develop?

10. Could future cultural exchange lead to completely new forms of music, food, art, or even lifestyles that do not exist today?

Personal: Which cultures would you love to see mixed together?

🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

Activity: The Perfect Global Neighborhood

Imagine you are designing a neighborhood for 1,000 people from 50 different countries. What rules, buildings, or spaces would it need to be truly inclusive and successful?

🟢 Round 2 — My Life Without Diversity (10 Units)▲
Speculative Alternatives

1. If there were no linguistic diversity and everyone spoke exactly the same language, what creative alternatives might people invent to express individuality?

Personal: Would you enjoy living in a world with only one language?

2. Without exposure to different cultures, would people become more curious about space, virtual worlds, or fictional civilizations instead?

Personal: Where do you currently get most of your exposure to different cultures?

3. If everyone shared the same perspective, would society become more peaceful—or would people invent new reasons to disagree?

Personal: Do you think disagreement is necessary for progress?

4. Without cultural differences, would people lose some adaptability, forcing schools to create artificial challenges and simulations to train it?

Personal: What challenges have helped you become more adaptable?

5. If language and culture were identical everywhere, would traditional misunderstandings disappear, or would misunderstandings with AI, robots, and virtual beings replace them?

Personal: What kinds of misunderstandings do you think people will have in the future?

6. If society became completely inclusive because everyone was essentially the same, would people start dividing themselves according to personality types, hobbies, or even genetic traits?

Personal: What new social groups might emerge in such a world?

7. Without cultural or linguistic differences shaping our identity, what would people use to define themselves? Their memories? Their online avatars? Their achievements?

Personal: How much of your identity comes from your culture?

8. If cultural stereotypes disappeared completely, would people create stereotypes based on professions, AI assistants, virtual communities, or lifestyle choices?

Personal: Do you think humans naturally create categories?

9. In a fully globalized world with one culture, could local traditions survive as hobbies, historical reenactments, or premium experiences similar to museums?

Personal: Which tradition from today's world would deserve preservation?

10. If there were no international cultural exchange because everyone already shared the same culture, where would innovation come from? Artificial cultures designed by AI? Colonies on Mars? Entirely virtual civilizations?

Personal: Which alternative source of cultural innovation sounds most exciting to you?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲

Explain me the problem→Explain to me the problem(Explain to someone)
It depends of the situation→It depends on the situation(Depends on)
I'm agree with you→I agree with you(Agree is a verb)
The persons in the city→The people in the city(Irregular plural)
