As I watched the leaves fall outside my window, tracing their graceful, inevitable descent to the earth, I couldn't help but wonder about choice. In a world that fights so fiercely to keep us alive at all costs, why is the final exit such an unspeakable taboo? Does having control over the end of our story bring peace, or does the legalization of euthanasia inadvertently shift the boundaries of hope, actually reducing the desperate, lonely rates of traditional suicide?

📝 Carrie's Diary: Rhetorical Reflection

"When we legislate the end of suffering, do we provide a clinical, orderly exit that robs us of our natural resilience? Or do we give those in desperate pain a quiet, reassuring key that makes the remaining room feel a little less small?"

📖 Session Vocabulary

Autonomy ≠ Subjugation
the right of self-government or independence ≠ the act of bringing under complete control.
The artificial intelligence fought for complete autonomy, rejecting any form of human subjugation.
Taboo
a social or religious custom prohibiting or forbidding a particular practice or association.
Describing end-of-life choices remains a strict taboo in many traditional families.
Palliative
relieving pain or alleviating a problem without dealing with the underlying cause.
The hospital provided exceptional palliative care to ensure her final days were comfortable.
Legislation
laws, considered collectively, or the process of making laws.
The government debated the new euthanasia legislation for several months.
Safeguard
a measure taken to protect someone or something or to prevent something undesirable.
Strict medical safeguards are essential to protect vulnerable individuals from coercion.
Despair
the complete loss or absence of hope.
Deep despair can make a person feel like there are no alternatives left.
Sanctity
the state or quality of being holy, sacred, or saintly.
Many ethical arguments are built around the sanctity of human life.
Inherent
existing in something as a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute.
There is an inherent dignity in allowing individuals to choose their own path.
Compassionate
feeling or showing sympathy and concern for others.
A compassionate society must address the suffering of terminally ill patients.
Dignified
having or showing a composed or serious manner that is worthy of respect.
Every human being deserves a dignified and peaceful end to their life.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
Linguistic feedback with mistake-highlight classes to accentuate errors precisely without full sentence strike-through.
I am agree with the active dying bill. → I agree with the active dying bill. ('Agree' is a verb in English, not an adjective. Avoid using the auxiliary verb 'to be' with it.)
The legislation was debated since months. → The legislation was debated for months. (Use 'for' to express a duration of time, and 'since' only for a specific starting point in the past.)
The doctor's explanation was very clear. → The doctor's explanation was exceptionally lucid / crystal clear / highly articulate. (Elevating basic vocabulary with sophisticated adjectives that fit formal debate registers.)
