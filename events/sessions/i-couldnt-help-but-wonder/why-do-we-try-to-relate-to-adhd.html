Sensitive Topic Note: This session discusses mental health, neurodiversity, and the modern trend of self-diagnosis. Discussion is intended to explore psychological trends with empathy, respect, and deep care.

As I sat doom-scrolling through endless social media feeds, I noticed how almost every viral video seemed to offer a checklist of ADHD symptoms. From simple forgetfulness to having multiple tabs open, it felt like the internet was desperately trying to diagnose the entire world. I couldn't help but wonder... Why do we try so hard to relate to ADHD, as if everyone has it? Is it because we are craving a medical label for our collective modern burnout, or are we just trying to find things in ourselves even when they aren't truly there?

📝 Carrie's Diary: Rhetorical Reflection

"Are we so lonely in our everyday struggles that we would rather share a clinical diagnosis with millions than face our own unique, un-labeled chaos?"

📖 Session Vocabulary

Diagnose
identify the nature of an illness or other problem by examination of the symptoms.
Doctors often find it challenging to diagnose ADHD in adult patients correctly.
Burnout
physical or mental collapse caused by overwork or stress.
Many people mistake severe work-related burnout for an attention deficit disorder.
Relate
feel sympathy for or identify with someone or something.
It is natural to relate to social media posts that describe common struggles with focus.
Neurodiversity
the range of differences in individual brain function and behavioral traits.
Celebrating neurodiversity helps us understand that everyone's brain works differently.
Symptom
a physical or mental feature which is regarded as indicating a condition of disease.
Being easily distracted can be a symptom of ADHD, but it is also a very common human trait.
Overestimate ≠ Underestimate
estimate something to be better or larger than it really is ≠ estimate something to be smaller or less important.
We often overestimate our own attention span on good days, but underestimate how much fatigue affects our focus on bad ones.
Label
a classifying phrase or name applied to a person or thing.
Receiving a clinical label can sometimes bring a great sense of comfort and clarity.
Distraction
a thing that prevents someone from giving their full attention to something else.
Smartphones provide a constant stream of distraction that ruins our productivity.
Hyperactive
abnormally or extremely active.
A hyperactive mind can generate brilliant creative ideas but struggles to sit still.
Empathy
the ability to understand and share the feelings of another.
Learning about neurodiversity increases our empathy for coworkers who work in different ways.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up — The Relationship Question▲
When you have a task to do, do you focus intensely on it or do you constantly check your phone?
  • Do you find yourself trying to find ADHD symptoms in your own daily behaviors?
  • Do you think social media has made us all feel a little hyperactive?
🔵 Round 1 — Column Analysis▲
Questions
Why do we try so hard to relate to complex psychological conditions like ADHD?
★ Have you ever felt a strong connection to a medical symptom described in an online video?
How does the modern world's endless stream of distraction mimic actual clinical symptoms?
★ What is the biggest source of distraction in your workplace or study routine?
Why has receiving a medical label become a fashionable badge of honor for some on social media?
★ Do you think labels help people understand themselves, or do they restrict them?
How can doctors accurately diagnose ADHD when almost everyone is experiencing modern burnout?
★ How do you distinguish between everyday tiredness and true burnout?
Does celebrating neurodiversity make us more accepting of different working styles?
★ Do you work better in a strict, structured office or in a flexible, quiet environment?
What is the most common symptom of a hyperactive mind in your daily routine?
★ Do you find your mind constantly racing when you try to fall asleep?
Do we often overestimate our own focus and attention span when making daily plans?
★ How long can you study or work on a single task before you feel the urge to check your phone?
How can we show genuine empathy to people who have clinical diagnoses without claiming their struggles as our own?
★ How do you usually support a friend or family member who is struggling with their health?
Why do we crave finding things in ourselves even when we do not actually have them?
★ Why do you think humans have such a deep psychological need to feel understood and validated?
Is our collective focus span being permanently damaged by high-speed digital technologies?
★ Have you ever tried a 'digital detox' to improve your concentration, and did it work?

🟢 Round 2 — Deep Speculation: Agree or Disagree?▲
Debate
Modern internet culture encourages us to diagnose and label every minor quirk, whereas past generations accepted diverse characters naturally.
★ Do you feel that medical labels help you understand yourself or do they put you in a box?
Our ancestors suffered from physical exhaustion, while modern white-collar life has created a new, existential form of mental burnout.
★ How do you distinguish between everyday tiredness and true burnout?
We try so hard to relate to online diagnostic criteria because we lack the deep, offline community connections of the past.
★ Have you ever felt a strong connection to a medical symptom described in an online video?
Older, structured educational models forced conformity, while modern appreciation for neurodiversity struggles to adapt to digital attention spans.
★ Do you work better in a strict, structured office or in a flexible, quiet environment?
High-speed digital technologies have artificially damaged our attention spans, creating a symptom profile that mimics clinical disorders.
★ How long can you read a physical book or watch a slow movie without checking your phone?
In past eras, we did not try to overestimate our focus capacity, accepting natural human limits without modern productivity guilt.
★ How organized is your daily routine: do you use planners and lists, or do you wing it?
Older generations viewed eccentric behaviors as simple 'character traits', while modern psychology tends to pathologize them with a clinical label.
★ What is a quirky habit of yours that you would never want to cure or change?
Modern high-speed notifications create constant distraction, a mental fragmentation that our slow-living ancestors never had to endure.
★ What is the biggest source of distraction in your workplace or study routine?
In older, active lifestyles, a hyperactive child was guided by physical work, whereas in modern sedentary schools they are medicated.
★ Do you work better when you are physically active, or do you prefer sitting at a desk?
We have replaced real, face-to-face empathy and neighborhood support with transactional, online validation.
★ How do you usually support a friend or family member who is struggling with their health?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I think everyone have ADHD these days. → I think everyone has ADHD these days. (Subject-verb agreement: 'Everyone' is singular and requires 'has'.)
We discussed about mental health topics. → We discussed mental health topics. (The verb 'discuss' is transitive and does not take the preposition 'about'.)
He is highly sensitive about distractions. → He is highly sensitive to distractions. (Preposition check: the adjective 'sensitive' is followed by 'to'.)
