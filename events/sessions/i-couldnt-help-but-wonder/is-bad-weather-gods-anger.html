As the thunder rattled my Manhattan windows and the sky turned a bruised shade of purple, I couldn't help but wonder about the original blame game. Long before we had meteorologists to blame for ruining our weekend plans, ancient civilizations looked at a storm and saw the anthropomorphic wrath of a deity. Is bad weather really about the gods being angry at our earthly transgressions, or is it just our desperate human desire to believe that even the sky cares enough to pick a fight with us?

📝 Carrie's Diary: Rhetorical Reflection

"Long before meteorology, we blamed the deities' wrath for our storms. Today, we blame retrograde planets or our exes. Is it possible we just need a big, dramatic sky-show to justify our own human tantrums?"

📖 Session Vocabulary

Wrath
extreme anger (chiefly used for humorous or rhetorical effect).
Ancient people often interpreted lightning as the wrath of the heavens.
Deity
a god or goddess.
She wondered if her relationship status was controlled by some mischievous deity.
Propitiate
win or regain the favor of a god, spirit, or person by doing something that pleases them.
He tried to propitiate his partner with flowers after their huge argument.
Cataclysm
a large-scale and violent event in the natural world.
A sudden volcanic eruption was considered a divine cataclysm.
Mythology
a collection of myths, especially one belonging to a particular religious or cultural tradition.
Greek mythology is filled with gods acting with human-like pettiness.
Omens
events regarded as portents of good or evil.
He searched for omens in the sky to decide whether to call her.
Benevolent
well-meaning and kindly.
We hope for a benevolent climate, but nature is often indifferent.
Superstition ≠ Rationality
irrational belief in supernatural forces ≠ the quality of being based on reason and facts.
The ancient village was bound by dark superstition, refusing any modern rationality.
Anthropomorphic
having human characteristics.
Giving a storm an anthropomorphic personality makes its destructive power feel personal.
Awe
a feeling of reverential respect mixed with fear or wonder.
Standing at the edge of the canyon, we were struck by pure awe.

🟢 Round 2 — Deep Speculation: Agree or Disagree?▲
Debate
Our ancestors viewed storms as a divine conversation, while modern rationalism has reduced the sky to atmospheric pressure, stripping away all wrath and awe.
★ When was the last time you felt a deep sense of wonder looking at the night sky or a storm?
In older times, we personified the weather as an emotional deity; today, we view natural disasters through the cold lens of insurance liabilities.
★ Have you ever found yourself talking to nature, a plant, or an animal as if it understood you?
Ancient people united in rituals to propitiate their fears of nature, while modern individuals simply complain about weather delays on social media.
★ How does bad weather affect your willingness to go out and socialize?
A sudden weather cataclysm in the past was a spiritual warning to correct moral behavior, while modern society views it as an environmental policy failure.
★ Do you feel a personal sense of responsibility when you hear about climate disasters?
We have built climate-controlled modern bubbles, separating ourselves from the raw geographic mythology our agricultural ancestors lived by.
★ Do you prefer a constant, mild climate or the dramatic shift of four distinct seasons?
Older generations relied on natural omens to schedule their labor, whereas modern humans view any natural delay as an unacceptable systemic error.
★ How do you react when a storm completely ruins your carefully planned vacation?
Ancient cultures surrendered to a non-benevolent nature, while modern science feeds the dangerous illusion that humans can control the climate.
★ Do you check the weather forecast daily, or do you prefer to just look out the window?
We have replaced ancient superstition with scientific data, but we have lost the comforting belief that 'everything happens for a reason'.
★ Do you believe that human actions have a direct moral consequence on the world around us?
Modern architecture uses brute force to ignore geography, unlike older societies that built anthropomorphic structures to blend with local winds.
★ What is your favorite type of traditional architecture that feels cozy in bad weather?
The modern dread of rainy days is a product of indoor lifestyles; to our ancestors, rain was the ultimate symbol of awe, fertility, and life.
★ Do you find rain soothing and cozy, or does it make you feel gloomy and unmotivated?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
People thought the gods are angry. → People thought the gods were angry. (Tense agreement: Past reporting verb 'thought' requires the past tense 'were')
I am full of awesness. → I am filled with awe / filled with wonder. (Word formation: The correct noun is 'awe'; 'awesomeness' exists but is highly informal slang)
We discussed about the topic. → We discussed the topic. (Do not use 'about' after the transitive verb 'discuss'.)
