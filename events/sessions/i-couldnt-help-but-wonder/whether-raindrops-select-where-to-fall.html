As I watched a single raindrop make its way down my window, I couldn't help but wonder about destination. In a world where we obsess over finding our perfect path, do raindrops actually select the exact place where they fall — landing on the hottest, thirstiest patches of the earth by choice? Or are we all just falling blindly through the sky, hoping to land exactly where we are needed most?

📝 Carrie's Diary: Rhetorical Reflection

"Maybe some people are like raindrops... falling haphazardly through life, hoping that when they finally land, it's on someone who has been waiting in a heatwave just for them."

📖 Session Vocabulary

Speculate
to form a theory or conjecture about a subject without firm evidence.
Scientists continue to speculate about the existence of life on other planets.
Trajectory
the path followed by a projectile flying or an object moving under the action of given forces.
The trajectory of the raindrop was altered by a sudden gust of wind.
Premeditated
planned or thought out beforehand.
It is fascinating to imagine that natural processes could be premeditated rather than accidental.
Haphazard
lacking any obvious principle of organization.
The rain fell in a haphazard manner across the dry fields.
Desolate
empty, bleak, and devoid of life.
Drops falling on a desolate desert are a rare and precious occurrence.
Sentient
able to perceive or feel things.
Some philosophers speculate whether nature itself is sentient and purposeful.
Coincidence
a remarkable concurrence of events or circumstances without apparent causal connection.
It was a beautiful coincidence that the storm began just as we arrived.
Heatwave
a prolonged period of abnormally hot weather.
During the intense heatwave, we prayed for a single raindrop to fall.
Condensation
the conversion of a vapor or gas to a liquid.
Rain is formed through the natural process of condensation in clouds.
Manifest
to show or demonstrate clearly.
The laws of physics manifest themselves in the intricate shapes of snowflakes.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
If raindrops would select where to fall, everything would change. → If raindrops selected where to fall, everything would change. (Unreal conditional: Use past simple in the 'if' clause instead of 'would' + verb)
I can't help but wondering about this theory. → I can't help but wonder / I can't help wondering about this theory. (Idiomatic expression: 'cannot help but' is followed by the bare infinitive 'wonder')
Whether rain falls depends of the temperature. → Whether rain falls depends on the temperature. (Preposition correction: 'depend' collocates with 'on', not 'of')
Yesterday we had a very strong rain. → Yesterday we had a very heavy / torrential rain. (Collocation check: Use 'heavy' or 'torrential' to describe intense rain, not 'strong')
