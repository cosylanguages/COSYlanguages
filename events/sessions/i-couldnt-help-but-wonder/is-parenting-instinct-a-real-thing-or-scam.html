Sensitive Topic Note: This session discusses parenting choices, societal pressures, and the biological versus social aspects of childcare. Discussion is intended to explore psychological expectations with empathy and respect.

While watching parents in the park effortlessly manage crying babies with seemingly infinite patience, I wondered if they were born with a magical handbook or if it was all a highly successful public relations campaign. In a world that expects us to instantly transform into selfless, perfect guardians the moment we hold a newborn, is there actually a biological biological trigger that guides our every choice? I couldn't help but wonder... Is the parenting instinct a real, biological truth, or is it just a clever scam designed to guilt-trip us into silent conformity?

📝 Carrie's Diary: Rhetorical Reflection

"Do we truly have a built-in guide for love, or is raising a child just the most beautiful, terrifying group project we never signed up to lead?"

📖 Session Vocabulary

Instinct
an innate, typically fixed pattern of behavior in response to certain stimuli.
Many species rely on pure instinct rather than learned behaviors to care for their young.
Nurture ≠ Nature
the care and encouragement given to development ≠ the basic biological characteristics of an organism.
Psychologists love to debate whether parent-child bonding is a result of active nurture or pure nature.
Scam
a dishonest scheme; a fraud or deceptive trick.
Calling parenting a natural instinct is a scam that makes struggling mothers feel like failures.
Maternal
relating to a mother, especially during pregnancy or shortly after childbirth.
The medical staff offered excellent maternal support to help the new mother adjust.
Paternal
relating to a father, showing the kindness or care of a father.
His paternal feelings grew stronger as he spent more time playing with his daughter.
Conformity
behavior in accordance with socially accepted standards or expectations.
Society demands absolute conformity to traditional gender roles when starting a family.
Bonding
the establishment of a close relationship or emotional link.
Skin-to-skin contact right after birth helps trigger immediate parent-child bonding.
Societal
relating to human society and its collective values or structures.
We must dismantle the societal pressures that demand parents to be completely flawless.
Expectation
a strong belief that something will happen or be the case in the future.
Having a high expectation of immediate happiness can lead to postpartum depression.
Altruism
selfless concern for the well-being of others.
Many evolutionary biologists view parenting as the ultimate form of animal altruism.

🟠 Warm-up — The Relationship Question▲
Do you believe that anyone can become a perfect parent with enough practice and preparation?
  • Have you ever felt a sudden 'instinct' to care for or protect a helpless creature?
  • Why does society assume that women possess a stronger natural parenting instinct than men?
🔵 Round 1 — Column Analysis▲
Questions
Do humans actually possess a biological parenting instinct, or is raising children entirely a learned social behavior?
★ How did you learn to take care of others when you were younger?
Why do we label parenting as an instinct, knowing it is highly influenced by nurture?
★ What is the most important lesson your parents taught you about care and support?
Is the concept of immediate maternal love a biological truth or a cultural myth?
★ Do you think fathers face less pressure to display immediate parental feelings than mothers?
How do societal expectations make new parents feel guilty when they struggle to bond with their newborns?
★ When have you felt pressured to meet unrealistic social expectations?
Is calling parenting a natural instinct a scam that hides the lack of public support for families?
★ Do you think the government should provide more financial support for new parents?
How does natural bonding progress between adoptive parents and children without a biological link?
★ Do you believe that blood relation is necessary for a parent-child bond?
Why does modern society place such a high expectation on parents to be perfectly happy and tireless?
★ How do you deal with the pressure of trying to look perfect to others?
Does the demand for social conformity force people to have children even when they don't want them?
★ Have you ever felt pressured by family or friends to make a major life choice?
Can genuine paternal affection match maternal instinct in terms of biological depth?
★ Who were you closer to growing up, your mother or your father?
How can we support new parents who are struggling without making them feel like failures?
★ What is the best support someone has offered you during a stressful time?

🟢 Round 2 — Deep Speculation: Agree or Disagree?▲
Debate
The internet hyper-analyzes parenting, making modern parents insecure and disconnected from the natural instinct older generations relied on.
★ Do you think modern parents overthink their methods compared to their own parents?
In older eras, childcare was a shared tribal effort, while modern individualism has turned the nature-versus-nurture debate into an isolated parental crisis.
★ Did you grow up with grandparents or extended family actively involved in your daily life?
Older societies accepted children as simple additions to life, while modern society has turned parenthood into a high-cost, high-pressure scam.
★ Do you think having children in the modern world is more stressful than in the past?
Traditional societies celebrated maternal sacrifices as sacred duties, whereas modern careers demand that mothers work as if they don't have children.
★ Do you believe it is possible to 'have it all' in terms of career and family?
Ancient cultures held no concept of active paternal involvement, while modern parenting expects fathers to share domestic labor equally.
★ Who were you closer to growing up, your mother or your father?
The modern demand for absolute social conformity has turned parenting into a competitive race for credentials, destroying the simple childhoods of the past.
★ Did you feel a lot of academic pressure when you were growing up?
Older generations relied on multi-generational wisdom, while modern parents struggle with bonding because they rely on commercial parenting books.
★ Who do you trust more for life advice: family elders or certified experts?
Traditional parenting demanded strict, unquestioning obedience, whereas modern societal values focus on emotional validation and bonding.
★ How would you describe the main difference between your upbringing and that of your parents?
We have traded the organic, simple play of the past for an over-scheduled expectation of academic and athletic success.
★ What was your favorite outdoor game or activity when you were a child?
Older societies viewed children as communal wealth, while modern economic structures penalize parental altruism with extreme living costs.
★ Do you think modern cities are welcoming places for large families?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
She has a maternalist instinct. → She has a maternal instinct. (Adjective derivation: the correct adjective related to mothers is 'maternal', not 'maternalist'.)
We discussed about children education. → We discussed child education / upbringing. (The verb 'discuss' is transitive; also, 'child upbringing' or 'child raising' is more natural than 'children education'.)
It is a very sensible topic to debate. → It is a very sensitive topic to debate. (False friend check: 'sensible' means reasonable/practical, whereas 'sensitive' means delicate or emotional.)
