Topic

Paid Subscriptions

The introduction of paid subscription models on major social media platforms marks a significant shift in how we access digital spaces. This session explores whether paying for verification and privacy is a fair trade-off or if it creates a digital divide between users. We will discuss the future of "free" internet services and the evolving value of personal data in a monetized online world.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Subscription
a regular payment to access a service or content
Example: Many platforms now use subscription models instead of free access.
Personalization
adapting a service to fit individual preferences
Example: Personalization improves user experience on social media.
Privacy
the right to control personal data and information
Example: Privacy concerns are growing on digital platforms.
Revenue
money a company earns from its business activities
Example: Advertising is a major source of revenue for Meta.
Creator
someone who produces online content such as videos or posts
Example: Many creators depend on social media income.
Bot
an automated account or program that performs online actions
Example: Bots are often used for spam or fake engagement.
Customization
changing features to match personal needs or taste
Example: Customization lets users design their profiles.
Innovation
the development of new ideas or technologies
Example: AI is driving innovation in the tech industry.
Engagement
interaction between users and online content
Example: Higher engagement increases visibility of posts.
Digital divide
the gap between people with and without access to technology
Example: Paid AI tools may widen the digital divide.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Paid vs Free
  • Would you pay $10 a month to use Instagram without ads?
  • Do you think social media is a "right" or a "luxury"?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
1. A: Subscription models could create “quiet zones” online where only serious users stay.
B: Free access could allow social media to remain a global “public square.”
2. A: Paid users might actually “own” their attention—less tracking, fewer manipulative ads.
B: Free platforms allow billions of people to communicate without thinking about payment.
3. A: Subscription revenue could reduce the need for emotional manipulation in ads.
B: Free platforms function like digital infrastructure for the world.
4. A: AI personalization could evolve into a “digital assistant version of you” that filters stress.
B: Free systems allow unexpected content discovery—sometimes changing people’s lives.
5. A: Paid identity systems could make bots economically inefficient.
B: Free platforms allow open participation for small voices without barriers.
6. A: Creators might get direct micro-subscriptions, supporting niche content.
B: Free platforms allow unknown creators to “go viral overnight.”
7. A: Paid users might create fully immersive “social identity layers.”
B: Free users benefit from universal design—everyone speaks the same language.
8. A: Subscription income could fund experimental emotion-aware feeds.
B: Free competition forces companies to innovate quickly to keep users.
9. A: Paid communities might shift toward “high-intent communication.”
B: Free platforms enable chaotic creativity—memes and global movements.
10. A: Paid AI tools might create a “cognitive gap” with advanced assistants.
B: Free access ensures even low-income users participate in global culture.

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
1. A: Social media could split into “silent luxury platforms” where influence depends on ability to pay.
B: Free platforms might become so overloaded that meaningful communication gets buried.
2. A: Even paid platforms may still collect behavioral data, just more subtly.
B: Free platforms may train users to accept surveillance as “normal life.”
3. A: Companies might invent new paid layers until basic usage feels incomplete.
B: Free platforms may push extreme engagement tactics that exploit emotions.
4. A: Hyper-personalization could trap users in “predictive identity loops.”
B: Lack of personalization could make feeds feel emotionally exhausting.
5. A: Paid systems might just create “elite bots” that look more human.
B: Free platforms may drown in fake engagement.
6. A: Creators might feel forced into constant monetization pressure.
B: Free platforms may reward sensational content over creativity.
7. A: Social identity could become a paid status symbol.
B: Uniform design may limit emotional expression.
8. A: Companies might prioritize profitable AI features over ethics.
B: Free models may struggle to fund AI safety and quality.
9. A: Paid spaces might lead to intellectual echo chambers.
B: Free spaces may become emotionally chaotic.
10. A: Wealthier users could get AI systems that actively shape their decisions.
B: Free users may still be exposed to overwhelming info without guidance.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I'm not agree with paying→I don't agree with paying(Agree is a verb)
The most of people use WhatsApp→Most people use WhatsApp(Generalization)
It depends of the revenue model→It depends on the revenue model(Depends on)
I am using Facebook since ten years→I have been using Facebook for ten years(Present Perfect Continuous)
It is a very useful news→It is very useful news(Uncountable news)
