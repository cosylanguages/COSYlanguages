Theme

Bees, Honey, and Green Cities

This session celebrates Urban Beekeeping Day—a special day to learn about bees in our cities. Bees are very important because they make honey and help plants grow. We will talk about how we can protect bees on our balconies and in our urban gardens. Join us for a simple and sweet discussion about nature in the city.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Bee
a yellow and black insect that makes honey.
I saw a busy bee on a flower in my garden this morning.
Honey
a sweet, sticky yellow liquid made by bees.
I love to put sweet honey in my warm tea.
Urban
relating to a town or city.
We need to build more green parks in urban areas.
Flower
the colored part of a plant from which the seed or fruit develops.
Bees fly from flower to flower to collect sweet nectar.
Protect
keep safe from harm or injury.
We must protect bees because they help grow our food.
Garden
a piece of ground next to a house, used for growing flowers, fruit, or vegetables.
My grandfather has a beautiful vegetable garden in the countryside.
Hive
a container for keeping bees; a colony of bees.
A single wooden hive can contain thousands of busy bees.
Sweet ≠ Bitter
having the pleasant taste of sugar ≠ having a sharp, pungent, or unpleasant taste.
The ripe strawberry was delicious and sweet, but the green skin of the lemon tasted very bitter.
Nature
the physical world including plants, animals, and landscapes.
I love to walk in nature and listen to the birds.
Beekeeper
a person who owns and takes care of bees.
The beekeeper wears a protective white suit when collecting honey.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • Do you like to eat honey? What is your favorite breakfast food?
  • Are you afraid of bees, or do you find them interesting?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Quiz / Discussion
Where can we find a busy bee in a large city?
★ Do you see bees on your balcony or near your windows?
Do you buy local honey from your city markets?
★ What is your favorite way to use honey at home?
Why is it important to have green areas in urban cities?
★ Is there a green park near your house?
What is your favorite flower in the garden?
★ Do you grow any flowers or plants at home?
How can we protect little insects in our cities?
★ Do you try to help animals or insects when you see them?
Is having a small garden good for your mental health?
Where is the best place to put a wooden hive in a city?
Do you prefer sweet food or salty food?
What is your favorite activity in nature?
Would you like to be a beekeeper for a day?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The bee have beautiful wings. → The bee has beautiful wings. (Subject-verb agreement: 'The bee' is singular, use 'has'.)
I saw a bee on yesterday. → I saw a bee yesterday. (Time adverb: do not use the preposition 'on' before 'yesterday'.)
