Theme

Bees, Honey, and Green Cities

This session celebrates Urban Beekeeping Day—a special day to learn about bees in our cities. Bees are very important because they make honey and help plants grow. We will talk about how we can protect bees on our balconies and in our urban gardens. Join us for a simple and sweet discussion about nature in the city.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Bee
a yellow and black insect that makes honey.
I saw a busy bee on a flower in my garden this morning.
Honey
a sweet, sticky yellow liquid made by bees.
I love to put sweet honey in my warm tea.
Urban
relating to a town or city.
We need to build more green parks in urban areas.
Flower
the colored part of a plant from which the seed or fruit develops.
Bees fly from flower to flower to collect sweet nectar.
Protect
keep safe from harm or injury.
We must protect bees because they help grow our food.
Garden
a piece of ground next to a house, used for growing flowers, fruit, or vegetables.
My grandfather has a beautiful vegetable garden in the countryside.
Hive
a container for keeping bees; a colony of bees.
A single wooden hive can contain thousands of busy bees.
Sweet ≠ Bitter
having the pleasant taste of sugar ≠ having a sharp, pungent, or unpleasant taste.
The ripe strawberry was delicious and sweet, but the green skin of the lemon tasted very bitter.
Nature
the physical world including plants, animals, and landscapes.
I love to walk in nature and listen to the birds.
Beekeeper
a person who owns and takes care of bees.
The beekeeper wears a protective white suit when collecting honey.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • Do you like to eat honey? What is your favorite breakfast food?
  • Are you afraid of bees, or do you find them interesting?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Quiz / Discussion
Where can we find a busy bee in a large city?
★ Do you see bees on your balcony or near your windows?
Do you buy local honey from your city markets?
★ What is your favorite way to use honey at home?
Why is it important to have green areas in urban cities?
★ Is there a green park near your house?
What is your favorite flower in the garden?
★ Do you grow any flowers or plants at home?
How can we protect little insects in our cities?
★ Do you try to help animals or insects when you see them?
Why is tending a small garden or caring for plants on a balcony so beneficial for our urban mental health?
★ Do you have a favorite green spot in your city where you go to destress?
How does placing a wooden hive on office rooftops help bridge the gap between human workspace and local biodiversity?
★ Would you feel comfortable working in an office building that has active bee hives on the roof?
How does the contrast of sweet honey versus the bitter realities of city pollution teach us about ecological balance?
★ Do you actively pay attention to where your food is produced and how it impacts the environment?
Why does a walk in nature feel completely different from walking through a beautifully landscaped city park?
★ When was the last time you felt truly disconnected from technology and connected to nature?
Why do traditional roles like being a beekeeper require a level of patience and observation that modern digital jobs lack?
★ If you had to choose a manual, outdoor profession for a year, what would you want to try?

🟢 Round 2 — Agree or Disagree: The Future▲
Predictions
In the future, every residential balcony in smart cities will have a mandatory wooden bee hive to support local ecosystems.
★ Would you be willing to host a hive on your balcony if it meant helping save the local bee population?
Urban honey will become a luxury currency, more expensive than chocolate or fine wine, due to its rare medicinal benefits.
★ How much are you willing to pay for organic, locally sourced food versus cheap, mass-produced items?
City centers will completely ban private cars, replacing roads with continuous green corridors of flowers and wild gardens.
★ Can you imagine a city where you walk through a lush forest instead of on concrete sidewalks to get to work?
In the future, robotic bees will completely replace real insects for pollinating plants in massive vertical indoor farms.
★ Do you feel comfortable eating food that was pollinated and harvested entirely by robotic systems?
We will eventually develop bio-engineered bees that do not sting, making urban beekeeping completely safe and accessible to everyone.
★ Would you still feel a respect for nature if we engineered all of its dangers and discomforts away?
As global temperatures rise, bees will migrate permanently to cities, making urban areas the primary sanctuaries for wildlife survival.
★ Do you think cities have a responsibility to design spaces for animals, or are cities only for humans?
All future primary school children will be required to care for active hives as a core part of their biology education.
★ Did your school have a garden or animals, and how did that shape your view of nature?
The widespread use of synthetic chemical pesticides will eventually lead to a global ecological collapse that vertical farms cannot fix.
★ Do you buy organic food to avoid pesticides, or are you skeptical of the 'organic' label?
In the future, we will have a global holiday where all city lights are turned off for a night to let nocturnal insects and nature thrive.
★ How would you spend a night in your city with absolute darkness and no electricity?
The rise of highly advanced urban beekeeping will lead to a new social movement of 'eco-primitivism' where people reject modern technology entirely.
★ If a group of friends invited you to live in a self-sustaining eco-village for a year, would you join them?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The bee have beautiful wings. → The bee has beautiful wings. (Subject-verb agreement: 'The bee' is singular, use 'has'.)
I saw a bee on yesterday. → I saw a bee yesterday. (Time adverb: do not use the preposition 'on' before 'yesterday'.)
