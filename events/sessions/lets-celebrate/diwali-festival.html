Diwali History 📖

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is one of the most significant and vibrant celebrations in Indian culture, symbolizing the spiritual victory of light over darkness and good over evil. This session explores the rich mythology, diverse traditions, and the deep sense of hospitality that define this five-day festival. We will discuss the evolution of Diwali in a globalized world and the balance between maintaining ancient customs and modern environmental concerns.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Diyas
small oil lamps made of clay, traditionally used during Diwali.
Lighting diyas symbolizes the victory of light over darkness.
Rangoli
an art form in which patterns are created on the floor using materials such as colored rice, flour, or flower petals.
Creating a beautiful rangoli at the entrance of the house welcomes guests and good luck.
Prosperity
the state of being successful, especially in material or financial terms.
Diwali is a time to pray for prosperity and health for the coming year.
Festivity
the celebration of something in a joyful and exuberant way.
The streets were filled with music and general festivity.
Firecrackers
small explosive devices used primarily to produce a large amount of noise.
Although popular, firecrackers are being limited in some cities due to pollution.
Hospitality
the friendly and generous reception and entertainment of guests.
Diwali is famous for the warm hospitality shown by families to their neighbors.
Mythology
a collection of myths, especially one belonging to a particular religious or cultural tradition.
According to Hindu mythology, Diwali celebrates the return of Lord Rama.
Feast
a large meal, typically a celebratory one.
Families gather for a grand feast featuring traditional sweets and savory dishes.
Cleanse
to make something thoroughly clean.
Many people cleanse and decorate their homes before the festival starts.
Symbolism
the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities.
The symbolism of light in Diwali is universal across many different faiths.

Agree or Disagree
Traditional fireworks will eventually be banned globally to protect our environment, making celebrations quieter and more introspective.

★ Should your country have more public holidays for different religions?

★ Do you prefer a physical card or a digital message?
