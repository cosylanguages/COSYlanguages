UN Asteroid Day 📖

International Asteroid Day, observed on the anniversary of the 1908 Tunguska event, aims to raise awareness about the potential risk of asteroid impacts and the science of planetary defense. This session explores the history of significant impacts and the technological advancements being made to detect and divert near-Earth objects. We will discuss the balance between the rare but catastrophic threat from space and the immediate challenges we face here on Earth.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Asteroid
a small rocky object that travels around the Sun.
Scientists are tracking an asteroid that will pass close to Earth next year.
Impact
a collision between two objects.
The impact of a large asteroid could cause serious damage.
Threat
something that may cause harm or danger.
Experts study possible threats from space.
Detect
to discover or notice something.
New telescopes can detect smaller asteroids than before.
Orbit
the path that an object follows around a planet, star, or moon.
The asteroid follows an orbit around the Sun.
Awareness
knowledge or understanding of a situation.
International Asteroid Day helps raise awareness about space hazards.
Predict
to say what is likely to happen in the future.
Scientists can often predict where an asteroid will travel.
Survival ≠ Extinction
continuing to live or exist ≠ the state of being wiped out or disappearing entirely.
The community's struggle for survival saved them from the brink of total extinction.
Planetary defense
efforts to protect Earth from dangerous objects in space.
Several countries are investing in planetary defense programs.
Breakthrough
an important discovery or development.
The new telescope was a major breakthrough in asteroid detection.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Space Exploration
  • What is the first thing that comes to your mind when you think of "outer space"?
  • Would you ever want to travel to space if you had the chance?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
What is the scientific difference between a harmless meteor shower and a potentially hazardous asteroid approaching our atmosphere?
★ Have you ever worried about a natural or space-related danger?
Why should modern nations invest in global planetary defense systems when we face so many pressing social crises here on Earth?
★ If you had to choose, what global problem would you invest in first?
How do astronomers use advanced telescope networks to detect dark, non-reflective space rocks before they get too close?
★ Do you usually prefer to detect problems early in your own life, or deal with them when they appear?
What kind of technological breakthrough is needed to successfully divert or destroy a massive object heading toward us?
★ Which recent scientific breakthrough has impressed you the most?
How does plotting the precise gravitational orbit of near-Earth objects help scientists determine the exact probability of a collision?
★ Have you ever learned something that completely changed how you understood a situation?
Why is public awareness about long-term space hazards so much lower than our concern for immediate environmental crises?
★ What topic do you think deserves much more public attention?
What would be the catastrophic environmental and climatic impact if a medium-sized space rock landed in the middle of an ocean?
★ Which historical event do you think changed the world the most?
How do science fiction movies about a space-related threat shape the public's understanding of real astronomical science?
★ Have you ever watched a disaster movie that made you think differently about real life?
How far in advance can astronomers realistically predict a collision, and how much time would humanity have to prepare?
★ Are you someone who likes predicting the future, or do you prefer surprises?
Why is global solidarity and cooperation essential for the survival of our species when facing an existential threat from space?
★ Which countries do you think cooperate especially well in science today?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Agree or Disagree
Governments will eventually hide data about an approaching asteroid to prevent global panic and maintain social order.
★ Would you prefer to know the truth about a global catastrophe even if nothing could be done to stop it?
In the future, asteroid mining corporations will intentionally divert small asteroids close to Earth, risking lives for resource collection.
★ Do you think space-based corporate greed is a realistic threat to our planetary safety?
Advanced AI systems will eventually make all planetary defense decisions, completely removing human error from the equation.
★ Would you trust an automated AI system to make split-second decisions that decide the fate of humanity?
As planetary defense technologies improve, fake asteroid threats will be fabricated by nations to justify space weaponization.
★ How skeptical are you when governments claim there is an invisible, external threat to national security?
Public 'asteroid awareness fatigue' will eventually set in, leading people to ignore real emergency warnings as scientific alarmism.
★ Do you ever feel overwhelmed or desensitized by the constant stream of crisis warnings in the news?
The technology to alter an asteroid's orbit will create dangerous geopolitical conflicts over who decides where the asteroid is redirected.
★ If a threat could be redirected to a different part of the world, how should humanity decide who takes the risk?
Humanity's long-term survival will eventually depend on colonizing other planets rather than defending Earth from space hazards.
★ Would you sign up to be one of the first colonists on a hostile planet if it meant saving the human species?
Private space tourism companies will eventually offer wealthy tourists front-row seats to view close-passing asteroids in space.
★ Would you pay for a dangerous space voyage to witness a spectacular but hazardous cosmic event?
In the distant future, the threat of an extinction-level asteroid will unite all human nations into a single, borderless global government.
★ Why does it seem to take an existential, external threat to get humans to cooperate with one another?
A major asteroid impact will eventually be seen as a natural 'reset' button for Earth's ecosystem that humans should not interfere with.
★ Do you believe humanity has a moral duty to preserve its own existence at all costs, or are we just another part of nature's cycle?
