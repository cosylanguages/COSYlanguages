Full Info 📖

This session is about National Workaholics Day and the balance between work and life. We will talk about why people work too much and how to rest. We will also learn easy words to describe our daily job and hobbies.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Balance
a state where different things are equal.
We need a good balance between work and sleep.
Career
a job or profession that you do for a long time.
She wants to have a successful career in education.
Overtime
extra time that you work at your job.
He works overtime on Fridays to get more money.
Relaxation
the state of resting and feeling calm.
Reading a book is a great way of relaxation.
Stress
a feeling of worry or pressure.
Too much work can cause a lot of stress.
Busy
having a lot of things to do.
I am very busy with my English homework today.
Holiday
a period of time when you do not work.
They are going to Spain for their summer holiday.
Leisure
time when you can relax and do what you want.
In my leisure time, I like to play football.
Office
a room or building where people work at desks.
Our new office is very close to the train station.
Task
a small piece of work that you must do.
Cleaning the kitchen is my daily task.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Introduce your job.
  • What is your job, and do you like it?
  • What time do you start and finish work every day?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Simple discussion about work habits.
Do you have a good work-life balance?
★ What is the most important thing for your daily balance?
Why do people want to have a fast career?
★ Do you want to change your career in the future?
How often do people in your country work overtime?
★ Did you work overtime last week?
What is your favorite method of relaxation?
★ How do you relax after a very busy day?
Does your job cause you a lot of stress?
★ What causes you the most stress at work or school?
Why are modern people always so busy?
★ Are you busy this weekend, or do you have free time?
Where do people go for their summer holiday?
★ Where did you go for your last holiday?
What are some popular leisure activities in your city?
★ What leisure activities do you do with your family?
Do you prefer to work in an office or from home?
★ Is your current office comfortable for you?
How do you organize your daily task list?
★ What is your most difficult task today?

🟢 Round 2 — Agree or Disagree: The Future▲
Predictions
Simple future predictions about jobs and rest.
In the future, the boundary between work and personal life will disappear completely as our brains are permanently connected to the network.
★ How do you successfully disconnect from work or study when your smartphone is always in your pocket?
Universal basic income will eventually eliminate the work ethic, causing humans to lose their sense of purpose and drive.
★ If you never had to work for money again, what would you do to feel useful and productive?
As artificial intelligence takes over all productive tasks, 'leisure' will become the most stressful part of human life.
★ Have you ever felt anxious or guilty during a long period of doing nothing?
Physical offices will become obsolete, and remote work will turn our homes into isolated, 24/7 productivity hubs.
★ Do you work better when there is a clear physical boundary between your office and your bedroom?
Future employers will use biometric brain-scans to monitor stress levels and force employees to take mandatory relaxation breaks.
★ Would you feel comfortable if your boss could track your emotional state and stress levels in real-time?
Working overtime will eventually be classified as an addictive behavior and treated with psychological therapy.
★ Have you ever used work or busywork as a way to avoid dealing with personal life problems?
In the future, a person's worth will be judged entirely by their level of creative self-expression rather than their career achievements.
★ What is a creative hobby you would love to pursue if you had unlimited free time?
We will see a massive global decline in retirement ages as automation makes lifelong labor unnecessary.
★ At what age do you realistically hope to stop working, and what does 'retirement' look like to you?
Highly successful, demanding careers will become an exclusive privilege for those willing to sacrifice having a family.
★ Do you believe it is truly possible to have a top-tier career and a thriving family life at the same time?
In the future, humanity will look back at 40-hour workweeks as a form of voluntary historical slavery.
★ If you could redesign the standard workweek, how many hours would be ideal for human happiness?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I have a busy job→I have a demanding job(Demanding is better for jobs than busy)
I am work in office→I work in an office(Simple present tense structure)
He work very hard→He works very hard(Third-person singular 's')
