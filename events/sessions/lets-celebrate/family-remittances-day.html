Official UN Day 📖

International Day of Family Remittances recognizes the crucial financial contribution that over 200 million migrant workers make to their families back home. This session explores the economic impact of these transfers on developing nations and the personal sacrifices made by those working abroad. We will discuss the challenges of migration, the importance of financial inclusion, and the future of global money transfers in a digital age.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Remittance
money sent to family in another country.
Many workers send remittances to support their parents back home.
Migrant worker
a person who moves to another country for work.
Migrant workers often send money to their families.
Financial support
money or help given to someone in need.
Remittances are a key form of financial support.
Living expenses
money needed for daily life (food, rent, transport).
Remittances often cover living expenses.
Bank transfer
moving money electronically between accounts.
Most remittances are sent via bank transfers.
Fee
money paid for sending money (transaction fee).
High fees reduce the value of remittances.
Developing country
a country with lower income and industrial growth.
Many remittances go to developing countries.
Household income
total money a family earns.
Remittances increase household income.
Economic impact
effect on the economy.
Remittances have a strong economic impact.
Financial inclusion
access to banking and financial services.
Digital tools improve financial inclusion.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Have you ever sent money to another city or country?
  • What was the reason? Was it easy to do?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Why has sending a regular remittance become a vital lifeline for millions of families separated by international borders?
★ Have you ever supported someone financially abroad through a bank transfer?
What are the biggest social and emotional challenges that a typical migrant worker faces when living far from their family?
★ Would you depend on remittances for your living expenses if you had to?
How does receiving direct financial support from relatives abroad change the educational opportunities of children in developing areas?
★ Do you think individual financial actions can change national economies?
Why do rising global living expenses make it harder for workers to save money while still supporting their families back home?
★ Would you change your service provider if fees were lower?
How did the shift from cash-based hand-delivery to secure bank transfer technology revolutionize global money transfers?
★ How important is stable income in your own life decisions?
Why do high transaction fee percentages charged by financial agencies disproportionately hurt low-wage migrant workers?
★ Which digital banking tools do you trust for a bank transfer?
In what ways can a developing country leverage incoming remittances to build better local infrastructure and business opportunities?
★ Would you prefer financial independence or external support?
How does a steady increase in household income from abroad affect the local cost of goods and services in small towns?
★ Do you think financial systems today are fair for everyone?
What is the overall economic impact when a nation becomes heavily dependent on foreign currency sent by citizens working abroad?
★ Would you feel pressure if your family depended on you financially?
How can mobile banking apps improve financial inclusion for unbanked populations in remote rural regions?
★ Do you think migration will increase or decrease in the future?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

Role-play: One person is a migrant worker who wants to send money home, and the other is a bank employee explaining the fees and the transfer process. Practice using 'fee', 'bank transfer', and 'financial inclusion'.

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Agree or Disagree
In the future, global remittances will be processed instantly with zero transaction fees, making traditional banks obsolete.
★ How much do you value speed versus safety when choosing a way to send money?
Universal Basic Income (UBI) will eventually replace the need for migrant workers to send financial support to their families.
★ If you received a guaranteed basic income every month, would you still want to work abroad?
Digital currencies and decentralized finance will completely replace national currencies for global money transfers.
★ Do you feel comfortable using decentralized digital currencies, or do you prefer traditional government-backed cash?
As remote work becomes universal, physical migration for jobs will end, and people will work for foreign companies from their home countries.
★ Would you prefer to work remotely from your hometown or physically move to a new country for a career?
In the future, the economic impact of global remittances will be managed entirely by AI algorithms to prevent local inflation.
★ Would you trust an artificial intelligence to manage your personal finances or savings?
The tradition of financially supporting extended family members will decline as young generations prioritize personal savings and independence.
★ Do you think we have a moral obligation to financially support our extended family, or should everyone be self-reliant?
Governments will eventually implement strict surveillance on all private international money transfers, ending financial privacy.
★ How much of your financial privacy are you willing to sacrifice in exchange for security and fraud prevention?
In the future, digital platforms will allow workers to send highly specific 'smart remittances' that can only be spent on education or healthcare.
★ If you sent money to a relative, would you want to control how they spend it, or do you believe in absolute trust?
As global wealth inequality grows, sending money home will become a mandatory international duty rather than a personal choice.
★ Do you believe that wealthy nations have a responsibility to share resources directly with individuals in developing countries?
The emotional stress and physical distance of working abroad will eventually lead to a global decline in migration.
★ Is any amount of financial gain worth the emotional cost of being separated from your loved ones?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I send money for my family.→I send money to my family.(Verb agreement)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
It is depend on you→It depends on you(Verb vs Adjective)
I have 25 years old→I am 25 years old(Age with 'to be')
He do not like it→He does not like it(Third person singular)
