📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Burnout
physical or mental collapse caused by overwork or stress
Example: Many employees are suffering from burnout due to long hours.
Productivity
the effectiveness of productive effort, as measured by the rate of output per unit of input
Example: Studies show that shorter weeks can actually increase productivity.
Well-being
the state of being comfortable, healthy, or happy
Example: Employee well-being should be a priority for every company.
Retention
the ability of a company to keep its employees
Example: A 4-day week can significantly improve staff retention.
Overhead
the regular costs of running a business (rent, electricity, etc.)
Example: Closing the office for an extra day can reduce overhead costs.
Flexibility
the quality of bending easily or being easily modified
Example: Modern workers value flexibility more than a high salary.
Trial
a formal test of something, typically in a workplace or laboratory
Example: The government is launching a national trial of the 4-day week.
Output
the amount of something produced by a person, machine, or industry
Example: Managers should focus on output rather than hours spent at a desk.
Commute
the journey between home and work
Example: A shorter work week means one less day of stressful commuting.
Implementation
the process of putting a decision or plan into effect
Example: The implementation of the new schedule took several months.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The 5th Day
  • If you had an extra day off every single week, what would you do with it?
  • Do you think you are more productive in the morning or the afternoon?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (10 Units)▲
Debate
1. A: A 4-day week significantly reduces burnout and improves mental health.
B: The extra pressure to finish work in 4 days might actually increase stress.
★ Have you ever felt close to burnout?
2. A: Shorter weeks increase productivity because people waste less time on "busy work."
B: Certain industries (like healthcare or retail) cannot afford to lose a day of work.
★ How much "waste time" do you have in a typical workday?
3. A: Companies with 4-day weeks find it much easier to attract and keep top talent.
B: Small businesses might struggle to pay full salaries for 20% less work.
★ Would you choose a job with a lower salary but a 4-day week?
4. A: Reducing commutes by 20% is a major win for the environment.
B: People might use their extra day off to travel more, increasing their carbon footprint.
★ How long is your daily commute?
5. A: An extra day allows for better "life admin," like doctor appointments and errands.
B: Society would have to change—if everyone is off on Friday, who runs the banks and shops?
★ Do you find it hard to manage "life admin" during the work week?
6. A: More free time encourages people to exercise and eat healthier.
B: People might just spend the extra day doomscrolling or watching TV.
★ How disciplined are you with your free time?
7. A: Parents can spend more time with their children, reducing the cost of childcare.
B: Schools would also need to switch to 4 days, which might hurt education.
★ Should school weeks also be 4 days?
8. A: It forces managers to focus on results rather than "hours at the desk."
B: It's harder to collaborate and schedule meetings when people have different off-days.
★ Do you prefer working alone or in a team?
9. A: A 4-day week is the natural evolution of work, just like the 5-day week was.
B: The 5-day week is a proven system that provides stability for the economy.
★ Do you think the 5-day week is "natural" or "outdated"?
10. A: It promotes gender equality as men have more time for domestic responsibilities.
B: It might widen the gap between "desk workers" and manual laborers.
★ Is a 4-day week a "luxury" for office workers?

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (10 Units)▲
Debate
1. If we switch to 4 days, will people eventually ask for a 3-day work week?
2. Can a country remain globally competitive if its neighbors work 5 or 6 days?
3. Will people be forced to work longer hours during those 4 days (e.g., 10-hour days)?
4. Is it fair if some sectors get it and others (like emergency services) don't?
5. Would a 4-day week lead to more "side hustles" instead of real rest?
6. Could it lead to wage stagnation as companies argue they are providing a benefit?
7. How would it affect the social culture of the office?
8. Would people feel more disconnected from their colleagues?
9. Should the government mandate it, or should it be a choice for each company?
10. What is the biggest risk to a company's success with this model?
✏️ Discussion (Mistakes)▲
I'm working in this company since 2010→I've been working at this company since 2010(Present Perfect Continuous)
I suggest to reduce hours→I suggest reducing hours(Suggest + -ing)
It is depend on the industry→It depends on the industry(Depends as a verb)
The most of employees like it→Most employees like it(Generalization)
He has many work to do→He has a lot of work to do(Uncountable 'work')
