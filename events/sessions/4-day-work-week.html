Level

Intermediate (B1)

Topic

Work-Life Balance / Productivity

The concept of a four-day work week is gaining global momentum as companies seek to balance productivity with employee well-being. This session explores the potential shift from the traditional five-day model to a more flexible, results-oriented schedule. We will discuss whether this change is a natural evolution of the modern workplace or a risky experiment for the global economy.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Burnout
physical or mental collapse caused by overwork or stress.
Many employees are suffering from burnout due to long hours.
Productivity
the effectiveness of productive effort, as measured by the rate of output per unit of input.
Studies show that shorter weeks can actually increase productivity.
Well-being
the state of being comfortable, healthy, or happy.
Employee well-being should be a priority for every company.
Retention
the ability of a company to keep its employees.
A 4-day week can significantly improve staff retention.
Overhead
the regular costs of running a business (rent, electricity, etc.).
Closing the office for an extra day can reduce overhead costs.
Flexibility
the quality of bending easily or being easily modified.
Modern workers value flexibility more than a high salary.
Trial
a formal test of something, typically in a workplace or laboratory.
The government is launching a national trial of the 4-day week.
Output
the amount of something produced by a person, machine, or industry.
Managers should focus on output rather than hours spent at a desk.
Commute
the journey between home and work.
A shorter work week means one less day of stressful commuting.
Implementation
the process of putting a decision or plan into effect.
The implementation of the new schedule took several months.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The 5th Day
  • If you had an extra day off every single week, what would you do with it?
  • Do you think you are more productive in the morning or the afternoon?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
1. A: A 4-day week significantly reduces burnout and improves mental health.
B: The extra pressure to finish work in 4 days might actually increase stress.
2. A: Shorter weeks increase productivity because people waste less time on "busy work."
B: Certain industries (like healthcare or retail) cannot afford to lose a day of work.
3. A: Companies with 4-day weeks find it much easier to attract and keep top talent.
B: Small businesses might struggle to pay full salaries for 20% less work. (Consider the role of Well-being here.)
4. A: Reducing commutes by 20% is a major win for the environment.
B: People might use their extra day off to travel more, increasing their carbon footprint. (Consider the role of Retention here.)
5. A: An extra day allows for better "life admin," like doctor appointments and errands.
B: Society would have to change—if everyone is off on Friday, who runs the banks and shops? (Consider the role of Overhead here.)
6. A: More free time encourages people to exercise and eat healthier.
B: People might just spend the extra day doomscrolling or watching TV. (Consider the role of Flexibility here.)
7. A: Parents can spend more time with their children, reducing the cost of childcare.
B: Schools would also need to switch to 4 days, which might hurt education. (Consider the role of Trial here.)
8. A: It forces managers to focus on results rather than "hours at the desk."
B: It's harder to collaborate and schedule meetings when people have different off-days. (Consider the role of Output here.)
9. A: A 4-day week is the natural evolution of work, just like the 5-day week was.
B: The 5-day week is a proven system that provides stability for the economy. (Consider the role of Commute here.)
10. A: It promotes gender equality as men have more time for domestic responsibilities.
B: It might widen the gap between "desk workers" and manual laborers. (Consider the role of Implementation here.)

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
1. A: If we switch to 4 days, people will eventually demand a 3-day week, harming the economy.
B: A 4-day week is a sustainable balance that reflects modern productivity. (How does Burnout affect this statement?)
2. A: Countries will lose global competitiveness against neighbors working 5 or 6 days.
B: Higher quality work and better retention will make the country more competitive. (How does Productivity affect this statement?)
3. A: Workers will be forced into stressful 10-hour days to compensate for the day off.
B: Redesigning work to remove "busy work" allows for the same output in normal hours. (How does Well-being affect this statement?)
4. A: It creates social inequality for sectors like emergency services that cannot close.
B: Different rotating shifts can ensure all workers receive equal rest periods. (How does Retention affect this statement?)
5. A: It will lead to more "side hustles" rather than the intended rest and recovery.
B: Employees should have the freedom to choose how they use their personal time. (How does Overhead affect this statement?)
6. A: Companies will use the extra day off as an excuse for long-term wage stagnation.
B: Competitive markets will still force companies to pay fair wages for top talent. (How does Flexibility affect this statement?)
7. A: The social culture and spontaneous collaboration of the office will be destroyed.
B: Office time will become more focused and social interactions more valuable. (How does Trial affect this statement?)
8. A: People will feel more disconnected from their teams and company mission.
B: Modern digital tools keep teams aligned and connected regardless of the schedule. (How does Output affect this statement?)
9. A: The government must mandate it to ensure all workers benefit equally.
B: It should remain a private choice to allow for industry-specific flexibility. (How does Commute affect this statement?)
10. A: The biggest risk is a significant drop in total industrial and service output.
B: The biggest risk is failing to adapt to the changing needs of the modern workforce. (How does Implementation affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I'm working in this company since 2010→I've been working at this company since 2010(Present Perfect Continuous)
I suggest to reduce hours→I suggest reducing hours(Suggest + -ing)
It is depend on the industry→It depends on the industry(Depends as a verb)
The most of employees like it→Most employees like it(Generalization)
He has many work to do→He has a lot of work to do(Uncountable 'work')
