This session is about National Simplicity Day and the beauty of simple living. We will talk about why people choose to have fewer things and live a quiet life. We will also learn simple words to describe our daily routines and hobbies.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Simple ≠ Complex
easily understood or done; presenting no difficulty ≠ consisting of many different and connected parts.
The basic idea was wonderfully simple, but the actual execution turned out to be incredibly complex.
Quiet
making little or no noise; peaceful.
He loves spending a quiet evening reading in his room.
Reduce
to make smaller or less in amount, degree, or size.
We should try to reduce our weekly screen time.
Nature
the physical world including plants, animals, landscapes, and natural features.
Walking in nature is highly beneficial for mental wellness.
Routine
a sequence of actions regularly followed.
Exercising in the morning is part of her daily routine.
Celebrate
to mark an important occasion with a festive social gathering or enjoyable activity.
They will celebrate his birthday with a small family dinner.
Declutter
to remove unnecessary items from an untidy or overcrowded place.
Decluttering your desk can improve your focus while studying.
Peaceful
free from disturbance; tranquil and calm.
She found a peaceful spot in the park to practice her English.
Hobby
an activity done regularly in one's leisure time for pleasure.
Gardening is a very relaxing and simple hobby.
Grateful
feeling or showing an appreciation of kindness; thankful.
I am very grateful for your warm hospitality and support.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to daily schedules.
  • What is the most relaxing part of your day?
  • Do you have too many things in your bedroom or house?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Simple discussion about simple living and habits.
What does it mean to live a simple life?
★ What is one simple thing that makes you very happy?
Why is it important to spend time in a quiet place?
★ Where do you go when you want a quiet moment?
How can we reduce our monthly expenses easily?
★ What monthly expense did you reduce recently?
Why do people feel more relaxed when they are in nature?
★ How often do you walk in a forest or park?
What is your morning routine?
★ Is your daily routine busy or relaxed?
How does your family celebrate traditional holidays?
★ How did you celebrate your last birthday?
Why is it satisfying to declutter your wardrobe?
★ Do you need to declutter your bedroom desk today?
What makes a neighborhood feel peaceful?
★ Is your current neighborhood peaceful or noisy?
Is having a physical hobby better than a digital one?
★ What hobbies do you practice in your free time?
Why should we feel grateful for simple things like fresh coffee?
★ What simple thing are you most grateful for today?

🟢 Round 2 — Simple Future Discussion▲
Predictions
Simple future predictions about modern lifestyle trends.
In the future, many people will move from big cities to quiet villages to find peace.
We will stop buying plastic products to reduce environmental waste.
People will work only three days a week to spend more time in nature.
Smartphones will have a mandatory silent mode after 9 PM.
We will celebrate a global 'No Screen' Day every single month.
Traditional physical hobbies (like painting and cooking) will become more popular than video games.
Minimalist houses (with very few things) will become standard for young couples.
We will find ways to produce food locally in every city neighborhood.
Everyone will have a personal garden to grow their own organic vegetables.
The concept of luxury will change from expensive cars to silent, peaceful spaces.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I prefer a life more simple→I prefer a simpler life(Comparative form of 'simple' is 'simpler')
I am work in a peaceful place→I work in a peaceful place(Simple present structure)
She declutters always her room→She always declutters her room(Adverbs of frequency come before the main verb)
