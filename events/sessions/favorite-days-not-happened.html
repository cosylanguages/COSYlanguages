Topic

Imagination, Idealism, and the "What Ifs" of Life

This session explores the psychological phenomenon of nostalgia for events that never actually took place—the "favorite days" we have built in our imagination. We will discuss how our idealized versions of the future or the past can act as both a source of inspiration and a barrier to appreciating our real lives. Join us as we examine the power of the human mind to find deep meaning in the purely hypothetical.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Idealized
regarded or represented as perfect or better than in reality.
Social media often presents an idealized version of people's lives that is far from the truth.
Hypothetical
based on or serving as a hypothesis; supposed but not necessarily real or true.
In the meeting, they discussed several hypothetical scenarios for the company's expansion.
Nostalgia
a sentimental longing or wistful affection for the past.
Old songs often trigger a deep sense of nostalgia for our teenage years.
Melancholy
a feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause.
The slow piano music filled the room with a sense of gentle melancholy.
Fulfillment
satisfaction or happiness as a result of fully developing one's abilities or character.
Many people seek personal fulfillment through their work rather than just a high salary.
Inspiration
the process of being mentally stimulated to do or feel something, especially something creative.
The beautiful landscape served as a direct inspiration for the artist's latest painting.
Disillusionment
a feeling of disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as one believed it to be.
The young politician faced a wave of disillusionment after seeing how the system actually worked.
Ambiguity
the quality of being open to more than one interpretation.
The ambiguity of the poem's ending allowed every reader to find their own meaning.
Anticipation
the action of anticipating something; expectation or prediction.
There was a great deal of anticipation among the fans before the concert started.
Perspective
a particular way of regarding something; a point of view.
Try to look at the problem from the perspective of your customer to find a better solution.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Think about your "perfect day."
  • Has your perfect day ever actually happened in real life?
  • Why do we often remember the past as being better than it really was?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Why is it so tempting to live in an idealized version of the future instead of focusing on the present?
In what ways can planning for hypothetical problems make us more resilient in real life?
Do you think nostalgia for things that never happened is a sign of a creative or an unhappy mind?
How does the melancholy of "what could have been" affect your current decision-making?
Is true fulfillment possible if we are always comparing our reality to our imagination?
Where do you find your greatest source of inspiration when you feel stuck in a routine?
How can we avoid the pain of disillusionment when a long-held dream finally comes true?
Why does the ambiguity of the future cause both fear and excitement in equal measure?
Is the anticipation of a holiday often more enjoyable than the holiday itself?
How does changing your perspective on a past failure help you move forward today?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I am nostalgia for my childhood→I feel nostalgia for my childhood / I'm nostalgic for my childhood(Noun vs Adjective)
It is a idealized version→It is an idealized version(Articles: a vs an)
I should of stayed at home→I should have stayed at home(Modal verbs in the past)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
It is depend on you→It depends on you(Verb vs Adjective)
