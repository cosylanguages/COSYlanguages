Is AI Inevitable? Why Do They Say So?

10 November 2026

Duration

60 minutes

Languages

🇬🇧 English

Level

Intermediate (B1-B2)

Theme

Technology & Modern Existentialism

Perhaps there is a silent resignation in the way we speak about the future of artificial intelligence. We are told, almost daily, that its dominance is inevitable—a force as certain as gravity or the change of seasons. In this session, we wander through the psychological roots of this modern technological fatalism. We will explore whether we truly believe in this machine-led future, or if we are simply too exhausted by modern living to imagine an alternative path of human autonomy. Together, let us reflect on why we say AI is inevitable, and what that says about our own collective self-worth.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Inevitable
certain to happen; unavoidable.
We are constantly told that the transition to automated jobs is completely inevitable.
Fatalism
the belief that all events are predetermined and therefore inevitable.
A strange sense of technological fatalism has made people stop protesting against algorithmic decisions.
Autonomy
the right or condition of self-government; personal independence.
We must protect human cognitive autonomy in an era where machines write our emails.
Resignation
the acceptance of something undesirable but inevitable.
She accepted the new automated workplace guidelines with a sigh of quiet resignation.
Algorithm
a process or set of rules to be followed in calculations, especially by a computer.
A complex algorithm now decides which candidate gets selected for the initial interview.
Overestimate ≠ Underestimate
estimate something to be better or larger than it really is ≠ estimate something to be smaller or less important.
We often overestimate what a machine can do in five years, but underestimate what a human can achieve in ten.
Obsolescence
the state of being no longer useful or needed.
Many traditional artists are deeply terrified of professional obsolescence due to generative software.
Cognitive
relating to the mental action or process of acquiring knowledge and understanding.
Outsourcing all writing tasks to AI might reduce our collective cognitive abilities over time.
Innovation
the action or process of innovating; introducing new ideas or methods.
We must distinguish between true human innovation and simple mathematical imitation.
Agency
the capacity of individuals to act independently and make their own free choices.
We surrender our personal agency every time we let an app choose our music.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • Perhaps you use artificial intelligence tools in your daily work or study. How does it make you feel about your own skills?
  • Do you feel excited or slightly anxious when you think about what the world will look like in twenty years?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
Questions
Why is it that we so easily accept the claim that AI is completely inevitable?
★ Have you ever felt a quiet resignation regarding the growing role of algorithms in your life?
How does technological fatalism prevent us from actively shaping our own future?
★ Do you think individuals can make a difference in how technology develops?
What happens to our personal autonomy when we rely on algorithms to make simple daily choices?
★ When was the last time you let an algorithm decide what you should read or watch?
How can we protect our cognitive independence in a world saturated with digital assistants?
★ Do you still try to memorize phone numbers and map directions, or do you outsource them?
Are we too quick to overestimate the intelligence of machines and underplay our own unique human traits?
★ What is one human quality you possess that no AI could ever replicate?
How does the fear of professional obsolescence affect employee mental health and creativity?
★ Has your industry started using automated tools, and did it make you feel threatened?
Why does modern society equate rapid technological innovation with absolute moral progress?
★ Do you feel that rapid technological innovation is always beneficial for human happiness?
How does our reliance on predictive algorithms slowly erode our sense of personal agency?
★ In what area of your life do you feel you have the greatest personal agency and control?
Is it possible that the inevitability of AI is a marketing narrative created by large tech corporations?
★ Do you feel that the rise of artificial intelligence is truly inevitable, or can society choose to limit it?
How can we design a future where machines serve human happiness rather than replacing human workers?
★ What skill do you think future human workers must develop to remain valuable in an automated world?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

Situational exercise: Perhaps you are a team leader. You must decide whether to replace three copywriters with an AI tool or keep them, knowing that your budget is extremely tight.

🟢 Round 2 — Dilemmas / Future▲
Debate
If everyone accepted that the rise of sentient machines was inevitable, would we stop trying to regulate technology altogether?
If you fell into a state of technological fatalism, would you still find motivation to learn new human skills?
If you had to sacrifice a portion of your personal autonomy for perfect safety guaranteed by AI, would you make the trade?
If a feeling of quiet resignation took over the creative community, would art become completely derivative and soulless?
If a perfect matchmaking algorithm chose your life partner, would you trust its choice or prefer the magic of unpredictable mistakes?
If we tend to overestimate short-term technological change but underestimate its long-term impact, how should we plan our careers?
If your professional skills faced sudden obsolescence due to automation, would you retrain in a manual craft or seek a digital career?
If a machine could perfectly replicate your cognitive voice and writing style, would your friends still value your actual letters?
If rapid corporate innovation was prioritized over human well-being, would our future cities be highly efficient but emotionally empty?
If you had to defend your unique human agency against an super-intelligent system, which of your flaws would you highlight as a strength?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The AI is inevitable to happen. → The rise of AI is inevitable / AI is bound to happen. (Grammar check: 'inevitable' is an adjective, typically used directly to describe a noun rather than with 'to happen' infinitives.)
I am fear of being obsolete. → I am afraid of being obsolete / I fear obsolescence. (Word class: 'fear' is a verb or noun; use 'afraid of' as an adjective phrase or the active verb 'I fear'.)
We discussed about the technology. → We discussed the technology. (Preposition error: 'discuss' is transitive and does not take 'about'.)
