Full Article 📖

Perhaps our societies have discovered that the easiest way to silence demands for deep structural equality is to sell a glossy, watered-down version of empowerment. In this session, we wander through the psychological landscape of purple washing—the practice of co-opting feminist language and gender equality goals to sanitize corporate reputations or justify restrictive state policies. We will dissect how superficial marketing phrases like 'girl boss' can mask ongoing, systemic wage disparities and labor exploitation. Together, we will reflect on how to distinguish genuine efforts toward gender equity from highly polished public relations campaigns.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Purplewashing
marketing or political strategy of using feminist rhetoric to burnish corporate reputations.
Many activists accused the fashion brand of purplewashing when they released 'feminist' shirts made in sweatshops.
Empowerment
the process of gaining power, confidence, and control over one's life.
True female empowerment requires equal pay, affordable childcare, and corporate representation.
Exploitation
the action or fact of treating someone unfairly in order to benefit from their work.
The fast fashion industry relies on the extreme exploitation of female workers overseas.
Instrumentalise
to use someone or something as a tool or instrument to achieve a selfish end.
Politicians often instrumentalise women's safety to pass harsh immigration laws.
Superficial
existing or occurring only on the surface; not deep, thorough, or sincere.
Changing the corporate banner to purple is a superficial gesture if the gender wage gap remains.
Rhetoric
persuasive or impressive language, which is often lacking in sincerity or substance.
We must look past the company's progressive rhetoric and examine their actual hiring practices.
Subvert
to undermine, corrupt, or reverse the power and values of an established movement.
Corporate interests often subvert radical protests by turning them into harmless marketing campaigns.
Matriarchy
a social system or form of government ruled primarily by women.
Anthropologists study whether true matriarchy has ever existed in historical civilizations.
Disparity
a great, unfair difference, especially relating to pay, wealth, or social status.
The significant pay disparity between male and female executives must be resolved.
Sincerity
the quality of being free from pretense, deceit, or hypocrisy.
The CEO's sincerity was questioned when she cut maternal leave benefits.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • What does the term 'girl boss' mean to you, and do you find it empowering or patronizing?
  • How can we ensure that gender equality is treated as a fundamental right rather than a marketing trend?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
Questions
What is the central purpose of purplewashing in modern business?
★ Have you ever noticed feminist slogans used to sell cosmetics or fashion?
How does corporate marketing redefine the meaning of female empowerment?
★ When in your life have you felt a genuine sense of empowerment?
Why is the exploitation of low-wage female workers so often ignored in feminist branding?
★ Do you check if the brands you buy have fair labor conditions for women?
How do state authorities instrumentalise gender equality to restrict other human rights?
★ Have you ever seen politicians use women's rights to justify harsh policies?
What makes a corporate diversity report merely superficial?
★ What structural changes would you like to see for women in your workplace?
Why does progressive rhetoric lose its impact when it is not backed by action?
★ How do you react when you hear public figures talk about equality?
How does commercial culture subvert the revolutionary goals of gender justice?
★ Do you feel that commercialized feminism has helped or harmed the cause?
How does a family dynamic change when it adopts elements of a matriarchy?
★ Who was the most influential female figure in your childhood?
What psychological toll does a persistent wage disparity take on professional women?
★ Have you ever experienced or witnessed a significant disparity in your field?
Why is absolute sincerity so hard to find in institutional advocacy?
★ How do you personally express your support for gender equality?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

Situational exercise: Analyze a hypothetical campaign from a bank promoting women's financial independence while maintaining a large gender wage gap internally.

🟢 Round 2 — Dilemmas / Future▲
Debate
If a corporation uses purplewashing but donates a significant portion of its profits to women's shelters, its marketing is ethically acceptable.
If we do not legally mandate equal representation on board directories, structural gender disparities will never disappear.
If the instrumentalisation of feminism helps normalize gender equality in conservative countries, we should accept it as a positive tool.
If a consumer chooses to buy a product labeled 'empowering' despite knowing it was made through exploitation, they share moral guilt.
If we completely replace patriarchy with matriarchy, society will become inherently more peaceful and cooperative.
If corporate rhetoric is highly successful at changing public opinion, we should focus on the positive outcome rather than the lack of sincerity.
If we do not challenge superficial diversity goals, major industries will continue to subvert real systemic reform.
If governments penalize companies for wage disparity, it will lead to corporations hiding their payroll data through complex structures.
If the language of women's safety is continuously used to justify xenophobic laws, feminist groups must publicly distance themselves from state policies.
If modern branding relies entirely on co-opting social movements, the public will eventually develop total apathy toward all moral causes.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
They are making purplewash to sell clothes → They are practicing purplewashing / resorting to purplewashing (Collocation: we 'practice', 'resort to', or 'criticize' purplewashing, rather than 'making' it)
The company wants to empowerment women → The company wants to empower women (Grammar: 'empowerment' is a noun; the verb form is 'empower')
There is a big disparities of payment → There is a significant pay disparity (Noun collocation: 'pay disparity' is the standard professional phrase, and 'disparity' should match the singular article 'a')
