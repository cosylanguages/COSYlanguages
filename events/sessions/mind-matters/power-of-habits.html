Atomic Habits Info 📖

Perhaps our habits are the invisible architecture of our daily lives, built stone by stone without our conscious consent. Our lives are largely defined by the small, repetitive actions we perform every day—our habits. This session examines the science of habit formation and the powerful "cue-habituation-gratification" loop that drives our behavior. We will discuss practical techniques for breaking bad habits and building new, positive ones that lead to long-term success.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Willpower
the ability to control your own thoughts and actions.
It takes a lot of willpower to quit smoking.
Consistency
the quality of always behaving in the same way.
Consistency is key to long-term success.
Discipline
the practice of training people to obey rules or a code of behavior.
Maintaining daily habituation requires discipline.
Trigger
an event that causes a particular action or process.
For many, coffee is a trigger to start working.
Gratification
pleasure or satisfaction, especially when gained from the satisfaction of a desire.
Our modern brain is constantly seeking immediate gratification through digital notifications.
Incremental
relating to or denoting an increase or addition.
Small, incremental changes lead to massive results.
Habituation
the psychological process of automatically behaving through repeating actions.
Through quiet habituation, tasks that once required effort become entirely second nature.
Automation
making a process automatic.
The goal of a habit is the automation of behavior.
Compound
to make up or constitute; to increase by adding to.
Small habits compound over time.
Subconscious
concerning the part of the mind of which one is not fully aware.
Most of our daily habits are subconscious.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Habit Loops
  • Perhaps our habits are the invisible architecture of our daily lives, built stone by stone without our consent. How much of who we are is just a repeating pattern of behavior?
  • Is it harder to start a good habit or break a bad one?
🔵 Round 1 — Deep Discussion▲
Questions
What is the biggest habit you've successfully changed, especially when considering the role of Willpower?
★ How did you feel before and after the change?
Why do most New Year's resolutions fail by February, especially when considering the role of Consistency?
★ Have you ever made a resolution that actually stuck?
How do "triggers" work in our daily environment, especially when considering the role of Discipline?
★ What is a common trigger that leads you to a bad habit?
What is the role of "gratification" in forming a lasting habit, especially when considering the role of Trigger?
★ Do you gratify yourself when you stick to a new habituation?
How long does it actually take to form a new habit, especially when considering the role of Gratification?
★ Do you find it takes more or less than the famous 21 days?
Is "motivation" reliable for long-term change, especially when considering the role of Incremental?
★ What do you do on days when you have zero motivation?
How can we use "habit stacking" to build a new habituation?
★ Can you think of a habit you could "stack" onto your morning coffee?
Does our identity influence our habits, or do our habits form our identity, especially when considering the role of Automation?
★ Do you describe yourself by your habits (e.g., "I am a runner")?
How can we make bad habits "invisible" in our environment, especially when considering the role of Compound?
★ Have you ever tried hiding your phone to focus better?
What is the "one-minute rule" for starting something difficult, especially when considering the role of Subconscious?
★ Is there something you could start today using only one minute?

🟢 Round 2 — Conditional Scenarios▲
Debate
If you had started your most important habit five years earlier, how would your life be different today, especially when considering the role of Willpower?
If schools taught habit management as a core subject, would the younger generation be more productive, especially when considering the role of Consistency?
If you were able to delete any bad habit instantly with a brain implant, would you use it, especially when considering the role of Discipline?
If you hadn't been so disciplined this morning, what tasks would you have failed to complete, especially when considering the role of Trigger?
If AI managed all of our daily habituations, would we lose our sense of personal responsibility, especially when considering the role of Gratification?
If we lived in a society where "willpower" didn't exist, how would people achieve their goals, especially when considering the role of Incremental?
If you were to change your environment completely, which of your habits would survive, especially when considering the role of Habituation?
If governments didn't use "nudges" to influence health habits, would we be less healthy, especially when considering the role of Automation?
If you had a limited "willpower budget" every day, how would you prioritize your morning, especially when considering the role of Compound?
If you were too disciplined, do you think you would miss out on the spontaneous joy of life, especially when considering the role of Subconscious?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I stopped to smoke→I stopped smoking(Stop + gerund)
I suggest to start small→I suggest starting small(Suggest + gerund)
It depends of my willpower→It depends on my willpower(Depends on)
I'm not used to wake up early→I'm not used to waking up early(Be used to + -ing)
I was sympathizing with her struggles → I was empathizing with her struggles / showed deep empathy (Linguistic register: 'sympathize' suggests feeling pity, while 'empathize' implies a deeper psychological understanding and shared feelings)
