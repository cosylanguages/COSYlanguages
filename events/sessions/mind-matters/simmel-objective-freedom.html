Theme

Sociology of the Stranger

We unpack Georg Simmel's famous theory of 'The Stranger' and the quiet liberation of entering new communities. This session explores how being unbound by local history or tradition allows us to shed the heavy weight of community judgment. We will discuss how being an outsider can help us discover a pristine, authentic self.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Authenticity
the quality of being genuine, real, or true to oneself.
Living with absolute authenticity brings a profound sense of inner peace.
Conformity
behavior in accordance with socially accepted conventions or standards.
The school rules demanded strict conformity, leaving no room for self-expression.
Autonomy
the right or condition of self-government; personal independence.
The young artist struggled to gain complete personal and financial autonomy.
Suppression
the act of keeping something from happening or being revealed.
The suppression of emotional needs often leads to intense internal conflict.
Rebellion
the action or process of resisting authority, control, or convention.
Her teenage rebellion was a necessary step toward discovering her true self.
Subconscious
the part of the mind which is not fully aware but influences actions.
Our childhood experiences are deeply buried in our subconscious.
Self-discovery
the process of acquiring insight into one's own character and potential.
Her travels in Asia were part of a lifelong journey of self-discovery.
Persona
the aspect of someone's character that is presented to or perceived by others.
He adopted a confident public persona to hide his deep-seated insecurities.
Individuality
the quality or character of a particular person that distinguishes them from others.
The curriculum should celebrate the unique individuality of each student.
Dignity
the state or quality of being worthy of honor or respect.
Every human being has the right to live their life with basic dignity.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • Have you ever traveled to a place where absolutely nobody knew your name?
  • Is it easier to be completely honest with a stranger than with a close family member?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
How does the pursuit of Authenticity influence our perception of success and failure in modern life?
★ When was the last time you had to confront Authenticity in your own personal journey?
Is it possible to find true balance when Conformity is either completely absent or excessive?
★ How comfortable are you with allowing Conformity to guide your daily decisions?
Why do we often struggle to cultivate Autonomy even when we know it is beneficial?
★ Have you ever felt a deep sense of conflict regarding Autonomy in a relationship?
How does Suppression reshape our emotional landscape during times of intense change?
★ Can you share a moment from your childhood where Suppression felt completely natural?
Can Rebellion act as a silent shield against the demands of social conformity?
★ How do you personally nurture Rebellion when you feel emotionally exhausted?
How do our childhood experiences shape our relationship with Subconscious as adults?
★ Have you ever had to choose between social conformity and personal Subconscious?
In what ways does Self-discovery challenge the rigid boundaries of logical thinking?
★ Who in your life best embodies the essence of Self-discovery, and what have they taught you?
How does embracing Persona change the way we handle interpersonal conflict?
★ In what area of your life do you feel Persona is most desperately needed right now?
Is Individuality a quality of quiet solitude, or does it thrive in noisy communities?
★ How has your personal understanding of Individuality shifted over the last five years?
What is the most profound lesson that Dignity can teach us about our own nature?
★ If you had to describe your personal experience with Dignity in one word, what would it be?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

A comparative reflection on our habits and self-perceptions.

🟢 Round 2 — Dilemmas / Future▲
If you could artificially enhance your capacity for Authenticity, would you do it even if it cost you some peace of mind?
If society were to completely lose its appreciation for Conformity, what kind of dystopian reality would we live in?
If you had to choose between a life of deep Autonomy and one of pure, effortless bliss, which would you pick?
If a pill could guarantee a perfect level of Suppression but made it impossible to feel intense joy, would you take it?
If children were never taught the concept of Rebellion, would they develop it naturally on their own as adults?
If we were to build a futuristic city governed entirely by Subconscious, would it be a utopia or an unbearable prison?
If your closest companion lacked any form of Self-discovery, would you still be able to maintain a deep bond with them?
If you had to sacrifice a significant portion of your wealth to double your Persona, would you consider it a worthy trade?
If you were given a chance to see your future level of Individuality, would you look, or would you rather let the future unfold naturally?
If a machine could perfectly replicate and share your inner experience of Dignity with others, would you allow it?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
He have went to Paris → He has gone to Paris (Present perfect third-person singular 'has' + past participle 'gone'.)
We discussed about the problem → We discussed the problem / had a discussion about the problem ('Discuss' is transitive and takes no preposition, or use 'have a discussion about'.)
I am agree with you → I agree with you (Use 'agree' directly as a verb rather than using it with the auxiliary 'am'.)
