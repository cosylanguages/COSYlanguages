Read Psychology Today 📖

Perhaps our minds are not perfectly rational instruments, but storytellers constantly twisting facts to suit our fragile egos. Human decision-making is often influenced by cognitive biases—mental shortcuts that can lead to errors in judgment. This session explores the most common types of bias and how they affect our perceptions of reality and other people. We will discuss strategies for recognizing these patterns in our own thinking to make more objective and rational choices.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Cognitive Bias
systematic pattern of deviation from rationality.
Confirmation bias is a very common cognitive bias.
Confirmation Bias
favoring information that confirms existing beliefs.
He only reads news that agrees with him because of confirmation bias.
Hindsight Bias
tendency to see events as predictable after they have happened.
"I knew it all along" is a classic sign of hindsight bias.
Anchor
to rely too heavily on the first piece of information offered.
The initial price anchors the negotiation.
Heuristic
mental shortcut that allows people to solve problems quickly.
Heuristics are useful but can lead to cognitive biases.
Rationality
the quality of being based on or in accordance with reason or logic.
Emotions can often cloud our rationality.
Inference
a conclusion reached on the basis of evidence and reasoning.
His inference was based on several observed patterns.
Correlation
a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things.
Correlation does not always imply causation.
Perception
the way in which something is regarded, understood, or interpreted.
Our perception of risk is often biased by recent events.
Dissonance
lack of agreement; inconsistency.
Cognitive dissonance occurs when we hold two conflicting beliefs.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Spot the Bias
  • Perhaps our minds are not perfectly rational instruments, but storytellers constantly twisting facts to suit our fragile egos. Why is it so comforting to believe we are always objective?
  • Do you think you are more or less biased than the average person?
🔵 Round 1 — Deep Discussion▲
Questions
Can you recall a time you were sure you were right, but later realized you only looked at one side, especially when considering the role of Cognitive Bias?
★ Have you ever tried to intentionally look for information that contradicts your opinion?
Why do we often think "I knew it all along" after an event happens, especially when considering the role of Confirmation Bias?
★ Can you think of a recent event where you felt hindsight bias?
How do marketers use anchoring to influence our spending, especially when considering the role of Hindsight Bias?
★ Do you usually check prices from different sources before buying something expensive?
Why is it so hard to change someone's mind once they have formed an opinion, especially when considering the role of Anchor?
★ Has anyone ever successfully changed your mind about a deeply held belief?
Do you think social media algorithms increase our cognitive biases, especially when considering the role of Heuristic?
★ Have you noticed your "feed" only showing you things you already like or believe?
What is the "halo effect" and how does it affect our judgment of people, especially when considering the role of Rationality?
★ Have you ever assumed someone was smart just because they were well-dressed or attractive?
How does the "availability heuristic" affect our perception of safety, especially when considering the role of Inference?
★ Are you more afraid of things that are frequently in the news, even if they are statistically rare?
Can we ever truly "eliminate" bias, or can we only manage it, especially when considering the role of Correlation?
★ What is your personal strategy for making objective decisions?
Why is "groupthink" dangerous in professional environments, especially when considering the role of Perception?
★ Have you ever stayed silent in a meeting because everyone else agreed on a bad idea?
Which bias do you think is the most dangerous in the modern world, especially when considering the role of Dissonance?
★ Do you think education can help people overcome these mental traps?

🟢 Round 2 — Conditional Scenarios▲
Debate
If you hadn't been influenced by confirmation bias, would you have made the same choice in your last major purchase? (How does Cognitive Bias affect this statement?)
If you were in charge of a large company, how would you ensure that hiring was completely unbiased? (How does Confirmation Bias affect this statement?)
If you had known about the "halo effect" years ago, would you have judged someone differently? (How does Hindsight Bias affect this statement?)
If you were to lose your ability to make mental shortcuts, how would your daily life change? (How does Anchor affect this statement?)
If social media didn't exist, would our political biases be less extreme? (How does Heuristic affect this statement?)
If you had realized your own biases sooner, would your career path have been different? (How does Rationality affect this statement?)
If an AI were perfectly objective, would you trust it to make your medical decisions? (How does Inference affect this statement?)
If we didn't have any biases, would we be able to make quick decisions in an emergency? (How does Correlation affect this statement?)
If you were to move to a completely different culture, which of your current biases would survive? (How does Perception affect this statement?)
If you had to pick one bias to eliminate from the world, which would it be? (How does Dissonance affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I suggest to read the article→I suggest reading the article(Suggest + -ing)
The brain trick us→The brain tricks us(Depends on)
I was sympathizing with her struggles → I was empathizing with her struggles / showed deep empathy (Linguistic register: 'sympathize' suggests feeling pity, while 'empathize' implies a deeper psychological understanding and shared feelings)
We discussed about the psychological impact → We discussed the psychological impact / had a discussion about the psychological impact (Underlined prepositional error. Better collocation: 'discuss' is transitive and takes no preposition, or use 'have a discussion about')
I have 25 years old→I am 25 years old(Age with 'to be')
