Theme

Brain & Narrative Empathy

We explore how reading masterpieces of world literature fundamentally rewires our brain's neural pathways. This session adopts a deeply reflective, gentle, and psychological voice to analyze narrative empathy, mental visualization, and emotional resonance. We will explore how books act as mirrors for our hidden feelings.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Curiosity
a strong desire to know or learn something new.
Childhood curiosity should be nurtured and encouraged by every teacher.
Ignorance
lack of knowledge, education, or awareness about something.
Admitting your own ignorance is the first step toward true wisdom.
Perception
the ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses.
Her perception of the situation was influenced by her past experiences.
Awareness
knowledge or perception of a situation or fact.
The campaign aims to raise public awareness about mental health issues.
Humility
a modest or low view of one's own importance; humbleness.
The great scientist accepted his mistakes with admirable humility.
Insight
a deep, intuitive understanding of a person or a complex thing.
The article provides valuable insight into the causes of economic crises.
Skepticism
a skeptical attitude; doubt as to the truth of something.
Healthy skepticism is necessary when reading unverified news online.
Intellect
the faculty of reasoning and understanding objectively.
She possessed a brilliant intellect and a sharp, analytical mind.
Perspective
a particular attitude toward or way of regarding something.
Traveling abroad gives you a completely new perspective on your own culture.
Illusion
a false idea or belief; a deceptive appearance or impression.
He clung to the sweet illusion that everything would return to normal.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • What book has had the deepest psychological impact on how you see yourself?
  • Do you feel a deep, physical reaction in your body when reading tense scenes?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
How does the pursuit of Curiosity influence our perception of success and failure in modern life?
★ When was the last time you had to confront Curiosity in your own personal journey?
Is it possible to find true balance when Ignorance is either completely absent or excessive?
★ How comfortable are you with allowing Ignorance to guide your daily decisions?
Why do we often struggle to cultivate Perception even when we know it is beneficial?
★ Have you ever felt a deep sense of conflict regarding Perception in a relationship?
How does Awareness reshape our emotional landscape during times of intense change?
★ Can you share a moment from your childhood where Awareness felt completely natural?
Can Humility act as a silent shield against the demands of social conformity?
★ How do you personally nurture Humility when you feel emotionally exhausted?
How do our childhood experiences shape our relationship with Insight as adults?
★ Have you ever had to choose between social conformity and personal Insight?
In what ways does Skepticism challenge the rigid boundaries of logical thinking?
★ Who in your life best embodies the essence of Skepticism, and what have they taught you?
How does embracing Intellect change the way we handle interpersonal conflict?
★ In what area of your life do you feel Intellect is most desperately needed right now?
Is Perspective a quality of quiet solitude, or does it thrive in noisy communities?
★ How has your personal understanding of Perspective shifted over the last five years?
What is the most profound lesson that Illusion can teach us about our own nature?
★ If you had to describe your personal experience with Illusion in one word, what would it be?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

A comparative reflection on our habits and self-perceptions.

🟢 Round 2 — Dilemmas / Future▲
In the future, technology will enable us to digitize and share our experience of Curiosity directly. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ How would you personally adapt if Curiosity became entirely obsolete in tomorrow's world?
The rapid speed of modern society will eventually make Ignorance an obsolete concept. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ Would you be willing to use technology to artificially boost your capacity for Ignorance?
Future educational systems will prioritize the cultivation of Perception over traditional academic skills. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ How do you want future generations of your family to remember your relationship with Perception?
As artificial intelligence advances, machines will exhibit a superior capacity for Awareness compared to humans. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ If you could design a futuristic school, how would you teach the value of Awareness to children?
In the coming decades, Humility will become a highly commercialized luxury commodity. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ Do you fear a future where Humility is fully managed or monitored by digital algorithms?
The survival of human civilization will depend on our collective ability to practice Insight. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ How do you personally prepare for a future where Insight is increasingly rare?
Future generations will look back at our current understanding of Skepticism with deep skepticism. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ If you could send a single message about Skepticism to people living 100 years in the future, what would it be?
Global digital connectivity will make true Intellect incredibly difficult to maintain in the future. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ Would you choose to live in a futuristic utopia that completely guarantees Intellect at the cost of your freedom?
In the future, social status will be determined by a person's capacity for Perspective rather than wealth. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ What role do you hope Perspective will play in your own personal future over the next decade?
A world fully governed by logic will have no space left for the mystery of Illusion. — Do you agree or disagree?
★ If a machine could perfectly replicate your experience of Illusion, would you still value it?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
He have went to Paris → He has gone to Paris (Present perfect third-person singular 'has' + past participle 'gone'.)
We discussed about the problem → We discussed the problem / had a discussion about the problem ('Discuss' is transitive and takes no preposition, or use 'have a discussion about'.)
I am agree with you → I agree with you (Use 'agree' directly as a verb rather than using it with the auxiliary 'am'.)
