The refrigerator is perhaps the most essential yet overlooked appliance in the modern home, fundamentally changing how we shop, eat, and live. This session examines the impact of food preservation on our daily routines and the global supply chain. We will imagine the logistical and health challenges of a life without artificial cooling and how it would return us to more traditional, local ways of consuming food.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Preserve
to keep food fresh for a longer time.
A fridge helps preserve meat and vegetables.
Spoil
to go bad and become unsafe to eat.
Milk spoils quickly without cooling.
Storage
keeping things in a safe place.
The fridge is used for food storage.
Freshness
the quality of being fresh and not old.
The fridge maintains the freshness of food.
Waste
to use or throw away something unnecessarily.
Without a fridge, people waste more food.
Leftovers
food that remains after a meal.
I store leftovers in the fridge.
Temperature
how hot or cold something is.
The fridge keeps a low temperature.
Convenience
something that makes life easier.
A fridge is a great convenience in daily life.
Shop daily
to buy food every day.
Without a fridge, people must shop daily.
Hygiene
cleanliness and health safety.
A fridge improves food hygiene.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Refrigerator Check
  • What is the oldest thing currently in your fridge?
  • What is the one item that is always in your fridge, no matter what?
🔵 Round 1 — Life With▲
Questions
How does a fridge help you preserve food and organize your daily life?
★ What foods do you always try to preserve in your fridge?
Why is a fridge essential for storing perishable food today? (Consider the role of Spoil here.)
★ Which perishable food would you miss the most if you couldn't keep it cold?
How does proper food storage save time, money and effort?
★ Do you think your fridge helps you save money?
How does a fridge help maintain the freshness of food throughout the week?
★ Which foods taste much better when they stay fresh?
In what ways does refrigeration reduce food waste?
★ Have you ever managed to avoid waste thanks to your fridge?
Why are leftovers useful in a busy adult lifestyle?
★ How often do you eat leftovers instead of cooking again?
How has the convenience of refrigeration changed modern family life? (Consider the role of Temperature here.)
★ What everyday convenience would disappear without your fridge?
Why do many people stock up on groceries instead of shopping every day? (Consider the role of Convenience here.)
★ How often do you stock up on food?
Which methods of food preservation would be much less practical than refrigeration today? (Consider the role of Shop daily here.)
★ Have you ever tried preserving food yourself?
Do you think the fridge is one of the most important inventions in the modern home? (Consider the role of Hygiene here.)
★ Could you imagine your current lifestyle without owning one?

🟢 Round 2 — Life Without▲
Dilemmas
If refrigerators had never existed, what methods would people use to preserve food, and which do you think would become the most popular?
Without refrigeration, many perishable foods would disappear from supermarkets. Which new dishes or eating habits do you think would replace them? (How does Spoil affect this statement?)
If food storage were much more difficult, how would your job, schedule or daily routine change?
How would people keep food freshness during long journeys, holidays or business trips?
Without refrigerators, would restaurants become more expensive because they couldn't keep leftovers or large amounts of ingredients? (How does Waste affect this statement?)
If people couldn't stock up on groceries, how would cities, neighbourhoods and local businesses look different today? (How does Leftovers affect this statement?)
Without refrigeration, would people produce more or less food waste, or would they simply become much better at planning? (How does Temperature affect this statement?)
Without the convenience of a fridge, what modern invention do you think would have developed much faster to solve the problem?
Imagine that refrigerators had never been invented because electricity was too expensive. How different would modern society be? (How does Shop daily affect this statement?)
If the entire world suddenly lost every refrigerator tomorrow, how long do you think society would need to adapt, and what would surprise people the most? (How does Hygiene affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I put the leftovers in the fridge since 3 days→I've had the leftovers in the fridge for 3 days(Present Perfect)
I suggest to buy a bigger fridge→I suggest buying a bigger fridge(Suggest + -ing)
The food have a good freshness→The food is fresh / stays fresh(Freshness as a state)
There is many foods in the fridge→There is a lot of food in the fridge(Uncountable food)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
