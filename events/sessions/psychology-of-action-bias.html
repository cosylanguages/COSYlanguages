📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Action bias
The tendency to do something rather than wait, even when waiting may be better.
Action bias made him reply immediately without thinking.
Uncertainty
A situation where the outcome is unknown.
People often feel uncomfortable with uncertainty.
Impulse
A sudden desire to act without careful thought.
Buying things online can sometimes be an impulse.
Decisive
Able to make decisions quickly and confidently.
Good leaders are often seen as decisive.
Regret
A feeling of disappointment about a past decision.
She felt regret after accepting the offer too quickly.
Gather information
To collect facts before making a decision.
It is wise to gather information before changing jobs.
Weigh the pros and cons
To consider advantages and disadvantages.
He weighed the pros and cons before moving abroad.
Patience
The ability to wait calmly.
Patience can prevent costly mistakes.
Pressure
Influence that pushes someone to act in a certain way.
Social pressure often affects our decisions.
Hasty
Done too quickly and without enough thought.
A hasty decision can create bigger problems later.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲

Share a time when you felt you had to do something immediately, even if it wasn't strictly necessary. How did it feel?

🔵 Round 1 — Analysis (Living With Constant Pressure to Act)▲
Questions
Why do human minds seem to treat uncertainty as something that must be immediately resolved through action?
Personal question: Do you feel uncomfortable when you don’t act quickly in uncertain situations?
Has modern life made action bias feel like a normal way of thinking rather than a bias at all?
Personal question: When do you notice yourself acting without reflection?
Why does doing nothing often feel psychologically heavier than making a hasty decision?
Personal question: When was the last time you chose action just to escape waiting?
What does it say about people that they often prefer an incorrect answer over prolonged uncertainty?
Personal question: Do you prefer a wrong decision or no decision?
How has constant digital stimulation changed our ability to gather information slowly and calmly?
Personal question: Do you still enjoy slow thinking, or does it feel unnatural now?
Why does the need to appear decisive persist even when decisions are clearly complex and uncertain?
Personal question: Have you ever pretended to be more certain than you were?
Can impulse sometimes feel like truth, even when it bypasses careful thinking?
Personal question: Do your impulses usually feel right in the moment?
Why do people often experience regret not because they acted, but because they acted too quickly?
Personal question: Do you regret actions more than inactions, or the opposite?
Has modern life made us more afraid of delay than of mistakes?
Personal question: What feels worse to you: waiting too long or acting too fast?
If action bias is built into modern life, can true patience still exist as a deliberate choice?
Personal question: When do you manage to slow your thinking down?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲
Role-play: One person has a major life decision to make (e.g., moving to a new city). The other person represents the 'Action Bias' voice, urging them to decide today. Practice using 'hasty', 'decisive', and 'gather information'.
🟢 Round 2 — Conditional Futures (Action and Inaction)▲
Debate
Would you rather live in a world where every decision must be made instantly, or a world where every decision forces a long period of waiting before any action is allowed?
Personal question: Which world would change your personality more?
If humans evolved to feel no discomfort in uncertainty, would action bias disappear or simply take a new form?
Personal question: Would you want to live without discomfort from uncertainty?
If you had never acted on your first impulse in your life, what kind of person would you have become?
Personal question: Can you think of an impulse that shaped your life?
Would you rather live with perfect memory of every regret, or perfect confidence in every decision you make?
Personal question: Which would be harder to live with emotionally?
If society began rewarding patience more than speed, would people become wiser or more hesitant?
Personal question: Do you think you would change your behaviour?
If every impulsive decision you made had been delayed by 24 hours, how different would your life story be?
Personal question: Which part of your life would change the most?
Would you rather always know when you are about to make a mistake, or never feel regret after making one?
Personal question: Which feeling guides you more: foresight or regret?
If AI could remove action bias from human thinking entirely, would people become freer or less human?
Personal question: Would you accept such a change in human nature?
Would you rather live in a world where nothing urgent ever happens, or a world where everything feels urgent all the time?
Personal question: Which environment feels more like today’s world?
If your life had been shaped by waiting instead of acting quickly, what kind of life would you have lived?
Personal question: Do you think waiting has ever changed your life path significantly?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I make a hasty decision.
I made a hasty decision.
Use past tense when referring to a specific event in the past.
I feel regret for it.
I regret it / I feel regret about it.
'Regret' can be a verb or a noun. 'About' is more common with the noun.
