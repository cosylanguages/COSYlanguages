Full Article 📖

Action bias is our tendency to favor action over inaction, even when there is no evidence that acting will lead to a better outcome. This session examines why we feel the pressure to be "decisive" and how this bias affects our professional and personal choices. We will discuss techniques for developing strategic patience in a world that demands speed.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Action bias
the tendency to do something rather than wait, even when waiting may be better.
Action bias made him reply immediately without thinking.
Uncertainty
a situation where the outcome is unknown.
People often feel uncomfortable with uncertainty.
Impulse
a sudden desire to act without careful thought.
Buying things online can sometimes be an impulse.
Decisive
able to make decisions quickly and confidently.
Good leaders are often seen as decisive.
Regret
a feeling of disappointment about a past decision.
She felt regret after accepting the offer too quickly.
Gather information
to collect facts before making a decision.
It is wise to gather information before changing jobs.
Weigh the pros and cons
to consider advantages and disadvantages.
He weighed the pros and cons before moving abroad.
Patience
the ability to wait calmly.
Patience can prevent costly mistakes.
Pressure
influence that pushes someone to act in a certain way.
Social pressure often affects our decisions.
Hasty
done too quickly and without enough thought.
A hasty decision can create bigger problems later.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Share a time when you felt you had to do something immediately.
  • How did it feel? Was it strictly necessary?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
Questions
Why do human minds seem to treat uncertainty as something that must be immediately resolved through action?
★ Do you feel uncomfortable when you don’t act quickly in uncertain situations?
Has modern life made action bias feel like a normal way of thinking rather than a bias at all?
★ When do you notice yourself acting without reflection?
Why does doing nothing often feel psychologically heavier than making a hasty decision?
★ When was the last time you chose action just to escape waiting?
What does it say about people that they often prefer an incorrect answer over prolonged uncertainty?
★ Do you prefer a wrong decision or no decision?
How has constant digital stimulation changed our ability to gather information slowly and calmly?
★ Do you still enjoy slow thinking, or does it feel unnatural now?
Why does the need to appear decisive persist even when decisions are clearly complex and uncertain?
★ Have you ever pretended to be more certain than you were?
Can impulse sometimes feel like truth, even when it bypasses careful thinking?
★ Do your impulses usually feel right in the moment?
Why do people often experience regret not because they acted, but because they acted too quickly?
★ Do you regret actions more than inactions, or the opposite?
Has modern life made us more afraid of delay than of mistakes?
★ What feels worse to you: waiting too long or acting too fast?
If action bias is built into modern life, can true patience still exist as a deliberate choice?
★ When do you manage to slow your thinking down?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲
Role-play: One person has a major life decision to make (e.g., moving to a new city). The other person represents the 'Action Bias' voice, urging them to decide today. Practice using 'hasty', 'decisive', and 'gather information'.
🟢 Round 2 — Dilemmas / Future▲
Debate
If you were forced to wait 24 hours before making any decision, how would your daily productivity change?
If humans hadn't evolved to fear uncertainty, would we still have survived as a species?
If you had never acted on your first impulse, would you be in the same profession you are in today?
If society rewarded patience more than speed, would the global economy be more or less stable?
If you could always know when you were about to make a hasty mistake, would you feel more confident or more anxious?
If you were to start your life over with zero action bias, which major mistake would you have avoided?
If AI managed all of our urgent decisions, would humans become wiser or more passive?
If you had to choose between never feeling regret and always making correct decisions, which would be more valuable?
If your career had been defined by waiting instead of acting, how would your level of achievement look today?
If you were to live in a world where nothing was ever urgent, how would you find the motivation to act?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I make a hasty decision.
I made a hasty decision.
Use past tense when referring to a specific event in the past.
I feel regret for it.
I regret it / I feel regret about it.
'Regret' can be a verb or a noun. 'About' is more common with the noun.
