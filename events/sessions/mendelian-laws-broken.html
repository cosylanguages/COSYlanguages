📖 Session Vocabulary

Epigenetics
changes in gene activity that do not alter DNA itself
Example: Epigenetics explains why identical DNA can produce different traits.
Inheritance
the passing of traits from parents to children
Example: Eye color is an example of genetic inheritance.
Trait
a characteristic of a living being
Example: Height is a heritable trait.
DNA methylation
a chemical process that can switch genes on or off
Example: DNA methylation can silence certain genes.
Allele
different versions of the same gene
Example: You inherit one allele from each parent.
Mendelian laws
classic rules of inheritance discovered by Gregor Mendel
Example: Mendelian laws explain dominant and recessive traits.
Non-Mendelian inheritance
inheritance that does not follow Mendel’s rules
Example: Some traits show non-Mendelian inheritance patterns.
Gene expression
the process of turning a gene into a working product
Example: Gene expression determines how cells function.
Paramutation
when one gene copy changes the activity of another copy
Example: Paramutation can silence or activate genes in offspring.
Environmental influence
effects of surroundings on biology or development
Example: Environmental influence can affect gene activity.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Rules of Biology
  • What did you learn about genetics in school?
  • Do you think science is mostly "fixed" or constantly changing?
🔵 Round 1 — Scientific Depth▲
Analysis
1. The study shows that some forms of inheritance do not follow Mendelian laws. What does this suggest about how fixed genetic rules really are?
★ Have you ever heard something in science that surprised you?
2. How does epigenetics change the way we understand heredity beyond DNA alone?
★ Do you think school science reflects real-life science well?
3. Why is DNA methylation important for controlling gene activity?
★ Do you think small biological processes can have big effects on life?
4. What does it mean when a trait appears in offspring without being present in either parent?
★ Have you noticed any traits in yourself that differ from your family?
5. How does this research challenge traditional ideas about alleles and inheritance?
★ Do you think science should be updated often in schools?
6. Why is the discovery of non-Mendelian inheritance in mammals important?
★ Do you enjoy learning about new scientific discoveries?
7. What role does gene expression play in differences between organisms with similar DNA?
★ Do you think people understand how the human body works well enough?
8. How could paramutation change the way we think about genetic inheritance?
★ Would you say biology is more mysterious or more predictable?
9. How might environmental influence affect genetic and epigenetic processes?
★ What environmental factor has influenced your life the most?
10. What does this study suggest about the relationship between genes, epigenetics, and evolution?
★ Do you think humans will change significantly in the future?
