This session explores the timeless romance classic, Breakfast at Tiffany's. We will analyze the iconic performance of Audrey Hepburn and the complex psychology of Holly Golightly. Students will focus on classic mid-century slang, character attachments, and Manhattan social climbing vocabulary.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Social climber
a person who attempts to gain a higher social position or status.
People often labeled Holly as a social climber in Manhattan's elite circles.
High spirits
a state of lively, cheerful, and optimistic energy.
Despite her financial worries, she was always in high spirits during parties.
Rattled
worried, flustered, or nervous due to an unexpected event.
He was visibly rattled when his past secret came to light.
Eccentric
unconventional, slightly strange, or quirky in behavior.
Her eccentric habits, like keeping a cat with no name, fascinated her neighbor.
Elegant
graceful and stylish in appearance or manner.
She wore an elegant black dress with a classic pearl necklace.
Loneliness
sadness because one has no friends or company.
The vastness of New York City intensified her inner feelings of loneliness.
Superficially
in a way that relates only to the surface or appearance.
Superficially, their glamorous lifestyle seemed absolutely perfect.
Attachment
an extra part that is joined to something, or an emotional bond.
She avoided forming any deep romantic attachment to keep her freedom.
Charming
pleasant, attractive, and extremely likable.
He was a charming young writer who quickly gained her trust.
Flee
to run away from a place or situation of danger.
She wanted to flee from her complex problems by traveling to Brazil.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Share your impressions of New York City in retro films.
  • Have you ever visited a jewelry store just to enjoy the peaceful atmosphere?
  • What makes old Hollywood movies feel so uniquely magical?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Explore complex emotional issues, humor, and relationships.
What is the difference between a highly ambitious entrepreneur and a calculating social climber?
★ Have you ever encountered a social climber in your professional circles?
How does Holly Golightly manage to stay in high spirits when facing financial difficulties?
★ What brings you into high spirits on a gloomy morning?
What specific unexpected situations leave you feeling completely rattled?
★ When did you last feel flustered or rattled?
Why are eccentric behaviors often viewed as highly endearing in romantic comedies?
★ Do you have any eccentric habits that define your personality?
What elements make a physical space or cafe feel truly elegant and cosy?
★ What is the most elegant restaurant you have ever visited?
How does a metropolitan city like New York amplify a person's inner loneliness?
★ How do you cope with transient feelings of loneliness?
Is it fair to judge a film's artistic worth superficially based on its age and colors?
★ Have you ever superficially misjudged a person?
Why do some people actively avoid forming any emotional attachment to pets or places?
★ Do you form a rapid attachment to new environments?
What qualities make a conversation partner feel genuinely charming rather than pretentious?
★ Who is the most charming person you know?
Under what circumstances is it acceptable to flee from your problems rather than face them?
★ Have you ever wished to flee to a quiet island for a weekend?

Predictions
Critically evaluate these statements regarding modern social patterns.
In the future, automated matchmakers will prevent us from forming any painful romantic attachment.
To stay in high spirits, we must disconnect completely from social news feeds.
People will eventually flee from metropolitan cities to escape digital surveillance.
Being extremely eccentric will become a standardized marketing tactic for social media influencers.
Modern cities will design specific architectural hubs to combat public loneliness.

A charming artificial intelligence chatbot will soon replace traditional human friendships.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
She was in very high spirit during the party→She was in very high spirits during the party(The idiom 'high spirits' is always plural)
The city made her to feel loneliness→The city made her feel loneliness(The causative verb 'make' takes a bare infinitive without 'to')
She decided to flee to Brazil for escape her past→She decided to flee to Brazil to escape her past(Use 'to' + infinitive instead of 'for' + infinitive to express purpose)
