Quote

"Knowledge isn't free. You have to pay attention."

This session explores Richard Feynman's quote about the cost of learning. We will talk about how we can focus better when we study. We will also share what we do to remember new words at home.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Knowledge
information, skills, and understanding that you get through learning.
Reading books is an excellent way to get new knowledge.
Attention
listening or looking carefully at someone or something.
Please pay attention to the teacher when she explains.
Free
costing no money; not needing payment.
This English website has a lot of free exercises.
Focus
to give all your attention to one thing.
I need to focus on my vocabulary list.
Remember
to keep in your mind; not forget.
Do you remember the meaning of the word 'focus'?
Learn
to get knowledge or skill in a new subject.
I want to learn how to speak Spanish.
Simple
easy to understand or do; not complex.
This grammar rule is very simple to understand.
Mind
the part of a person that thinks, feels, and remembers.
Meditation helps to calm the busy mind.
Share
to tell other people about your ideas or feelings.
I would like to share my study tips with you.
Difficult
hard to do, understand, or deal with; not easy.
This exercise is too difficult for me.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to paying attention.
  • Is it easy for you to study when you are tired?
  • What do you do when you cannot focus at work or school?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Simple discussion about Feynman's quote.
What is the best way to get knowledge?
★ What are you studying to get more knowledge now?
Why must we pay attention to the teacher?
★ Is it easy for you to pay attention for one hour?
What is the difference between a free course and a paid course?
★ Have you ever done a free online course?
How do you stay focused when you study?
★ Where do you go when you need to focus on your homework?
Why is it hard to remember new words?
★ Do you have a special way to remember vocabulary?
How can adults learn a second language faster?
★ Why do you want to learn English?
Do you prefer simple exercises or complex ones?
★ Do you find English grammar simple or complex?
How does learning change our mind?
★ What is on your mind today?
Why is it good to share our ideas with others?
★ Would you like to share your favorite book with the group?
What makes English spelling so difficult?
★ What is the most difficult part of English for you?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I want to study more hard→I want to study harder(Comparative form of 'hard' is 'harder')
I am study every day→I study every day(Simple present structure)
It depend of your focus→It depends on your focus(Depends + on, singular s)
