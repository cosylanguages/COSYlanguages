While strolling down the pristine, symmetrical aisles of a modern supermarket, I noticed a separate, heavily discounted crate of twisted, asymmetrical carrots and lumpy oranges. In a society that obsessively filters, edits, and standardizes every aspect of our lives, why have we imposed these same unrealistic beauty standards on the natural food we eat? I couldn't help but wonder... By rejecting these perfectly nutritious but "ugly" fruits and vegetables, are we starving our souls of the authentic, chaotic beauty of nature?

📝 Carrie's Diary: Rhetorical Reflection

"If we can't accept a double-legged carrot or a bruised orange, how can we ever expect to accept each other's complex imperfections? Has our search for food symmetry turned us into superficial consumers of nature?"

📖 Session Vocabulary

Carrie's Columnist Vocabulary Guidelines:
- Calibrate vocabulary for B1-C2 level (elevated, sophisticated, but accessible and natural in conversation).
- Exactly 10 units are required. Each entry must have a word, definition, and example ending with a period.
- Ensure no "Example: " prefix inside `.vocab-example` text elements.
Symmetrical
made up of exactly similar parts facing each other or around an axis.
Supermarkets demand symmetrical produce because they look better on display.
Asymmetrical
having parts or aspects that are not equal or equivalent; lopsided.
An asymmetrical tomato tastes exactly the same as its perfectly round counterpart.
Anti-waste
actions or campaigns aimed at preventing the discard of usable resources.
The anti-waste campaign by Intermarché saved hundreds of tons of perfectly good fruits.
Calibrate
mark, standardize, or adjust against a precise standard or scale.
Sorting machines calibrate food sizes so that every potato matches the exact same box.
Pristine
in its original condition; unspoiled or clean.
The supermarket's pristine displays can make shoppers highly critical of minor natural flaws.
Superficial
existing or occurring at or on the surface; shallow.
Rejecting food based on minor scars is a superficial behavior that feeds global hunger.
Deformed
misshapen or distorted out of its natural shape.
They renamed deformed vegetables as "ugly" to make them sound charming and lovable.
Unrealistic
not representing or showing things as they are in real life.
Imposing unrealistic standards on farm produce leads to massive ecological waste.
Discard
get rid of someone or something as no longer useful or desirable.
Farms are often forced to discard nearly forty percent of their harvest.
Nutritious
efficient as food; containing highly beneficial substances.
These lumpy carrots are actually more nutritious than the ones grown with heavy chemical fertilizers.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
Linguistic feedback with mistake-highlight classes to accentuate errors precisely without full sentence strike-through.
This vegetable is more cheap than the other. → This vegetable is cheaper than the other. (Comparative grading: One-syllable adjectives form their comparative with the suffix '-er', not with the modifier 'more')
They throw too much foods away. → They throw too much food away / too many food items away. (Noun countability: 'food' is generally an uncountable noun when referring to quantity, so it should be singular and paired with 'much')
We discussed about the topic. → We discussed the topic. (Do not use 'about' after the transitive verb 'discuss'.)
