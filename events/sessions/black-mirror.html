This session explores the groundbreaking British speculative fiction anthology series, Black Mirror. We will analyze how near-future technology interacts with human psychology and modern societal behaviors. Students will expand their conversational vocabulary concerning user ratings, privacy, and digital obsession.

📖 Session Vocabulary

1. Generic Discussion Vocabulary

Obsession
an idea or thought that continually preoccupies a person's mind.
Our current obsession with user ratings is affecting real-world mental health.
Dystopia
an imagined state or society with great suffering or injustice.
The episode presents a chilling dystopia where popularity determines housing access.
Speculative
engaged in, expressing, or based on conjecture.
Speculative fiction helps us evaluate where our current technological choices lead.
Technology
the application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes.
Near-future technology has the power to both unite and isolate us.
Surveillance
close observation, especially of a suspected spy or criminal.
State-wide surveillance limits our freedom of personal expression.
Addicted
physically and mentally dependent on a particular activity.
Many teenagers feel completely addicted to their smartphone notifications.
Consequence
a result or effect of an action or condition.
The sudden consequence of her low rating was social exclusion.
Manipulate
to handle or control in a devious manner.
Companies use psychological triggers to manipulate online user behavior.
Identity
the fact of being who or what a person or thing is.
Our digital identity has become more important than our physical presence.
Outcast
a person who has been rejected by society.
He was labeled a social outcast after refusing to use the rating app.

2. Authentic Series Quotes & Expressions

Opt-in
to choose to participate in a system or activity.
Users must explicitly opt-in to the new tracking protocol.
Social rating
a system measuring value based on digital feedback.
Her social rating dropped below four points, causing panic.
Glitch
a sudden, temporary malfunction in software or hardware.
A minor glitch in the neural implant caused visual hallucinations.
System failure
a state where an entire digital framework stops working.
The automated city suffered a complete system failure.
Five-star rating
the highest score of approval in digital networks.
She smiled artificially to earn a five-star rating from her flight attendant.
Block
to digitally restrict someone from contacting you.
He chose to block his father, leaving him as a gray silhouette.
Cookie
a digital replica of a human consciousness stored in a device.
The digital cookie was programmed to manage her smart home preferences.
Unsubscribe
to choose to stop receiving digital notifications.
You cannot unsubscribe from the mandatory system feed.
Virtual reality
a computer-generated simulation of a 3D environment.
They escaped into virtual reality to experience life without boundaries.
Feed the trolls
an idiom meaning to encourage provocative online behavior.
Do not reply to those aggressive comments, don't feed the trolls.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Introduce your personal feelings about smartphone screen time and tracking.
  • Are we too dependent on digital validation like likes and follows?
  • Do you read user reviews before visiting a new restaurant or booking a hotel?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
In-depth discussion about tech ethics, digital isolation, and social limits.
How does our current obsession with social status modify our genuine relationships?
★ Have you felt pressured to look happy on your social accounts?
What are the key elements that turn a technology-driven city into a terrifying dystopia?
★ What is the most dystopian concept you have seen in sci-fi media?
Why is speculative fiction highly valuable for analyzing the dangers of artificial intelligence?
★ What are your main concerns regarding AI tools?
How has modern technology altered our understanding of family privacy?
★ Do you have a smart device in your bedroom?
Is high levels of corporate surveillance justifiable for protecting intellectual property?
★ Would you allow your boss to track your keystrokes?
What are the physiological signs that prove someone is completely addicted to screens?
★ How do you limit your screen time on weekends?
What is the long-term consequence of relying solely on algorithms for finding a partner?
★ Do you trust matching algorithms?
How do digital platforms manipulate our attention loops to show us more ads?
★ Have you bought a product due to targeted ads recently?
How does maintaining a separate online identity affect your physical-world relationships?
★ Is your digital persona identical to your true self?
Why does refusing to use digital services quickly make you a social outcast?
★ Could you live for a week without a smartphone?

🟢 Round 2 — Future Hypotheses & Technological Dilemmas▲
Predictions
Critically evaluate these statements regarding future technological trends.
In the future, a person's digital identity will completely replace their physical ID cards.
Refusing to submit to continuous government surveillance will make you an international outcast.
Most children will attend school entirely in technology-enabled virtual reality rooms.
We should expect a global consequence where human interaction is heavily restricted by social credit algorithms.
It is impossible to build a successful career today without allowing platforms to manipulate your personal data.
The definition of a dystopia will shift from physical wars to complete algorithmic control of information.
Our obsession with efficiency is making us lose the beauty of physical accidents and surprises.
Medical clinics will treat digital addictions exactly like drug or alcohol dependency.
We should gird ourselves for a future where neural links are mandatory for most high-paying corporate roles.
Speculative fiction movies will eventually be banned because they make the public overly skeptical of progress.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
Many people is addicted to their phones→Many people are addicted to their phones(The noun 'people' is plural, so it requires 'are')
The technology affects how do we communicate→Technology affects how we communicate(Do not use auxiliary 'do' in indirect embedded questions, and exclude the definite article for general abstract concepts)
I blocked him because he sent always spam→I blocked him because he always sent spam(Place frequency adverbs before the main verb)
