Diwali History 📖

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is one of the most significant and vibrant celebrations in Indian culture, symbolizing the spiritual victory of light over darkness and good over evil. This session explores the rich mythology, diverse traditions, and the deep sense of hospitality that define this five-day festival. We will discuss the evolution of Diwali in a globalized world and the balance between maintaining ancient customs and modern environmental concerns.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Diyas
small oil lamps made of clay, traditionally used during Diwali.
Lighting diyas symbolizes the victory of light over darkness.
Rangoli
an art form in which patterns are created on the floor using materials such as colored rice, flour, or flower petals.
Creating a beautiful rangoli at the entrance of the house welcomes guests and good luck.
Prosperity
the state of being successful, especially in material or financial terms.
Diwali is a time to pray for prosperity and health for the coming year.
Festivity
the celebration of something in a joyful and exuberant way.
The streets were filled with music and general festivity.
Firecrackers
small explosive devices used primarily to produce a large amount of noise.
Although popular, firecrackers are being limited in some cities due to pollution.
Hospitality
the friendly and generous reception and entertainment of guests.
Diwali is famous for the warm hospitality shown by families to their neighbors.
Mythology
a collection of myths, especially one belonging to a particular religious or cultural tradition.
According to Hindu mythology, Diwali celebrates the return of Lord Rama.
Feast
a large meal, typically a celebratory one.
Families gather for a grand feast featuring traditional sweets and savory dishes.
Cleanse
to make something thoroughly clean.
Many people cleanse and decorate their homes before the festival starts.
Symbolism
the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities.
The symbolism of light in Diwali is universal across many different faiths.

🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Diwali is celebrated by several different religions, not just Hindus. (Consider the role of Diyas here.)
★ Are there any multi-religious festivals in your country?
The festival usually lasts for five days. (Consider the role of Rangoli here.)
★ Do you have any long multi-day celebrations in your culture?
Cleaning the house is a mandatory preparation for Diwali. (Consider the role of Prosperity here.)
★ Do you usually clean your home before a big holiday?
Sweets (Mithai) are the most common gift exchanged during the festival. (Consider the role of Festivity here.)
★ What is the most common gift in your culture?
Diwali is timed according to the lunar calendar, so the date changes every year. (Consider the role of Firecrackers here.)
★ Do you prefer fixed dates or lunar dates for holidays?
Wearing new clothes is a tradition for many families. (Consider the role of Hospitality here.)
★ Do you usually buy new clothes for special occasions?
Gambling (with small amounts) is sometimes a traditional activity during Diwali. (Consider the role of Mythology here.)
★ Are there any "unusual" traditions in your family?
Rangoli patterns are intended to welcome Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth. (Consider the role of Feast here.)
★ How do you welcome guests into your home?
Diwali is also known as the "Deepavali". (Consider the role of Cleanse here.)
★ Does your favorite holiday have more than one name?
It is the most widely celebrated festival in India and its diaspora. (Consider the role of Symbolism here.)
★ Which festival brings the most people together in your country?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Agree or Disagree
Traditional firecrackers will be banned worldwide to protect the environment. (Consider the role of Diyas here.)
★ Would you miss fireworks if they were replaced by silent laser shows?
Large cultural festivals like Diwali will eventually lose their spiritual meaning. (Consider the role of Rangoli here.)
★ Do you think gift-giving is the most important part of a holiday?
Atheists will find more meaning in religious festivals than religious people do. (Consider the role of Prosperity here.)
★ Have you ever celebrated a holiday from a culture that wasn't your own?
Major cultural holidays will be recognized as public holidays in every country. (Consider the role of Festivity here.)
★ Should your country have more public holidays for different religions?
Digital greetings will completely replace the tradition of visiting family. (Consider the role of Firecrackers here.)
★ Do you prefer a physical card or a digital message?
Mass-produced plastic decorations will eventually be banned by law. (Consider the role of Hospitality here.)
★ Do you value items more if you know they were made by hand?
"Cleansing the home" will become an exclusively digital activity in the future. (Consider the role of Mythology here.)
★ How often do you "cleanse" your digital life?
"Fusion" holidays will be seen as more authentic than "pure" traditional ones. (Consider the role of Feast here.)
★ What is your favorite example of cultural fusion?
The symbol of the "candle" will become obsolete as we move toward high-tech lighting. (Consider the role of Cleanse here.)
★ Why do humans find hope in the symbol of a candle?
Global society will adopt extreme hospitality as its core survival value. (Consider the role of Symbolism here.)
★ What is the most hospitable thing a stranger has ever done for you?
