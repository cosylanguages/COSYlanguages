Theme

Work-Life Balance, Holidays, and Well-being

Holidays and vacations are traditionally viewed as essential for rest and mental recovery, yet many modern professionals struggle to completely disconnect from work. This session compares a life enriched by regular vacations with a life driven by constant, uninterrupted productivity. We will discuss the psychological costs of never taking a break, and how changing our resting habits can change our entire life perspective.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Disconnect
break the connection of; detach oneself from work or digital devices.
It is incredibly hard to completely disconnect from office emails during a summer holiday.
Restorative
having the ability to restore health, strength, or well-being.
A quick 3-day weekend trip can be highly restorative if you leave your laptop at home.
Leisure
use of free time for enjoyment; free time.
Having quiet hours of leisure is crucial for maintaining creative problem-solving skills.
Burnout
physical or mental collapse caused by overwork or stress.
Without regular holidays, employees can easily fall into severe professional burnout.
Productivity
the state or quality of being productive; efficiency.
Taking a relaxing vacation actually boosts your long-term productivity and focus.
Rejuvenate
make someone look or feel younger, fresher, or more lively.
A silent walk through a green forest can completely rejuvenate a tired mind.
Obligation
the condition of being morally or legally bound to do something.
We feel a strong social obligation to reply to work messages even on weekends.
Guilt
a cognitive or emotional experience that occurs when a person believes that they have compromised their own standards of conduct.
Many professionals experience intense guilt when they are not actively working or studying.
Detrimental
tending to cause harm or damage.
Working non-stop without regular breaks is extremely detrimental to your physical health.
Well-being
the state of being comfortable, healthy, or happy.
Prioritizing your emotional well-being is more important than achieving corporate success.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up — The Life Comparison▲
When you have a week of holidays, do you travel to a busy tourist destination, or do you stay at home to sleep?
  • Have you ever spent a full week without checking any work emails or messages?
  • Do you feel like you are wasting your time when you are doing absolutely nothing?
🔵 Round 1 — My Life With Holidays▲
Discussion
How does taking regular holidays help rejuvenate your focus and daily productivity?
★ What is the most restorative holiday you have ever taken?
Why is it so difficult for modern employees to completely disconnect from work obligations during vacation?
★ Have you ever had to answer an urgent work call while resting on a beach?
How do you spend your hours of leisure to maximize your emotional well-being?
★ What is your absolute favorite way to spend a completely free Sunday?
Does having a pre-planned holiday itinerary reduce the stress of travel logistics?
★ Are you a spontaneous traveler, or do you need a minute-by-minute plan?
How can we support colleagues who are struggling with heavy workloads while we are away?
★ Have you ever felt guilty about leaving your coworkers with your work while on vacation?
What is your favorite vacation activity to completely clear your mind?
★ Do you prefer active sports or lying by the pool when you want to relax?
Why do we feel more creative and inspired when we return from a new destination?
★ Have you ever come up with a brilliant idea or made a major life decision while on holiday?
How do holidays with family differ from holidays spent entirely alone?
★ Would you rather travel alone for a week or with a large group of relatives?
Do you believe that travel is the best way to invest your personal savings?
★ What is the most expensive trip you have ever paid for, and was it worth it?
What is the most beautiful sunrise you have ever watched while on holiday?
★ Are you an early bird who wakes up for sunrises, or do you prefer the sunset?

🟢 Round 2 — My Life Without Holidays▲
Debate
Living a life without any holidays is extremely detrimental to your long-term mental sanity and health.
★ Have you ever gone a very long time without a holiday, and how did it affect you?
Constant productivity without breaks is a recipe for severe emotional burnout.
★ What are the first warning signs of burnout that you notice in yourself?
People who never take vacations often experience intense subconscious guilt when they try to rest.
★ Do you feel guilty when you spend a whole day doing absolutely nothing productive?
A workplace that discourages vacations is violating its basic moral obligation to support employee health.
★ Have you ever worked for an employer who made it difficult for you to take time off?
True creative inspiration cannot survive in a life driven entirely by non-stop physical and mental labor.
★ Where do you usually get your most creative ideas—during work or during rest?
We can achieve a perfect state of daily rest through mindfulness without needing to ever travel.
★ Do you practice mindfulness, meditation, or any form of daily mental rest?
The pressure to constantly document our travels on social media ruins the genuine purpose of a holiday.
★ Do you post a lot of photos during your holidays, or do you stay offline?
Some people use workaholism as an ultimate shield to escape from their personal relationship problems.
★ Have you ever used work or a busy schedule to avoid dealing with a personal issue?
To permanently eliminate career fatigue, we must design a daily life that we don't need to escape from.
★ What is one small change you could make to your daily routine to feel less tired?
A life spent entirely on leisure and travel is ultimately empty and lacks deep, satisfying purpose.
★ If you won the lottery, would you stop working completely and travel forever?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I am not comfortable to disconnect from work. → I am not comfortable disconnecting from work. (Gerund construction: the adjective phrase 'comfortable' is followed by a gerund 'disconnecting'.)
We discussed about holidays. → We discussed holidays. (Transitive verb: do not use the preposition 'about' after the verb 'discuss'.)
I prefer stay at home rather than travel. → I prefer staying at home to traveling / I would rather stay at home than travel. (Preference structures: 'prefer' is followed by gerunds, whereas 'would rather' takes bare infinitives.)
