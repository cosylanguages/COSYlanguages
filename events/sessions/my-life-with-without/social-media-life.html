Social media has fundamentally rewired our social interactions, offering unprecedented connectivity while simultaneously introducing new psychological pressures. This session explores the balance between the digital world and real-life experiences, focusing on the impact of constant scrolling and online comparison on our mental health. We will imagine what our lives might look like if we returned to a pre-digital social landscape and how we can maintain an authentic identity in an algorithmic age.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Doomscrolling
spending excessive time scrolling through bad news online.
I spent the whole evening doomscrolling instead of sleeping.
Detox
a period of time in which one abstains from unhealthy behaviors.
A weekend digital detox helped me feel more present.
Validation
recognition or affirmation that a person is worthwhile.
Many people seek validation through likes on social media.
FOMO
Fear Of Missing Out.
Social media can often trigger a sense of FOMO.
Engagement
the degree to which people interact with content.
High engagement rates are important for influencers.
Influencer
a person with the ability to influence potential buyers.
She became a successful travel influencer on Instagram.
Anonymous
not identified by name; of unknown name.
Many people post anonymous comments online.
Echo Chamber
an environment where a person only encounters information that reinforces their own.
Social media can easily become an echo chamber.
Comparison
estimate of the similarities or dissimilarities between two things.
Constant comparison with others can lead to unhappiness.
Authentic
of undisputed origin; genuine.
It is difficult to be truly authentic on social media.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Digital World
  • How many apps do you check before breakfast?
  • Do you remember life before social media?
🔵 Round 1 — Life With▲
Questions
Have you ever caught yourself doomscrolling late at night when you should have been sleeping?
★ What was the hardest app to give up?
How has taking a voluntary digital detox influenced your mood and focus on daily tasks?
★ Do you think online connection is "good enough"?
Do many people rely on likes and comments for psychological validation in their personal lives?
★ Do you check the news more often than you should?
How does constant exposure to other people's exciting lives trigger a deep sense of FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)?
★ Have you ever met a real friend online?
How do algorithms optimize user engagement to keep us hooked on social media applications?
★ What is the first app you open when you are bored?
Do you believe that a popular influencer can offer genuine inspiration, or are they mostly focused on marketing?
★ What is one thing you would never post online?
Would you prefer to interact on a fully anonymous forum, or do you value knowing who you are talking to?
★ Does it feel like a waste of time or a form of rest?
How easily do social media algorithms trap users inside a polarized echo chamber of their own beliefs?
★ Which hobby have you neglected because of your phone?
Why does constant comparison with curated online profiles often lead to feelings of inadequacy?
★ Are you more confident or more careful when posting?
Is it actually possible to project a truly authentic identity in a highly aestheticized digital landscape?
★ Do you keep your phone out of the bedroom?

🟢 Round 2 — Life Without▲
Dilemmas
Without the urge for endless scrolling, would we completely eradicate the modern epidemic of late-night doomscrolling?
★ Who is the first person you would call or visit if you couldn't message them online?
If social media disappeared, would we still need to announce a formal digital detox to find peace of mind?
★ Have you ever felt bad about your own life after looking at someone's perfect social media profile?
If we stopped seeking online validation, would our self-esteem become more stable and self-reliant?
★ What is one hobby or activity you enjoy doing completely in private, without telling anyone?
Without constant status updates from friends, would the psychological pressure of FOMO completely dissolve?
★ How did you find your favorite band, book, or movie—was it recommended by a friend or an algorithm?
If software companies didn't measure success by active user engagement, how would the web look different?
★ Do you often see opinions online that make you genuinely angry or frustrated?
If everyone had to use their real identity instead of staying anonymous, would the internet be a kinder place?
★ Have you ever written a comment or post anonymously, and did it change how you behaved?
If we stopped sharing our daily movements, how would our definition of an anonymous, private life change?
★ What is one part of your life that you will absolutely never share on the internet?
If we eliminated the algorithmic echo chamber, would public political debates become more respectful?
★ Do you follow any digital influencers or online content creators for inspiration?
Without the endless opportunity for social comparison, would people feel more content with their daily lives?
★ Would you be willing to delete all your online accounts forever to protect your privacy?
Can we return to a more authentic way of communicating with others if we abandon social networks forever?
★ Do you remember what you used to do during your free time before you owned a smartphone?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I spend many time on phone→I spend a lot of time on my phone(Uncountable time)
He is an influencer since long time→He has been an influencer for a long time(Present Perfect + for)
The social media is dangerous→Social media is dangerous(Generalization)
I am interesting in his posts→I am interested in his posts(-ed vs -ing)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
