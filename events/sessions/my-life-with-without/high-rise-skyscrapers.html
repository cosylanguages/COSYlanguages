Focus

Skyscrapers

This session explores the practical and visual impact of skyscrapers on modern urban environments and our personal lives. We will discuss the experience of living or working in a towering high-rise building versus a traditional low-rise neighborhood. Participants will share stories of how vertical cities affect our daily stress levels and sense of community.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Skyscraper
a very tall building of many stories.
The city skyline is dominated by a newly built glass skyscraper.
High-rise
a multi-story building.
Living in a high-rise apartment offers a wonderful panoramic view of the harbor.
Panoramic
with a wide view surrounding an observer.
The rooftop restaurant offers a panoramic view of the mountain range.
Density
the degree of compactness of a substance or population.
High population density in cities requires vertical housing models.
Isolation ≠ Connection
the state of being separate from others ≠ a feeling of being linked or integrated.
His physical isolation made him crave any form of genuine human connection.
Urbanization
the process of making an area more urban.
Rapid urbanization has transformed sleepy villages into bustling high-rise cities.
Infrastructure
the basic physical and organizational structures needed for society.
High-rise neighborhoods require advanced water and waste infrastructure.
Community
a group of people living in the same place or having a particular characteristic in common.
Low-rise suburban neighborhoods often have a stronger sense of community.
Tranquility
the quality or state of being tranquil; calm.
He escaped the noisy city center to find some tranquility in the countryside.
Elevator
a platform or compartment housed in a shaft for raising and lowering people or things.
A fast-moving elevator is essential for any skyscraper resident.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to city heights.
  • Have you ever visited or lived in a high-rise apartment on a very high floor?
  • Do you feel dizzy or nervous when looking down from great heights?
🔵 Round 1 — My Life With▲
Narrative
Analyze your experiences with high-rise buildings and skyscrapers.
How does living in a high-rise apartment affect your daily routine?
★ What floor do you currently live on, and do you like it?
What are the advantages of having a panoramic view from a skyscraper?
★ What is the most beautiful urban view you have ever seen?
Does high population density make city life more exciting?
★ Do you prefer bustling urban environments or quiet, empty spaces?
Why do some people experience a feeling of isolation in giant high-rise blocks?
★ Have you ever felt lonely in a large city?
How does rapid urbanization transform local community structures?
★ Has your home city changed much since you were a child?
What are the challenges of managing water and power infrastructure in vertical cities?
★ Have you ever experienced a power outage in a high-rise building?
Why is it harder to build a strong neighborhood community in a skyscraper?
★ Do you know your neighbors' names in your current building?
Can we find any tranquility in modern vertical metropolis areas?
★ How do you find a quiet, tranquil moment during a busy work week?
How do modern, fast elevator systems make skyscrapers practical?
★ Have you ever been trapped inside a stuck elevator?
How does architectural design shape our mood and stress levels?
★ Does modern concrete architecture make you feel inspired or depressed?

🟢 Round 2 — My Life Without▲
Projections
Imagine a life in a low-rise city with no high-rise structures.
If skyscrapers were completely banned, how would urban housing rates change?
★ Would you be willing to pay higher rent to live in a low-rise house?
Without high-rise density, would we spend more time traveling to work?
★ How would a longer commute change your lifestyle?
Would a life closer to the ground make people feel more connected to nature?
★ Do you feel happier when you can step directly onto green grass?
Without vertical blocks, would suburban sprawl destroy natural green spaces?
★ Do you prefer protected forests or spacious suburbs?
Would city skies look more beautiful and natural without massive glass blocks?
★ What is your favorite time of day to look at the city sky?
If we built cities horizontally, would we rely more on bicycle infrastructure?
★ Would you ride a bicycle to work if there were no high-speed highways?
Without giant corporate towers, would we work primarily from home?
★ Do you feel more productive working in a home office?
Would human stress levels decrease if we eliminated massive vertical complexes?
★ Does crowded public transport make you feel highly stressed?
Without high-rise structures, would neighbor communities become stronger?
★ Do you think small town residents are friendlier than big city residents?
Can a modern, vertical civilization survive without relying on elevators and skyscrapers?
★ Which city do you think is the best model for a happy future?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I live in a high floor→I live on a high floor(Use preposition 'on' for floors)
The skyscrapers are beautifuls→Skyscrapers are beautiful(Adjectives do not have plural forms, omit 'the' for general plurals)
It depend of your city→It depends on your city(Depends + on preposition)
