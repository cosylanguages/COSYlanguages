🔵 Part 1 — Dialogue, Themes & Grammar (10 Items)▲
Screenplay Discussion
Deconstruct the character conversations and thematic developments. Actively practice these target terms and integrate the grammar focus Comparatives and Superlatives into your responses.
Look at how Princess Odette and Prince Derek feels in the world of a classic fairy tale kingdom and Swan Lake. How does childhood friendships change how they talk to the villainous Rothbart, Jean-Bob, and Speed? Use Comparatives and Superlatives to give them some advice.
★ If you were in a classic fairy tale kingdom and Swan Lake, how would you stay safe from childhood friendships?
Is the way Princess Odette and Prince Derek deals with devotion good or bad? How can we say what is right or wrong here using Comparatives and Superlatives?
★ Have you ever had a hard time where you had to choose devotion over a friend like the villainous Rothbart, Jean-Bob, and Speed?
How does the film show fairy tale logic in a classic fairy tale kingdom and Swan Lake? Does it look like our real world? Talk about it using Comparatives and Superlatives.
★ Does the story of fairy tale logic in the movie feel like real life to you?
Talk about Princess Odette and Prince Derek and the villainous Rothbart, Jean-Bob, and Speed. How does protagonist make their lives harder? Try using Comparatives and Superlatives.
★ If you had a problem about protagonist with the villainous Rothbart, Jean-Bob, and Speed, what would you do?
Find an important part in the film The Swan Princess where antagonist changes the whole story of defeating dark curses and proving true romantic devotion. Explain it using Comparatives and Superlatives.
★ Do you remember a time in your life when antagonist changed your own goals?
The characters in The Swan Princess use the word 'No offense'. In what scene do they say it to the villainous Rothbart, Jean-Bob, and Speed? Use Comparatives and Superlatives.
★ Have you or your friends ever used a word like 'No offense' in real life? Tell us about it using Comparatives and Superlatives.
How does using words like 'archnemesis' help us understand the characters in a classic fairy tale kingdom and Swan Lake? Discuss using Comparatives and Superlatives.
★ Do you like learning slang words like 'archnemesis', or do you prefer normal words? Share your ideas using Comparatives and Superlatives.
In The Swan Princess, the phrase 'vow' is used in a funny or tense scene between Princess Odette and Prince Derek and the villainous Rothbart, Jean-Bob, and Speed. Explain it using Comparatives and Superlatives.
★ Is there a word like 'vow' in your own language? How is it different?
The characters use 'wicked' to hide what they really think during defeating dark curses and proving true romantic devotion. What are they hiding? Explain using Comparatives and Superlatives.
★ When you talk, do you use local words like 'wicked' or normal, formal words?
What other interesting words or Comparatives and Superlatives did you notice in their conversations?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I have seen that movie yesterday→I saw that movie yesterdayUse the simple past for actions completed in the past.
She is more older than him→She is older than himDo not double comparatives; 'older' is already comparative.
He gave to her a beautiful book→He gave her a beautiful bookDirect object doesn't require 'to' after 'give' in English.
