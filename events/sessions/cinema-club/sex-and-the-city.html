🔵 Part 1 — Dialogue & Themes (10 Items)▲
Screenplay Discussion
Deconstruct the character conversations and thematic developments. Actively practice these target terms.
How does the concept of Modern friendship influence the choices of characters in the opening scenes of Sex and the City?
★ Have you ever experienced a similar situation where Modern friendship played a significant role?
In what ways does shopping culture drive the narrative conflict between the main characters of Sex and the City?
★ How would you react if you encountered the same dilemma regarding shopping culture in your own life?
How does the screenplay of Sex and the City use metropolitan love to challenge or subvert the audience's expectations?
★ Do you find metropolitan love to be a relatable theme in modern society, or is it unique to the world of the film?
Which pivotal character in Sex and the City is most defined by Narrative pacing, and how does this shape their destiny?
★ If you were in the protagonist's shoes, would you let Narrative pacing dictate your final decision?
How does Character arc symbolize the larger social, cultural, or political context depicted in Sex and the City?
★ What personal values do you associate with Character arc, and how do they compare to the film's perspective?
Can you highlight a specific dialogue in Sex and the City where Labels becomes the central point of tension?
★ Can you share a time when a conversation about Labels changed your mind about someone?
What are the immediate or long-term dramatic consequences of fabulous in the middle acts of Sex and the City?
★ Have you ever witnessed the unexpected consequences of fabulous in your professional or personal life?
How does the visual directing, lighting, or setting of Sex and the City enhance the emotional weight of gossip?
★ Does the emotional atmosphere of gossip in this scene resonate with your own memories or experiences?
In your opinion, does Sex and the City portray martini as a positive force of growth or as a destructive weakness?
★ Do you agree with how the film defines martini, or do you have a different personal interpretation?
How does the resolution of Sex and the City address, resolve, or leave open-ended the conflicts surrounding Pivotal moment?
★ How has watching Sex and the City changed your outlook on the importance or role of Pivotal moment?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
She is more older than him→She is older than himDo not double comparatives; 'older' is already comparative.
He gave to her a beautiful book→He gave her a beautiful bookDirect object doesn't require 'to' after 'give' in English.
I will resign if they skimp always on budget→I will resign if they always skimp on budgetFrequency adverbs like 'always' come before the main verb.
