🔵 Part 1 — Dialogue, Themes & Grammar (10 Items)▲
Screenplay Discussion
Deconstruct the character conversations and thematic developments. Actively practice these target terms and integrate the grammar focus Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity) into your responses.
Look at how Remy the talented rat feels in the world of a prestigious Parisian restaurant kitchen. How does passion change how they talk to Linguini, Colette, Gusteau, and food critic Anton Ego? Use Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity) to give them some advice.
★ If you were in a prestigious Parisian restaurant kitchen, how would you stay safe from passion?
Is the way Remy the talented rat deals with overcoming prejudice good or bad? How can we say what is right or wrong here using Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity)?
★ Have you ever had a hard time where you had to choose overcoming prejudice over a friend like Linguini, Colette, Gusteau, and food critic Anton Ego?
How does the film show culinary arts in a prestigious Parisian restaurant kitchen? Does it look like our real world? Talk about it using Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity).
★ Does the story of culinary arts in the movie feel like real life to you?
Talk about Remy the talented rat and Linguini, Colette, Gusteau, and food critic Anton Ego. How does paris make their lives harder? Try using Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity).
★ If you had a problem about paris with Linguini, Colette, Gusteau, and food critic Anton Ego, what would you do?
Find an important part in the film Ratatouille where character arc changes the whole story of overcoming societal prejudice and kitchen hierarchy to cook freely. Explain it using Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity).
★ Do you remember a time in your life when character arc changed your own goals?
The characters in Ratatouille use the word 'Chef'. In what scene do they say it to Linguini, Colette, Gusteau, and food critic Anton Ego? Use Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity).
★ Have you or your friends ever used a word like 'Chef' in real life? Tell us about it using Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity).
How does using words like 'gustatif' help us understand the characters in a prestigious Parisian restaurant kitchen? Discuss using Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity).
★ Do you like learning slang words like 'gustatif', or do you prefer normal words? Share your ideas using Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity).
In Ratatouille, the phrase 'soubrette' is used in a funny or tense scene between Remy the talented rat and Linguini, Colette, Gusteau, and food critic Anton Ego. Explain it using Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity).
★ Is there a word like 'soubrette' in your own language? How is it different?
The characters use 'ragoût' to hide what they really think during overcoming societal prejudice and kitchen hierarchy to cook freely. What are they hiding? Explain using Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity).
★ When you talk, do you use local words like 'ragoût' or normal, formal words?
What other interesting words or Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity) did you notice in their conversations?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I will resign if they skimp always on budget→I will resign if they always skimp on budgetFrequency adverbs like 'always' come before the main verb.
We are living here since two years→We have been living here for two yearsUse present perfect continuous with 'for' to express duration.
They don't allow her leave the house→They don't allow her to leave the houseUse 'allow someone to do something' construction.
