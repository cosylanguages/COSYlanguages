Welcome to our specialized Cinema Club session. This session is designed to explore the deep screen adaptation, cinematic storytelling, and screenplays of Ratatouille. Perfect your target language comprehension by analyzing character dialogs, tone markers, and core cinematic motifs. In this session, we pay special attention to Le Subjonctif Présent (Necessity), practicing how it is naturally used in authentic dialogue and scene interactions.

🎬 Aperçu du Thème: Ratatouille

Dans cette session, nous aborderons :

  • Protagoniste: Remy the talented rat
  • Cadre de l'histoire: a prestigious Parisian restaurant kitchen
  • Conflit principal: overcoming societal prejudice and kitchen hierarchy to cook freely

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I will resign if they skimp always on budget→I will resign if they always skimp on budgetFrequency adverbs like 'always' come before the main verb.
We are living here since two years→We have been living here for two yearsUse present perfect continuous with 'for' to express duration.
They don't allow her leave the house→They don't allow her to leave the houseUse 'allow someone to do something' construction.
