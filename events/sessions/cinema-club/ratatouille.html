🔵 Part 1 — Dialogue & Themes (10 Items)▲
Screenplay Discussion
Deconstruct the character conversations and thematic developments. Actively practice these target terms.
How does the concept of Passion influence the choices of characters in the opening scenes of Ratatouille?
★ Have you ever experienced a similar situation where Passion played a significant role?
In what ways does overcoming prejudice drive the narrative conflict between the main characters of Ratatouille?
★ How would you react if you encountered the same dilemma regarding overcoming prejudice in your own life?
How does the screenplay of Ratatouille use culinary arts to challenge or subvert the audience's expectations?
★ Do you find culinary arts to be a relatable theme in modern society, or is it unique to the world of the film?
Which pivotal character in Ratatouille is most defined by Paris, and how does this shape their destiny?
★ If you were in the protagonist's shoes, would you let Paris dictate your final decision?
How does Character arc symbolize the larger social, cultural, or political context depicted in Ratatouille?
★ What personal values do you associate with Character arc, and how do they compare to the film's perspective?
Can you highlight a specific dialogue in Ratatouille where Chef becomes the central point of tension?
★ Can you share a time when a conversation about Chef changed your mind about someone?
What are the immediate or long-term dramatic consequences of gustatif in the middle acts of Ratatouille?
★ Have you ever witnessed the unexpected consequences of gustatif in your professional or personal life?
How does the visual directing, lighting, or setting of Ratatouille enhance the emotional weight of soubrette?
★ Does the emotional atmosphere of soubrette in this scene resonate with your own memories or experiences?
In your opinion, does Ratatouille portray ragoût as a positive force of growth or as a destructive weakness?
★ Do you agree with how the film defines ragoût, or do you have a different personal interpretation?
How does the resolution of Ratatouille address, resolve, or leave open-ended the conflicts surrounding poubelle?
★ How has watching Ratatouille changed your outlook on the importance or role of poubelle?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I will resign if they skimp always on budget→I will resign if they always skimp on budgetFrequency adverbs like 'always' come before the main verb.
We are living here since two years→We have been living here for two yearsUse present perfect continuous with 'for' to express duration.
They don't allow her leave the house→They don't allow her to leave the houseUse 'allow someone to do something' construction.
