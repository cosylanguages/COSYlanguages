In our fast-paced, digital world, the role of grandparents in supporting children's mental health is more vital than ever. This session explores the unique emotional stability and historical perspective that elder family members provide to the younger generation. We will discuss the psychological benefits of intergenerational bonds and how they act as a buffer against modern social pressures.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Extended family
family members beyond parents and children, such as grandparents, aunts, and uncles.
In some cultures, the extended family plays a big role in raising children.
Mental health
a person's emotional and psychological well-being.
Good mental health helps people cope with stress.
Emotional resilience
the ability to recover from difficulties and challenges.
Sports can help children develop emotional resilience.
Achievement pressure
stress caused by expectations to succeed.
Many teenagers experience achievement pressure at school.
Sense of purpose
a feeling that your life has meaning and direction.
Volunteering gave her a stronger sense of purpose.
Empathy
the ability to understand another person's feelings.
Empathy helps us build better relationships.
Encouragement
support that gives someone confidence or hope.
A few words of encouragement can make a big difference.
Criticism
expressing disapproval or pointing out mistakes.
Constant criticism can damage a child's confidence.
Growth mindset
the belief that abilities can improve through effort and learning.
A growth mindset helps people learn from failures.
Cope with
to deal successfully with a difficult situation.
Children need support to cope with disappointment.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Role of Family
  • Did you grow up spending a lot of time with your grandparents or extended family?
  • In your culture, who is primarily responsible for a child's emotional support?
🔵 Round 1 — Theoretical Discussion▲
Questions
Why do you think researchers believe the extended family has become more important for children's mental health in modern society?
★ Do you think the role of the extended family has changed during your lifetime?
The article suggests that children need supportive relationships more than constant success. Why might strong relationships improve emotional resilience better than rewards or achievements?
★ Which relationship has helped you develop your own emotional resilience the most?
According to the psychologist, achievement pressure is increasing. Why do you think modern society places so much importance on achievement?
★ Have you ever experienced too much achievement pressure at work, university, or in another part of your life?
The article argues that children need a stronger sense of purpose than simply getting good grades. Why might a sense of purpose protect people's mental health?
★ What gives you the strongest sense of purpose today?
The psychologist recommends talking to children about kindness and empathy. Why might conversations be more powerful than simply giving instructions?
★ Who taught you the importance of empathy?
The article describes small moments of encouragement as "molecules of emotional health." Why do you think small positive interactions can have long-term effects?
★ What type of encouragement motivates you the most?
Why does frequent criticism often reduce motivation instead of improving behaviour?
★ When do you find criticism useful, and when does it become unhelpful?
The article recommends praising effort instead of talent to develop a growth mindset. Why might this approach lead to better learning?
★ Have you ever improved at something mainly because you kept trying rather than because you were naturally talented?
Why do supportive conversations help children cope with anxiety, disappointment, and failure more effectively than punishment?
★ Who usually helps you cope with stressful situations?
The article suggests that emotional support may be more valuable than practical advice. To what extent do you agree?
★ If you could give one piece of emotional support to the next generation, what would it be?

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Agree or Disagree
In the future, children who have strong relationships with caring adults from their extended family or community will have better mental health than those who rely only on their parents.
★ If you could become an important adult in a young person's life, what would you hope to teach them?
As technology becomes a bigger part of childhood, developing emotional resilience will become more important than achieving perfect grades.
★ Would you rather future generations become more emotionally resilient or more academically successful?
If society continues increasing achievement pressure, young people will become less willing to take creative risks.
★ Would you support fewer exams if it reduced achievement pressure?
Future schools should spend as much time helping students develop a sense of purpose as teaching traditional academic subjects.
★ If schools added one new life subject, what should it be?
Teaching empathy from an early age will become one of the most effective ways to improve society.
★ If people showed more empathy, what problem in your community might improve first?
In the future, regular encouragement from trusted adults will become more valuable than expensive educational resources.
★ Which has influenced your life more: someone's encouragement or formal education?
If parents, teachers, and employers reduced unnecessary criticism, future generations would become more confident and innovative.
★ Would you rather receive honest criticism immediately or encouraging feedback first?
Developing a growth mindset will become one of the most important skills for adapting to future technologies and careers.
★ If you had to completely change careers tomorrow, how would a growth mindset help you?
As the world becomes more unpredictable, people who learn to cope with failure from an early age will be happier and more successful adults.
★ If failure became less socially embarrassing, how do you think people would behave differently?
Even if AI becomes an excellent teacher, coach, and therapist, future children will still need real human relationships to protect their mental health.
★ Do you think AI could ever replace the emotional support provided by caring human relationships?
