How Sleep Works 📖

Sleep is a vital physiological process that remains one of the most intriguing mysteries of human biology. This session examines the science of circadian rhythms, REM sleep, and the critical role of rest in memory consolidation and emotional stability. We will discuss the impact of modern technology on our sleeping habits and the consequences of chronic sleep deprivation on our daily performance.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Circadian rhythm
the natural internal process that regulates the sleep-wake cycle.
Looking at blue light at night can disrupt your circadian rhythm.
Insomnia
habitual sleeplessness; inability to sleep.
Stress is one of the most common causes of temporary insomnia.
REM sleep
Rapid Eye Movement sleep; a unique phase of sleep characterized by random movement of the eyes and dreaming.
Most vivid dreaming occurs during the REM sleep stage.
Sleep hygiene
habits and practices that are conducive to sleeping well on a regular basis.
Good sleep hygiene includes keeping your bedroom cool and dark.
Cognitive function
mental processes including attention, memory, and decision-making.
Even one night of poor sleep can significantly impair cognitive function.
Melatonin
a hormone that your brain produces in response to darkness, helping with sleep.
Darkness triggers the release of melatonin in the brain.
Sleep deprivation
the condition of not having enough sleep.
Long-term sleep deprivation is linked to serious health problems.
Consolidation
the process by which the brain turns short-term memories into long-term ones.
Sleep is essential for the consolidation of information learned during the day.
Nap
a short sleep, especially during the day.
A 20-minute power nap can boost alertness and mood.
Drowsiness
a feeling of being sleepy and lethargic; sleepiness.
Excessive daytime drowsiness could be a sign of a sleep disorder.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Sleeping Brain
  • Are you a morning person (lark) or a night person (owl)?
  • What is the first thing you do if you can't fall asleep?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
Questions
Why do you think the brain needs to "shut down" every night?
★ How do you feel if you get less than 6 hours of sleep?
What is the most common "bad habit" that affects sleep quality?
★ Do you use your phone in bed?
How does sleep deprivation affect your emotional stability?
★ Are you more irritable or stressed when you are tired?
Why is REM sleep important for creativity and learning?
★ Do you often remember your dreams?
How has modern technology (screens, electricity) changed human sleep patterns?
★ Do you think our ancestors slept better than we do?
Does your sleep schedule change significantly during the weekend?
★ Do you try to "catch up" on sleep or maintain a consistent routine?
What are the benefits and drawbacks of taking daytime naps?
★ Have you ever woken up from a nap feeling worse than before?
How does caffeine influence your circadian rhythm?
★ What is the latest time in the day you can drink coffee?
Why is memory consolidation considered one of the primary functions of sleep?
★ Have you ever "slept on a problem" and found the solution in the morning?
If you could survive on only 2 hours of sleep with no side effects, would you do it?
★ What would you do with the extra 6 hours every day?

🟢 Round 2 — Dilemmas / Future▲
Debate
If schools and offices were to start at 10 AM, would the general level of public health improve within a single year?
If you had optimized your sleep environment years ago, how much more productive would you have been during your studies?
If a company were to fire you for not answering midnight emails, would you take legal action against them?
If you could replace 8 hours of normal sleep with 1 hour of "compressed" sleep via a brain implant, would you choose to have the surgery?
If napping were mandatory in your workplace, would your relationship with your colleagues become more or less professional?
If you hadn't used your phone before bed last night, would you have felt more alert during this discussion?
If scientists were able to prove that dreams predict the future, would you be more afraid to fall asleep at night?
If we didn't require any sleep at all, what new hobbies or skills would you have mastered by now?
If you were to record your dreams and show them to your friends, which secret part of your personality would they discover?
If society valued rest as much as work, would we have achieved more or less technological progress by 2026?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I suggest to sleep more→I suggest sleeping more(Suggest + gerund)
I have difficulty to fall asleep→I have difficulty falling asleep(Have difficulty + -ing)
I am used to wake up at 6am→I am used to waking up at 6am(Be used to + -ing)
It depends of my rhythm→It depends on my rhythm(Depends on)
I didn't slept well→I didn't sleep well(Past simple negative)
