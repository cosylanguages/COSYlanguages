📖 Session Vocabulary

Neural activity
electrical and chemical activity in the brain
Example: Neural activity increased before the fish moved toward another fish.
Social behavior
actions involving interaction with others
Example: Social behavior includes approaching and communicating with others.
Approach
to move toward someone or something
Example: The fish began to approach another fish in the tank.
Motivation
the internal reason for doing something
Example: Social motivation can influence how often we interact with others.
Predict
to say what will happen before it happens
Example: Scientists can sometimes predict behavior from brain signals.
Signal
a sign that gives information
Example: Brain signals can indicate an upcoming action.
Coordinate
to work together in an organized way
Example: Different brain areas coordinate during decision-making.
Individual
a single person or living organism
Example: Each individual fish showed different levels of social behavior.
Interaction
communication or action between beings
Example: Social interaction begins before actual movement.
Pre-decision state
brain activity before a choice is made
Example: The fish entered a pre-decision state before swimming.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Brain vs. Mind
  • Do you think you are in full control of every social move you make?
  • Have you ever felt your body react to a person before you even realized you were looking at them?
🔵 Round 1 — Understanding the Study▲
Questions
1. What kind of neural activity do you think happens in your brain when you decide to approach someone new?
★ Is it easy for you to approach strangers in social situations?
2. How does social behavior change when people have strong or weak motivation to interact?
★ What usually increases your motivation to talk to others?
3. What kind of signal do you think the brain sends before a social action?
★ Can you notice “signals” in your own behavior before acting?
4. Why is it important for different brain areas to coordinate during decision-making?
★ Do you prefer spontaneous or coordinated group activities?
5. Do you think scientists can really predict human social behavior from brain data?
★ Would you like your own behavior to be predictable?
6. How does interaction between individuals start before any words are spoken?
★ Do you feel nervous before social interaction begins?
7. Why might one individual show more social behavior than another?
★ Do you see yourself as very social or more quiet?
8. What does a pre-decision state tell us about how the brain works?
★ Have you ever acted before fully thinking?
9. How could stronger neural activity influence social confidence?
★ When do you feel most confident socially?
10. Do you think motivation or environment has more influence on social behavior?
★ What environment makes you more social?

🟢 Round 2 — Future Focus▲
Agree or Disagree
1. In the future, scientists will be able to identify a neural signature of a “social decision” before a person becomes aware of it.
★ Would you feel comfortable if science could detect your decisions before you notice them?
2. People may start to believe that free will is actually a decision pathway that begins before awareness.
★ Do you think your choices feel fully free, or partly automatic?
3. Technology could one day predict your next social interaction just from your brain’s neural activity.
★ Would you like or dislike having your social behavior predicted?
4. In the future, education might train people to recognize their own pre-decision state and pause it.
★ Would you want to “interrupt” your brain before acting socially?
5. Society may begin to treat social behavior as something shaped more by biology than personality.
★ Do you think you are more “biological” or “personality-driven” in social life?
6. Scientists will likely discover clear links between motivation and how strongly people show social drive.
★ What increases your motivation to connect with others?
7. Future AI systems may analyze your brain patterns and suggest the best moment to approach someone socially.
★ Would you trust AI advice in real social situations?
8. People might become more aware of their own unconscious processes, but still act on impulse.
★ Do you often follow impulse even when you understand your behavior?
9. Understanding neural activity will make human interaction more predictable—but possibly less spontaneous.
★ Do you prefer predictable or spontaneous conversations?
10. In the future, knowing someone’s social tendency could be used to shape relationships and friendships.
★ Would it be helpful or dangerous to choose friends based on brain data?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I'm not agree with this study→I don't agree with this study(Agree is a verb, not an adjective)
It depend of the brain→It depends on the brain(Depend takes 'on')
Before I decide it→Before I make a decision(More natural phrasing)
The brain make signals→The brain makes signals(Third person singular -s)
Humans are social beings, isn't it?→Humans are social beings, aren't they?(Question tag agreement)
