Homework should be banned

Level

Intermediate (B1)

Topic

Education / Work-Life Balance

📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Homework
school work that students do at home
Example: Many students spend an hour each evening doing homework.
Assignment
a task given by a teacher
Example: Our English assignment is due on Friday.
Workload
the amount of work someone has to do
Example: Some students have a heavy workload during exam season.
Balance
a healthy mix of different parts of life
Example: It is important to have a balance between study and free time.
Pressure
feelings of stress or worry
Example: Too much pressure can affect motivation.
Responsibility
something that you must do
Example: Homework can teach responsibility.
Concentrate
to give full attention to something
Example: It is easier to concentrate in a quiet place.
Achievement
something successful that you have done
Example: Finishing a difficult project was a great achievement.
Independent
able to do things without help
Example: Homework encourages students to become more independent.
Motivation
the desire to do something
Example: Positive feedback can increase motivation.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Homework Debate
  • Did you enjoy doing homework when you were at school?
  • How much time did you spend on assignments each day?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (10 Units)▲
Debate
1. A: Banning homework would give people more time to build stronger family relationships and a healthier balance between work, study, and personal life.
B: Keeping homework helps people develop responsibility, a skill that remains valuable throughout adulthood.
★ Which idea is closer to your own experience?
2. A: Without extra assignments, students could spend more time learning practical life skills such as cooking, budgeting, or repairing things.
B: Regular assignments prepare people for professional careers where deadlines are common.
★ What practical skill do you wish you had learned instead of doing homework?
3. A: A smaller workload outside school could improve mental health and increase long-term motivation.
B: A manageable workload teaches people how to organize their time before entering the workplace.
★ When do you feel most motivated to learn?
4. A: People learn better when they choose activities freely instead of completing compulsory homework.
B: Completing homework creates a sense of achievement that can build confidence.
★ What gives you a stronger feeling of achievement: finishing a task or learning something new?
5. A: Evenings without homework could encourage adults and children to exercise, travel, volunteer, or develop hobbies.
B: Homework encourages people to concentrate independently without constant guidance.
★ How do you usually spend your evenings?
6. A: Removing homework could reduce educational inequality because not everyone has the same support at home.
B: Homework allows independent learners to explore subjects beyond what is taught in class.
★ Do you enjoy learning independently?
7. A: Less academic pressure may help people become more creative and willing to take risks.
B: A reasonable amount of pressure prepares people for demanding careers and important responsibilities.
★ Do you work better with a little pressure or with none at all?
8. A: In a world full of AI, banning traditional homework would encourage learning through real experiences instead.
B: Homework helps people learn how to use AI responsibly rather than depending on it completely.
★ How has AI changed the way you learn?
9. A: Without daily assignments, people might become more curious because learning would feel less like an obligation.
B: Regular assignments help people build discipline, even when their motivation is low.
★ What usually motivates you to keep learning?
10. A: Banning homework could make education more attractive for adults who want to return to studying later in life.
B: Homework helps people keep learning habits that remain useful throughout their careers.
★ Would you ever return to formal education?

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (10 Units)▲
Debate
1. A: If homework disappeared completely, future generations might expect success without much personal responsibility.
B: If homework remained compulsory, people might begin to see learning as a lifelong punishment instead of an opportunity.
★ Which future seems more dangerous?
2. A: If schools stopped giving every assignment, companies might spend years teaching new employees basic self-management skills.
B: If schools kept increasing the number of assignments, fewer people might choose demanding careers because they would already feel exhausted before adulthood.
★ Which situation could have a bigger effect on the job market?
3. A: Without homework, AI assistants might become everyone's main teacher, reducing people's ability to concentrate on difficult problems alone.
B: If homework continued as usual, AI might simply complete most of it, making achievement less meaningful.
★ How should schools react to AI?
4. A: If homework disappeared, schools might replace it with more exams, creating even greater pressure.
B: If homework stayed important, exams and homework together might create an impossible workload.
★ Which causes you more stress: one big exam or many small tasks?
5. A: Without homework, families might argue more about how children should spend all their free time.
B: With homework every evening, families might slowly stop eating meals or travelling together.
★ Which family problem is easier to solve?
6. A: If homework were banned worldwide, expensive private tutoring could become much more popular, creating new educational inequality.
B: If homework continued everywhere, wealthy families might still have an advantage because they can afford more support.
★ Can education ever become completely fair?
7. A: Without regular homework, people might become less independent because they would always expect someone else to organize their learning.
B: Too much homework might make people so independent that they become less willing to cooperate with others.
★ Which skill is more important today: independence or teamwork?
8. A: If homework disappeared, universities might make courses much longer to reach the same level of achievement.
B: If homework became more demanding every year, fewer adults might decide to return to education later in life.
★ Would either future discourage you from studying?
9. A: Without homework, future politicians might promise "easy education" instead of high-quality education, simply because it sounds popular.
B: If homework remained traditional forever, education systems might become too slow to prepare people for new technologies.
★ Should education change quickly or slowly?
10. A: If homework were banned for the next fifty years, future generations might become excellent at finding quick answers but poor at solving complex problems without help.
B: If homework became an even bigger part of education, future adults might protect their free time so strongly that they would avoid learning anything after leaving school.
★ Which future would be harder for society to recover from?
