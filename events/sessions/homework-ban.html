Homework should be banned

The long-standing tradition of homework is facing increased scrutiny as educators and parents debate its true impact on learning and mental health. This session examines the arguments for and against banning mandatory assignments outside of school hours, focusing on the balance between academic achievement and personal growth. We will explore how removing homework might transform family dynamics, life skills development, and the overall educational experience.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Homework
school work that students do at home.
Many students spend an hour each evening doing homework.
Assignment
a task given by a teacher.
Our English assignment is due on Friday.
Workload
the amount of work someone has to do.
Some students have a heavy workload during exam season.
Balance
a healthy mix of different parts of life.
It is important to have a balance between study and free time.
Pressure
feelings of stress or worry.
Too much pressure can affect motivation.
Responsibility
something that you must do.
Homework can teach responsibility.
Concentrate
to give full attention to something.
It is easier to concentrate in a quiet place.
Achievement
something successful that you have done.
Finishing a difficult project was a great achievement.
Independent
able to do things without help.
Homework encourages students to become more independent.
Motivation
the desire to do something.
Positive feedback can increase motivation.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Homework Debate
  • Did you enjoy doing homework when you were at school?
  • How much time did you spend on assignments each day?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
A: Banning homework would give people more time to build stronger family relationships and a healthier balance between work and personal life.
B: Keeping homework helps people develop professional responsibility, a skill that remains valuable throughout adulthood.
A: Without extra assignments, students could spend more time learning practical "survival" skills such as budgeting, cooking, or repairing things.
B: Regular assignments prepare people for the high-pressure professional careers where meeting deadlines is a core requirement. (Consider the role of Assignment here.)
A: A smaller workload outside of school hours could significantly improve mental health and increase long-term passion for learning.
B: A manageable homework workload teaches people how to organize their time and prioritize tasks before entering the workplace.
A: People learn more effectively when they choose their own educational activities freely instead of following compulsory curricula.
B: Completing difficult, teacher-led assignments creates a powerful sense of achievement that builds deep self-confidence. (Consider the role of Balance here.)
A: Evenings without homework could encourage a surge in community volunteering, physical exercise, and creative hobbies.
B: Homework encourages people to develop the ability to concentrate deeply and work independently without constant external guidance. (Consider the role of Pressure here.)
A: Removing homework could eliminate the "privilege gap" where students with wealthy, educated parents have an unfair academic advantage.
B: Homework allows ambitious independent learners to explore subjects in far more depth than is possible in a standard classroom. (Consider the role of Responsibility here.)
A: Less academic pressure at home allows people to become more creative, original, and willing to take professional risks later in life.
B: Facing a reasonable amount of academic pressure prepares people for the reality of demanding leadership roles and societal responsibilities. (Consider the role of Concentrate here.)
A: In a world dominated by AI, banning traditional homework would encourage learning through real, human-centric physical experiences.
B: Creative homework tasks help people learn how to use AI tools responsibly as "digital partners" rather than relying on them as crutches. (Consider the role of Achievement here.)
A: Without daily obligations, people might become naturally more curious because learning would feel like a choice rather than a chore.
B: Regular assignments help people build the internal discipline needed to perform even when their personal motivation is low. (Consider the role of Independent here.)
A: Banning homework could make lifelong education far more attractive for adults who want to return to university later in their careers.
B: Maintaining homework habits ensures that the brain remains "plastic" and capable of learning new complex skills throughout adulthood. (Consider the role of Motivation here.)

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
A: Total removal of homework might create a future workforce that expects professional success without any personal effort or preparation.
B: Compulsory homework for life would turn our homes into "mini-offices," permanently destroying the boundary between work and rest. (How does Workload affect this statement?)
A: Without the discipline of assignments, we might lose the cognitive ability to focus on complex, boring tasks for more than ten minutes.
B: Continuing the homework tradition risks producing a society of "excellent sheep" who can follow orders but cannot think for themselves. (How does Balance affect this statement?)
A: Banning homework might trigger a massive surge in screen addiction as people struggle to fill their newly found "unstructured" free time.
B: Keeping homework ensures that people never have enough free time to discover who they truly are outside of their social or professional roles. (How does Pressure affect this statement?)
A: A world without homework would force schools to rely exclusively on high-stakes, stressful exams to measure student performance.
B: A world with too much homework creates a permanent state of "academic burnout" that can last long into a person's adult career. (How does Responsibility affect this statement?)
A: If parents are no longer involved in homework, they might lose all connection and insight into their children's intellectual development.
B: If parents remain "homework supervisors," the constant conflict over assignments can permanently damage the parent-child relationship. (How does Concentrate affect this statement?)
A: A homework ban might lead to a new "black market" for education where only the rich can buy the extra knowledge their children need.
B: Maintaining traditional homework ensures that only families with quiet, stable homes can ever achieve high academic honors. (How does Assignment affect this statement?)
A: Without solitary study, people might become completely unable to solve problems without a team or an internet connection.
B: Excessive solitary study produces socially isolated adults who struggle to communicate or cooperate with their peers in real-world settings. (How does Independent affect this statement?)
A: If homework becomes illegal, the total time required to master a new language or skill might double, slowing down human progress.
B: If homework remains mandatory, the "joy of discovery" will be permanently replaced by the "fear of failure" across all levels of education. (How does Motivation affect this statement?)
A: A society without homework might become highly creative but fundamentally unable to maintain the complex systems that keep civilization running.
B: A society obsessed with homework might become highly efficient but fundamentally unable to imagine a different or better future for itself. (How does Achievement affect this statement?)
A: The ultimate risk of banning homework is the loss of intellectual rigor and the "death of the expert."
B: The ultimate risk of keeping homework is the total exhaustion of the human spirit and the "death of curiosity."
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The teacher gave me many homeworks→The teacher gave me a lot of homework(Uncountable homework)
I am agree with the ban→I agree with the ban(Agree is a verb)
If I was a student, I would...→If I were a student, I would...(Second conditional)
I stopped to do assignments→I stopped doing assignments(Stop + -ing)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
