Homework should be banned

Level

Intermediate (B1)

Topic

Education / Work-Life Balance

The long-standing tradition of homework is facing increased scrutiny as educators and parents debate its true impact on learning and mental health. This session examines the arguments for and against banning mandatory assignments outside of school hours, focusing on the balance between academic achievement and personal growth. We will explore how removing homework might transform family dynamics, life skills development, and the overall educational experience.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Homework
school work that students do at home.
Many students spend an hour each evening doing homework.
Assignment
a task given by a teacher.
Our English assignment is due on Friday.
Workload
the amount of work someone has to do.
Some students have a heavy workload during exam season.
Balance
a healthy mix of different parts of life.
It is important to have a balance between study and free time.
Pressure
feelings of stress or worry.
Too much pressure can affect motivation.
Responsibility
something that you must do.
Homework can teach responsibility.
Concentrate
to give full attention to something.
It is easier to concentrate in a quiet place.
Achievement
something successful that you have done.
Finishing a difficult project was a great achievement.
Independent
able to do things without help.
Homework encourages students to become more independent.
Motivation
the desire to do something.
Positive feedback can increase motivation.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Homework Debate
  • Did you enjoy doing homework when you were at school?
  • How much time did you spend on assignments each day?
🔵 Round 1 — Advantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
1. A: Banning homework would give people more time to build stronger family relationships and a healthier balance between work, study, and personal life.
B: Keeping homework helps people develop responsibility, a skill that remains valuable throughout adulthood.
2. A: Without extra assignments, students could spend more time learning practical life skills such as cooking, budgeting, or repairing things.
B: Regular assignments prepare people for professional careers where deadlines are common.
3. A: A smaller workload outside school could improve mental health and increase long-term motivation.
B: A manageable workload teaches people how to organize their time before entering the workplace.
4. A: People learn better when they choose activities freely instead of completing compulsory homework.
B: Completing homework creates a sense of achievement that can build confidence. (Consider the role of Balance here.)
5. A: Evenings without homework could encourage adults and children to exercise, travel, volunteer, or develop hobbies.
B: Homework encourages people to concentrate independently without constant guidance. (Consider the role of Pressure here.)
6. A: Removing homework could reduce educational inequality because not everyone has the same support at home.
B: Homework allows independent learners to explore subjects beyond what is taught in class. (Consider the role of Responsibility here.)
7. A: Less academic pressure may help people become more creative and willing to take risks.
B: A reasonable amount of pressure prepares people for demanding careers and important responsibilities. (Consider the role of Concentrate here.)
8. A: In a world full of AI, banning traditional homework would encourage learning through real experiences instead.
B: Homework helps people learn how to use AI responsibly rather than depending on it completely. (Consider the role of Achievement here.)
9. A: Without daily assignments, people might become more curious because learning would feel less like an obligation.
B: Regular assignments help people build discipline, even when their motivation is low. (Consider the role of Independent here.)
10. A: Banning homework could make education more attractive for adults who want to return to studying later in life.
B: Homework helps people keep learning habits that remain useful throughout their careers. (Consider the role of Motivation here.)

🟢 Round 2 — Disadvantages (A/B Ideas)▲
Debate
1. A: If homework disappeared completely, future generations might expect success without much personal responsibility.
B: If homework remained compulsory, people might begin to see learning as a lifelong punishment instead of an opportunity.
2. A: If schools stopped giving assignments, companies might spend years teaching new employees basic self-management skills.
B: If schools kept increasing assignments, fewer people might choose demanding careers due to pre-adulthood exhaustion.
3. A: Without homework, AI assistants might become the main teacher, reducing the ability to concentrate on difficult problems alone.
B: If homework continues as usual, AI might simply complete most of it, making personal achievement less meaningful. (How does Workload affect this statement?)
4. A: If homework disappeared, schools might replace it with more high-stakes exams, creating even greater psychological pressure.
B: If homework stays important, the combination of exams and daily tasks creates an impossible total workload. (How does Balance affect this statement?)
5. A: Without homework, families might argue more about how children should spend their unproductive free time.
B: With homework every evening, families might slowly stop eating meals together or sharing meaningful social time. (How does Pressure affect this statement?)
6. A: Banning homework globally could lead to a surge in expensive private tutoring, creating new forms of educational inequality.
B: Continuing traditional homework allows wealthy families to maintain an advantage by hiring more external support. (How does Responsibility affect this statement?)
7. A: Without regular homework, people might become less independent and always expect external organization for their learning.
B: Too much homework might make people so isolated and independent that they become less willing to cooperate in teams. (How does Concentrate affect this statement?)
8. A: If homework disappeared, universities might be forced to extend course durations to reach the same level of academic achievement.
B: If homework becomes more demanding, fewer adults will decide to return to formal education later in their careers.
9. A: Without homework, future politicians might prioritize "popular" easy education over high-quality, rigorous standards.
B: Traditional homework structures are too slow to prepare modern society for the rapid shifts in new technologies. (How does Independent affect this statement?)
10. A: A fifty-year ban on homework might create generations expert at finding quick answers but poor at solving complex, solitary problems.
B: An increase in homework might cause future adults to protect their free time so fiercely they avoid learning anything new. (How does Motivation affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
The teacher gave me many homeworks → The teacher gave me a lot of homework (Uncountable homework)
I am agree with the ban → I agree with the ban (Agree is a verb)
If I was a student, I would... → If I were a student, I would... (Second conditional)
I stopped to do assignments → I stopped doing assignments (Stop + -ing)
It is a very useful information → It is very useful information (Uncountable information)
