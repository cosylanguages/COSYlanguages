Social media has fundamentally rewired our social interactions, offering unprecedented connectivity while simultaneously introducing new psychological pressures. This session explores the balance between the digital world and real-life experiences, focusing on the impact of constant scrolling and online comparison on our mental health. We will imagine what our lives might look like if we returned to a pre-digital social landscape and how we can maintain an authentic identity in an algorithmic age.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Doomscrolling
spending excessive time scrolling through bad news online
Example: I spent the whole evening doomscrolling instead of sleeping.
Detox
a period of time in which one abstains from unhealthy behaviors
Example: A weekend digital detox helped me feel more present.
Validation
recognition or affirmation that a person is worthwhile
Example: Many people seek validation through likes on social media.
FOMO
Fear Of Missing Out
Example: Social media can often trigger a sense of FOMO.
Engagement
the degree to which people interact with content
Example: High engagement rates are important for influencers.
Influencer
a person with the ability to influence potential buyers
Example: She became a successful travel influencer on Instagram.
Anonymous
not identified by name; of unknown name
Example: Many people post anonymous comments online.
Echo Chamber
an environment where a person only encounters information that reinforces their own
Example: Social media can easily become an echo chamber.
Comparison
estimate of the similarities or dissimilarities between two things
Example: Constant comparison with others can lead to unhappiness.
Authentic
of undisputed origin; genuine
Example: It is difficult to be truly authentic on social media.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Digital World
  • How many apps do you check before breakfast?
  • Do you remember life before social media?
🔵 Round 1 — My Life With▲
Narrative
1. Have you ever tried a "digital detox"? How long did it last?
★ What was the hardest app to give up?
2. How has social media changed your relationships with distant friends?
3. Do you feel more or less connected to the world because of your phone?
4. What is the most positive experience you've had on social media?
5. Have you ever felt "addicted" to a certain app?
6. Do you share your personal life openly or are you private?
7. How do you feel after spending an hour scrolling?
8. What would you do with the time if social media didn't exist?
9. Do you think your personality is different online vs offline?
10. What is one rule you have for yourself regarding phone use?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲
📱
Instant Messaging
☕
Face-to-Face

Which one feels more "real" to you?

🟢 Round 2 — My Life Without▲
Projections
1. If social media disappeared tomorrow, how would you maintain your relationships with distant friends?
★ Who is the first person you would call on the phone?
2. Without constant online comparison, would people feel more content with their own lives?
★ Do you think your self-esteem would improve without social media?
3. How would our hobbies and free time change if we weren't always documenting them for an audience?
★ What activity do you enjoy purely for yourself?
4. Without algorithms, how would you discover new music, books, or news?
★ Do you miss the "old way" of discovering things?
5. Would society become more or less politically polarized without social media echo chambers?
★ Have you ever changed your mind because of an online debate?
6. If everyone had to be anonymous online, would the internet be a kinder or more toxic place?
★ Do you feel safer being anonymous?
7. How would the concept of "privacy" change if we stopped sharing our lives publicly?
★ What is one thing you would never share online?
8. Without social media influencers, would fashion and trends move slower?
★ Do you think slow-moving trends are better for the environment?
9. If you had to choose between a world with perfect connectivity but zero privacy, and zero connectivity with perfect privacy, which would you pick?
★ Which one feels more like a prison?
10. Can we ever truly return to a "pre-social media" way of thinking, or has our psychology changed forever?
★ Do you think the next generation will even understand the concept of privacy?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I spend many time on phone→I spend a lot of time on my phone(Uncountable time)
He is an influencer since long time→He has been an influencer for a long time(Present Perfect + for)
The social media is dangerous→Social media is dangerous(Generalization)
I am interesting in his posts→I am interested in his posts(-ed vs -ing)
I stopped to use Instagram→I stopped using Instagram(Stop + gerund)
