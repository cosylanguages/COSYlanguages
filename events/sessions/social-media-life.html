Social media has fundamentally rewired our social interactions, offering unprecedented connectivity while simultaneously introducing new psychological pressures. This session explores the balance between the digital world and real-life experiences, focusing on the impact of constant scrolling and online comparison on our mental health. We will imagine what our lives might look like if we returned to a pre-digital social landscape and how we can maintain an authentic identity in an algorithmic age.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Doomscrolling
spending excessive time scrolling through bad news online.
I spent the whole evening doomscrolling instead of sleeping.
Detox
a period of time in which one abstains from unhealthy behaviors.
A weekend digital detox helped me feel more present.
Validation
recognition or affirmation that a person is worthwhile.
Many people seek validation through likes on social media.
FOMO
Fear Of Missing Out.
Social media can often trigger a sense of FOMO.
Engagement
the degree to which people interact with content.
High engagement rates are important for influencers.
Influencer
a person with the ability to influence potential buyers.
She became a successful travel influencer on Instagram.
Anonymous
not identified by name; of unknown name.
Many people post anonymous comments online.
Echo Chamber
an environment where a person only encounters information that reinforces their own.
Social media can easily become an echo chamber.
Comparison
estimate of the similarities or dissimilarities between two things.
Constant comparison with others can lead to unhappiness.
Authentic
of undisputed origin; genuine.
It is difficult to be truly authentic on social media.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Digital World
  • How many apps do you check before breakfast?
  • Do you remember life before social media?
🔵 Round 1 — Life With▲
Questions
Have you ever tried a "digital detox"? How long did it last? (Consider the role of Doomscrolling here.)
★ What was the hardest app to give up?
How has social media changed your relationships with distant friends? (Consider the role of Detox here.)
★ Do you think online connection is "good enough"?
Do you feel more or less connected to the world because of your phone? (Consider the role of Validation here.)
★ Do you check the news more often than you should?
What is the most positive experience you've had on social media? (Consider the role of FOMO here.)
★ Have you ever met a real friend online?
Have you ever felt "addicted" to a certain app? (Consider the role of Engagement here.)
★ What is the first app you open when you are bored?
Do you share your personal life openly or are you private? (Consider the role of Influencer here.)
★ What is one thing you would never post online?
How do you feel after spending an hour scrolling? (Consider the role of Anonymous here.)
★ Does it feel like a waste of time or a form of rest?
What would you do with the time if social media didn't exist? (Consider the role of Echo Chamber here.)
★ Which hobby have you neglected because of your phone?
Do you think your personality is different online vs offline? (Consider the role of Comparison here.)
★ Are you more confident or more careful when posting?
What is one rule you have for yourself regarding phone use? (Consider the role of Authentic here.)
★ Do you keep your phone out of the bedroom?

🟢 Round 2 — Life Without▲
Dilemmas
If social media disappeared tomorrow, how would you maintain your relationships with distant friends? (How does Doomscrolling affect this statement?)
Without constant online comparison, would people feel more content with their own lives? (How does Detox affect this statement?)
How would our hobbies and free time change if we weren't always documenting them for an audience? (How does Validation affect this statement?)
Without algorithms, how would you discover new music, books, or news? (How does FOMO affect this statement?)
Would society become more or less politically polarized without social media echo chambers? (How does Engagement affect this statement?)
If everyone had to be anonymous online, would the internet be a kinder or more toxic place? (How does Influencer affect this statement?)
How would the concept of "privacy" change if we stopped sharing our lives publicly? (How does Anonymous affect this statement?)
Without social media influencers, would fashion and trends move slower? (How does Echo Chamber affect this statement?)
If you had to choose between a world with perfect connectivity but zero privacy, and zero connectivity with perfect privacy, which would you pick? (How does Comparison affect this statement?)
Can we ever truly return to a "pre-social media" way of thinking, or has our psychology changed forever? (How does Authentic affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I spend many time on phone→I spend a lot of time on my phone(Uncountable time)
He is an influencer since long time→He has been an influencer for a long time(Present Perfect + for)
The social media is dangerous→Social media is dangerous(Generalization)
I am interesting in his posts→I am interested in his posts(-ed vs -ing)
I stopped to use Instagram→I stopped using Instagram(Stop + gerund)
