📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Humility
modest view of one's importance
Example: He accepted the award with great humility.
Wisdom
the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment
Example: Socrates is famous for his philosophical wisdom.
Ignorance
lack of knowledge or information
Example: Ignorance of the law is no excuse.
Ethics
moral principles that govern behavior
Example: He is studying the ethics of artificial intelligence.
Paradox
a seemingly absurd statement that may be true
Example: The Socratic paradox is a central theme in his work.
Dialogue
conversation between two or more people
Example: Plato's dialogues feature Socrates as the main speaker.
Virtue
behavior showing high moral standards
Example: Patience is a virtue.
Logic
reasoning conducted according to strict principles of validity
Example: Use logic to support your argument.
Perspective
a particular attitude toward something
Example: We need to see this from a different perspective.
Contemplate
look thoughtfully for a long time at
Example: He sat alone to contemplate the meaning of life.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Wisdom & Humility
  • What does "wisdom" mean to you?
  • Is it easy to admit when you are wrong?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis (10 Questions)▲
Questions
1. Do you think it's possible to "know nothing" in the information age?
★ When was the last time you changed your mind about something important?
2. Why is humility considered a foundation for learning?
3. How does the internet affect our perception of our own knowledge?
4. Is there a difference between "information" and "wisdom"?
5. Why did Socrates focus so much on questioning others?
6. Can someone be wise without having a formal education?
7. What are the dangers of thinking you know everything?
8. How can we practice Socratic questioning in our daily lives?
9. Does modern society value wisdom or speed more?
10. If you could ask Socrates one question, what would it be?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲
🎓
Expertise
🔍
Curiosity

Which one is more valuable today?

🟢 Round 2 — Philosophical Application (10 Units)▲
Debate
1. Is "ignorance bliss", or is knowledge always better?
2. Should ethics be taught as a mandatory subject in all schools?
3. Is it possible to be a good person without knowing why you are doing good?
4. Does science make philosophy obsolete?
5. Should we prioritize logical thinking over emotional intelligence?
6. Is there such a thing as "absolute truth"?
7. Can a machine ever be truly wise?
8. Is the Socratic method too aggressive for modern conversation?
9. If everyone followed Socrates' advice, would the world be a better place?
10. What is the most important "truth" you have discovered in your life?
✏️ Discussion (Mistakes)▲
I am more wise→I am wiser
Socrates was an expert of questioning→Socrates was an expert at questioning(Preposition "at")
I didn't knew that→I didn't know that
He think that it is true→He thinks that it is true(Subject-verb agreement)
