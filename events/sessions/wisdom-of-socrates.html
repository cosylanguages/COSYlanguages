Quote

“The only true wisdom is in knowing you know nothing.”

Socrates' paradox of wisdom is a cornerstone of Western philosophy, emphasizing the importance of humility and the recognition of our own limitations. This session explores how acknowledging our ignorance can lead to deeper understanding and more effective learning in all areas of life. We will discuss the relevance of Socratic questioning in the modern world and how it can help us navigate a sea of information and misinformation.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Humility ≠ Pride
a modest view of one's own importance ≠ a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's achievements.
Her genuine humility endeared her to everyone, unlike his boastful pride.
Wisdom
the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment.
Socrates is famous for his philosophical wisdom.
Ignorance
lack of knowledge or information.
Ignorance of the law is no excuse.
Ethics
moral principles that govern behavior.
He is studying the ethics of artificial intelligence.
Paradox
a seemingly absurd statement that may be true.
The Socratic paradox is a central theme in his work.
Dialogue
conversation between two or more people.
Plato's dialogues feature Socrates as the main speaker.
Virtue
behavior showing high moral standards.
Patience is a virtue.
Logic
reasoning conducted according to strict principles of validity.
Use logic to support your argument.
Perspective
a particular attitude toward something.
We need to see this from a different perspective.
Contemplate
look thoughtfully for a long time at.
He sat alone to contemplate the meaning of life.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Wisdom & Humility
  • What does "wisdom" mean to you?
  • Is it easy to admit when you are wrong?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Do you think it's possible to "know nothing" in the information age? (Consider the role of Humility here.)
★ When was the last time you changed your mind about something important?
Why is humility considered a foundation for learning? (Consider the role of Wisdom here.)
How does the internet affect our perception of our own knowledge? (Consider the role of Ignorance here.)
Is there a difference between "information" and "wisdom"? (Consider the role of Ethics here.)
Why did Socrates focus so much on questioning others? (Consider the role of Paradox here.)
Can someone be wise without having a formal education? (Consider the role of Dialogue here.)
What are the dangers of thinking you know everything? (Consider the role of Virtue here.)
How can we practice Socratic questioning in our daily lives? (Consider the role of Logic here.)
Does modern society value wisdom or speed more? (Consider the role of Perspective here.)
If you could ask Socrates one question, what would it be? (Consider the role of Contemplate here.)

🟢 Round 2 — The Future: Agree or Disagree?▲
Debate
Is "ignorance bliss", or is knowledge always better? (How does Humility affect this statement?)

Does science make philosophy obsolete? (How does Ethics affect this statement?)

Is there such a thing as "absolute truth"? (How does Dialogue affect this statement?)

Is the Socratic method too aggressive for modern conversation? (How does Logic affect this statement?)

What is the most important "truth" you have discovered in your life? (How does Contemplate affect this statement?)
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲

The most of people think...→Most people think...(Generalization)
If I was him, I would...→If I were him, I would...(Second conditional)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
