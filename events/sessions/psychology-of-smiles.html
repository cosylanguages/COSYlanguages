📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Duchenne smile
a genuine smile involving both mouth and eye muscles, linked to real enjoyment
Example: Researchers use the Duchenne smile to distinguish real happiness from polite expressions.
To fake a smile
to deliberately show a smile without actually feeling happiness
Example: She had to fake a smile during the meeting even though she was tired.
Social smile
a polite or controlled smile used in social situations, not necessarily linked to real emotion
Example: A social smile is common when greeting strangers or customers.
Facial cues
visible facial signs that give information about emotions
Example: We often rely on facial cues to judge whether someone is happy or uncomfortable.
Micro-expression
a very quick, involuntary facial expression that reveals true emotion
Example: A micro-expression can last less than a second but still show real feelings.
Eye involvement
activation of eye muscles that signals a genuine smile
Example: Eye involvement is what separates a genuine smile from a fake one.
Emotional leakage
when real emotions appear despite attempts to hide them
Example: Even while smiling politely, emotional leakage showed he was actually stressed.
Non-verbal communication
communication without words, using facial expressions, gestures, and body language
Example: Smiling is a key part of non-verbal communication in every culture.
Social masking
hiding true feelings and showing socially acceptable emotions
Example: People often use social masking at work to appear positive.
Cultural display rules
cultural norms that control emotional expression
Example: Cultural display rules explain why people smile more in some countries than others.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Power of the Smile
  • How many times a day do you think you smile?
  • In your opinion, can a smile change the atmosphere of a room?
🔵 Round 1 — What Faces Really Reveal (10 Questions)▲
Questions
1. Why do humans rely on smiling so heavily in social life, even when it has nothing to do with real emotion?
★ Do you think your smile is more emotional or more social?
2. If a Duchenne smile is “real happiness” and a social smile is “performance,” how often do you think you switch between the two in a normal day?
★ Do you ever feel like your smile has an “automatic mode”?
3. Why is faking a smile often easier than explaining how we actually feel?
★ What emotions do you most often hide behind a smile?
4. If facial cues are so important, why do we still so often misread people?
★ Have you ever completely misjudged someone based on their expression?
5. Why do micro-expressions tend to appear most when people are under emotional pressure?
★ Have you ever noticed a “split second” emotion on someone’s face that contradicted what they said?
6. What does eye involvement reveal that a normal smile can hide?
★ Do you trust eyes more than words when judging sincerity?
7. Why do we sometimes notice emotional leakage in others before we notice it in ourselves?
★ Has anyone ever pointed out an emotion you were trying to hide?
8. If non-verbal communication is more honest than words, why do we still rely so much on speech?
★ In conflict situations, do you trust tone/body language or spoken arguments more?
9. Is social masking a necessary skill for modern life, or does it slowly disconnect us from ourselves?
★ In which environments do you feel you “perform” the most?
10. If smiling is shaped by cultural display rules, how much of your smile is actually “yours” and how much is cultural training?
★ Have you noticed big differences in smiling behavior between cultures?

🟢 Round 2 — Conditional Scenarios (10 Units)▲
Conditionals
1. If you could instantly distinguish every fake smile from a real one, how would your relationships change over time?
★ Would you actually want to live with that level of emotional clarity?
2. If you had grown up in a family where emotional control was strongly valued, how different would your current emotional expression be?
★ Do you think you learned your emotional style or inherited it?
3. If you were never allowed to hide your emotions behind a smile, what would become the hardest part of your social life?
★ Where do you rely most on “social masking” today?
4. If people could see your true emotional state every time you smiled, what situations would suddenly feel uncomfortable or impossible?
★ Which emotion do you protect most from others?
5. If you had realized earlier in life that smiling can function as a form of self-presentation, how would your trust in people be different today?
★ Do you think you are easily influenced by someone’s smile?
6. If society rewarded emotional authenticity more than emotional control, how would your daily interactions change?
★ Would you personally prefer a world like that, or not?
7. Would you rather live in a world where nobody can fake emotions, or a world where nobody can read emotions at all?
★ Which world feels more psychologically safe for you?
8. If you could erase one emotional habit connected to smiling (like automatic politeness or masking), what would you remove?
★ Do you think your smile is more a habit or a feeling?
9. If your emotions were always visible on your face, how would your confidence in social situations change?
★ Which emotion would be the most difficult for you to expose?
10. If you met someone who only smiled when they truly meant it, would you trust them more deeply or feel emotionally distant from them?
★ Do you value emotional warmth more or emotional honesty more?
