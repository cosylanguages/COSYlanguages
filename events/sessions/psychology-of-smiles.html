Smiles are more than just expressions of happiness; they are complex social tools used for communication, masking, and building rapport. This session explores the psychological depth of facial expressions and how they influence our interactions with others. We will analyze the difference between genuine and polite smiles and the cultural rules that govern their use in daily life.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Duchenne smile
a genuine smile involving both mouth and eye muscles, linked to real enjoyment.
Researchers use the Duchenne smile to distinguish real happiness from polite expressions.
To fake a smile
to deliberately show a smile without actually feeling happiness.
She had to fake a smile during the meeting even though she was tired.
Social smile
a polite or controlled smile used in social situations, not necessarily linked to real emotion.
A social smile is common when greeting strangers or customers.
Facial cues
visible facial signs that give information about emotions.
We often rely on facial cues to judge whether someone is happy or uncomfortable.
Micro-expression
a very quick, involuntary facial expression that reveals true emotion.
A micro-expression can last less than a second but still show real feelings.
Eye involvement
activation of eye muscles that signals a genuine smile.
Eye involvement is what separates a genuine smile from a fake one.
Emotional leakage
when real emotions appear despite attempts to hide them.
Even while smiling politely, emotional leakage showed he was actually stressed.
Non-verbal communication
communication without words, using facial expressions, gestures, and body language.
Smiling is a key part of non-verbal communication in every culture.
Social masking
hiding true feelings and showing socially acceptable emotions.
People often use social masking at work to appear positive.
Cultural display rules
cultural norms that control emotional expression.
Cultural display rules explain why people smile more in some countries than others.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Power of the Smile
  • How many times a day do you think you smile?
  • In your opinion, can a smile change the atmosphere of a room?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
Questions
Why do humans rely on smiling so heavily in social life, even when it has nothing to do with real emotion?
★ Do you think your smile is more emotional or more social?
If a Duchenne smile is “real happiness” and a social smile is “performance,” how often do you think you switch between the two in a normal day?
★ Do you ever feel like your smile has an “automatic mode”?
Why is faking a smile often easier than explaining how we actually feel?
★ What emotions do you most often hide behind a smile?
If facial cues are so important, why do we still so often misread people?
★ Have you ever completely misjudged someone based on their expression?
Why do micro-expressions tend to appear most when people are under emotional pressure?
★ Have you ever noticed a “split second” emotion on someone’s face that contradicted what they said?
What does eye involvement reveal that a normal smile can hide?
★ Do you trust eyes more than words when judging sincerity?
Why do we sometimes notice emotional leakage in others before we notice it in ourselves?
★ Has anyone ever pointed out an emotion you were trying to hide?
If non-verbal communication is more honest than words, why do we still rely so much on speech?
★ In conflict situations, do you trust tone/body language or spoken arguments more?
Is social masking a necessary skill for modern life, or does it slowly disconnect us from ourselves?
★ In which environments do you feel you “perform” the most?
If smiling is shaped by cultural display rules, how much of your smile is actually “yours” and how much is cultural training?
★ Have you noticed big differences in smiling behavior between cultures?

🟢 Round 2 — Dilemmas / Future▲
Debate
If you could instantly distinguish every fake smile from a real one, how would your social interactions change over time?
If you had grown up in a culture where smiling was less common, how different would your current personality be?
If you were never allowed to hide your true feelings, what would have happened during your last difficult professional meeting?
If people could see your exact emotional state at all times, would you feel more vulnerable or more empowered?
If you had realized the power of non-verbal cues ten years ago, would you have been more successful in your career?
If society rewarded absolute emotional honesty, how would our service industries (like hotels or restaurants) change?
If you had to choose between a world where nobody could smile and a world where everyone smiled constantly, which would be more exhausting?
If you were to stop "polite smiling" for one entire week, how many of your daily interactions would become awkward?
If you had to pick one bias to eliminate from the world, which would it be?
If you had known more about the "Duchenne smile" earlier, would you have trusted different people in your past?
