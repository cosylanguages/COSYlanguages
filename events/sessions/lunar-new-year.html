📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Prosperity
the state of being successful and flourishing
Example: The red colour symbolizes prosperity.
Tradition
the transmission of customs from generation to generation
Example: It is a tradition to have a big family dinner.
Ritual
a religious or solemn ceremony consisting of a series of actions
Example: Cleaning the house before the New Year is a common ritual.
Symbolize
to be a symbol of something
Example: Firecrackers symbolize scaring away evil spirits.
Ancestor
a person from whom one is descended
Example: Many people pay respects to their ancestors during the festival.
Festival
a period of celebration
Example: The Lunar New Year is the most important festival in China.
Lunar
determined by or resembling the moon
Example: The date is based on the lunar calendar.
Zodiac
a belt of the heavens within which are the paths of the sun, moon, and planets
Example: Each year is associated with a zodiac animal.
Reunion
the act of coming together again
Example: The reunion dinner is the highlight of the celebration.
Offering
a thing offered, especially as a gift or contribution
Example: Fruit offerings are placed on the family altar.

🔵 Round 1 — Cultural Trivia (10 Questions)▲
True or False
1. Lunar New Year is only celebrated in China.
★ Is it common to see these celebrations in your city?
2. The date of the Lunar New Year is the same every year.
3. Red is considered a lucky color in many Asian cultures.
4. Only children receive red envelopes.
5. Each year is named after a mythical creature.
6. Dumplings are a traditional food for the New Year.
7. People clean their houses to "wash away" bad luck.
8. Firecrackers were originally used to scare away a monster named Nian.
9. The celebration lasts for 15 days.
10. It is the largest annual human migration on Earth.

🟢 Round 2 — Future of Traditions (10 Units)▲
Debate
1. Are digital "red envelopes" as meaningful as physical ones?
2. Is it important for young people to keep ancient traditions alive?
3. Should festivals be more about religion or family gathering?
4. Has the Lunar New Year become too commercialized?
5. How will traditions change in a globalized world?
6. Are traditional foods being replaced by fast food during festivals?
7. Should governments subsidize traditional cultural events?
8. Is the concept of "luck" helpful or harmful in modern society?
9. Can we create "new" traditions that are as meaningful as old ones?
10. What festival from another culture would you like to celebrate?
