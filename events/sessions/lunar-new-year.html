Read Article 📖

Lunar New Year, also known as Spring Festival, is a major celebration observed by multiple cultures across East and Southeast Asia, marking the beginning of a new year on the lunar calendar. This session explores the rich symbolism, familial traditions, and festive foods that define this period of renewal and reunion. We will discuss the significance of the Chinese Zodiac, the traditional red envelopes, and how the holiday is celebrated by the global Asian diaspora.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Prosperity
the state of being successful and flourishing.
The red colour symbolizes prosperity.
Tradition
the transmission of customs from generation to generation.
It is a tradition to have a big family dinner.
Ritual
a religious or solemn ceremony consisting of a series of actions.
Cleaning the house before the New Year is a common ritual.
Symbolize
to be a symbol of something.
Firecrackers symbolize scaring away evil spirits.
Ancestor
a person from whom one is descended.
Many people pay respects to their ancestors during the festival.
Festival
a period of celebration.
The Lunar New Year is the most important festival in China.
Lunar
determined by or resembling the moon.
The date is based on the lunar calendar.
Zodiac
a belt of the heavens within which are the paths of the sun, moon, and planets.
Each year is associated with a zodiac animal.
Reunion
the act of coming together again.
The reunion dinner is the highlight of the celebration.
Offering
a thing offered, especially as a gift or contribution.
Fruit offerings are placed on the family altar.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
New Year Symbols
  • Do you have special traditions for the New Year?
  • What is your favorite festival of the year?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Lunar New Year is only celebrated in China. (Consider the role of Prosperity here.)
★ Is it common to see these celebrations in your city?
The date of the Lunar New Year is the same every year. (Consider the role of Tradition here.)
★ Do you prefer holidays with fixed or changing dates?
Red is considered a lucky color in many Asian cultures. (Consider the role of Ritual here.)
★ What is the "lucky color" in your culture?
Only children receive red envelopes. (Consider the role of Symbolize here.)
★ When was the last time you received money as a gift?
Each year is named after a mythical creature. (Consider the role of Ancestor here.)
★ Do you know your zodiac animal?
Dumplings are a traditional food for the New Year. (Consider the role of Festival here.)
★ What is the most important "holiday food" in your family?
People clean their houses to "wash away" bad luck. (Consider the role of Lunar here.)
★ Do you usually do a big "spring cleaning"?
Firecrackers were originally used to scare away a monster named Nian. (Consider the role of Zodiac here.)
★ Are you afraid of loud noises like fireworks?
The celebration lasts for 15 days. (Consider the role of Reunion here.)
★ Could you celebrate for two weeks straight?
It is the largest annual human migration on Earth. (Consider the role of Offering here.)
★ Do you like traveling during the holidays?

Globalization will cause all unique local traditions to disappear. (Consider the role of Ancestor here.)
Traditional holiday foods will be replaced by high-tech synthetic meals. (Consider the role of Festival here.)
Governments will start taxing traditional festivals to fund modern infrastructure. (Consider the role of Lunar here.)

Humans will create new, entirely digital traditions in the metaverse. (Consider the role of Reunion here.)
Cultural appropriation will make it socially unacceptable to celebrate "other" holidays. (Consider the role of Offering here.)
