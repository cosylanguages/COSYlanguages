The quote "You don't have a soul. You are a soul. You have a body" (often attributed to C.S. Lewis) challenges the materialist view of human existence. This session explores the concept of consciousness as the primary element of our being rather than a secondary function of the brain. We will discuss the philosophical and practical implications of identifying as a soul in a world that often prioritizes physical appearance and material gain.

☕ The Philosophers' Ledger: Café Debates

"Let us see how many interpretations exist! There is never only one correct answer to any profound human question." — Oscar Wilde

📖 Session Vocabulary

Essence
the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something.
Many believe that kindness is the true essence of humanity.
Consciousness
the state of being awake and aware of one's surroundings.
The mystery of human consciousness continues to baffle even the most brilliant scientists.
Materialism
a tendency to consider material possessions and physical comfort as more important than spiritual values.
Extreme materialism often leads to a sense of emptiness, despite having many possessions.
Transcendent
beyond or above the range of normal or merely physical human experience.
Listening to great music can be a transcendent experience that takes you out of your daily worries.
Spirituality
the quality of being concerned with the human spirit or soul as opposed to material things.
Personal spirituality can provide a source of strength during difficult life challenges.
Incarnation
a person who embodies in the flesh a deity, spirit, or abstract quality.
In many traditions, the body is seen as a temporary incarnation of the eternal soul.
Perception
the ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses.
Our perception of reality is often limited by our past experiences and biases.
Existential
relating to existence.
Facing a crisis often triggers an existential search for meaning and purpose.
Intangible
unable to be touched or grasped; not having physical presence.
The most important things in life, like love and peace, are completely intangible.
Identity
the fact of being who or what a person or thing is.
If you are a soul, your true identity is independent of your physical body.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Analyze the quote: "You are a soul. You have a body."
  • When do you feel most "disconnected" from your physical body?
  • Do you think of your mind and your body as two separate things or one unit?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
What is the essence of who you are, if we remove your name, job, and appearance?
How does practicing mindfulness help you become more aware of your own consciousness?
Why does modern materialism often lead to a neglect of our inner spiritual lives?
Have you ever had a transcendent experience while in nature or during meditation?
Is spirituality possible without following a specific organized religion?
How would your life change if you viewed your body as just a temporary incarnation?
How does our perception of death change if we believe the soul is eternal?
Why is an existential crisis often seen as a prerequisite for deep personal growth?
In a world of data, how can we prove the existence of intangible things like the human spirit?
How is your sense of identity affected by the aging process of your physical body?

It is a very deep essence→It is a very deep essence(Spelling: essence)
I am not believing in souls→I don't believe in souls(State verbs in present simple)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
