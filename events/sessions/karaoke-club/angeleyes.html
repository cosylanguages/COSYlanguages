📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

Deceptive
giving an appearance or impression different from the true one.
The calm sea can be highly deceptive during a storm.
Opposite: Honest
Gaze
look steadily and intently.
He would gaze at the stars for hours in the backyard.
Opposite: Glance
Disguise
give a new appearance to hide identity.
She wore a wig as a clever disguise.
Opposite: Reveal
Regret
feel sad, repentant, or disappointed over.
He expressed deep regret for his past mistakes.
Opposite: Satisfaction
Warning
a statement or event indicating possible danger.
The loud siren served as a warning to evacuate.
Opposite: Encouragement

🗣️ Life, Actions & Connection

Lure
tempt a person or animal to do something.
The sweet smell of pie would lure us into the kitchen.
Opposite: Repel
Painful
causing physical or mental pain.
It was a painful memory that she preferred to forget.
Opposite: Painless
Obsession ≠ Indifference
state of being obsessed with someone or something ≠ lack of interest, concern, or sympathy.
Her passionate devotion bordered on obsession, while his attitude was one of complete indifference.
Illusion
a thing that is likely to be wrongly perceived.
The oasis in the desert was just an optical illusion.
Opposite: Reality
Trust
firm belief in the reliability or truth.
Trust is the foundation of any strong relationship.
Opposite: Distrust
