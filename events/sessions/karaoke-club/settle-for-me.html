📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

settle
resolve or reach an agreement about, or compromise on something.
They decided to settle their minor differences and work together as a team.
nice
pleasant, agreeable, or satisfactory.
It was a nice and warm afternoon, perfect for a walk in the park.
stable
not likely to change, fail, or give way; firmly established.
After months of volatility, the economic situation has finally become stable.
true
in accordance with fact or reality; loyal and faithful.
A true friend will always support you through difficult times.
search
an act of searching for someone or something.
The rescue team conducted a thorough search for the lost hikers.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
