📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

ruined
completely spoiled, damaged, or destroyed.
Our outdoor picnic plans were ruined by the sudden, heavy downpour.
ring
a small circular band, typically of precious metal, worn on a finger.
He gave her a beautiful silver ring as a symbol of their friendship.
mess
a dirty or untidy state of things or of a place.
The children left a huge mess of toys in the living room.
strife
angry or bitter disagreement over fundamental issues.
The continuous internal strife within the organization led to its collapse.
dying
gradually ceasing to exist, function, or live.
The dying embers of the campfire cast a warm, orange glow.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
