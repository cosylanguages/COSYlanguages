📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

damaged
harmed or spoiled; not in perfect condition.
The old guitar was damaged during the long journey.
Opposite: intact
normal
conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected.
It is completely normal to feel nervous before a big presentation.
Opposite: abnormal
special
better, greater, or otherwise different from what is usual.
She prepared a special dinner to celebrate her promotion.
Opposite: ordinary
different
not the same as another or each other; unlike in nature.
They have completely different opinions about the new movie.
Opposite: identical
movies
motion pictures or films shown in a theater.
They love to watch bad movies on a rainy Sunday afternoon.
Opposite: live theater

🗣️ Life, Actions & Connection

summer
Target vocabulary word meaning in 'Seventeen'.
This is an elegant example of using 'summer'.
dancing
the activity of moving the body to music.
Dancing is a beautiful way to express your emotions and joy.
Opposite: standing still
tonight
on the present or coming night.
We are going to attend a live music concert tonight.
Opposite: today
choose
pick out or select someone or something as being the best.
You must choose a career path that truly inspires you.
Opposite: reject
right
morally good, justified, or acceptable; correct.
She did the right thing by telling the truth to her friend.
Opposite: wrong

✨ Stage 5: Language Focus▲

The Modal Verb 'Must' for Obligation and Deduction

We use 'must' to express a strong obligation or a logical deduction directly from the lyrics.

Let's Practise — Three Examples:
  • We must dare to make our own music.
  • The song is so beautiful, she must be a great artist.
  • You must listen carefully to fill in the missing gaps.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
