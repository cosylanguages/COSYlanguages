❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

Domestic
Target vocabulary word meaning in 'Coming Around Again'.
This is an elegant example of using 'Domestic'.
Resilience ≠ Fragility
the capacity to recover quickly from difficulties ≠ the quality of being easily broken or damaged.
True resilience is forged by understanding and accepting our own emotional fragility.
Routine
a sequence of actions regularly followed.
Establishing a morning routine can boost your productivity.
Opposite: Novelty
Breakdown
Target vocabulary word meaning in 'Coming Around Again'.
This is an elegant example of using 'Breakdown'.
Optimism ≠ Pessimism
hopefulness and confidence about the future ≠ a tendency to see the worst aspect of things.
Her natural optimism kept her going, whereas his pessimism made him want to give up.

Language Focus: Present Continuous for Imminent Reoccurrence

In the song Coming Around Again, the repetitive lyric chunk "I believe in love... it's coming around" highlights the use of Present Continuous for Imminent Reoccurrence. This structure supports: Expressing cyclical recovery and returning bonds.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
