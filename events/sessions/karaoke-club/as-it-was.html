📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

Change
rendre différent, modifier profondément.
Il a décidé de changer de carrière pour être plus heureux.
Opposite: Stability
Isolation
the state of being separate from others.
The remote cabin offered a perfect sense of isolation.
Opposite: Integration
La nostalgia ≠ La modernidad
sentimiento de pena por la lejanía o el tiempo pasado ≠ cualidad de lo moderno o actual.
Sintió una profunda nostalgia por el pueblo antiguo, un contraste total con la fría modernidad.
Suburban
relating to or characteristic of a suburb.
They lived in a quiet, peaceful suburban neighborhood.
Opposite: Urban
Gravity
the force that attracts a body toward the earth.
Gravity keeps our feet firmly planted on the ground.
Opposite: Levity

🗣️ Life, Actions & Connection

Disconnect
an instance of breaking contact or connection.
There was a temporary disconnect in our phone line.
Opposite: Connect
Melancholy
a feeling of pensive sadness.
A gentle melancholy settled over the autumn garden.
Opposite: Cheerfulness
Expectation
a strong belief that something will happen.
They met every high expectation of their teachers.
Opposite: Surprise
Routine
a sequence of actions regularly followed.
Establishing a morning routine can boost your productivity.
Opposite: Novelty
Reflection
the throwing back of light, heat, or sound.
The quiet lake offered a beautiful reflection of the trees.
Opposite: Absorption

Language Focus: Comparative *As... as* with Past Habits

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
