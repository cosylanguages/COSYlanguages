📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

Generalize
make a general or broad statement by inferring from specific cases.
It is unfair to generalize about an entire group of people based on one person's actions.
Opposite: Specialize
Satire
the use of humor, irony, exaggeration, or ridicule to expose and criticize people's stupidity.
The show is a brilliant satire of modern television culture.
Opposite: Solemnity
Condescending
having or showing a feeling of patronizing superiority.
He spoke in a condescending tone that offended everyone in the room.
Opposite: Humble

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
