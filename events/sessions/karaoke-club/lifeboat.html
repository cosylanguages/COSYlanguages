📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

ocean
a very large expanse of sea, in particular, each of the main areas of saline water.
The vast blue ocean stretched as far as the eye could see.
Opposite: desert
lifeboat
a small boat kept on a ship for use in an emergency.
The ship was equipped with enough lifeboats for everyone on board.
Opposite: ship
sink
go down below the surface of something, especially in liquid.
The heavy iron anchor will sink quickly to the bottom of the sea.
Opposite: float
captain
the person in command of a ship, aircraft, or team.
The experienced captain guided the ship safely through the storm.
Opposite: crew
weakest
lacking physical strength or resolve; the least strong.
We must protect and support the weakest members of our community.
Opposite: strongest

🗣️ Life, Actions & Connection

fighting
the action of defeating or resisting something; struggling.
Fighting for social justice requires courage and perseverance.
Opposite: surrendering
storm
a violent disturbance of the atmosphere with strong winds and rain.
The loud thunder and lightning of the storm woke me up.
Opposite: calm
wrong
not correct or true; incorrect.
He gave the wrong answer to the simple physics question.
outfit
a set of clothes worn together, typically for a particular occasion.
She picked out a beautiful and stylish outfit for the wedding.
Opposite: uniform
crowded
full of people, leaving little or no room.
The popular beach was very crowded on the warm summer weekend.
Opposite: empty

✨ Stage 5: Language Focus▲

The Modal Verb 'Must' for Obligation and Deduction

We use 'must' to express a strong obligation or a logical deduction directly from the lyrics.

Let's Practise — Three Examples:
  • We must dare to make our own music.
  • The song is so beautiful, she must be a great artist.
  • You must listen carefully to fill in the missing gaps.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
