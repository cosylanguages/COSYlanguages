📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

Dreaming
indulging in daydreams or fantasies.
He was dreaming of a warm sunny beach in California.
Opposite: Waking
Preacher
a person who delivers sermons or religious discourses.
The preacher delivered an inspiring sermon on Sunday morning.
Winter ≠ Summer
the coldest season of the year ≠ the warmest season of the year.
She loves the quiet snow of winter, whereas he prefers the bright sunshine of summer.
Warm ≠ Cold
having or showing a moderate degree of heat ≠ of or at a low or relatively low temperature.
The warm fireplace offered a beautiful sanctuary from the freezing cold winter winds outside.
Safe ≠ Dangerous
protected from or not exposed to danger or risk ≠ able or likely to cause harm or injury.
Keep your precious belongings in a safe place, far away from any dangerous elements.

🗣️ Life, Actions & Connection

Sky
the region of the atmosphere visible from the earth.
The blue sky was dotted with fluffy white clouds.
Opposite: Earth
Brown
of a color produced by mixing red, yellow, and black.
The autumn leaves turned a beautiful shade of brown.
Opposite: Green
Leave
go away from.
They decided to leave the party before it got too late.
Opposite: Arrive
Pray
address a solemn request or expression of thanks.
They gather to pray for peace and prosperity.
Opposite: Curse
Sensation
a physical feeling or perception.
The new roller coaster gives an exciting sensation of speed.
Opposite: Numbness

✨ Stage 5: Language Focus▲

The Second Conditional ('would')

Used to talk about hypothetical situations in the present or future. In the song: 'I'd be safe and warm if I was in L.A.'

Let's Practise — Three Examples:
  • I would travel to California if I had enough money.
  • If the weather were warmer, we would walk in the park.
  • She would sing along if she knew the full lyrics.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
