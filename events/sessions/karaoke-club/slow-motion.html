📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

motion
the action or process of moving or being moved.
The steady, rhythmic motion of the train put the baby to sleep.
cool
of or at a fairly low temperature, or fashionably attractive.
He wore a very cool leather jacket that suited him perfectly.
hair
any of the fine threadlike strands that grow from the skin.
The gentle spring breeze blew softly through her long hair.
care
the provision of what is necessary for the health, welfare, maintenance, and protection.
The rare plants require specialized care and attention to thrive.
heroes
people admired for their courage, outstanding achievements, or noble qualities.
The brave firefighters were celebrated as national heroes.

✨ Stage 5: Language Focus▲

Language Focus: Prepositional Phrase of Manner (*in slow motion*)

In the song Slow Motion, the repetitive lyric chunk "Everything is moving in slow motion" highlights the use of Prepositional Phrase of Manner (*in slow motion*). This structure supports: Describing shock, realization, and panic.

Let's Practise — Three Examples:
  • Example 1: Practice using this structure actively during the discussion rounds.
  • Example 2: Notice how this pattern is repeated to build rhythmic familiarity.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
