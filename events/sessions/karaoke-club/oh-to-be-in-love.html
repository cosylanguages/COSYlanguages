💬 Stage 4: Discussion▲

🔵 Round 1 — Lyric Analysis & Discussion

How does the message of unconditional devotion in 'Oh To Be in Love' by Kate Bush redefine how we understand Infatuation?
★ When was the last time you experienced a deep sense of Infatuation without needing any words?
In 'Oh To Be in Love', do you feel that Euphoria is portrayed as a source of emotional strength, or does it expose a hidden vulnerability?
★ Do you agree that Euphoria is something we must actively protect, or does it survive on its own?
Analyzing the lyrics of Kate Bush, how does the presence of Vulnerable contrast with the typical struggles of modern relationships?
★ How has your personal perspective on Vulnerable evolved over the last five years?
In what ways does 'Oh To Be in Love' illustrate that true Intense requires us to look past our partner's flaws?
★ In your daily life, do you find it easy or difficult to show Intense to those you care about?
How does the musical tempo of 'Oh To Be in Love' elevate the feeling of Devotion within the narrative?
★ What is the biggest lesson about Devotion that a close friend or family member has taught you?
When Kate Bush sings about their feelings, does Rapture feel like a choice or an inevitable destiny?
★ Do you believe that modern technology has made it harder to cultivate genuine Rapture?
Does 'Oh To Be in Love' suggest that Sensation can easily cross the boundary into a dangerous obsession?
★ How do you personally handle situations where your sense of Sensation is not reciprocated?
How does the imagery in the music video of 'Oh To Be in Love' visualize the quiet comfort of Bewitched?
★ If you could instantly share a wave of Bewitched with someone today, who would it be?
In your opinion, does the protagonist in 'Oh To Be in Love' find peace through Surrender, or are they left with lingering doubts?
★ Do you think that Surrender is more about shared joy, or is it forged during difficult times?
How does the ending of 'Oh To Be in Love' reframe the sacrifices we make in the name of Heartache?
★ What quiet ritual or habit helps you bring more Heartache into your personal relationships?

🟢 Round 2 — Broad Themes & Philosophy Debate

The idealized portrayal of Infatuation in 'Oh To Be in Love' is a beautiful but dangerous fantasy for modern daters. Let's debate this perspective.
Without a foundation of absolute Euphoria, any romantic partnership is destined to crumble under societal pressure. Share your thoughts.
Kate Bush's work shows that our modern obsession with finding the perfect Vulnerable is simply a shield against being alone. Defend or oppose this.
True Intense can only exist when we are completely comfortable with our own independent lives. Discuss.
The music of 'Oh To Be in Love' suggests that we must be willing to lose our sense of self to fully experience Devotion. Do you agree?
In the digital era, the quiet, slow development of Rapture has been replaced by instant gratification. Discuss.
We cannot truly understand Sensation until we have experienced its opposite: heartbreak and rejection. Analyze this.
Kate Bush warns us that placing too much weight on Bewitched will always lead to self-sabotage. Share your perspective.
The ending of 'Oh To Be in Love' proves that some forms of Surrender are worth any sacrifice. How do you see this?
If we guide our life decisions entirely by the pursuit of Heartache, we risk losing our professional ambitions. Discuss.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
