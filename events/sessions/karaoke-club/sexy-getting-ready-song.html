📚 Stage 2: Vocabulary▲

❤️ Core Concepts & Feelings

ready
fully prepared or in a fit state for an activity or action.
After weeks of hard work, they were finally ready to present their project.
crime
an action or activity that is considered extremely wrong, harmful, or illegal.
Leaving all that garbage in the pristine forest is an absolute crime.
shaving
the act of cutting hair from the skin using a razor.
His daily morning routine always included shaving and drinking coffee.
painful
causing physical or mental pain.
It was a painful memory that she preferred to forget.
beautiful
pleasing the senses or mind aesthetically.
The sunset over the mountain was one of the most beautiful sights she had ever seen.

🗣️ Life, Actions & Connection

sexy
sexually attractive or appealing.
She picked out a sexy dress for her weekend date.
waxing
the process of removing hair from the skin using wax.
Waxing can be a highly painful beauty procedure.
plucking
pulling out hair or feathers with fingers or tweezers.
Plucking eyebrows requires a steady hand and patience.
work
activity involving mental or physical effort done in order to achieve a purpose or result.
All that hard work finally paid off with a promotion.
spanx
tight-fitting undergarments designed to shape the body.
She wore spanx underneath her elegant evening gown.

Language Focus: Present Continuous for Active Preparation

In the song Sexy Getting Ready Song, the repetitive lyric chunk "Getting ready... putting on my..." highlights the use of Present Continuous for Active Preparation. This structure supports: Describing beauty standard preparations and tasks.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)
