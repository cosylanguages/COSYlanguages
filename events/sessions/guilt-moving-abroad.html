Perhaps moving abroad is not just a change of address, but a quiet betrayal of the people and places that made us, a slow tearing of emotional roots. This session explores the psychological and emotional complexities of moving abroad. We will discuss the unspoken immigrant guilt of leaving family behind, the emotional cost of self-actualization, and the search for belonging in a foreign culture. Join us as we examine how to navigate these complex feelings and build a balanced life in a new home.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Self-actualization
the realization or fulfillment of one's talents and potentialities.
Moving to a major global city offered her the career self-actualization she needed.
Estrangement
the state of being alienated or separated from family or culture.
Long physical distance can sometimes lead to emotional estrangement from childhood friends.
Guilt-ridden
deeply troubled by persistent feelings of guilt.
Many expats feel guilt-ridden when celebrating major life milestones away from their parents.
La nostalgia ≠ La modernidad
sentimiento de pena por la lejanía o el tiempo pasado ≠ cualidad de lo moderno o actual.
Sintió una profunda nostalgia por el pueblo antiguo, un contraste total con la fría modernidad.
Alienation
the experience of being isolated or separated from a group.
Not speaking the local language can intensify a feeling of complete social alienation.
Resentment
bitter indignation at being treated unfairly or left behind.
She struggled with subconscious resentment from family members who stayed behind.
Adaptability
the quality of being able to adjust easily to new conditions.
Moving abroad requires high emotional adaptability and openness to change.
Belonging
an affinity for a place, or a feeling of acceptance in a social group.
It can take several years of living in a country to develop a true sense of belonging.
Sacrifice
an act of giving up something valued for the sake of something more important.
Leaving your native country is a massive personal sacrifice for future opportunities.
Reconcile
to find a way of making two different situations or feelings exist together.
Expats must learn how to reconcile their old cultural roots with their new lives.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Think about how living away from home affects your personality.
  • Perhaps moving abroad is not just a change of address, but a quiet betrayal of the people and places that made us. Why do we carry the weight of the home we left behind?
  • Do you get homesick easily, or do you thrive in brand new environments?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
Is leaving your hometown the only way to achieve complete personal and professional self-actualization?
★ What is your highest personal goal that you hope to achieve this year?
How can expats prevent long-term physical distance from turning into complete family estrangement?
★ How often do you call or text your parents and extended family?
Why do people feel so guilt-ridden about choosing their own happiness over their family's comfort?
★ Have you ever made a major life decision that disappointed your family?
How does sweet, positive nostalgia differ from painful, paralyzing homesickness?
★ What physical object or smell makes you feel most nostalgic for your childhood?
How can we support immigrants who are experiencing deep cultural and social alienation?
★ Have you ever entered a social gathering where you felt like an complete outsider?
Why do staying relatives sometimes hold subconscious resentment toward those who move away?
★ Have you ever felt jealous of a friend who achieved success abroad?
How does our level of emotional adaptability affect our speed of language learning?
★ Do you find it easy to adapt to a sudden change of plans?
Is it possible to feel a true sense of belonging in two completely different countries simultaneously?
★ Where is the place in the world where you feel most comfortable and accepted?
What is the most difficult personal sacrifice you have had to make for your career?
★ Have you ever sacrificed a hobby or a trip to focus on your study or work?
How do we reconcile our duty to our aging parents with our personal ambitions?
★ Do you believe that children have a moral obligation to live near their parents?

🟢 Round 2 — Critical Statements▲
Debate
Chasing individual self-actualization is ultimately an act of extreme selfishness.
Physical distance makes emotional estrangement from family members completely inevitable over time.
An immigrant will always feel guilt-ridden no matter how successful they become in their new home.
Excessive nostalgia is a dangerous psychological trap that prevents people from building a happy future.
Total social alienation is a necessary trial that every successful immigrant must endure.
Family resentment is a natural consequence of escaping the narrow boundaries of your hometown.
Emotional adaptability is a static genetic trait that cannot be cultivated in adulthood.
A deep sense of cultural belonging is something that can only be built during childhood.
No career opportunity is worth the personal sacrifice of living away from your aging parents.
It is impossible to reconcile a highly ambitious lifestyle with a stable, traditional family structure.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I am living abroad since five years→I have been living abroad for five years(Present Perfect Continuous + 'for' duration)
I feel guilt about leaving them→I feel guilty about leaving them / I feel guilt over leaving them(Feel + adjective 'guilty' OR noun 'guilt' with preposition 'over/about')
It was hard to adapt to the country→It was hard to adapt to living in the country(Adapt to + gerund/noun)
