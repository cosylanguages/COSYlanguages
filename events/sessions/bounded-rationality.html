Full Article 📖

Perhaps our choices are never fully free, but confined by the limits of our energy, our history, and our fragile focus. This session explores the psychological concept of Bounded Rationality, first proposed by Herbert Simon. We will analyze how human decision-making is limited by cognitive capacity, available information, and time constraints. Participants will discuss how we use shortcuts and heuristics to make choices in our daily lives.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Rationality
the quality of being based on or in accordance with reason or logic.
Perfect rationality would require infinite computing power.
Bounded
limited or restricted within boundaries.
Our physical strength and mental capacities are bounded by biology.
Heuristic
a hands-on or shortcut method that is not guaranteed to be perfect but is practical.
We use the availability heuristic to judge the likelihood of events.
Cognitive
relating to the mental processes of perception, memory, and judgment.
Cognitive limitations prevent us from analyzing all options.
Satisficing
choosing an option that is acceptable or good enough, rather than searching for the absolute best.
Satisficing is a very practical decision-making strategy when time is short.
Optimize
make the best or most effective use of (a situation, opportunity, or resource).
It is impossible to optimize every single choice we make.
Constraint
a limitation or restriction.
Time constraint is a major factor during job interviews.
Bias
prejudice in favor of or against one thing, person, or group compared with another, usually in a way considered to be unfair.
Confirmation bias makes us ignore facts that disagree with our opinion.
Incomplete
not having all the necessary or appropriate parts.
Making a choice based on incomplete data can lead to errors.
Simplify
make something simpler or easier to do or understand.
Our brain uses mental models to simplify a complex reality.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Short introduction to making choices.
  • Perhaps our choices are never fully free, but confined by the limits of our energy, our history, and our fragile focus. How do we make peace with our imperfect decision-making?
  • Have you ever regretted spending too much time comparing options?
🔵 Round 1 — Deep Discussion▲
Questions
Analyze how limitations shape our daily rationality.
What is the difference between perfect rationality and bounded rationality?
★ When was the last time you made a choice that was not 100% rational?
What is a mental heuristic, and why does our brain use them?
★ What mental shortcut do you use when shopping for groceries?
How does cognitive fatigue affect our decision-making capability?
★ Do you find it harder to make decisions in the evening?
What does Herbert Simon mean by the concept of satisficing?
★ Are you a perfectionist, or are you happy with options that are good enough?
Why is it impossible to optimize every single choice?
★ What part of your budget or schedule do you try to optimize?
How does a tight time constraint affect the quality of an exam?
★ How do you handle stressful deadlines at work?
How does cognitive bias lead to bad business choices?
★ Have you ever noticed your own confirmation bias during a debate?
How do we make decisions when we have incomplete information?
★ Have you ever accepted a job offer based on incomplete information?
How do smart marketing campaigns use biases to influence consumers?
★ Do you think you are easily influenced by advertising?
How can we simplify complex decisions to reduce stress?
★ What is your method for simplifying major life decisions?

🟢 Round 2 — Conditional Scenarios▲
Projections
Discuss these hypothetical situations regarding human intelligence.
If a mobile app could completely eliminate cognitive biases, users would make perfect financial choices.
If we had unlimited time to make decisions, we would still use simple heuristics because of mental laziness.
If modern schools taught bounded rationality models, children would become less vulnerable to online advertising.
If we relied on algorithms to optimize all major decisions (marriage, career, home), human satisfaction would decrease.
If cognitive overload continues to increase, satisficing will become the only sustainable way to survive in big cities.
If artificial intelligence has no biological constraints, it will eventually replace all human managers and judges.
If people were forced to make decisions with complete transparency, they would suffer from extreme anxiety.
If we simplified tax laws completely, we would reduce the need for professional financial advice.
If we could download new mental models directly to our brain, our bounded rationality limits would widen.
If consumers were fully rational, the entire luxury fashion industry would go bankrupt within a year.
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
We are bounded with time→We are bounded by time(Use preposition 'by' after passive bounded)
I took a decision fastly→I made a decision quickly(Collocation 'make a decision', adverb 'quickly')
He explained me the theory→He explained the theory to me(Structure of explain + object + to + person)
