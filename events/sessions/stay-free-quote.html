📖 Session Vocabulary

Authentic
being real and true to your own personality, values, and beliefs
Example: He stayed authentic even when others wanted him to change.
Identity
the qualities, beliefs, and characteristics that make a person who they are
Example: Traveling helped her understand her identity better.
Pressure
influence or force that encourages someone to behave in a certain way
Example: Many teenagers feel pressure to fit in with their peers.
Conform
to change your behavior or opinions to match those of a group
Example: He refused to conform to social expectations.
Independent
able to think, act, and make decisions without relying on others
Example: She became more independent after moving to another city.
Values
important principles or beliefs that guide a person's decisions and actions
Example: Honesty and respect are among his strongest values.
Expectation
a belief that something should happen or that someone should behave in a particular way
Example: There is often an expectation that young people attend university.
Individuality
the qualities and characteristics that make someone unique
Example: The company encourages employees to express their individuality.
Freedom
the ability to act, think, or live without unnecessary restrictions
Example: Many people value freedom more than wealth.
Self-expression
the act of showing your thoughts, feelings, or personality through words, actions, or creativity
Example: Art gives people a powerful way of self-expression.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Choice & Authenticity
  • What does "staying free" mean to you personally?
  • Do you think we have as much choice in life as we believe?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis▲
Questions
1. Do people really have no choice in many life situations, or is it more about feeling under pressure?
★ Have you ever felt that you had no real choice because of pressure from others?
2. Why do people often conform even when they don’t agree?
★ Have you ever conformed just to avoid problems or conflict?
3. Is it true that most of our life decisions are influenced by expectations from others?
★ What expectations have influenced your own decisions the most?
4. What does it mean to still be authentic when you feel you have limited choices?
★ Can you think of a moment when you tried to stay authentic?
5. Do people confuse lack of freedom with difficult or stressful decisions?
★ When was the last time you felt “not free,” even though you actually had options?
6. How does pressure from society affect a person’s identity over time?
★ Do you feel your identity has changed because of other people’s influence?
7. Is listening to your inner voice more important than following others?
★ Have you ever ignored your inner voice and later regretted it?
8. Can self-expression help people feel free even when their choices are limited?
★ How do you usually express yourself when you feel restricted?
9. Are our values strong enough to protect us from external pressure?
★ Have your values ever helped you make a difficult decision?
10. What does it really mean to “stay free” if life is full of limits and expectations?
★ In your life, what does “staying free” look like in a real situation?

🟢 Round 2 — Future Focus▲
Agree or Disagree
1. In the future, people may not notice when AI starts choosing small things for them (what to watch, buy, or even think about).
★ Would you trust AI to make small daily decisions for you?
2. Future people might feel “free,” but their choices will actually be guided by invisible systems and algorithms.
★ Do you already feel that apps sometimes influence your choices?
3. In the future, pressure to show a “perfect life” online may become stronger than real-life pressure from family.
★ Do you think social media already creates this kind of pressure for you or others?
4. People in the future may conform not to society, but to algorithms that decide what is “normal.”
★ Have you ever followed something online just because “everyone seems to do it”?
5. In the future, students might be trained to “fit systems” rather than develop their own identity.
★ Do you feel education today helps or limits your personal identity?
6. Some future societies may see authenticity as a problem, because it is harder to control than behavior.
★ When do you feel most authentic in your own life?
7. In the future, people might lose their inner voice because they will rely more on external feedback than their own feelings.
★ Do you often trust yourself more, or other people’s opinions?
8. Future self-expression might be “filtered” so much that people no longer know what is real and what is performance.
★ Do you ever feel that people online are “performing” instead of expressing themselves?
9. In the future, values may change faster than identity, making people unsure what they truly believe.
★ Have your own values ever changed because of new experiences or people?
10. The biggest future paradox may be this: people will have unlimited choice, but feel completely stuck and not free.
★ Have you ever had many choices but still felt confused or unable to decide?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
None of us really have a choice→None of us really have a choice(Keep)
I am more free today→I am freer today / I have more freedom today(Comparatives)
It depends of the expectation→It depends on the expectation(Depends on)
I am not agree with this quote→I don't agree with this quote(Agree as a verb)
I don't conform since I was a child→I haven't conformed since I was a child(Present Perfect + since)
