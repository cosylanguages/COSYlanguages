📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Emotional support
help that makes someone feel calmer or happier emotionally
Example: Pets often give emotional support when people feel stressed.
Companionship
having someone (or something) that makes you feel less alone
Example: A dog can provide companionship every day.
Responsibility
duties you must take care of regularly
Example: Feeding and walking a pet teaches responsibility.
Loneliness
the feeling of being unhappy because you are alone
Example: Pets can reduce loneliness in older people.
Routine
a regular set of daily activities
Example: Dogs help owners build a daily routine.
Affection
a feeling of love and care for someone or something
Example: Many people show affection to their pets.
Adoption
the act of taking an animal from a shelter into your home
Example: Adoption helps homeless animals find families.
Training
teaching an animal to behave in a certain way
Example: Dog training takes time and patience.
Allergy
a physical reaction to something that makes you sick
Example: Some people cannot have pets because of allergies.
Welfare
the health and happiness of animals or people
Example: Animal welfare is an important ethical issue.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Pet Person
  • Are you a dog person, a cat person, or neither?
  • What is the first animal you ever touched?
🔵 Round 1 — My life WITH pets (10 Questions)▲
Personal Depth
1. How do pets provide emotional support that feels different from human advice?
★ Do you prefer comfort or solutions when you are upset?
2. Why can companionship from pets feel more stable than some friendships?
★ What makes a relationship feel “stable” for you?
3. How does caring for a pet slowly reshape a person’s identity and habits?
★ What habit has changed your personality the most?
4. In what surprising ways do pets control or influence daily routines?
★ Do you feel your routines control you or you control them?
5. Why do people sometimes feel safer expressing affection to animals than to people?
★ Is emotional expression easier or harder for you?
6. Why does adoption often create a stronger emotional story than simply owning a pet?
★ Do you value stories or outcomes more in life decisions?
7. How can training a pet reveal a person’s patience, frustration, and empathy?
★ When do you notice your own patience breaking?
8. Why do pets often become invisible “social bridges” in unexpected situations?
★ Have you ever experienced a random social connection?
9. Why can silence with a pet feel more comforting than conversation with people?
★ When do you prefer silence over talking?
10. How do pets quietly shape what people define as “home”?
★ What turns a place into a real home for you?

🟢 Round 2 — My Life WITHOUT pets (10 Units)▲
Conceptual Analysis
1. If emotional support from animals never existed in society, what kinds of emotional support systems would naturally become dominant instead?
★ Do you think emotional support is something we build individually or something society designs for us?
2. Without animal-based companionship as a concept in everyday life, how might people redefine what “companionship” actually means?
★ Do you think companionship today is more emotional or more practical?
3. If caregiving for animals had never become part of human behavior, how might the idea of “daily responsibility” look completely different?
★ What responsibility in your life feels most identity-shaping?
4. Without animal-related routines ever forming culturally, how might humans structure time differently at home and work?
★ Do you think routines are created or discovered?
5. If non-verbal affection toward animals was never part of human expression, how might emotional communication between people evolve differently?
★ Are words or behavior more trustworthy to you?
6. If adoption of animals had never become a social practice, how might humanitarian focus shift in society overall?
★ Which type of help do you think society values most today: emotional or practical?
7. Without the historical idea of training animals, what kinds of discipline systems might humans have developed earlier for themselves?
★ Do you think discipline is internal or learned externally?
8. If animals were never integrated into public social interaction patterns, how might spontaneous interactions between strangers evolve?
★ What usually makes you talk to a stranger?
9. If a whole category of natural emotional comfort never entered human life, what alternative coping mechanisms might have become culturally central instead?
★ Do you think modern coping is more healthy or more artificial?
10. If society had never developed relationships with animals at all, how might the definition of “well-being” be fundamentally different today?
★ What do you think is the core ingredient of a good life: stability, meaning, or connection?
