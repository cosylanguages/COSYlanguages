Atomic Habits Info 📖

Our lives are largely defined by the small, repetitive actions we perform every day—our habits. This session examines the science of habit formation and the powerful "cue-routine-reward" loop that drives our behavior. We will discuss practical techniques for breaking bad habits and building new, positive ones that lead to long-term success.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Willpower
the ability to control your own thoughts and actions
Example: It takes a lot of willpower to quit smoking.
Consistency
the quality of always behaving in the same way
Example: Consistency is key to long-term success.
Discipline
the practice of training people to obey rules or a code of behavior
Example: Maintaining a daily routine requires discipline.
Trigger
an event that causes a particular action or process
Example: For many, coffee is a trigger to start working.
Reward
a thing given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
Example: Treat yourself to a small reward after a productive week.
Incremental
relating to or denoting an increase or addition
Example: Small, incremental changes lead to massive results.
Routine
a sequence of actions regularly followed
Example: A morning routine can set a positive tone for the day.
Automation
making a process automatic
Example: The goal of a habit is the automation of behavior.
Compound
to make up or constitute; to increase by adding to
Example: Small habits compound over time.
Subconscious
concerning the part of the mind of which one is not fully aware
Example: Most of our daily habits are subconscious.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Habit Loops
  • What is one habit you do every single day without thinking?
  • Is it harder to start a good habit or break a bad one?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
1. What is the biggest habit you've successfully changed?
★ How did you feel before and after the change?
2. Why do most New Year's resolutions fail by February?
3. How do "triggers" work in our daily environment?
4. What is the role of "rewards" in forming a lasting habit?
5. How long does it actually take to form a new habit? (Is it 21 days?)
6. Is "motivation" reliable for long-term change?
7. How can we use "habit stacking" to build a new routine?
8. Does our identity influence our habits, or do our habits form our identity?
9. How can we make bad habits "invisible" in our environment?
10. What is the "one-minute rule" for starting something difficult?

🟢 Round 2 — Conditional Practice (2nd, 3rd, Mixed)▲
Grammar Focus
1. If you had started your most important habit five years earlier, how would your life be different today?
2. If schools taught habit management as a core subject, would the younger generation be more productive?
3. If you were able to delete any bad habit instantly with a brain implant, would you use it even if it had side effects?
4. If you hadn't been so disciplined this morning, what tasks would you have failed to complete?
5. If AI managed all of our daily routines, would we lose our sense of personal responsibility?
6. If we lived in a society where "willpower" didn't exist, how would people achieve their goals?
7. If you were to change your environment completely, which of your habits would survive the transition?
8. If governments hadn't used "nudges" to influence public health habits, would the population be less healthy today?
9. If you had a limited "willpower budget" every day, how would you prioritize your morning activities?
10. If you were too disciplined, do you think you would miss out on the spontaneous joy of life?
✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲

I suggest to start small→I suggest starting small(Suggest + gerund)
It depends of my willpower→It depends on my willpower(Depends on)
I'm not used to wake up early→I'm not used to waking up early(Be used to + -ing)
It's a very difficult habit→It's a very difficult habit to break
