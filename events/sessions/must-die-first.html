Psychology Today 📖

The quote "You must die first before you can truly live" suggests that significant personal growth often requires letting go of old identities and habits. This session explores the concept of metaphorical death as a catalyst for profound life changes. We will discuss the courage needed to face our own limitations and the potential for rebirth that lies on the other side of failure.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Transformation
a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance.
The caterpillar's transformation into a butterfly is a classic symbol of rebirth.
Metaphorical
characteristic of or relating to metaphor; figurative.
When he spoke of "dying first," he was using a metaphorical expression for personal growth.
Catalyst
a person or thing that precipitates an event.
The loss of his job served as a catalyst for him to finally start his own business.
Identity
the fact of being who or what a person or thing is.
Moving to a new country often forces people to re-examine their sense of identity.
Renunciation
the formal rejection of something, typically a belief, claim, or course of action.
Her renunciation of her old lifestyle allowed her to find peace in a simpler way of living.
Limitation
a restriction or a point beyond which something does not or may not extend.
Recognizing your own limitation is the first step toward overcoming it.
Resilience
the capacity to recover quickly from difficulties.
The city's resilience after the disaster was an inspiration to the entire nation.
Paradigm
a typical example or pattern of something; a model.
The discovery shifted the scientific paradigm and changed how we see the universe.
Evolution
the gradual development of something.
The evolution of his artistic style over twenty years is quite remarkable.
Vulnerability
the state of being exposed to the possibility of being harmed.
Opening up to new experiences requires a level of emotional vulnerability.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
Analyze the quote: "You must die first before you can truly live."
  • What is one habit or belief you have already "killed" in your life?
  • Do you think fear of change is actually a fear of losing yourself?
🔵 Round 1 — General Discussion▲
Questions
How does a major life transformation often feel like a loss at the beginning?
In what ways can a metaphorical death of an old ego lead to better relationships?
What has been the most powerful catalyst for change in your professional life?
Why is it so painful to let go of an identity that no longer serves us?
Is the renunciation of material success necessary to find true spiritual fulfillment?
How does hitting a physical or mental limitation force you to reinvent your approach?
How can we develop the resilience needed to "die" and be "reborn" multiple times in one life?
What current paradigm in your industry do you think needs to be completely destroyed?
How does the evolution of your values over time make you a different person than you were ten years ago?
Why is vulnerability the key to starting over after a significant failure?

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
I must to die first→I must die first(Modal verbs + bare infinitive)
It is a thorough transformation→It is a thorough transformation(Spelling: thorough)
I'm not used to take risks→I'm not used to taking risks(Used to + -ing)
I am agree with you→I agree with you(Verb agreement)
It is depend on you→It depends on you(Verb vs Adjective)
