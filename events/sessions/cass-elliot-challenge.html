💬 Stage 4: Discussion▲

🔵 Round 1 — Lyric Analysis & Discussion

How does the narrator in 'Make Your Own Kind of Music' view themselves? What internal conflicts or aspirations do they reveal?
★ When was the last time you felt a conflict between how you feel inside and how you must act?
What is the main message of 'Make Your Own Kind of Music' about authenticity and self-expression? What does it teach us about standing up for ourselves?
★ How easy or difficult is it for you to speak up and defend your opinions in a crowd?
What feelings of determination, pride, or vulnerability are emphasized in Cass Elliot's vocal delivery?
★ Which of these emotions do you find most powerful and inspiring in art or music?

🟢 Round 2 — Broad Themes & Philosophy Debate

In a world that often demands conformity, how can we successfully discover and maintain our true identity?
How should we define success and self-worth? Is it determined by external approval or internal satisfaction?
What are the best ways to cope with expectations from family, friends, or society while remaining true to oneself?
✨ Stage 5: Language Focus▲

The Modal Verb 'Must' for Obligation and Deduction

We use 'must' to express a strong obligation or a logical deduction directly from the lyrics.

Let's Practise — Three Examples:
  • We must dare to make our own music.
  • The song is so beautiful, she must be a great artist.
  • You must listen carefully to fill in the missing gaps.

Imagine the sequel of this song: Role-play a conversation between the main characters 5 years later.

✏️ Teacher's Note (Linguistic Corrections)▲
There is a big improvement of my English → There is a big improvement in my English (Use 'in' after 'improvement' to express domain.)
It was a great relief of hearing the news → It was a great relief to hear the news (Use 'relief' + infinitive 'to hear'.)
She is in the choir since three years → She has been in the choir for three years (Use present perfect + 'for' to show duration of state.)

💬 Stage 4: Discussion▲

🔵 Round 1 — Lyric Analysis & Discussion

What kind of crisis or tragedy are the characters in 'It's Getting Better' facing? How are they attempting to survive?
★ How do you usually respond to unexpected major challenges or sudden changes in your life?
What is the main message of 'It's Getting Better' regarding the human cost of conflict, pain, or loss?
★ How do you process feelings of sadness or distress when looking at world news?
How do the dark melody and Cass Elliot's performance convey deep sadness, anger, or a desperate search for hope?
★ Is there a specific song or piece of art that has helped you process a personal loss?

🟢 Round 2 — Broad Themes & Philosophy Debate

What are the long-term emotional and psychological consequences of war, conflict, or severe trauma on individuals and families?
What are the most effective ways for individuals and communities to support each other and heal in the aftermath of a crisis?
Why do people sometimes turn to dangerous coping mechanisms or illusions to escape harsh realities, and how can they find real support?
