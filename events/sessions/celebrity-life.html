Focus

Celebrity Culture & Fame

The allure of fame often hides the significant personal costs of living in the public eye. This session examines the paradox of celebrity life—where extreme visibility meets a total loss of privacy. We will discuss the benefits of recognition versus the freedom of anonymity, imagining how our own lives might transform under the constant gaze of the media.

📖 Session Vocabulary

Public figure
a well-known person in society or media
Example: Being a public figure means constantly attracting attention.
Fandom
a community of fans who strongly support someone famous
Example: Her fandom defended her online after the interview.
Influence
the ability to affect people’s opinions or behavior
Example: Celebrities have huge influence on young audiences.
Celebrity culture
society’s strong interest in famous people and their lives
Example: Social media has completely changed celebrity culture.
Paparazzi
photographers who follow celebrities to take private photos
Example: The actor escaped from the paparazzi through the back door.
Role model
a person people admire and want to imitate
Example: Many athletes become role models for teenagers.
Tabloid
media that focuses on gossip, scandals, and celebrity news
Example: The tabloid published shocking rumors about the singer.
Fame
the state of being famous and widely recognized
Example: Fame can completely change a person’s lifestyle.
Privacy
freedom from public attention or observation
Example: Some celebrities move abroad to protect their privacy.
Endorsement
public support or promotion of a product by a famous person
Example: The company paid millions for the celebrity endorsement.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
The Star Effect
  • If you could have dinner with any celebrity (alive or dead), who would it be?
  • What is the most "celebrity-like" thing you have ever done?
🔵 Round 1 — My Life With▲
Personal Perspective
1. Celebrities often become important role models for young people.
★ Who was a role model for you when you were younger?
2. Modern celebrity culture makes entertainment feel more exciting and global.
★ Do you think entertainment would feel less interesting without famous people?
3. Some public figures use their influence to support charities or social causes.
★ Should celebrities speak more about world problems?
4. Large online fandoms can create strong communities and friendships.
★ Have you ever joined a fandom or online fan community?
5. Celebrity endorsements strongly affect what people buy and wear.
★ Have you ever bought something because a celebrity promoted it?
6. The lives of celebrities shown in tabloids can be entertaining for the public.
★ Why do people enjoy reading celebrity gossip?
7. Some celebrities lose their privacy because of constant media attention.
★ Could you personally handle that kind of public life?
8. The paparazzi help satisfy public curiosity about famous people.
★ Do you think paparazzi should have stricter limits?
9. Many people dream about fame because it looks glamorous and exciting.
★ Why do you think fame attracts so many people?
10. Celebrities can influence fashion, language, and even opinions through their influence online.
★ Do celebrities shape society more today than in the past?

🟢 Round 2 — My Life Without▲
Speculative Frontiers
1. Without traditional celebrities, society might shift admiration toward “micro-heroes” — everyday people whose actions are celebrated locally in real time.
★ Would you prefer local heroes you could actually meet instead of global stars?
2. Fame might not disappear, but become temporary and rotating, where people become “famous for a week” based on one meaningful act, then return to normal life.
★ Would short-term fame feel fairer than lifelong celebrity status?
3. Instead of actors and singers, we might follow AI-generated personalities that adapt perfectly to our tastes and moods.
★ Would you trust an artificial “perfect celebrity”?
4. Social media could evolve into skill-based fame systems, where people are admired only for verified abilities (teaching, cooking, problem-solving).
★ Would this make admiration more honest or less exciting?
5. Entertainment might shift from celebrities to collective creators, where films, music, and stories are made anonymously by global groups.
★ Would art lose identity or become more democratic?
6. Instead of paparazzi, we might have “attention algorithms” that decide what becomes visible in society, replacing human obsession with automated selection.
★ Would you trust an algorithm to decide what is “important”?
7. Fashion and trends could be shaped by real-time crowd data, where styles emerge instantly from millions of ordinary people rather than influencers.
★ Would trend creation become more chaotic or more creative?
8. Without celebrity culture, people might build emotional connections with fictional universes, treating characters as “real public figures.”
★ Are fictional heroes a better source of inspiration than real people?
9. Instead of tabloids, we might have “life transparency platforms”, where everyone voluntarily shares fragments of their lives.
★ Would a more transparent society feel free or uncomfortable?
10. Humanity might still generate symbols of admiration, but they could be ideas, movements, or achievements rather than people.
★ Should societies celebrate ideas more than individuals?
