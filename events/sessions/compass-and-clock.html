The Greatest Quotes

The Compass and the Clock

25 June 2025

← Back to Club

Duration

60 minutes

Languages

🇬🇧 🇫🇷 🇪🇸 🇮🇹 🇷🇺

Level

Intermediate (B1)

Quote

“The compass was invented before the clock because direction was more important than how long it took to get there.”

📖 Session Vocabulary (10 Units)

Direction
the path or course someone chooses to follow
Example: Finding the right direction in life is often difficult.
Destination
the place where someone wants to arrive
Example: Success may not be the final destination.
Journey
the process of traveling from one place to another
Example: Personal growth is often a long journey.
Purpose
the reason for doing something
Example: Having a clear purpose can motivate people.
Goal
something a person wants to achieve
Example: She worked hard to reach her goal.
Progress
movement toward a better situation or result
Example: Small daily actions can lead to great progress.
Priority
something considered more important than other things
Example: Family is a priority for many people.
Meaningful
important and valuable in a personal way
Example: Many people want meaningful work.
Fulfillment
a feeling of satisfaction from achieving something important
Example: Helping others can bring fulfillment.
Path
a way of living or developing
Example: Everyone follows a different path in life.

🎙️ Discussion Structure

🟠 Warm-up▲
  • What do you think was the inventor's main point with this quote?
  • In your daily life, are you more focused on the "clock" (time/efficiency) or the "compass" (direction/purpose)?
🔵 Round 1 — Analysis (10 Questions)▲
Questions
1. Is choosing the right direction in life more important than reaching success quickly?
★ Have you ever changed direction even though it meant delaying your goal?
2. Can a person enjoy the journey even if they never reach their original destination?
★ Has an unexpected change ever led you somewhere better than you planned?
3. Should adults regularly reconsider their purpose, or should they stay committed to one life path?
★ Has your sense of purpose changed over the years?
4. Which deserves more attention: making continuous progress or achieving one major goal?
★ Are you someone who celebrates small progress or only big achievements?
5. Can someone have a successful career without finding fulfillment?
★ What brings you more fulfillment: your work, your hobbies, or your relationships?
6. Should financial security always be the top priority, or can other priorities be more important?
★ If you had complete financial freedom, what would become your biggest priority?
7. Is following your own path more difficult today because of social media and other people's expectations?
★ Have you ever felt pressure to follow someone else's path instead of your own?
8. Is a meaningful life created by careful planning or by unexpected opportunities?
★ What unexpected opportunity has had the biggest impact on your life?
9. Does knowing your destination always help you make better decisions?
★ Do you usually plan several years ahead, or do you prefer adapting as life changes?
10. If you had to choose only one, would you rather always know the right direction or always know how much time it will take to reach your goal?
★ Which would reduce stress in your life more?
🟣 Let's Speak Together▲

Debate the following statement as a group:

"It is better to be lost on a beautiful path than to arrive at a destination that has no meaning."
🟢 Round 2 — Future Focus (10 Statements)▲
Agree or Disagree?
1. As AI becomes better at planning our lives, people will stop asking whether they are moving in the right direction and only focus on efficiency.
★ Would that be a positive or dangerous future?
2. Future generations will reach more goals than any generation before them but experience less fulfillment because they never chose those goals themselves.
★ Who influences your biggest goals today?
3. One day, society will judge success by the speed of people's progress, even if their journey has no real purpose.
★ Do you already see this happening?
4. In the future, changing your life's path several times will be considered wiser than following one fixed direction forever.
★ How many completely different careers could you imagine having?
5. Technology will eventually tell us the fastest route to every destination, but it will never answer where we should actually go.
★ Who or what should help people choose their direction?
6. As life becomes more comfortable, choosing the right priority will become more difficult than solving practical problems.
★ What priorities do you think future adults will struggle with most?
7. Future societies will discover that endless progress without a clear purpose creates unhappy people.
★ Can you think of examples where success doesn't bring happiness?
8. In the future, many people will realize too late that they climbed the wrong ladder because they never questioned their destination.
★ What helps people avoid this mistake?
9. The greatest achievement of the future will not be traveling farther but helping people find a more meaningful direction in life.
★ Which invention could help people live more meaningful lives?
10. A hundred years from now, the wisdom in this quote will be even more important than it is today.
★ Which part of the quote—the compass or the clock—do you think future society is most likely to forget, and why?
✏️ Discussion (Mistakes)▲
I am agree with the quote→I agree with the quote("Agree" is a verb)
He give good advices→He gives good advice(Uncountable "advice")
If I will go there, I am happy→If I go there, I will be happy(First conditional)
It is more better for me→It is better for me(Double comparative)
