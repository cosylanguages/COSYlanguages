English — Grammar Reference
Verb TO BE — Present Simple Affirmative, Negative & Question · Essential forms
✅ Affirmative
I am I am a teacher.
You / We / They are We are at home.
He / She / It is She is French.
❌ Negative
I am not I am not tired.
You / We / They are not They aren't here.
He / She / It is not He isn't ready.
❓ Question
I Am I …? Am I late?
You / We / They Are you …? Are you ready?
He / She / It Is he …? Is she a doctor?
Short answers: Yes, I am. / No, I 'm not.  |  Yes, she is. / No, she isn't.

Verb TO HAVE — Present
✅ Affirmative
I / You / We / They have I have a car.
He / She / It has She has a cat.
❌ Negative — use don't / doesn't
I / You / We / They don't have We don't have time.
He / She / It doesn't have He doesn't have money.
Word Order — Questions, Affirmatives & Negatives
❓ Questions
What is it?
Is it coffee?
✅ Affirmative   ❌ Negative
Yes, it is coffee.
No, it is not / isn't coffee.
If you have more sentences, the word order stays the same:
It isn't coffee, but it is tea.
It is a coffee and a tea.
Present Simple — Regular Verbs
📌 Use: habits, routines, general truths
I / You / We / They work I work every day.
He / She / It works  (+s) She works in Paris.
❌ Negative — use don't / doesn't
I / You / We / They don't work I don't work on Sundays.
He / She / It doesn't work He doesn't like coffee.
❓ Question — use Do / Does
I / You / We / They Do you …? Do you speak French?
He / She / It Does he / she …? Does she live here?
⚠️ With doesn't / does, the verb has no -s: ✅ She does like it.   ❌ She does likes it.
There is / There are
Singular → There is / There's There's a park near here.
Plural → There are There are three cafés.
❌ Negative
Singular There isn't There isn't a supermarket.
Plural There aren't There aren't any buses.
❓ Question
Singular Is there …? Is there a hotel?
Plural Are there …? Are there any taxis?
Articles — a / an / the
a consonant sound → a car, a book
an vowel sound → an apple, an hour
the specific / known → the car I bought
No article → general: I love coffee. She plays guitar.
Can / Can't — Ability & Permission
✅ Ability I can swim.
❌ Inability She can't drive.
❓ Question Can you play piano?
⚠️ can + infinitive (no to): ✅ I can swim  ❌ I can to swim
Plural Nouns
Regular noun + -s (book → books)
Ending in -ch, -sh, -x, -s noun + -es (bus → buses)
Ending in -y drop -y + -ies (city → cities)
Present Perfect — have / has + past participle
📌 Use: experience (ever/never), recent news (just), unfinished time (for/since), result visible now
I / You / We / They have + past participle I have lived here for 5 years.
He / She / It has + past participle She has just arrived.
❌ Negative
I / You / We / They haven't + p.p. I haven't seen that film.
He / She / It hasn't + p.p. He hasn't called yet.
❓ Question — Have / Has + subject + p.p.
Experience Have you ever …? Have you ever been to Japan?
Present Perfect vs Past Simple:   "I have been to Paris." (experience, no time)  |  "I went to Paris last year." (specific time → past simple)
Conditionals — Types 1, 2 & 3
1st Conditional — real / possible future
Structure If + present simple, … will + infinitive
If it rains, I will stay home.    If you study, you will pass.
2nd Conditional — unreal present / unlikely
Structure If + past simple, … would + infinitive
If I had more money, I would travel.    (But I don't have the money — it's imaginary.)
3rd Conditional — impossible past / regret
Structure If + had + p.p., … would have + p.p.
If I had studied, I would have passed.    (But I didn't study — it's too late.)
⚠️ Never use would in the if-clause: ✅ If I had time…   ❌ If I would have time…

Articles — le / la / les / un / une
Défini (The)
Masculinle café / l'ami
Fémininla banque / l'eau
Plurielles hôtels
Indéfini (A/An)
Masculinun homme
Fémininune femme
Plurieldes livres
Pluriel des noms
Règle généralemot + s
Exempleun ami → des amis
-au / -eau+ x (un gâteau → des gâteaux)
Le -s et le -x final ne se prononcent pas.
Passé Composé — avoir / être + participe passé
📌 Avec AVOIR (la plupart des verbes)
j'ai parléJ'ai parlé à Marie.
il / ellea mangéElle a mangé une pizza.
📌 Avec ÊTRE (DR MRS VANDERTRAMP + pronominaux)
je (masc.)suis alléJe suis allé à Paris.
je (fém.)suis alléeJe suis allée à Paris.
Avec être, le participe s'accorde avec le sujet : il est allé / elle est allée / ils sont allés / elles sont allées

Русский — Справочник по грамматике
Глагол БЫТЬ — настоящее время (отсутствует)
📌 В настоящем времени глагол «быть» обычно опускается
я—Я учитель. (I am a teacher.)
ты—Ты дома? (You at home?)
он / она / оно—Она врач. (She is a doctor.)
В прошедшем и будущем времени: я был / я буду
У меня есть (I have)
I haveУ меня естьУ меня есть машина.
You haveУ тебя естьУ тебя есть вопрос?
He hasУ него естьУ него есть собака.
She hasУ неё естьУ неё есть время.
❌ Отрицание — У меня нет... (+ Генитив)
I don't haveУ меня нетУ меня нет денег.
Порядок слов — утверждение, отрицание, вопрос
✅ Утверждение
Этокофе.
❌ Отрицание — не перед глаголом
Этоне кофе.
❓ Вопрос — интонация или вопросительное слово
Это кофе?Что это?
В русском языке порядок слов гибкий, но не всегда стоит перед глаголом.
